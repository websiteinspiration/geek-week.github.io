To write selling Web copy, you must know your audience, goal and product. Ask five questions:

  1. What is the problem? ¬¨‚Äì What issue does the target audience want resolved? To get an answer, you may have to tell your audience members that they have a problem and then demonstrate that you care about them enough to solve the dilemma for them.

Web copy based on these issues should generate emotion. For example, ask if customers are broke, depressed, lonely or fed up with their jobs. Generating Results

Good Web copy is interesting, enticing and actionable. It clearly illustrates the benefits and attributes of your offering and shows prospects how your product could improve their lives. The Power of E-Mail

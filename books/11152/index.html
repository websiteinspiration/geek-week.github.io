The Era of Commoditization

Growing commoditization explains why chief marketing officers (CMOs) have job tenures of about two years. If you are a marketer who wants to sell a product, nothing is wrong with a simple, obvious approach. This is not a new concept. Robert R.   • Simple ‚Äì The right solution to a problem will be plainly apparent. Too often, people seek complex solutions, when simpler approaches would resolve their issues.

  • Easy to explain ‚Äì An obvious concept is easy to write in brief. The right idea should be simple to say in short words assembled into short paragraphs. If you cannot express the idea succinctly, it probably is too complex to be understood or easily implemented.
  • Instinctive ‚Äì People should see the idea‚Äôs merit quickly. Their first reaction should be, ‚ÄúWhy didn‚Äôt we think of that?‚Äù If people react slowly or ask for explanation, revisit the idea: It is too complex.

‚ÄúBusiness complexity is fed by the ever-increasing amount of information that is being piped into the business world.‚Äù

In many cases, the obvious solution is a matter of common sense. But because people distrust their own feelings, they complicate solutions. Common sense doesn‚Äôt require any special technical knowledge. Anyone can appreciate a good idea.

They teach jargon instead of demonstrating the clear utility of common sense. To appreciate the obvious, focus on reality, not on your ego. Look at things in context. Listen to others. Be a bit cynical to filter out the prejudices expressed by people who are pursuing their own agendas.

A company‚Äôs appreciation for the obvious starts with the CEO. Re-Evaluating Technology

Too much technology impedes progress because it does not allow people the quiet time they need to think. People tend to react to e-mail messages quickly and to depend too much on the latest gadgets.

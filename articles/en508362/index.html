High Performance TSDB benchmark VictoriaMetrics vs TimescaleDB vs InfluxDB

VictoriaMetrics, TimescaleDB, and InfluxDB were compared in a previous dataset article with a billion data points belonging to 40K unique time series.







A few years ago was the era of Zabbix. Each bare metal server had no more than a few indicators - processor usage, RAM usage, disk usage and network usage. Thus, metrics from thousands of servers can fit in 40 thousand unique time series, and Zabbix can use MySQL as a backend for these time series :)







Currently, a single node_exporter with default configurations provides more than 500 metrics on the average host. There are many exporters for various databases, web servers, hardware systems, etc. All of them provide many useful indicators. All more and more applications are beginning to expose the various indicators over. There are Kubernetes with clusters and pods that reveal a lot of metrics. This causes servers to expose thousands of unique metrics to the host. Thus, the unique 40K time series is no longer high power. It is becoming a mainstream that should be easily handled by any modern TSDB on a single server.







What is the large number of unique time series at the moment? Probably 400K or 4M? Or 40m? Let's compare modern TSDBs with these numbers.







Benchmark Installation

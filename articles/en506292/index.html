Successful launch. 40th in 2020. 13th from China. Oceanographic satellite Haiyang-1D

Vyacheslav Ermolin, June 10, 2020



Mission:
Oceanographic satellite of the Haiyang series ("Ocean"). Ocean monitoring and surveillance. Optical characteristics: chlorophyll concentration, surface temperature, observation of sea ice, etc. AIS system.



Motto:
 ‚ÄúThe First Chinese Civil Oceanographic Satellite System‚Äù

Time and place of launch:
June 10 from 18:31 UTC (06/11/2020 from 02:31 BJT).
Taiyuan Cosmodrome, launch pad LC9.

Booster:
CZ-2C - Chang Zheng-2C. Lightweight two-stage launch vehicle.

Payload:
45721 / 2020-036A. Haiyang-1-04 (HY-1D). The satellite weighs 442 kg.

COCTS: 10-channel optical multispectral scanner with a resolution of 50 m and a capture band of 2900 km. Weight is 50 kg.

CZI: 4-channel UV scanner with a resolution of 1.1 km and a capture bandwidth of 500 km. Weight is 15 kg.
Optional spectrometer. Solar battery and batteries. A drive for storing information. AIS marine tracking equipment.

Orbit:
Solar-synchronous orbit, 770 x 786 km x 98.438 ¬∞. Global coverage of the territory 1 day (COCTS) or 7 days (CZI). To work in tandem with the HY-1C satellite of the same type.

Interesting:

  • China's 13th launch this year. Two start up emergency.
  • The 334th launch of the Chang Zheng launch vehicles (The Great Campaign).
  • The 33rd launch of the Chang Zheng-2C launch vehicle. Two start up emergency.
  • 6th launch of the Haiyang ("Ocean") oceanographic satellite.
  • The cost of launching the launch vehicle is about $ 30 million.
  • The cost of withdrawing 1 kg of payload on LEO is at least $ 8,000.

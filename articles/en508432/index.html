PhD thesis and school project



The film does not describe your project in a very detailed way from a technical point of view, tell us in more detail how it started and what came later?



It all started with my father. He is a doctor and researcher. When I was in eighth grade, he managed to interest me in his work related to movements in the hip joint. At that moment, I began to be interested in robotics, and my father suggested that the results of his research could be an innovation in this area and that they could be used in a school project.







Then we learned that there are various contests and I decided to participate in them. I am not shy that I realized the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãmy father and always try to mention him. It would be very strange if an eighth-grader who did not study anatomy had the idea. I started by studying the ball joint, which can be called an analogue of the human hip joint.



Tell me more about this idea that interests you?



An idea from the field of biomechanics. In his dissertation, my father wrote about the role that the ligament of the hip joint plays in the development of disorders in arthrosis. At the same time, he studied in detail the features of the functioning of this ligament and its role in movement. In our joint work, we suggested that the use of a mechanism that simulates the operation of the ligament will help change the nature of the movements of biomorphic robots and reduce energy costs for making movements.







At that time, I was very impressed with the video of Japanese robots and the promising developments of Boston Dynamics. And I decided that this is what I want to do in life. At the moment when the school offered to do some kind of project, I decided to consult with my father and said that I want to develop a robot or something similar. And he offered me this idea. The key to this project was the use of a ball joint with flexible elements, the prototypes of which were just the ligaments of the hip joint. Those. in fact, then I began to engage in engineering bionics.



The prototype that I completed in 10th grade was not a robot in the usual sense. It was an isomorphic biped walking machine. In the process of creation, I studied and borrowed a lot from the mechanical models that my father built for his dissertation. The machine was needed in order to assess the future prospects of the project. It was quite simple, walked with levers connected to the prototype by a system of cables and rods. We tried to make a connection that partially repeated the physics of the hip joint.







As far as I understand, you did the first project before you completed the basic school course in human anatomy. Did you have problems understanding what is written in your father‚Äôs dissertation, how did you get out of this situation?



I was less attached to biology (anatomy), there is more physics. This is not to say that I needed to understand my father‚Äôs thesis completely, the idea was partially borrowed from it, but the dissertation itself was not at all connected with the project.



I moved from simple to complex. Started by building graphs of the center of mass and determining its trajectory. This was necessary to build a theoretical model of the future walking machine. Throughout the work on the project, of course, I came across moments that I did not understand.







But most of the questions still concerned physics. With these questions regularly got teachers. For example, in the 11th grade there were problems with calculating the lever in the hinge with a single-bearing step period.



How many prototypes were made and how long did it take?

There were five prototypes in total, they were made over 3 years. The first prototypes I assembled with my dad, due to their mechanical complexity, as well as the complexity of materials (steel, plywood).







I designed and manufactured part of the prototypes myself within the walls of the Quantorium Technopark, and the teachers helped me with the technical part of the work (directly with printing and laser cutting). But at the same time, the electronics that my last 3 models were equipped with was completely executed and thought out by me, as well as the software for it.



"You're lying!"



A rather complicated topic for a school project. Has the jury of competitions, teachers, etc. been faced with skepticism?



Yes, I came across this constantly. I came to competitions, and there was no mistrust in them. It was believed that I was not doing anything in the project, but I was just bison some terms. There was a competition in which I took part for several years in a row, where one old man in the jury said to my eyes: ‚ÄúYou're lying,‚Äù and that‚Äôs all - I left.







At the same time, I never hid the fact that I did not do the project alone, my father and the head of my QUANTORIUM technopark in Korolev helped me, Dmitry Igorovich Smorodin, he helped me solve local problems related to design issues. But you can‚Äôt say that they did everything for me, I independently developed a model of the pedipulator and printed it on a 3D printer. I owe the background to my father and scientific scientist, but the impressive part of the work was independent, so doubts at the competitions touched me. Yes, there were difficulties, I collected very crookedly.







At first, I also distrusted the fact that you yourself did everything - you had to understand not only bionics, but also electronics, programming, etc. Have you used pre-built code to control prototypes?



I did not find ready-made drive control algorithms for these prototypes; they may be in the public domain. Therefore, in the 11th grade, I had to master programming at a basic level in order to write them. I found out how animals move their legs in a passage, and I wrote code for the Arduino in a simplified version of C ++ that controls the drives to simulate this movement.







As a programmer, I‚Äôm a little ashamed of that code , now I program much better. But then it was enough for me to describe the basic control of drives. The prototypes did not receive a stabilization system, mainly due to the fact that this system required calculations that I could not do in grade 11 myself.



The walk of the Fedor robot is not impressive yet, as are the early Atlas prototypes



If you present the result of the project and compare it with existing serial models, what benefits should hypothetically arise?



At the time I was making my first prototypes with Boston Dynamics, the first Atlas prototypes appeared. Now they are great finalized. And at that moment, the early versions of the prototypes had serious problems with gait, and I even brought them for comparison in 2017.



When I presented my project at competitions, I often talked about the fact that today anthropomorphic robots have a fairly clubfoot gait. It resembles the gait of people with torn ligaments. The essence of the project is to create second-order levers in the ‚Äújoints‚Äù of the robot with the help of an analogue of the ligament, thanks to which the pressure on the surface of the support is reduced in the middle of a single support step. This ultimately increases stability when moving, reduces the load on the drive and energy consumption. Those. the main advantage is more optimized movements and, as a result, a reduction in energy costs.



What do you think of Russian robotic developments?



I heard that we developed the anthropomorphic robot Fedor. Unfortunately, it is difficult for me to form an opinion, because today there is not a lot of available information, especially not staged shots for the media, but records from laboratory tests. Those videos where he walks are not very impressive to me, just as the walk of early Atlas prototypes was not impressive.



Does your mixed opinion about Russian robots influence your desire to live and work in Russia?

In general, no. I know that in the USA and Western Europe I will have difficulty adapting to a different culture, it will be difficult for me to find a common language with people who have a different identity, mentality. Therefore, I would like to live in Russia. But I am quite considering the possibility of studying somewhere in the USA, as well as gaining professional experience abroad, but not a permanent job. I have no prejudices about this, it‚Äôs just easier for me to understand those around me.



What it all came to



As far as I know, now you are not involved in the project that brought you fame and made you the heroine of the film, why?



I have plans to return to this topic later. However, with age and experience, you understand that the goals of such projects are much further from implementation than we initially imagine. At the moment, I realize that the creation of a full-fledged mathematical model to describe what I want is perhaps not comparable in labor costs, with the economic effect of an increase in efficiency and energy savings that can be obtained. These thoughts still stop me from continuing. My father and I filed a joint application for an invention until the matter went further.



Where do you work now, study?

I study at the Mechanics and Mathematics Department of Moscow State University, it is likely that this faculty will give me a specialty that will be in demand in robotics. I also work as an intern programmer at Exoatlet. They are engaged in exoskeletons, it is very close to me. Perhaps I will continue to work there.

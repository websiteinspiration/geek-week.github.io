The most reliable HDDs according to Backblaze Q1 2020


Despite all the quarantine ups and downs, data centers continue to work. And with a greater load than before, because the volume of Internet traffic has grown significantly. Which hard drives do the job best, Backblaze found out once again. A well-known cloud storage service released a report on HDD reliability - for the I quarter of 2020.

The number of HDDs at Backblaze is constantly increasing. At the time of writing the report, the company had 132,339 disks. Of these, 2,380 are bootable, 129,959 are data storage disks. The report displays statistics on disk failures of different companies and different capacities.

So, I quarter 2020



By the end of the reporting period, the number of accounting hard drives was 129,959. These are the HDDs on which customer data is stored. The statistics on the disks that are being tested, as well as models, the number of which does not exceed 60 copies, are removed from the statistics. After such filtering, 129 764 remains. The number of days worked by the disks is 11.4 million. The number of failures is 355.


Company Comments


The annual failure rate (AFR) was 1.07%. This is the minimum indicator for all the time monitoring the operation of hard drives, that is, since 2013. For comparison, in the first quarter of 2019, AFR was 1.56%.

During the reporting quarter, 4 HDD models from three manufacturers never failed. Toshiba 4TB and Seagate 16TB drives showed zero failure rates . But for these models, the operating time was small - only about 10,000 days in total for the entire quarter. Accordingly, in the event of a failure of even one Seagate 16TB AFR drive, it would be 7.25% per quarter. Under similar conditions, a 4TB Toshiba AFR would be 4.05%.

In contrast, HGST modelsmuch more days worked, so here AFR is not so volatile. If the 8 TB model fails, the AFR would be only 0.4%; if the 12 TB model fails, the AFR model would increase to 0.26% for the quarter. The zero failure rate is that in the first, which in the second cases is impressive.

Method for calculating the annual failure rate
Regardless of the observation period (month, quarter, etc.), Backblaze recalculates all failures for a year. According to the following formula:

AFR = (Disk Failures / (Runtime / 366) * 100

Where:

  • Disk Failures - the number of HDDs that failed during the monitoring period.
  • Runtime - the number of days during which the monitoring of the operation of the discs lasted.

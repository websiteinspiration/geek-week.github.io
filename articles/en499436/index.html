With spinal injuries and strokes, in addition to direct damage to nerve fibers, secondary processes associated with initial damage are also dangerous - overproduction of free radicals (reactive oxygen species) and developing inflammation.

Upon impact, in the case of a spinal injury, or rupture of a vessel in the event of a stroke (stopping blood flow during arterial spasm or blockage during a heart attack), hypoxia occurs in the tissues closest to the organ, a pathological process associated with a lack of oxygen. This blocks the final link of the respiratory chain in the cells and causes the formation of an excessive amount of free radicals.

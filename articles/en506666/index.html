ORM textures, but is it worth it?

Recently I read an article and saw a description of the process of creating an interesting texture map, which will be discussed later ...



Introduction


What is ORM ? In short - this “Occlusion, Roughness, Metallic” is an unusual combination of three different PBR cards packed in one texture.

I will show an example of creating such a texture in Substance Painter , but it can also be done in Photoshop (for me this is a more time-consuming way, while Substance Painter does it automatically, the main thing is to configure the configuration correctly). For rendering, I will use Blender 2.8.

Problem


To adjust the texture on the object, working in Blender , as in any other editor, very often you have to add about five main texture maps ( BaseColor, Normal, AO, Metallic, Roughness ), or even more. This process takes, although not a big, but still precious time of the artist. Therefore, in this article I want to analyze a topic such as combining maps in ORM and find out what it gives an advantage over traditional textures and whether it gives it at all.

Parsing


Ambient Occlusion, Roughness, Metallic - these are three black and white ( Grayscale ) cards. This means that their color lies in only one channel. For reference, in texturing we can use textures consisting of 3-4 channels ( RGB, RGB + A ).

Then why store these cards separately when you can make a small image archive, in three channels of which three whole black and white textures will fit? This is the advantage of ORM .



We figured out the theory. But before showing project customization in Substance Painter and Blender, I would like to give some measurements of rendering the same 3D object using different engines, alternately connecting two different materials (one with three separate maps, the other using ORM ).



For rendering, I used engines like Eevee, Cycles (CPU and GPU ) and Octane Render (all settings are standard, sampling is 128).

(I want to say right away that I'm not a pro at Octane , and if you notice I have inaccuracies in setting up materials, I will only be glad if you point to them).



Now the choice is yours. As you can see, the rendering results are very close. I want to remind you that this article was written primarily to show how to work with ORM , and not about estimating rendering time on different engines.

Texture customization


First, create your configuration in Substance Painter and add three cells for textures to it, the first and second - RGB + A (three connected channels and one unconnected), and the third - R + G + B (three unconnected channels). Now we transfer the existing cards to the cells. The result should be such a picture.



When exporting, I used the addition of an alpha channel and Dilation + Transparent so that the texture baked only in place of UV shells and nowhere else. The figure shows what kind of cards turned out in the end.

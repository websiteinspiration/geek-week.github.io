Artificial intelligence for image and video analysis. Webinar from Jet Infosystems

on June 26 at 15:00 live a leading expert of the Machine Learning Center will talk about the experience of developing their own solutions in the company, as well as the use of IBM Visual Insights and the possibilities of combining both approaches.



At the webinar, we will discuss in which areas active implementation is taking place and how the basic steps are carried out - from data collection to training the first prototypes of neural networks, which are further improved and implemented as a complete solution.



How to find the most attractive places for visitors on the trading floor? Are there simple solutions for tracking cashier actions and queue lengths in stores? Do employees comply with safety requirements and remember to use protective equipment? How data science can help experts in narrow areas - for example, estimating the coefficient of full wood for a loaded timber truck (and other specific tasks).



You will learn how Jet Infosystems data scientists work in such projects, which is required to develop the implementation of image or video recognition from the point of view of data science, if there is no ready-made solution for the problem or its quality is not satisfactory.

Let's talk a bit about the general tasks of the sphere and how we see the current needs of the market.



The webinar will also focus on tools that can be used for machine learning tasks. In particular, we will talk about the possibilities of creating and implementing models using the IBM Visual Insights tool, and also discuss how the search for specific objects / recognition of the action performed on the video and image classification is implemented.



And, of course, we will talk about our cases in the field of machine vision and image recognition. Consider the services that are already working for the classification and selection of minerals, monitoring the actions of employees at an agro-industrial enterprise, monitoring the safety at a construction site, recognizing numbers and wheelsets of wagons using video cameras mounted on drones, and selecting raw materials in the mining industry and logging.

Dogville style management

Does the boss make you work at night or on the weekend? Yelling at all obscenities? Allows himself vulgar jokes? Colleagues constantly and urgently need help? The prize is still not given, but the salary is not raised? Is your outrage at best a laugh?

Basic algorithm



If you build some kind of abstraction, then it turns out like this. You come to a new job, agree on everything - salary, responsibilities, schedule, business trips, extracurricular, career prospects, the possibility of remote work, take leave to heal your teeth, etc. Clap your hands and get to work.

And most importantly, they demonstrated that they are ready to make concessions.



Dogville Paradox



The worst thing in Dogville is that you cannot get rid of it completely and forever. Except when you completely leave the environment in which it happened. In the same environment, the chain will start and develop again and again.



For example, in everyday life there is such advice against Dogville - "how you put yourself." This was said in my childhood before being sent to a new school, dormitory, army or prison. He relaxed, let himself in, allowed the superfluous, overlooked, warmed the snake around his neck, became decrepit, lost his grip - there are a lot of names, one essence.



Specifically in the working environment there is a specific reason for Dogville's infinity: staff turnover.



You showed your boss teeth, he knows that you are angry and scary, where you sit, there you get down. And then bam - the boss quits, and another one comes from outside the company. In less than a couple of days, Dogville will begin again.



You can, of course, ask for a meeting and tell how smart, angry, tough or helpful you are. I say without irony - I did it myself, because been in an environment with high turnover of executives. The boss in response, at best, will not laugh.



I played such a swing for several years. Each new director completely nullified the boundaries built with his predecessor, and everything had to start from the beginning. Then he dumped - tired of doing demarcation anew every year.

How to deal with Dogville?



I know about two strategies: do not give in and roll back. Go well together.

As I said at the beginning, everyone, including you, is playing Dogville. This is one of the basic instincts of all pack animals - to expand its territory and influence. The question is only in goals, means and awareness.



I am for proactive Dogville. Most people at work have some kind of plans and goals - career, financial, personal. Total



Dogville is quite manageable in the work team. A certain percentage of unconscious, animal attempts to expand borders will remain, of course - nothing can be done with nature. But the bulk of Dogville can be brought into a controlled area.

Data center air corridor insulation systems: basic rules for installation and operation. Part 1. Containerization

One of the most effective methods of increasing the energy efficiency of a modern data center and reducing the cost of its operation are insulation systems. In another way they are called containerization systems for hot and cold corridors. The fact is that the main consumer of excess data center power is the cold supply system. Accordingly, the lower the load on it (reduction of electricity bills, uniform distribution of load, reduction of wear of engineering systems), the higher energy efficiency (the ratio of total spent power to useful (spent on IT load) power).

This approach is widespread. This is a generally accepted standard of operation both in the world and in Russian data centers. What you need to know about insulation systems in order to use them as efficiently as possible?

To begin with - how it is generally arranged and how the cooling system works. In the data center there are mounting cabinets (racks) in which IT equipment is installed. This equipment needs constant cooling. To avoid overheating, it is necessary to apply a cold stream of air to the front door of the cabinet and take the hot one coming out of the back. But, if there is no barrier between the two zones - cold and hot, both flows can mix and thereby reduce cooling and increase the load on the air conditioners.
In order to prevent the mixing of hot and cold air, it is necessary to build an air containerization system.



Principle of operation: a closed volume (container) accumulates chilled air, preventing it from mixing with hot air and allowing highly loaded cabinets to receive a sufficient amount of cold.

Location: The air container should be between two rows of enclosures or between a number of enclosures and the wall of the room.

Construction: all sides of the container that separate the hot and cold zones should be separated by partitions so that cold air passes only through IT equipment.

Additional requirements: the container should not interfere with the installation and operation of IT equipment, the laying of communications, the operation of monitoring, lighting, fire extinguishing systems, and also be able to integrate into the access control system of the hall.
Cost: this is a fairly positive point. Firstly, the containerization system is far from the most expensive part of the entire air conditioning system. Secondly, it does not require further maintenance costs. Thirdly, it has a positive effect on savings, since the separation of air flows and the elimination of local overheating points reduces and evenly distributes the load between the air conditioners. In general, the economic effect depends on the scale of the machine room and the cooling architecture.

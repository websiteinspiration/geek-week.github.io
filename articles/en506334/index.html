What to do if siloviki came to your hoster

KDPV - Reuters

If you rented a server, then you do not have full control over it. This means that at any time specially trained people can come to the hoster and ask for any of your data. And the hoster will give them back if the requirement is formalized by law.

You really do not want your web server logs or user data to get to someone else. The ideal defense cannot be built. It is almost impossible to defend against a hoster who owns a hypervisor and provides you with a virtual machine. But it may be possible to slightly reduce the risks. Encryption of rental cars is not as useless as it seems at first glance. At the same time, let's look at threats to extract data from physical servers.

Threat Model


As a rule, the hoster will try to protect the interests of the client as much as possible according to the law. If in a letter from the authorities only access logs were requested, the hoster will not give dumps to all your virtual machines with databases. Should not, at least. If they ask for all the data completely, the hoster will copy the virtual disks with all the files and you will not know about it.

Regardless of the scenario, your main task is to make the attack too complicated and expensive. There are usually three main options for a threat.

Official


Most often, a paper letter is sent to the hoster's official office with a request to provide the necessary data in accordance with the relevant decree. If everything is framed correctly, the hoster provides the necessary access logs and other data to official authorities. Usually they just ask to send the necessary data.

Occasionally, if you really need to, law enforcement officials come to the data center in person. For example, when you have your own dedicated server and the data from there you can only pick up physically.

In all countries, access to private territory, searches, and other activities require evidence that the data may contain important information for the investigation of a crime. In addition, a search warrant issued in accordance with all regulations is required. There may be nuances associated with the peculiarities of local legislation. The main thing to understand is that if the official path is correct, the representatives of the data center will not let anyone go further than the checkpoint.

Moreover, in most countries it is impossible to just pick up and pull out working equipment. For example, in Russia, by the end of 2018, according to Article 183 of the Code of Criminal Procedure of the Russian Federation, part 3.1, it was guaranteed that during the extraction, the removal of electronic storage media is carried out with the participation of a specialist. At the request of the legal owner of the seized electronic storage media or the owner of the information contained therein, a specialist involved in the seizure, in the presence of those understood from the seized electronic storage medium, information is copied to other electronic storage media.

Then, unfortunately, this item was removed from the article.

Secret and unofficial


This is already the territory of activity of specially trained comrades from NSA, FBI, MI5 and other three-letter organizations. Most often, the legislation of countries provides for extremely broad powers for such structures. Moreover, there is almost always a legislative prohibition on any direct and indirect disclosure of the very fact of cooperation with similar law enforcement agencies. In Russia there are similar legal norms .

In the event of a similar threat to your data, they will almost certainly be pulled out. In addition to the simple withdrawal, the entire unofficial arsenal of backdoors, zero-day vulnerabilities, data extraction from the RAM of your virtual machine, and other joys can be used. At the same time, the hoster will be obliged to help the specialists of power structures as much as possible.

Dishonest employee


Not all people are equally good. Someone from the data center admins may decide to earn extra money and sell your data. Further, the development of events depends on his authority and access. The worst part is that an administrator with access to the virtualization console has full control over your machines. You can always remove the snapshot along with all the contents of the RAM and then slowly study it.

Vds


So you have a virtual machine that the hoster gave you. How can you organize encryption to protect yourself? In fact, almost nothing. Moreover, even someone else‚Äôs dedicated server may end up being a virtual machine into which the necessary devices are thrown.

If the task of the remote system is not just storing data, but performing some calculations, then the only option for working with an untrusted machine will be to implement a homomorphic encryption . In this case, the system will carry out calculations without the ability to understand what exactly it does. Unfortunately, the overhead for implementing such encryption is so great that their practical application is currently limited to very narrow tasks.

Plus, at the moment when the virtual machine is running and performing some actions, all encrypted volumes are in an accessible state, otherwise the OS simply will not be able to work with them. This means that having access to the virtualization console, you can always remove the snapshot of a running machine and extract all the keys from the RAM.

Many vendors have attempted to organize hardware RAM encryption so that even the hoster does not have access to this data. For example, Intel Software Guard Extensions technology, which organizes areas in a virtual address space that are protected from reading and writing from outside this area by other processes, including the kernel of the operating system. Unfortunately, you cannot fully trust these technologies, since you will be limited by your virtual machine. In addition, ready-made examples of successful attacks on this technology already exist . And yet, encrypting virtual machines is not as meaningless as it might seem.

Bare metal


It is not so easy to put your own server in a data center. An alien dedicated may turn out to be a virtual machine where all devices are thrown. But something interesting in terms of protection begins when you have the opportunity to place your trusted physical server in the data center. Here you can already use traditional dm-crypt, VeraCrypt or any other encryption of your choice to the utmost.

You need to understand that when implementing total encryption, the server will not be able to independently rise after reboot. It will be necessary to raise the connection to the local IP-KVM, IPMI or other similar analogue. After which we manually enter the master key. The scheme looks so-so in terms of continuity and fault tolerance, but there are no special alternatives if the data is so valuable.


NCipher nShield F3 Hardware Security Module

A softer version assumes that the data is encrypted, and the key is located directly in the server itself in a special HSM (Hardware Security Module). As a rule, these are very functional devices that not only provide hardware cryptography, but also have mechanisms for detecting physical hacking attempts. If someone starts picking your server with a grinder, then the HSM with an independent power source will reset the keys that it stores in its memory. The attacker will get the encrypted minced meat. In this case, a reboot may occur automatically.

Removing keys is a much faster and more humane option than activating a termite checker or electromagnetic discharger. For such devices you will be beaten for a very long time by the neighbors at the counter in the data center. Moreover, in the case of using TCG Opal 2 encryption on the media themselves, you practically do not experience any overhead. All this happens transparently for the OS. True, one should trust the conditional Samsung and hope that there is an honest AES256, and not a banal XOR.

At the same time, one should not forget that all unnecessary ports must be physically disabled or stupidly filled with a compound. Otherwise, you give attackers the opportunity to conduct DMA attacks. If you have a PCI Express or Thunderbolt sticking out, including USB with its support, you are vulnerable. The attacker will be able to conduct an attack through these ports and gain direct access to the memory with keys.



In a very sophisticated version, the attacker will be able to conduct a cold boot attack. At the same time, he simply pours a good portion of liquid nitrogen into your server, roughly extracts the frozen memory slats and removes a dump from them with all the keys. Often, an ordinary cooling spray and a temperature of -50 degrees are enough to carry out an attack. There is a more accurate option. If you haven‚Äôt disabled downloading from external devices, then the attacker‚Äôs algorithm will be even simpler:

  1. Freeze memory slats without opening the case
  2. Connect your bootable USB flash drive
  3. Using special utilities, remove data from RAM that survived the reboot due to freezing.

Divide and rule


Ok, we only have virtual machines, but I want to at least somehow reduce the risks of data leakage.
You can, in principle, try to review the architecture and distribute data storage and processing in different jurisdictions. For example, a frontend with encryption keys is hosted in the Czech Republic, and a backend with encrypted data is somewhere in Russia. In the event of a standard seizure attempt, it is highly unlikely that security forces will be able to do this simultaneously in different jurisdictions. Plus, this partially insures us from the scenario with the removal of the snapshot.

Well, or you can consider a completely clean option - End-to-End encryption. Of course, this is beyond the scope of the TOR and does not imply performing calculations on the side of the remote machine. Nevertheless, this is a perfectly acceptable option when it comes to storing and synchronizing data. For example, it is very conveniently implemented in the same Nextcloud. At the same time, synchronization, versioning and other buns of the server part will not go anywhere.

Total


There are no perfectly protected systems. The task is only to make the attack more expensive than the potential benefits.

Some reduction in the risks of accessing data on a virtual one can be obtained by combining encryption and separate storage from different hosts.

A more or less reliable option is to use your own iron server.

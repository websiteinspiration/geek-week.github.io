Why are women lucky and what have the vessels to do with it?

Cuperosis can also begin at a young age.

Cuperosis is a pathology of capillaries, very similar in form to varicose veins of the extremities. All pathological age-related changes in blood vessels in one way or another lead to several key problems:

  1. Loss of vessel elasticity.
  2. Local areas of thinning of the walls of blood vessels and their deformation.
  3. Pathological compensatory proliferation of blood vessels.

On average, the cell should be located no further than 700 microns from the nearest vessel in order to breathe and eat normally. If there is no normal vessel nearby, and the cell will miss this, it will begin to degrade, and it will be replaced by others that are more resistant to hypoxia.

10 useful English-language podcasts for programmers worth subscribing to in 2020

Today let's talk about such a cool thing as podcasts. For some reason, many miss this tool and do not use it at all. But podcasts are just mega-convenient.

You can listen to them in transport on the way to work or in the morning run. This format does not require full involvement.

We picked up 10 interesting English-language podcasts for programmers to help developers improve their knowledge and skills. The selection is subjective because it is based on our experience, but we guarantee that all podcasts here are really useful and interesting. Go!

Code newbie


Release frequency: 1 episode per week
Number of listeners: 73,000
Working level of English: advanced

Let's start with a great podcast for beginners in programming. If you want to ‚Äúget into IT,‚Äù but have no idea how to do this, then this podcast will definitely help you.

The authors clarify a lot of unobvious things for beginners, help to decide which programming languages ‚Äã‚Äãto learn and how to do it correctly in general. The issues are full of interesting and useful information for those who begin their first steps in the profession.

But English is pretty complicated here. Lecturers speak fluently and often fuzzily articulate individual words. So you need at least an advanced level of English to understand speech in full.

Software Engineering Daily


Frequency of releases: 1 episode per day
Number of listeners: 20,000
Working level of English: intermediate

Great podcast for software developers. In each separate issue, the authors reveal in detail one or more nuances from different areas of development: creating programs for Windows, Linux and macOS, developing mobile applications for iOS and Android, web development. The authors do not hesitate to cling to any interesting topics.

The project website has a large number of text materials for all levels of programmers: from Junior to Senior.

As for the English language, it is almost perfect here. Well-delivered speech, very clear articulation of words - listening is a pleasure. The only negative is the high pace of speech, but you can get used to it quite quickly.

But if you wish, you can download the transcription of the podcast and see obscure places using the dictionary.

Talk Python To Me

An absolute must have for Python developers. The podcast is entirely dedicated to Python. The authors analyze in detail the individual architectural features, frameworks, patterns.

Useful information is just tons, but you need to understand that the podcast is not for absolute beginners. To understand the issues, you need to understand the topic at least at the Middle level. Interestingly, the authors invite experts who have created dozens and hundreds of projects in Python, so even Senoirs will be able to learn something useful.

English in the podcast is pretty fast. There are many specialized vocabulary, but in general there are no difficulties with understanding, if you have a well-developed listening comprehension skill.

A podcast in which they easily and naturally talk about programming and programmers.

The topics of the releases are mainly about soft-skills of developers. How to learn programming languages, how to set up your own workflow, how to develop and make more money. There are also many topics for team leaders and project managers. In general, a very suitable podcast is not only for individualists, but also for those specialists who work in a team.

True, English is fast here. Lecturers do not follow the speech at all, it is in full flow. At the same time, the content is very high - there is almost no water. But if you manage to cope with such a wild pace, then you will get just a lot of usefulness.

Coding blocks


Release frequency: 2 episodes per month
Number of listeners: 7000
Working level of English: from intermediate to fluent

This podcast is literally about everything. We don‚Äôt know by what criteria authors choose topics, but they discuss almost all aspects of programming: from creating sites to personal effectiveness.

Interestingly, podcasts often have links and reviews of other useful things: channels, books, articles, interviews and seminars. So from there you can draw a bunch of sources.

There are a lot of lecturers, so English is different in every episode of the podcast. There are simply wonderful monologues that are not inferior to stage ones, but there is a fluent chewing speech that not all native speakers will understand. In general, choose episodes that you can handle.

Front End Happy Hour


Release frequency: 3 episodes per month
Number of listeners: 11,000
Working level of English: upper-intermediate

Layout designers, hello to you! Because in this podcast all the usefulness for you. The authors themselves are front-end developers, therefore they share interesting libraries, tools and chips, help students solve common problems with layout.

Among the regular guests of the podcast are layout artists Netflix, Twitch, Microsoft, Reddit and LinkedIn. So it will be definitely interesting.

English in the podcast is fluent, but clear enough to understand everything without much effort. The upper-intermediate level is quite enough.

Test and code


Frequency of releases: 1 episode per week
Number of listeners: 5000
Working level of English: upper-intermediate


Another podcast for followers of Python. Actually, the podcast is called: "Python testing for software developers." But here, more attention is paid not to writing code, but to testing it.

Also a lot of information about automation, various integrations and working with databases.

As for English, the language is good. It is quite adequate speech, but with a lot of specialized vocabulary. True, there is a nuance in some episodes, especially with invited experts, a very bad sound, so you have to cope not only with the language, but also with technical noise.

Learn to Code With Me


Frequency of releases: 2 episodes per month
Number of listeners: 31,000
Working level of English: intermediate


Just a great newbie podcast. If you suddenly learn some programming language yourself, then this podcast can become a good helper in this. There are many materials not only on programming languages, but also on many other areas. For example, web design, frontend, statistics and analytics.

In this case, you do not even need to perfectly know English. All vocabulary and lecture notes are under each issue. If something is not clear, you can always look and figure it out with the help of a dictionary. An additional plus - you can learn the main vocabulary in this way.

Fragmented


Frequency of releases: 1 episode per day
Number of listeners: 11,000
Working level of English: intermediate


Here's how to make perfect mobile apps on the Android platform. The authors themselves are practitioners who are engaged in development, so they tell a lot of useful things, especially for the Junior and Middle levels.

A very suitable podcast for anyone who is even indirectly associated with the development of mobile applications.

As for the English language here, it is quite simple. The only minus is that the authors themselves and many invited participants speak with accents, which will make it difficult for an unusual listener to understand them. You need to get used to it, that it is definitely worth it.

Number of listeners: 5000
Working level of English: intermediate

The speech is measured and clear, almost all vocabulary is common. The intermediate level is enough with your head, but you can even try with pre-intermediate.

Podcasts are a great format for professional growth. But in order to draw useful information from them, you need to be good at listening to English. Learn English and improve in your profession!

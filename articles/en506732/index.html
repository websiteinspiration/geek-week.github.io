Questions about the components

Organizing components together, on the other hand, is a big burden on teams involved in sharing components. Teams have to make considerable efforts to do this, and these efforts must be justified. For example, teams are under pressure to release new opportunities for their development. If you compare this with the development of components intended for use only in the application that a certain team is engaged in, organizing the joint use of components requires more efforts to design them and to build the right abstractions. But what if the components designed for their repeated use, no one, in fact, will not use? Preliminary design of components, which seems to be necessary for components,intended for sharing, in principle, deprives programmers of the desire to publish such components.

Our decision



Solution Overview

We, on the way to our own solution for reusing components, had to constantly practice in search of a balance between the technical feasibility of a certain idea and the convenience of a solution. We are talking about the relationship between automation of solving certain problems and manual labor. We decided to use a system based on an environment designed to work with components. With its help, we were going to create and visualize components. This approach avoids the complexity of creating components, while helping component authors control their development. Many tools for organizing environments for working with components take over the configuration of Webpack / Babel, which allows you to use them without additional settings, but at the same time makes it possible, if necessary, to change the standard parameters.

Among many such tools, we chose the Storybook. The reason for this was the widespread use of this environment both in the open source community and within PayPal. In addition, Storybook supports a powerful plug-in system that we could use to organize component quality audits and other tasks.

Component Explorer is a proprietary PayPal solution used by the company to share components. This solution, at a high level, allows you to find components. It is responsible for hosting components and for their output using a uniform interface. Component Explorer uses a Storybook and command line tool component-clito publish components. A GitHub application is used to automate the work.component-bot. In other words, components are created and documented using the Storybook, after which they are automatically shared with component-cliand component-bot. Moreover, component quality audits are automatically performed. This is done using the set of Storybook add-ons included in our pre-configured storybook-preset.

Component Explorer allows you to share both components designed as npm packages and components that are (not yet) published in npm. This is very important, since most of the components created by product teams are not presented as npm packages. Such components exist only as parts of the respective applications. The ability to share components that are not npm packages allows you to publish components to a wider audience of developers. Commands do not have an urgent need to extract the component to a separate repository and publish it to npm. The component can be shared publicly from its own repository.

Moreover, the organization of general access to "unpackaged" components allows you to get rid of the problem of preliminary design of components, which we have already talked about. This makes a special model of developer collaboration possible. The component is laid out in the public domain by default: the team only needs to make some effort if members of another team who decide to use its component contact it. Or, even more interesting, thanks to module federation technology from Webpack 5, you can share components directly from Cx without publishing them to npm.

And finally, the organization of general access to ‚Äúunpackaged‚Äù components is applicable to components that are not planned to be used repeatedly. Such components, for example, pages presented in the form of components, are conveniently used to organize simple and quick access to some materials.

Pages presented as components



What is a component?

Components can be not only atomic units of the user interface, but also something more. Components can be combined, a larger structure can be formed from them. Such structures, assembled together, can form a whole page. With this in mind, programmers can lay out components of various scales in Cx: from small buttons to widgets, such as forms for entering credit card data, and even to entire pages and sections of sites.

If programmers share something like whole pages, it helps people start working more openly and efficiently.. This, in addition, helps teams and company management better navigate the existing product portfolio. Our vision for this issue is for company employees to have open and free access to every page of PayPal products in Cx.

Community


Our Component Explorer system owes its success to the community of specialists of the company, which consists of programmers, designers and product managers. Creating a community is not only the use of certain tools. It is also the formation of a culture of organization, and the creation of the right attitude among people. Using Component Explorer is just a good start to changing how teams design user interfaces. It is, for example, that the components, by default, are laid out in the public domain. These components open up new opportunities for interaction between teams. We see the value of Component Explorer in the following:

  • This system allows teams to interact more openly and efficiently, which increases the speed of development and innovation.
  • It weakens the influence on the organization of such phenomena as the isolation of teams from each other, the waste of efforts of different teams on the implementation of the same functionality, the inconsistency of the teams.
  • It contributes to creating values ‚Äã‚Äãthat support the community and go beyond the success of individual teams.
  • It helps those who have created something successful get community recognition.

PayPal is now in the early stages of introducing a reusable component system. We are still exploring this issue, experimenting, looking for successful ways to perform various tasks. It is entirely possible that we will return to this topic in future publications.

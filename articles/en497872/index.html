Unusual and quite obvious factors affecting the traffic of corporate networks and the work of providers

Let's talk about a few interesting dependencies related to traffic spikes in company networks, as well as the cybersecurity of IT infrastructure. For example, even a simple installation of second monitors at employees' workplaces can cause additional load on the network .


/ Unsplash / Fotis Fotopoulos

More monitors - more traffic


One of the major providers of network monitoring solutions conducted a study by interviewing more than 200 professionals at last year's Infosecurity Europe conference. The vast majority of respondents said that their company equips workstations with two monitors to increase employee productivity. But half of them use a second display for non-working tasks - streaming music or video. These services may take more than half of corporate traffic. It turned out that in 73% of such situations, companies simply do not have the infrastructure that could effectively cope with the additional load.

A number of information security experts also note that employees using second monitors for non-working tasks open up new attack vectors for attackers. Vulnerabilities in streaming applications is one of the main reasons for malware to enter corporate networks.

Just a month ago, in one of the applications for watching television, they discovered the bug CVE-2020-9380 , which allows the hacker to gain full control over the operating system. Therefore, companies should be more careful about the ‚Äúsecond display‚Äù policy.

Long Patch - Long Risk Management


According to analysts from Kollective, more than half of large organizations ‚Äî whose computer fleet exceeds 100 thousand units ‚Äî spend more than one month on closing a vulnerability. This fact simplifies the work of hackers - in most cases, they use the well-known software vulnerabilities to crack corporate networks and realize their plans due to the slowness of administrators who drag out the installation of ready-made patches.

So, for hacking the Equifax bureau in 2017, attackers took advantage of a vulnerability in the Apache Struts framework ( CVE-2017-5638 ). "Patch" for her was released two months before the attack.

One of the reasons for the slow installation of patches is bureaucracy and poor process automation. A number of companies still work with information about patches and their updates in regular office software and tables. One Fortune 100 organization even has a dedicated department that populates spreadsheets with vulnerability information. To avoid situations similar to the case with Equifax, information security experts recommend automating such processes.

Emergency applications - emergency response


Today, providers note traffic growth of 45% or more. The situation is similar with VPN and cloud services. But with all this, sharp declines are observed.


/ Unsplash / Francisco Andreotti

One of the western cloud providers noted an interesting pattern. When country leaders apply to citizens with statements, the load on the network in the corresponding region is significantly reduced (everyone watches / listens to the speech live on TV or on the radio).

A sharp drop in traffic was recorded in the Netherlands during a government appeal on March 15. A similar situation occurred in France - when the authorities announced the introduction of quarantine measures, the country 's traffic was reduced by about half.

All these points - although some of them are quite obvious - indicate that the work of providers and administrators of corporate networks is constantly complicated.

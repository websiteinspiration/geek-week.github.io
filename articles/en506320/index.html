So what is Robotic Process Automation?


RPA or Robotic Process Automation is a technology that today allows you to configure computer software or a "robot" to emulate the actions of people working in digital systems to carry out business processes. RPA robots use the user interface to collect data and use applications like people do. They interpret, initiate responses, and communicate with other systems to perform a wide variety of repetitive tasks. The only difference: the RPA software robot never rests and makes no mistakes. Well, almost does not allow.

For example, an RPA robot can process files attached to letters, recognize text, amounts, last names, after which the received information will automatically be entered into any accounting system. In fact, RPA robots can mimic many, if not all, user actions. They can enter applications, move files and folders, copy and paste data, fill out forms, extract structured and semi-structured data from documents, and much more.

RPA technology did not pass by, and, well-known to us, Microsoft Power Automate.

R Markdown. How to make a report in the face of uncertainty?

A small recipe that will be useful when creating dynamic reports.







Quite often there is a situation where the size and content of the reporting form will depend on the composition of the data transferred to the input. It is a story-telling report, not a simple output of a table. In this case, depending on the content of the input data, individual items, graphs, tables, text may appear or disappear.







What will the classic Excel-Word analyst do? For each new request, make a custom report and save it in a separate file. But you can look a little under the hood and make the computer do everything on its own.







R Markdown lets you do all this in an elegant way. Some technical details below.







main idea



We have data whose contents may vary. For example, you need to build a report on trucks transporting goods (an abstract example). The number and numbers of cars leaving the flight are different every day, but the summary for each machine is the same in structure. We will build on this.







R Markdown is performed in several passes. In this case , the output representation is Rmd



first formed .md



, and then the presentation is formed from it, the html format is the most popular.







Total, 2 options are outlined.







  1. You can insert the .Rmd



    necessary blocks (assembled or template) into the compiled stage at the stage of compiling the report .
  2. You can insert the .md



    necessary markdown inserts into the intermediate at the stage of compiling the report .

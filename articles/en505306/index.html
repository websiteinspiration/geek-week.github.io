As it was before


The two-year program of the Technopark starts twice a year - in spring and autumn, and lasts four semesters. It is called the main program, 13 subjects are taught at it and 400+ students are taught per year.

A feature of 2 semesters in the close integration of three of the five disciplines:

  • backend development;
  • front-end development;
  • interface design.

Milestone controls and classes are synchronized, the final project in three disciplines is a common one.

For six years in the Technopark, the backend development course gave Java. Odnoklassniki and other services are written in Java, so it was chosen for students. In addition, in the third semester, the guys studied mobile development for the Android platform, and knowledge of Java was welcome.

As a final semester project, students created mobile games: puzzles, strategic board games, shooters, games based on Warcraft, 2D tanks. In the final, all teachers, students and guests came to public defense.

Pros of the game as a final project


  • The opportunity to think through the mechanics of the game in advance: students from the beginning of the program knew about the upcoming development and began to invent scripts in the first semester.

The transformation of the program took 3 semesters, of which 2 transitional - when they changed one component and did not touch the other. In the fall of 2019, the program was completely updated: all students studied the Go language and prepared final projects related to e-commerce.

At the beginning of the fall semester, the guys broke into teams and determined what projects they would do for the final defense. As a guide, we took web services Kinopoisk, Trello, Yula and the like. Projects were prepared as the semester disciplines passed - backend, frontend development and interface design.

Protection took place at the very end of 2019. Students presented thoughtful, quality products. For example, one of the teams developed an audio service like Spotify, the other a job board.

During the transformation of the program, the opinions of students were taken into account, profiles and comments were analyzed. As a result, student feedback is positive, each team still has a mentor, and the learning process has not been affected. After the updated course, students are as prepared as possible for an internship at the company. 1/10 of all interns in 2019 are developers on Go.

RPA to the rescue: how to recognize a passport without a single line of code

Passport recognition technology has long been in the register of necessary solutions for large corporations that provide B2C services. The big three telecom operators, large banks and insurance companies, railway and air carriers, ticket aggregators have successfully integrated Smart IDReader document recognition technologies into their own information systems , taking the quality of the service provided to a new level. But for many companies that are related to medium and small businesses, automation of the processes of entering data from customer documents is necessary, and the integration of new functionality by embedding libraries is expensive or even not available. And how should they be? In today's article, we will tell you about how to implement passport recognition in almost any information system in half an hour.



RPA (Robotic Process Automation) is the transfer of the functions of employees performing routine work to a software module (robot) that operates according to a specific algorithm. Remember, in my previous article about passport scanners, I said that our scanner can ‚Äúpretend to be a keyboard‚Äù in order to return recognized data to the desired text fields in the same way as a person does? So, RPA is also an emulation, not only at the hardware, but at the software level. In fact, we get a ‚Äúdigital employee‚Äù who is able to reproduce human actions when interacting with various information systems that we use in business processes.

Frankly, our personal attitude to the RPA is not the most positive: the automation of serious industrial business processes with the help of robots is more likely associated with a hastily hand-made article twisted in the garage and rewound from top to bottom with blue electrical tape. But let's not get into this ‚Äúgarden‚Äù, it‚Äôs better just to look at the IT market and understand that they genuinely love RPA and such ‚Äúgarage crafts‚Äù. In addition, in some cases (for example, when using legacy systems), there is simply no other way.

Back to passport recognition. Today‚Äôs article in step-by-step format will tell you how to add recognition functionality to almost any information system with a user interface in 30 minutes. As such an information system, we will have Microsoft Excel with form functionality.



Step 1. Choosing an RPA System


Recently, many software products that provide RPA functionality have grown: from simple and free to sophisticated and expensive. Almost all of them can run general-purpose OCR engines; some RPA systems even have specialized modules for recognizing typed documents. So, the Smart IDReader SDK already comes with a special module for UiPath , the Russian ELMA RPA product will contain an integrated passport recognition engine right in the base package. By the way, both of these programs have a free Community version.

Step 2. Formalization of the procedure for entering passport data


At this stage, we should specify our business process. You need to understand when the robot should start, how to capture the image (using a scanner, webcam or by selecting an image from a directory on the disk), establish which fields to fill in, how the transition between fields is ensured, what actions to take in case of successful automatic input data and what to do if some fields (or even the entire passport) could not be recognized.

In our example, we will enter passport data from an image stored on a computer. The robot we created will be launched by pressing a key combination, allowing you to select an image, recognize and fill in the target fields.

Step 3. Programming the data entry script


The most interesting step. Using the RPA process designer, you need to transform the business process information that was meaningful at the last step into the concrete actions of the robot! Our robot is implemented by a sequence of the following elements: file selection, file recognition and text information input (this action is repeated for all fields of the passport).



In this scheme, the ‚ÄúProcess image file‚Äù element is just responsible for recognizing the passport from the file using the Smart IDReader engine . Features of settings for text output elements depend on the use of a specific RPA system, but it‚Äôs trivial everywhere: often just ‚Äúpoke with a mouse‚Äù in the field on the form where the RPA will have to enter the recognized text information (all the system routine associated with obtaining a unique field identifier, the RPA takes to myself).

Step 4. Debugging the robot


Before installing the robot on combat vehicles, you should run it in debug mode and check the operation. Debugging the process of robotization is no different, in fact, from debugging a regular program: you can also set breakpoints, go through the process step by step, start the process from the desired point, and so on.

Step 5. Installing the robot


Once the robot is ready, it needs to be installed on workstations. Depending on the specific RPA system, this is done in different ways.

Here is how you can add passport recognition to any business process without programming, integration, and serious technical problems. Actually, for this, the RPA was invented. There is no need to worry about the security of personal data in this solution, because Smart IDReader is a library, not a service, due to which it works inside the robot without resorting to third-party resources.

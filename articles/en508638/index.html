The Brainbow model aims to map the relationships of brain neurons. In order to create such a model, it is necessary to knock out those genes that a biotechnologist is interested in using the so-called Cre-LoxP system tool . This system consists of Crerecombinases and LoxP sites (DNA sequence consisting of 34 nucleotide pairs). This system was spied and borrowed from nature, namely the phage P1. In phage P1, this system allows for the normal life cycle of this virus to be inserted with the phage embedded in the bacterial genome. In the experiment, scientists introduce a cassette with the genes of 4 fluorescent proteins and six LoxP sites ( 3 pairs : 1, 2, and 3) between the genes so that the pairs are directed in the same direction. This method of introducing LoxP sites allows cutting out a portion between identical LoxP sites. Provides this enzyme Cre- recombinase. So, the enzyme CreŒ≤-recombinase folds the DNA molecule in such a way as to build identical LoxP sites opposite each other, and produces a cross between these sections of the DNA molecule. This leads to the fact that the site that was located between the two LoxP sites is enclosed in a loop and subsequently cut out of the molecule. Sites that were to the right and left of the LoxP sites are connected together. Therefore, such a system is called knockout - as it turns off certain genes. But the Brainbow model is not just a knockout , but a Conditional Knockou t, that is, a knockout under certain conditions. For this, Cre- recombinase is activated only in hippocampal cells.. In order for Cre- recombinase to start only in specific cells, a special promoter is chosen, in this case the THY1 gene promoter . In this design, there are four parts at once that encode fluorescent proteins: orange, red, yellow and blue. If the recombination takes place at the sites of the first pair, the orange fluorescent protein is removed from the construct, and the red fluorescent protein is connected to the promoter. If recombination occurs at the sites of the second pair, the orange and red fluorescent proteins will be removed, and the promoter will combine with yellow. Well, the third option is to remove orange, red and yellow, and only blue will remain. This leads to the fact that each cell is stained in its own color. Due to combinations of different fluorochromes and colors, about 70 can be achieved.individual color combinations that are distinguishable by a computer.



From such cells where successful homologous recombination has occurred, transgenic animals can be obtained. When mice with the Cre- recombinase gene were crossed with a line, the offspring bearing both the Cre- recombinase gene and the ‚Äúmulticolor‚Äù cassette were obtained . Such mice will recombine and the neurons will be stained, it will be easy for scientists to trace where the processes of nerve cells begin and end, and, therefore, it is possible to track the relationship of brain neurons.

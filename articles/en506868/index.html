Sam Altman: Generating Ideas



The most common question asked by potential startup founders is how to come up with ideas for startups. The second most common question is whether you have any ideas for launching them.

But giving the founders an idea almost always fails. Having ideas is one of the most important qualities for a startup founder - you will need to generate many new ideas during the startup process.

Y Combinator once tried an experiment to fund seemingly good founders without any ideas. I think that every company in this study without ideas failed. It turns out that good founders have a lot of ideas for everything, so if you want to be a founder and can't come up with an idea for a company, you should probably work on becoming a good idea generator first.

How to do it?

It is very important to be in the right environment among worthy people. You want to be near people who have a good sense of the future, make incredible plans, are optimistic, creatively smart and have a very large flow of ideas. Such people tend to think without the restrictions that most people have, they do not have a lot of filters and do not care too much about what other people think.

The best ideas are fragile; most people don‚Äôt even start talking about them because they sound stupid. Perhaps most of all you want to be around people who do not make you feel stupid due to the mention of a bad idea, and who, of course, never feel stupid because they do it themselves.

Stay away from people who are tired of the world and belittle your ambitions. Unfortunately, this is a big part of the world. But they hold on to the past, and you want to live in the future.

You want to be able to transfer yourself for 20 years into the future, and then think from there in the opposite direction. Trust yourself, 20 years is a very long time; It's okay if your ideas seem too radical.

Another way to do this is to think about the most important tectonic shifts taking place right now. How is a fundamental change in the world? Can you identify the front of change and the opportunity they offer? The explosion of mobile phones in 2008-2012 - the latest significant example of this - we were late for another one!

With such a tectonic shift, the world is changing so fast that large officials are usually defeated by fast-moving and focused startups. (By the way, it‚Äôs useful to learn how to distinguish real trends from fake ones well. The key difference is that the new platform is widely used by a small number of people or is used a little by a large number of people.)

Each time it comes to mind that something is possible in this year and it was not possible in the past, you must be careful. Perhaps you have the foundation of a great startup idea. This is especially true if next year is already too late.

When you can say, ‚ÄúI‚Äôm not sure that we will be the only ones who will do this‚Äù is a good sign. So it was with Uber. After I first used it, it was clear that we would no longer wait for a taxi as long as before, but I was not sure that Uber would conquer this niche.

A good question to ask yourself at the very beginning of the process of thinking about an idea is: ‚Äúcan it become grandiose if it works?‚Äù There are many good ideas in the world, but only a few of them have the inherent advantages that can make a startup massively successful. Most companies do not generate valuable cumulative benefits as they scale. Think in advance about why an idea may have this property. This is obvious to Facebook or Airbnb, but often it exists in a more sophisticated manner.

It is also important to think about why you are well suited. This is difficult to do with pure introspection; Ideally, you can ask a mentor or some of the people you have worked with about what you are particularly good at. I came to the conclusion that founder / company compliance is just as important as product / market matching.

Finally, a good test for an idea is if you can articulate why most people think it‚Äôs a bad idea, but you understand what makes it a good idea.

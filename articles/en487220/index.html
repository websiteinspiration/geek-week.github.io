Color rendering and spectrum




The tested samples of LED lamps E14 and E27 from Remez provide maximum color reproduction, at the level of 97-98 CRI. In conventional light sources, it is 70-80 CRI. This parameter determines how natural you will see colors. With a low CRI value, the colors of the objects will be faded, that is, they will differ from the real ones for the worse.



For example, this is especially important for artists, photographers, for those who need to see the real color. The colors will differ depending on the type of lighting, in the sunlight they will be natural, when using ordinary LED bulbs they will be dimmer.



The main feature of the spectrum of conventional LEDs is a large amount of blue light, which is not in incandescent and sunlight.

Life time

Unlike most laboratories, I carry out testing for a long service life, constantly using samples for lighting. Many modern lamps have a short service life due to poor assembly and lack of thermal paste.

How we automated marketing Perekrestok.ru

In May 2020, the Perekrestok online supermarket took 11 thousand orders a day or 7.6 orders per minute, but the history of this direction began only 3 years ago - in April 2017. The arrival of Perekrestok online became part of the digital transformation of our business: we wanted to better understand our customers and adapt to their preferences (which tend to change, and this must be taken into account). Therefore, it was decided to allocate e-commerce in an independent direction, and from a familiar online supermarket to create a full-fledged online store under an umbrella brand. And, of course, automation occupies a special place in such a process, including direct communications with customers. By the end of last year, automatic mailings brought a little less than half of direct marketing revenue, every fifth recipient opened letters,and the most conversion letter (abandoned basket with a promotional code) led to a purchase in one case out of 12.







And this despite the fact that when the online store just started, marketing automation was not a priority for us. We just did mass mailing without any segmentation and a constant schedule. Basic automatic mechanics, such as welcome letters, onboarding and work with abandoned baskets, were out of the question. And to make a one-time mailing, managers had to manually unload contacts from CRM. Well, you imagine.



In 2019, we decided to radically change the situation. After all, it is one thing when a company does not have the opportunity to communicate with customers, and it hangs up a banner on the site in anticipation of being seen. And it’s completely different when the company has a shareware e-mail channel with which you can convey personalized information to customers.



So the hypothesis was born that the introduction of targeted marketing will lead to an increase in the share of revenue that direct online supermarket communication channels bring up to 5%. There were no strict KPIs - these figures were considered as the desired result. To achieve these indicators, it was necessary, firstly, to start collecting and storing customer information, secondly, to automate communications through e-mail newsletters and web pushing, and thirdly, to determine the most effective promotions. In solving these problems, we relied on the Mindbox marketing automation platform and the help of Out of Cloud agency.



Mindbox Integration



Teams from Perekrestok.ru, Mindbox and Out of Cloud worked on the terms of reference for integrating Mindbox with a self-written online supermarket and back office. Our product team spent several sprints on developing resources, and in total, all integration took 3 months. During this time, by joint efforts, we set up the transfer of all necessary data. Data was collected from the site about product views, favorites and shopping carts, and from the back office — about registration, editing and changing orders.



Mindbox has become a full-fledged element of two-way data exchange between systems, albeit in the external circuit. The project’s master base is the back office of our online supermarket. At the same time, the information that the Mindbox platform receives enriches client data in the back office.



The critical functionality related to the personal data of customers remained in the internal circuit. For example, if a client wants to unsubscribe and clicks on the “Unsubscribe” button, then he gets to the site form, and not to the Mindbox unsubscribe page. For this, an authorization token is used, which is generated on our side.



Collection and storage of information



All the information we collect can be divided into two categories: personal data and customer activity history. Personal data includes gender, date of birth, e-mail, phone number and geographical area. The latter takes into account not only the city, but also the specific warehouse or darkstore to which the client belongs.



Customer action history is at the heart of trigger mailings. The system records information about the behavior of even those users who left their address after they had performed any actions on the site. Even if several days have passed since the transition to the site to the transfer of e-mail, this information will still be saved.



Communications automation



We believe that all successful manual mechanics should be automated, so we are constantly increasing the number of triggers. To successfully implement this practice, it was necessary to make sure that the customers really agreed to receive our newsletters, so we conducted them through a double confirmation of the subscription. People are so arranged that they are frankly furious when, after registering on some site, this site perceives the user's mail as a box to which now everything can be sent. And this is a normal reaction to that. Therefore, it’s better to complicate the process a bit and double the confirmation of the subscription, so that at the exit you get a client who definitely wants to receive mailings.



Thus, by the end of 2019, 21 triggers and 9 automatic campaigns were launched on the project. Five of them included mailing lists and pushies.



Welcome chain



Includes four letters. In the first, the promo code is 1500 rubles, which can be spent not immediately, but in three calls. It is sent immediately after confirmation of the subscription. The rest of the letters are reminders.



For example, a second letter is sent if the client received the first letter, but did not buy anything within three days. Thirdly, if the client once used the promotional code, but didn’t buy anything else during the week. And finally, the fourth one is sent if the client has applied the promotional code twice, and then disappeared and bought nothing within a week. If the client regularly performs all the actions with the promotional code, in the next three letters he will be reminded of how many purchases he has with the promotional code.



Reactivation



Two triggers are involved in this campaign - reactivation of the outflow and reactivation of the outflow. In the first case, the client receives a letter with new promotions and a promotional code for a discount, if he placed three orders, but did not buy anything within 30-45 days. In the second, we recall the opportunity to replenish the refrigerator in the online supermarket. It is received by clients who did not open our letters within 30 days and at the same time did not receive a letter for pre-flow within three months.



Abandoned basket



Here we included two letters and web push. The first letter with the customer’s name and the list of goods left in the basket is sent half an hour after the end of the session, and only to those customers who have not placed an order within 24 hours. The second - 12 hours after the first, if the client hasn’t ordered anything, and the basket contains goods worth more than 4,500 rubles. It also shows the prices of goods at a discount and without if the product falls under any stock.

Web Push Crossroads, we send only to those customers who have not left an email address, or if there is an address, but it is not valid. And also to those who did not agree to receive the newsletter. Push is sent an hour after the end of the session with a frequency of no more than once a week.



Product is in stock



There is only one letter and push. They are received by customers who did not order anything during the week, but visited the site a day or two ago. Information that the product is again available for order is transmitted to Mindbox from our back office.

Newsletter about water



This newsletter takes into account typical customer behavior: customers who ordered water in five-liter bottles more than two weeks ago receive a letter and a push reminding them that the water is over - it's time to order more. Web push, as in the case of an abandoned basket, is received only by those who indicated the wrong address or did not subscribe to the newsletter.





Web push will remind customer that it’s time to replenish water supplies



Determining the optimal conditions for the promotion



At this stage, we tried to figure out what works best - a percentage discount coupon or a coupon for the same amount in rubles. To do this, we randomly divided the outflow segment - customers who made no more than three orders in all, and more than 45 days have passed since the last order - in half, and different coupons were sent to each group.



Thanks to the end-to-end analytics method and detailed funnel, it was possible to determine that the percentage discount is more attractive than in rubles. In the first version, the open rate is 1% lower than in the second, but the click rate is 24% higher, and the conversion is all 80%. Thus, we decided to automate the newsletter at a percentage discount.





Cosmically beautiful newsletter, created by testing discount options



Conclusions and plans for the future



Now standard and not very mechanics of targeted marketing bring 5% of the total revenue of the Perekrestok online supermarket. We were satisfied with the result and do not plan to rest on our laurels - there are many hypotheses left that can and should be verified, and then adjusted to the overall CRM strategy.



In the near future we plan to add a newsletter with messages about products from favorites, the price of which has decreased, and also start sending letters about regular purchases (as in the example with water delivery). At the same time, we plan to determine the distribution time using an algorithm based on machine learning.

But there are other fire robots, or rather robotic fire systems, which are installed in premises for extinguishing a fire in the early stages. That's about them and will be discussed.

Such complexes are installed after the construction or repair of the building - they are the main standard fire extinguishing system. "Artificial intelligence" is needed in order not to work out how much in vain and extinguish only a fire, and not flood the entire room. Fire robot systems, or if it‚Äôs simpler, it‚Äôs a stationary fire carriage (in other words, a device for supplying a jet of water or foam), which can rotate around its axis and supply water up and down 180 degrees. An IR sensor is mounted on the robot to direct the jet to a fire. Plus a control system in the form of a controller. Such robots can be installed both on the floor, and on supporting columns, ceiling or special towers - to extinguish, which is convenient.



The main disadvantage of the robots are stationary - they can not move in space. Several such robots should be placed on the premises. In our experience, 6 units are needed per 33 x 105 m workshop, which is expensive.

In our case, a ‚Äúdouble protection‚Äù is obtained - first the stationary sensors must ‚Äúcatch‚Äù the fire, then the infrared sensor of the robot must localize it. If you install sensitive sensors in the room, then there will be no false extinguishing. But this allows you to prevent fire at the stage of decay.

For example, in a certain workshop or warehouse, wiring began to smolder at night. Gas sensors (which record an increase in CO level) have detected this. Our fire robots (2 pcs.) Arrived at the signal, but did not detect a fire. So, it is not necessary to fill in and the room is safe for people. In standby mode, robots travel along rails, scanning rooms (just in case). And the attendants visit the room - they check it, deal with the causes of the alarm and, as a result, de-energize the smoldering wiring, which is replaced on the next business day by the duty shift of electricians. There is practically no damage. Otherwise, after several hours of smoldering, a fire would have appeared, it would have spread to the room. And only then would the fire system work. And would flood everything ...

April 4-5 will be a selection for online intensives in the framework of the festival RuCode

April 4-5, the selection for online intensives within the framework of the RuCode festival will be held.

This is a program for beginners in the field of sports programming (divisions C and D) and artificial intelligence. Everyone is invited to participate - schoolchildren, university and college students, specialists and so on.

You can register and apply for participation until April 3, inclusive, on the site rucode.net .



During the selection process it will be necessary to decide:

  • on the track for sports programming: individual (!) tour of 10 tasks in 4 hours;
  • to the artificial intelligence track: 10 math problems, 3 programming tasks and 2 AI tasks in an individual format.

What will happen next


Further training will take place on two tracks:

Sports programming - the best participants in the selection will be divided into divisions by difficulty level - C and D. Interactive intensives will be held from April 22 to 24 in the format of online lectures, contests and online reviews. For three days to master 10 hours of lecture classes and 15 hours of practical. Topics: computational geometry, dynamic programming, number theory, features of working with algorithms and others. Lecturers of the course are teachers of the largest universities in Russia (MIPT, UrFU, NSU, FEFU).

Artificial Intelligence- training will take place in the format of online lectures and work on your project. From April 10 to 21, 8 online lectures will be held. The lecturers of the course are MIPT teachers and experts from large IT companies. According to the results of intensive studies, 25 best students will present their projects. The track and tasks were developed by MIPT, Sberbank and the Analytical Center under the Government of the Russian Federation.

All graduates will be invited without selection to participate in the online RuCode Championship on April 25-26.

The RuCode program was developed by the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, Sberbank, MegaFon, Yandex and the Analytical Center under the Government of the Russian Federation with the support of the PhysTech Schools Development Fund using a grant from the President of Russia provided by the Presidential Grants Fund.

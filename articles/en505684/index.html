Difficulty must dwell somewhere

The fight against complexity is a constant topic in the field of software development, which I meet again and again. This is something that I constantly see in discussions at all levels, well, for example, how many comments should accompany methods and functions? What is the ideal "amount" of abstraction? We are trying to get rid of complexity, curb it and seek simplicity. I think that imagining the essence of things this way is the wrong way. Difficulty must live somewhere.


There is one thing of discipline construction of sustainable systems (resilience engineering), who taught me the concept of the necessary variation (requisite variety) of cybernetics only complexity can be kept under the control of a different complexity.


When you are dealing with build tools, a few things appear and become apparent:


  • if you make assembly tools simple, they cannot cover all the oddities of boundary cases that arise in practice
  • if you want to handle these strange cases, you have to move away from the agreements and norms that you would like to establish
  • if you want to facilitate the use of typical cases, the rules for them should be created both in the tool itself and well known to users who adjust the structure of their systems in accordance with the expectations of the build system
  • if you allow advanced configuration or script support, you give users a way to define new rules, which in turn should be extended to other users and the tool will remain compatible with their systems
  • if you want to keep the instrument simple, you are forced to force users to play only according to certain rules within the set parameters, so that the instrument remains simple

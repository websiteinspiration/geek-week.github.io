Reflections on the coronavirus epidemic COVID-19

I decided to write this article because the coronavirus epidemic and the events it accompanies amaze me. There is a lot of information, it is scattered and contradictory, so I decided to make an attempt to analyze the facts first of all for myself, well, and share my findings with someone else. It is especially important, in my opinion, to pay attention to things that are not found in every first article of news sites, but which lie close to the surface, you only need to dig a little.

Part 1. About health


People were told and constantly reminded:

  • wear masks
  • to wash hands
  • self insulate
  • keep your distance in public
  • and in some places quarantined with fines

But something I don’t hear regularly from the same sources that

  • self-insulating, i.e. staying at home constantly is a lack of exercise that leads to serious health consequences especially for older people
  • self-isolation is a lack of communication , which can lead to psychological problems, stress, and for some people in general to mental disorders

There are other negative events that are not directly related to health, such as unemployment, rising prices for antiviral drugs, masks, gloves, hand gels, closing borders, etc., which is not the topic of the article.

Open questions:

  1. Was an analysis of each of the negative factors and a comparison of the sum of these factors with the benefit (if any) of the current recommendations to confront the epidemic? There is an assumption that these factors may increase mortality , and without the direct involvement of coronavirus.
  2. Why aren't recommendations made how to reduce stress from negative news, these graphs and statistics of deaths, how to increase immunity, how to reduce negative factors of self-isolation, washing hands, wearing masks?

After all, gullible people, especially the elderly, who do not really understand the situation, usually do what they are told on TV and on the main news sites. And it turns out that news can drive public health.

Part 2. About statistics and news


I sincerely do not understand what the graphs and tables show, which have flooded the Internet in abundance, why they show us what useful conclusions can be drawn from them.
Which of the news is clear for sure:

  • there are tests for coronavirus
  • in some cases, tests are carried out
  • sometimes positive tests
  • sometimes negative
  • sometimes people die
  • it all somehow gets into the statistics
  • some of this statistics is leaked to the media

All.

Let's try to figure it out.

Fact No. 1


Contacts of the 1st and especially the 2nd level in a densely populated city cannot be determined with 100% accuracy. To understand this,

a) just look at any photo of the metro station of the city with a million-plus population during rush hour . How many people were next to the infected person, who exactly and where to look for them, and how, in turn, to search for those with whom these next people already contacted?

b) you need to know that the SARS-CoV-2 virus carrier may have mild symptoms. And WHO generally admits that some data show that you can get infected from a person with an asymptomatic course of the disease .

c) optional, but it is worth noting that the so-called 0th patientcannot always be correctly determined, especially taking into account point b. Despite the winning news from China about closed Wuhan, breathtaking videos about how they disinfected the streets in anti-plague costumes, I have big doubts that the virus remained sealed inside and did not spread to the rest of China. In addition, there is a contradiction: the virus flew from Wuhan to different countries by air, but China supposedly did not penetrate the rest, although the passenger flow between Wuhan and the rest of China is much greater than between Wuhan and the rest of the world (only by air). And this is all assuming that the 0th patient was really in Wuhan and really at the time that the Chinese were reporting, and not earlier and not elsewhere.

Fact No. 2


The entire population of the country is not tested every day without a trace in order to really understand at least something useful about the epidemic.

Ideally, you need data for every day:

  1. How many people in total (country's population)
  2. How many people today are carriers of the virus, i.e. keep getting sick
  3. How many people are already ill today, are not carriers and have antibodies (i.e. they can relax and stop being afraid)
  4. How many people today have not yet become infected, i.e. do not have antibodies
  5. How many people died today
  6. How many of the people who died today at the time of death were carriers of the virus, i.e. were ill with COVID-19 at the time of death
  7. How many of the people who died today were ill and had antibodies, i.e. at the time of death were not carriers of the virus

Only by comparing these data from day to day (or at least from week to week) can one really follow up on something. Perhaps doctors and data analysts could still add the necessary parameters, but certainly not remove it. And we do not have this either. So why then all these statistics in the news.

PS: I decided not to address in the article slippery issues such as conspiracy theories (chipization and biological weapons), vaccines and an enhanced immune response, confusion over deaths from “coronavirus” or “with coronavirus”, the interest of insurance and pharmaceutical companies, false-positive and false negative test results, questions like “look for who benefits from this,” etc. since there is too much speculation or uncertainty in these topics.

Be healthy, maintain immunity in the norm, I suggest that you independently reflect on this interesting situation and draw your own conclusions.

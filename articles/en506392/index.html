Hard copy vs electronic document: notification form on the processing of personal data at Roskomnadzor


When sending notifications on the processing of personal data to the Federal Service for Supervision of Communications, Information Technologies and Mass Communications, operators often ask themselves whether they need to send a letter with a printed notification or just fill out the electronic notification form on the Roskomnadzor portal.

FZ-152, as well as Clause 3.2 of the Guidelines for the notification of the beginning of the processing of personal data, approved. by order of Roskomnadzor No. 94 dated 05/30/2017 they are allowed to send a notification on paper or in the form of an electronic document.

Despite this, it was not possible to completely exclude paper media from the workflow - since paper documents are the legal basis for registration actions, operators are obliged to send paper media regardless of whether they were submitted electronically.

In practice, the notification of personal data to Roskomnadzor takes place in two stages:

  1. filling out the notification form of Roskomnadzor in electronic form - it is necessary to expedite the work of entering data into the register and to obtain a finished completed form for printing on paper;
  2. sending the notification form on paper - is the basis for registration actions by Roskomnadzor (the notification is registered with the authorized body and is the basis for the operator to be entered in the register).

In the future, a full transition to electronic document management is planned, but until this moment, operators cannot avoid paper work and must send issued and signed notifications on paper.

Roskomnadzor notification form on the processing of personal data in 2020


FZ-152 does not approve the mandatory form or form for notification of the processing of personal data. According to the law, the main thing is to provide information that is specified in the norm.

The notification form for the processing of personal data recommended for use by Roskomnadzor is contained in Appendix No. 1 to the Methodological Recommendations for Notification of the Start of Processing of Personal Data, approved. by order of Roskomnadzor No. 94 dated 05/30/2017 . This form contains the information required by Art. 22 FZ-152 and necessary for inclusion in the registry of operators.

In practice, the easiest and fastest way to get a completed notification of personal data to Roskomnadzor is to fill out a formproposed on the personal data portal and without changes to print it on paper for sending to Roskomnadzor. Filling out a notification to Roskomnadzor: signing, processing and sending notifications on the processing of personal data


When issuing and sending notifications about the processing of personal data, operators often have questions related to the signing and execution of notifications. Below are the most popular ones.

Who can sign a notification of personal data to Roskomnadzor?

According to FZ-152, a notification to Roskomnadzor must be signed by an authorized person. There are no special requirements for signing notifications. Based on the general provisions of the law, several options are possible here:

  • Signing a notification by a person who has the right to sign without a power of attorney - managers most often refer to them (you can check it in the USRLE ).
  • Signing a proxy notice - may be signed by another person. In this case, a power of attorney for the right to sign must be attached to the notification.

Are there any additional requirements for notifications?

The operator needs to print, sign a notice, stamp it and send it to the territorial office of Roskomnadzor. The notice on paper must indicate the current date of sending the notice in paper form. If the notice is signed by proxy, you must also attach to it a executed power of attorney or a duly certified copy.

Of the additional requirements, Roskomnadzor also recommends placing a notice on the operator‚Äôs letterhead.

At the same time, it is important to understand that the receipt of a paper document by Roskomnadzor is the basis for registration actions, therefore, fixing the fact of receiving a letter by Roskomnadzor is in the interests of the operator.

Filling out a notification to Roskomnadzor: terms and procedure for sending notifications on the processing of personal data


FZ-152 obliges personal data operators to send a notice of the beginning of the processing of personal data. Operators should not neglect the fulfillment of this requirement - failure to notify Roskomnadzor may result in administrative liability in accordance with Article 19.7 of the Administrative Offenses Code of the Russian Federation
Notification to Roskomnadzor implies the inclusion of an operator in the register of personal data processing operators . To be included in the register, the operator must fill out and send a notice on the processing of personal data.

The term for sending a notification by the operator is before the processing of personal data begins. The registration deadline is 30 days from the date of registration of the notification by Roskomnadzor. As a rule, the operator is not notified of inclusion in the registry. You can track the status of the notification yourself by the link . To check the status of a notification, you need to remember its number and key - they can be found at the end of each electronic notification after filling it out.

Filling out a notification to Roskomnadzor: the goals of processing a notification about the processing of personal data


The sending of a notification about the processing of personal data is a condition for the beginning of the processing of personal data, and its incorrect completion may be assessed by Roskomnadzor as providing incomplete or inaccurate information about the processing of data.

Despite the apparent simplicity of filling out a notification, operators often encounter difficulties in determining the purposes of processing personal data and continue to indicate in the notification an incomplete list of purposes for processing them.

Defining the goals of data processing

The purposes of processing personal data should be specific, predetermined, legal and relevant to the activities in which such processing is carried out.
In determining the goals of processing personal data and filling out a notice, the following should be analyzed:

  1. The objectives of the operator‚Äôs activities, as defined in its charter, local acts, consent to the processing of personal data, questionnaires, agreements providing for the transfer of personal data, etc.
  2. The grounds for obtaining personal data, the activities that the operator carries out when receiving and processing personal data on such grounds.

The purposes indicated in the notification should cover all cases of processing personal data. At the same time, one must take into account both the external activity of the company - work with customers, users, the conclusion and execution of contracts, marketing activity, etc., as well as the internal - selection and admission of personnel, collection of data about employees, organization of access control.

Examples of filling out information about the purposes of processing personal data in notifications:
the purpose of the processing, registration of information necessary for the provision of services to students in the field of education, personal data of employees, information about their professional performance

the purpose of the processing, registration of information necessary for the provision of housing and communal services to owners, tenants of premises, personal data of employees, information about their professional performance

When setting goals, it is recommended to attract specialists who are familiar with all the activities of the company, and not employed in one department - this will ensure the correctness and completeness of work in the field of personal data processing.



We fill out notifications about the processing of personal data: Categories of personal data


Indication of an incomplete list of processed personal data is one of the typical violations in the field of processing personal data. In order to avoid difficulties in filling out the notification, we will figure out what information about personal data should be indicated in the processing notification.

It follows from the law that categories of personal data are a list of personal data, specific information about the subject, with the help of which he is identified or can be identified.

Personal data are grouped depending on their characteristics, on the basis of which they are classified as special, biometric and other types of categories of personal data.

Electronic Notification Formon the Roskomnadzor portal, it proposes, when determining the categories of personal data, to indicate specific information that the operator will collect - name, date, place of birth, marital and social status, etc.

The difficulty in determining the category of personal data when filling out a notification is due to the fact that the list itself personal data is not exhaustive.
In the electronic notification form there is a column ‚ÄúOther categories of personal data not specified in this list‚Äù, in which the operator independently enters the processed personal data.



Operators do not indicate all ‚Äúother categories‚Äù and, as a result, violate legal requirements. So, operators often do not indicateinformation about the composition of the family; on labor and general experience, military registration; TIN; SNILS, etc.

In order to avoid incorrect filling out of the processing notice, it is necessary to clearly define the purposes of processing personal data, and then indicate which data of the subjects of personal data will be processed to achieve them.

In this case, it is necessary to take into account the peculiarities of the interpretation of the provisions of the legislation regarding the classification of certain data as personal. So, according to the approach of Roskomnadzor , biometric personal data may include not only image data of a fingerprint, iris, etc., but also facial images.

We fill out notifications on the processing of personal data: categories of subjects of personal data


Article 22 of the Federal Law-152 obliges operators to indicate the categories of entities whose personal data are processed. It is logical that individuals are the subjects of personal data, but the question remains how to identify them in the notification of Roskomnadzor.

Categories of personal subjects - this is an indication of the legal status of personal data subjects, determining the place of individuals in relations with the operator.

So, if the operator processes the information in order to process the personal data of the personnel, then such an individual is defined in the notice as ‚Äúemployee‚Äù, which is the category of personal data subjects.

Counterparties must be identified as a party to the type of civil contracts concluded with them.

Roskomnadzor in the Guidelines for the notification of the beginning of the processing of personal data, approved. by order of Roskomnadzor No. 94 dated 05/30/2017 recommends to indicate in the notifications the types of relations of the operator with the subjects of personal data, i.e. determine what relations (labor, civil law, etc.) link the operator and the individual subject of personal data.

Thus, to indicate complete and reliable information about the processing of personal data, to properly notify Roskomnadzor, it is necessary to describe the categories of personal data according to the following pattern:
employees in labor relations with the operator; individuals (subscriber, passenger, customer), in contractual or other civil law relations with the operator

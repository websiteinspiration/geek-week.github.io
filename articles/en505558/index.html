Amazon DeepLens Deep Learning Camera. Unpacking, connecting and deploying a project



At the beginning of the year, my colleague and I began to be interested in various technologies related to artificial intelligence and machine learning. To begin with, we decided to try to master the Amazon product - AWS DeepLens camera. We ordered the device on the official website and want to share the experience of use. There are many nuances in setting up and connecting the camera, and at the moment there is little structured documentation on this issue, which leads to a lot of labor during installation and configuration. We hope this tutorial helps you avoid wasting time and you can quickly start using the device.

What is AWS DeepLens?


AWS DeepLens is an Amazon Deep Learning camera. It is a camera mounted on a small system unit with an Intel Atom processor and Ubuntu 16.04 LTS system. About her a couple of years ago there was news on Habr√© .

The peculiarity of the device is in the initial setup for working with Deep Learning. AWS DeepLens has preinstalled special software that allows you to deploy several projects prepared for working with Deep Learning.

The following projects have been prepared for deployment:

  • Accurate detection and recognition of objects;
  • Classification of food according to the criterion of "hot dog or not a hot dog";
  • Dog and cat recognition using DeepLens;
  • Recognition of about two hundred species of birds;
  • Recognition of more than 30 types of actions, such as brushing your teeth, applying lipstick or playing the guitar;
  • Detection of human faces;
  • Determining the position of the head at nine different angles.

Each project is a trained model, ready to use. Model training itself is not possible on AWS DeepLens. Projects are downloaded from the Internet. But after loading the camera can draw logical conclusions without the Internet - locally.

To prepare and train the model, you must use a separate service - AWS SageMaker. Once the model is prepared and trained, it can be transferred to AWS DeepLens and already tested and modified here. AWS DeepLens can also import an external-trained model.

Connection and setup


The entire connection and configuration process consists of several steps:

  • Initial connection of the camera to the computer through a wire and product registration locally;
  • Wi-fi internet connection and registration in the AWS Management Console;
  • Creating a new project and choosing a prepared model;
  • Download the project with the model to the AWS DeepLens device (on the device 16 GB of internal memory + 32 GB card included);
  • Deployment of the project on the device;

After all the steps are completed, you can work with the input data of the camcorder and receive the necessary logical responses from the device.

1. Connect the camera to the Internet

A secure certificate is required for secure, encrypted data transfers between the camera and Amazon servers. It can be downloaded from the link specified in the installation steps in deeplens.config.

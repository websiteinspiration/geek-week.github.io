VKontakte, MIPT and GSOM SPbSU have created a machine learning course for computer science teachers

VKontakte together with MIPT and GSOM SPbSU launches a free educational course for teachers of computer science. They will learn the basics of machine learning and will be able to use new knowledge in their work. Also among the topics are IT project management and student motivation. Teachers who complete the course will receive a certificate of professional development.

Any teacher can take the course. Recording is already open: stepik.org/course/71439/promo

The course will be hosted on the stepik.org platform. It consists of 21 videos, each of which is devoted to a separate topic - classes can be completed at a convenient pace until August 24. In half of them, theory will be understood, and in the other half, teachers will have to put new knowledge into practice. Videos last an average of 7-15 minutes - it is convenient to watch on the road or in a pause between other things. Teachers will also receive homework and materials for further study, and in the special community VKontakte will be able to ask clarifying questions to the organizers and share experiences with colleagues.

Among the teachers of the course: Andrey Raigorodsky, Director of the PhysTech School of Applied Mathematics and Computer Science of the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, Pavel Kalaydin, Director of Artificial Intelligence Research on VKontakte, and Margarita Gladkova, Senior Lecturer, Department of Operational Management, Graduate School of Management, St. Petersburg State University.

The course is divided into three modules.

  • Eight weeks devoted to ML. Teachers will study the theoretical foundations of machine learning and will consider how VKontakte applies them in practice: in advertisements, smart news feeds, content promotion, safe searches and other products.
  • For two weeks, teachers will comprehend the management of IT projects. They will learn how to build projects from scratch, and will also receive methodological recommendations with which they can transfer skills to students.
  • The last lesson will affect the features of motivation of students. Teachers will learn more about effective ways to work with students and approaches to maintaining attention.

When the course on stepik.org is completed, all videos will be posted on VK. Anyone can see them, but in this case, receive feedback and a certificate of advanced training will not work.

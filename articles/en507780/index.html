Recursion of consciousness is a trap of thinking and a barrier to progress. Historical essay

Since the emergence of language as a second signaling system - an instrument for thinking and communication, mankind has gained a critical advantage over all other living beings, whose behavior is controlled by first-level systems and therefore is fundamentally limited in levels of abstraction.

But, giving a person a tool of tremendous power, language and abstract thinking at the same time create restrictions and traps that lead to problems, conflicts and destructive behavior. What mankind has been doing for the last 5000 years.(Maybe 500,000, but no written evidence).

Most wars, conflicts, quarrels and killings occur or are justified by ideological, ideological, religious, political and other abstract reasons lying in the sphere of ideas and information, and not the material world. And before war, conflict, quarrel leads to real actions, it is always preceded by a stage of intention, purpose, preparation, planning, escalation, organization, which takes place at the level of words and information.

The source and instrument of all wars, murders, discrimination, the Inquisition, oppression is language.

But he is also an instrument of science, culture, progress, development, civilization.

Since ancient times, the best minds understood this and tried to fix it. Of course, Aristotle made the greatest contribution to the development of civilization. The development of formal logic has provided a simple, but still universally recognized and easily applicable tool for resolving disputes and searching for truth. In essence, this created the foundation, the foundation of all Western civilization. Although still understands and knows how to use formal logic, according to various estimates, no more than 1-10% of the world's population. And already in those days, philosophers and thinkers, using this tool, came across a trap of recursion, which lies at the heart of a significant part of paradoxes, aporias and logical contradictions.

Exits from the logical impasse began to be found only with the development of natural and exact sciences somewhere around the beginning of the 20th century. They were found, as might be expected, by representatives of the most formal and exact science - mathematics. B. Russell in his Foundations of Mathematics (1903) showed that a class cannot include itself as an element of a class, and a type cannot be a subtype of itself.

In this article, the author shares his experience of interviewing at Microsoft and gives some tips.

About 2-3 months ago, I noticed on Linkedin several messages from Microsoft employees about a campaign to select candidates for a summer internship. I waited a long time for this and did not want to miss the opportunity.

I sent a resume with a detailed description of my achievements and experience, including all my projects, to 15-20 people from Microsoft in the hope that at least one of them would invite me to an online test.

Key points:

  1. Do not be afraid to contact strangers. In the end, you are trying to make contact. The simpler you relate to this, the easier it will be to achieve your goal.
  2. When contacting, do not write ‚ÄúHello! Could you hire me to work for your company? ‚Äù Instead, write a good message, describe your achievements, experience, projects, give them a reason to invite you.

Within 1-2 hours of correspondence, I received an invitation from Microsoft for the first round of interviews, which is a round of coding.

It was in the courtyard on September 22, 2019.

I got three tasks and a decent time to solve them.

When there were 5 minutes left until the end of the round, my laptop broke down. The worst thing that could happen. Fortunately, I found the opportunity to continue testing after a while.

I managed to solve all three problems, pass all control tests, including additional questions.

I was very positive and was looking forward to the results.

The results were announced on October 11th and yes, I was invited to the second round.


The second round was an online video interview. I expected the interview to be mainly about data structures, and I began to prepare for it.

The interview took place on October 14th. It began with a short introduction, then the interviewer began to ask questions about OOP. I was delighted because I never studied this topic in college, all my concepts were as simple as 10 + 2 (2 * 2).

I was asked questions about inheritance, polymorphism, the difference between reloading and overriding methods, the virtual keyword and abstraction.

Key moment:

  • If you do not know the answer to the question, do not say what is horrible, but carefully listen to the interlocutor and agree with him. Moreover, as much as possible inquire about this interlocutor. This will show that you want to learn new things. I was not familiar with the virtual keyword and, instead of talking nonsense, I honestly admitted that I had never heard of it, after which we had an interesting discussion for 2-3 minutes.

The second half of the interview was devoted to solving problems. The main goal is to test our thinking and approaches to solving new problems.

The task was as follows:

Suppose you have such a three-dimensional array sorted in the following spiral order:

	01 02 03 04
	12 13 14 05
	11 16 15 06
	10 09 08 07

What is the best way to find a number in such an array?

At first I could not come up with a good solution and called the worst (O (n 2 )), then I began to think and optimize.

Since I was thinking out loud, the interlocutor gave me a little help, which ultimately led me on the right path.

Key points:

  1. Think Aloud. Let your interlocutor understand what you are thinking and direct your thoughts in the right direction.
  2. Ask your interlocutor clarifying questions if something is not clear to you.
  3. Start with the worst solution and then improve it.
  4. Whatever solution you propose, be prepared to implement it in code.

PS: I started with a solution with complexity O (n 2 ), and ended with O (log (n)), and the pleasure of this is hard to put into words.

I was positive, despite some difficulties that arose during this round, and, fortunately, I received an invitation to the next round to the campus.


I arrived at the Microsoft office in Bangalore (India) on November 17 right in the middle of the examination session, because I did not want to miss such a great opportunity.

Honestly, their campus is a dream job. He is awesome!



Since I arrived early, I had to wait at the front desk. I did not waste time repeating data structures on GeekforGeeks.

Soon we were invited to the meeting room and announced that we (about 20 students) were chosen from more than 11 thousand applicants.

After the greeting, the interview began.

Round one


This round was dedicated to the resume. The interlocutor stated that they liked my resume. He asked me about projects, trying to better understand what work I had to do.

Key points:

  1. You must be sure of every word of your resume.
  2. Do not write what you are not good at.

After that, the interlocutor decided to test my thinking abilities by asking two questions about data structures.

PS: Microsoft strives for excellence, it was noticeable in each interlocutor. Each algorithm and code has been tested.

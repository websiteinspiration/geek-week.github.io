Business protection for Internet entrepreneurs: where to open a company and account, what to look for

The era of the Internet, as well as the coronavirus epidemic, have shown that business is finally and irrevocably moving to the Internet. The pandemic situation has proved that only someone who is on the Internet and offers their products and services remotely can really count on surviving in a modern dynamic world.

However, simply creating a website and launching advertising on the Internet will not allow you to create a successful and long-lived company. An online business is still a business and requires an appropriate attitude.

In addition, the online space can be no less cruel to entrepreneurs than the physical world. Thefts, courts, hacking, dissatisfied customers and negative reviews - this also exists and if handled carelessly, it can cause permanent damage to your company.

That is why when starting your Internet business, you should think about protection.
As part of this article, we will discuss the following issues:

  • What kind of business can I do online?
  • Where to register a company for online business (and is it needed at all)?
  • Where to put the server for the site?
  • Where to open an account for the company?

Individual data protection


However, before moving on to the company, it is important to recall the obvious thing that is so often forgotten: individual responsibility.



The protection of personal data, including passport data, passwords from Internet banking and payment systems, contracts and agreements, is the responsibility of the entrepreneur. It is often easier to steal this data in a digital environment than in a physical office.

That is why no matter what business you run, whatever systems you use and in which country you open an account, do not forget about basic security. Including:

  • A variety of passwords for different systems and devices and their constant update;
  • Use of secure connections;
  • Data encryption;
  • Lack of access for relatives and children to your corporate data (sometimes a random selfie with your monitor can damage the company's reputation and financial well-being);
  • Well thought out contracts;
  • Restrained behavior.

These "little things" seem insignificant exactly until they cause millions of losses even to the largest companies. What can we say about a smaller business or a startup.

Where to put a server for Internet business?


A little unexpected question for a newcomer to the Internet business, but quite important in a strategic perspective. The fact is that according to the legislation of some countries, for example, the USA, the presence of a server in the country actually makes the business local. Even if the company that uses this service is registered in Hong Kong or Nicaragua.



In this case, it is necessary to comply with the laws of not only the country in which the company is created, but also the laws in which the server with the site on board is located.

So if for some reason you decide to create a project that could potentially violate the laws of a particular country and place a server with a website in that same country, do not be surprised that law enforcement agencies may contact you at some point.

Personal Data Protection


A few words about information protection.

Why protect personal data? If strangers know that you are the owner of the company, various scammers may reach you. Especially when the business goes uphill. Lenders, greedy relatives, fraud specialists are ready to study your online business, evaluate it in your own way and demand a share, I manipulate each with my own argument.

Therefore, protecting data from prying eyes will be useful.

At the same time, remember: hiding information about the true owner from the bank and even more so from the tax leads to unpleasant consequences.

There are countries where the data on owners and directors are in the public domain: I went to a special site, chose a company and it is immediately clear who the business owner is.

There are jurisdictions where the register of owners (beneficiaries - true beneficiaries) exists, but is available only to the authorities or by special request.

In addition, you can build company structures. For example, a holding company is the owner of another company. Or a trust, fund, or other form of asset ownership is used.

Can I open a company remotely?


Everything is done remotely on the Internet, so the logical question is: is it possible to open a company, including a foreign one, remotely?

The answer is unequivocal - yes, it can.

Many countries allow companies to be registered remotely, including to foreigners. The minimum set of documents required to register a company:

  • Copy of the passport of the owner and director (translated and certified);
  • Proof of address of residence - most often a utility bill;
  • A set of corporate documents - often a standard charter, memorandum, etc. However, if you wish, you can make adjustments.

In some countries, you can open a company yourself, and in some, for example, in classic offshore companies, this can only be done through a licensed registration agent.



Remotely register a company after consulting a specialist. Even if you refuse his services in the registration itself, his advice will help you find the right jurisdiction for registration, as well as circumvent pitfalls.

For example, now the demand for classic offshore companies has sharply intensified: Nevis, the Marshall Islands, Belize, and the British Virgin Islands. These are excellent jurisdictions for business, however, it is not always possible for companies in them to open a bank account easily and quickly. This may take several months. But not everyone wants to wait. As a result, unnecessary emotions and experiences. The specialist will tell you where it is possible to open an account and for how long it is worth counting.

Where to open a company?


In which country to open a company for online business and protect yourself? The choice is strictly individual and depends on your tasks, target audience and type of activity. This may be your country of residence or foreign jurisdiction.

Before choosing a country, answer yourself the following questions:

What taxes am I willing to pay?


The first answer, of course, is as little as possible. But in fact, there are nuances everywhere. For example, open an offshore in the Marshall Islands. Zero tax, great reputation. It will take time to open an account, but it is virtually impossible to obtain a banking, insurance or gaming license. But for a normal online trading business, it fits perfectly.



Or Canada: at first glance, high taxes, but an excellent reputation, the ability to open accounts in Canada and around the world. If you know how, you can completely officially make corporate tax in Canada equal to zero.

Or Hong Kong - the tax is, relatively low. But if you get profit outside the country, then you can avoid taxes.

So you can find opportunities everywhere - the main thing is to ask the right questions.
Who is my target customer and where is it located? Where are my partners and contractors located?

If your customers reside in Germany, it is wise to open a company in Germany. Or at least within the framework of the European Union. For example, if you created a fintech startup, you can get to open a company in Estonia, and get an EMI license in Lithuania, and then work in Europe.

The main point: the presence of customers or partners in a particular country may be the reason for opening accounts. Otherwise, foreign bank accounts may not be easy to open.

Where to open an account for online business?

And the last item on the list, but not in importance, is opening an account. Why can this item be safely related to security and business protection? Because without a working account, your online business will rise instantly.

It should be understood that in online business two accounts are often used: corporate (business settlement account) and merchant account. The second is needed in order to accept payment using cards and other means of payment, for example, cryptocurrencies or checks.

You can open a merchant account directly at a financial institution, or you can use payment gateways that allow you to combine several payment methods. A fee is charged for using a merchant account, usually for each transaction.

A popular international payment gateway is Stripe. There are also other systems. In Russia, for example, these are Robokassa, Freekassa and others.

However, from these accounts it is impossible, most often, to pay employees or company accounts, including taxes. It is required to withdraw funds to a regular corporate account. You can open an account with a bank at the place of registration of the company, in another country, or even in the payment system.

When we talk about international business and the protection of assets in it, it is worth diversifying risks whenever possible. This means that you do not need to store all the eggs in one basket. Ideally, open multiple accounts in different countries. Even if one account is closed, you can continue to work.

Although at the start you can open an account in the country of registration of the company - today it is the most predictable option. If this is not possible (for example, you have opened a company in Estonia, but your business has no connection with Estonia), then payment systems will be useful. It‚Äôs easier to open an account in them than in banks. Although they offer a similar service, and often faster and cheaper.

Choosing an account for a business is worth it before you open a company. The choice of bank affects where and in what form you will register your business. In addition, you can immediately prepare the required documents, for example, copies of statutory documents with an apostille - during registration this can be done cheaper than after all procedures are completed.

In the future, we recommend that everyone have at least 2 corporate accounts - this will give strength to the business, for which online transactions are the foundation of existence.

Considering that they can close an account for no reason, simply because the state policy has changed, as happened in Latvia, insurance will not hurt.

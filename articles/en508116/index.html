7 useful extensions for learning English in Google Chrome

We always say that learning English is a must. Even when you are working with documentation or watching memes. If you work a lot at the computer, then browser add-ons can greatly help you learn English.



We will not pickle for a long time. Here are 7 of the most useful browser-based add-ons for upgrading English in Google Chrome. Go.









Ginger





A great extension for those who want to work with English texts in the ecosystem format.



The extension is very feature rich. It is designed specifically to work with the tests that you write. With it, you can check the text for grammatical errors, the logical construction of sentences, pick up synonyms or translate the text.



Although the quality of the translation is inferior to Google Translate, it is very convenient.



We should also mention the instant dictionary feature. To recognize an unknown word, you need to double-click on it. A popup window appears with the value. An important point is not with the translation, but with the explanation in English. This is a great tool that helps expand your vocabulary.



Grammarly





Top resource for grammar checking. Best to date, to be precise. No kidding.



It has a very wide pool of settings, with which you can check for errors in messages on Facebook, Twitter or other social networks, and correct emails. The system will tell you which phrases are best replaced by synonyms, and which ones - completely removed.



You can also turn on dictionary mode and see the meanings of words that you don‚Äôt know.



True, there is a minus. The functionality of the free version is pretty much cut back. The ecosystem of the language, which includes the extension, is fully disclosed in the paid version.



A premium package costs $ 30 per month, so it makes sense to buy it only for those whose work is directly related to the texts in English. The free version will be a good help for those who work with English not constantly, but from time to time.



ED Translator





A simple but functional tool for learning English. With it, you can translate individual words or phrases and add them to an interactive dictionary for further study.



An extension is part of the EdTech ecosystem, so you can train new words that you added using it in a mobile application or on a web portal.



In general, it helps to achieve two goals at once: it simplifies working with English texts of any complexity and helps to pump vocabulary.



EachWord Translator





A focused tool for expanding vocabulary. When you work with text and see an unknown word, you can right-click to see its meaning and send it to the dictionary.



Then, with the browser open, you will occasionally be shown cards with words in which you need to select the correct meaning of the word.



You need to get used to this technique, because the cards show you both during work and during rest - you only need a running browser. But after a few months, the result will be noticeable. In this way, you can learn up to 3-5 words per day.



Kypris language immersion





An extremely interesting tool that replaces some of the words in the texts in your own language with English vocabulary. It looks rather unusual.



But this is just a great way to learn words in context. This is easier than reading the entire English text, while ensuring high engagement. Therefore, this technique is suitable for people with Pre-Intermediate and Intermediate proficiency who are not yet able to read English texts freely.



To see the original word, just click on it. You can turn absolutely any text into educational material: from a meme story to an art book. For beginners in English - just a must have.



But if your level of knowledge already allows you to understand at least 60% of the vocabulary in the text, read better in the original.



ProWritingAid Grammar Checker & Writing Coach





A great resource that helps improve the writing style of the English language. This is a cross between a corrector who corrects too obvious mistakes and a tutor who advises how to write a sentence or phrase more naturally and accurately.



The main advantage of the tool is that with its help you can use the language in its entirety, not limited to "student" phrases. For example, the phrase "he walked across the room quickly" the system will offer to replace with "he rushed across the room". The meaning of the phrase has not changed, but it began to sound more natural.



Over time, you can get rid of such simplified phrases and use normal ones that a native speaker would use in your place.



Reverso Translate in Context





The extension duplicates the functionality of the well-known Reverso Context site and allows you to translate words and phrases directly in the context of their use. But the extension is much more convenient than the web version.



You just select the phrase, and in the pop-up window appears its translation and examples of sentences that are in the service database. That is, you can fully understand the context of the phrase and its nuances.



A very useful tool also for those who write in English. After all, the resource allows you to find out the most natural phrases that are used by users most often.



***



Noticed that we did not specify Google Translate? The reason is that part of its functions are already embedded in the Google Chrome browser. Therefore, installing one of the extensions that we talked about above will be much more useful.

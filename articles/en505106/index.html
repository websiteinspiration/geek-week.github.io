Dry water and sea knee-deep

Sitting locked up on a June afternoon, we decided to dispel you a bit, share the news about the fire extinguishing systems in our vast, and at the same time tell about interesting objects protected by dry water systems.

Dry water is a liquid gas, fluorinated ketone, used as a gas extinguishing agent around the world. If you want to get more detailed information about the features of dry water, you can see the note of 2013, where we tell in detail what it is and how it is applied.

Dry water, as they called Novec 1230 in Russia, is an innovative solution that first appeared in Russia in 2006, just 14 years ago. But did you know that today you see Novec 1230 almost every day, protecting property in server rooms, switchboards, archive rooms and storages? This liquid gas is used in business centers, metro, banks, railway stations, museums and libraries, airports, even in stores, not to mention such important infrastructure facilities as data centers, hydroelectric power stations or nuclear power plants.

What's new in dry water fire extinguishing systems


The arsenal of systems for Novec 1230 has replenished with interesting new solutions to match a variety of applications. If normally a gas fire extinguishing system is understood as floor modules from 50 to 180 liters filled with fire extinguishing gas and propellant, connected by a common collector and pipe wiring to nozzles, then for Novec 1230 there are also compact plug & play systems, for example, to protect electrical cabinets with refractory handset, systems for protecting server racks the size of only 1 unit and a mobile cabinet system for protecting small server racks. All these solutions already include all the necessary subsystems, including detection, start-up, monitoring, etc. and can help quickly, without fancy and welding in the room to solve the fire safety problem, and without the need to choose between efficiency and safety of people.

Fast Lifehack for Application Growth - ASO in Other Languages



“Need more gold users ...” A familiar problem? We will tell you how to solve it due to ASO-optimization in other languages.

Many games and applications to expand the audience go to foreign markets. And this is a great growth hack , but you need to translate not only the game itself, but also its page - that is, do ASO localization. Otherwise, imagine: your game is localized in 10 languages, but, having stumbled upon it in the store, the German will see a description and screenshots in English. Of course, he will think that there is no German version.

Localization of metadata is needed so that the user can easily find the application in their language. Here, for example, is how RetouchMe's positions have soared.(applications for professional retouching) after localization of metadata in 10 languages. Following this success, RetouchMe was localized in 35 languages .



What is included in ASO optimization?


Speaking about ASO, the first thing they remember about keywords. But keywords are not the only thing that increases the output of your application and affects the installation.

Let's see which page elements are included in ASO optimization:

  • Application Name
  • Subtitle
  • Application description
  • Keywords
  • Icon
  • Screenshots
  • Video

Let's go through each and see if everything needs to be localized.

Localization of text on the page


Title and subtitle. Usually there is one English name that is saved in all languages. Sometimes the name can be transliterated: for example, Bon Voyage - Bon Voyage, Tinder - 틴더 (sounds like "Tindeo" in Korean). Ideally, when the name appears universal words that are known throughout the world - for example, War Robots. Pixonic said that the game was originally called Walking War Robots, but in the end it was reduced to a more comprehensive and understandable War Robots.

See how the name of the game Blocky Cars changes depending on the locale. For China and Turkey, the name was translated, but in parentheses they still left the original - Blocky Cars.



You can see in this picture that different keywords are used for each locale. For Italy, the name used the keyword "online games", for the UK - "online shooting", for Turkey - the game about tanks, and for China and France, the title contains English keywords at all. All these words are the result of analytical work: it was necessary to find out what keywords would be in the top for each locale and use them in the title.

The subtitle is only in the App Store, and it can be said to compensate for the short name (in the App Store it is only 30 characters, while in Google Play - 50). You can add keywords to the subheading that did not fit in the title.

Description . Google Play has a short description- the first 80 characters of the description, those same 1-2 lines that you can click on and read the full description. We advise you to regularly conduct A / B testing of the short description.

Some developers only translate these few lines to attract new users. But if your description contains important information about game modes, conditions of use, etc., you must translate the entire description.

Remember that the App Store and Google Play index different metadata: the App Store indexes the name, subtitle, keywords, and Google Play indexes all the text on the application page, including the name of the developer. Here is a handy tablet from ASOdesk, where you can clearly see which side is indexing:

From the article “6 principles for working with metadata”

Thus, for Google Play, you will need to carefully push the keywords in the description so that they fit seamlessly into the text. It is believed that the first 160 characters have the greatest weight for indexing, so you should use the most relevant keywords at the beginning of the description.

Keywords. There is an excellent article from ASOdesk on how to select keywords for a title and description.

We add that although ASO is often called "SEO for mobile applications", you can not judge the popularity of a keyword by its frequency in web searches (Google, Wordstat) - in app stores their keyword tops!

Keep in mind that sometimes it’s better to take two keywords with medium popularity instead of one with high score, since there is almost no chance to break through with keywords such as “game”, “taxi”, etc. There are different tools for checking the popularity of keywords and chances to rise in the search results with specific keywords, such as Mobile Action, Apple Search Ads, etc.

How to choose keywords in languages ​​that you don’t speak? There are a number of tools for this task in ASOdesk. Keyword Manager provides keyword hints relevant to your application. They are accompanied by an automatic translation into English, which simplifies the selection of keys in other languages.

Keyword Manager also has the Missing Ranked Keywords tool, which shows queries that are not yet tracked, but by which the application is ranked in a specific country. An automatic translation into English is also available for this tool. You can test these tools for ASO in other languages ​​with free access to ASOdesk .

Important: if ASO works great in the USA, and you decide to try the same set of keywords in the UK, Canada and Australia, this will not work. The fact is that, although the language in these countries is common, there are differences depending on the country. For example, the word "vacation" in American English is vacation, and in British is holiday.

Accordingly, the search terms will be different. Same story with Portuguese in Brazil vs. Portuguese in Portugal, French in France and Canada, as well as with Spanish-speaking countries: what works in Spain may not work in Mexico, Argentina, etc.

Some countries speak several languages, so you can add 2-3 language for this country and increase audience reach. For example, in the USA it is English (US) and Spanish (Mexican version), in Switzerland - German, Italian, French and English (UK). The full table of additional languages ​​in the App Store can be found here .

Localization of graphics on the page


In addition to texts, graphic images, such as an icon and screenshots, also belong to ASO optimization.

The icon most often does not change in any way for different locales, but sometimes you can add bright elements to the icon to tell users that new content has appeared in the application. If such content in the game is dedicated to holidays in a particular locale, then, of course, you will change the appearance of the icon only for that locale.

Pixonic came up with interesting icons. For most countries, background elements change slightly, but for Turkish users, the flag of Turkey was placed on the icon.



ScreenshotsMany optimize screenshots for specific locales. This is less relevant for games, but for applications it is often justified, especially if people are shown in the screenshots. Most often there is one version of screenshots for Europe and America, and the second for Asia (namely, for Japan, Korea and China; other European countries are shown “European” screenshots). Depending on the type of application, they can draw screenshots specifically for the Brazilian audience and the audience from the Arab countries.

In PicsArt, individual screenshot options are presented for almost every country for which there is an application localization! Here are some of them:



Now many games have a video on the page , it also increases the conversion to installation. If it has callouts, they must also be translated.

Important!ASO optimization for Google Play and App Store will be different. Sometimes it is very different. Not only do the store have different requirements for screenshots and text volume, but also keywords should be selected separately. That is why applications in the App Store and Google Play often do not have the same name.

That is, we remember: you can’t take the same text, keywords and screenshots and use this set on Google Play and the App Store. If so, then the effectiveness of ASO in one of the parties - and maybe in both - will be low.

The Impact of ASO Localization on Real-World Examples


Let's take a look at the impact of ASO on a real Full Studio studio case . They translate two popular games with us, free-to-play action games Blocky Cars and Mad GunZ . These games are available in 12 languages, and their total downloads reach 50 million. They appeared in Google Play featured, and ASO optimization played a significant role in this.

In the screenshots below you can see the conversion rates for the game Blocky Cars in Germany:



On the left are the conversion rates when the game’s page was not localized to German (i.e. the description and keywords were in English). The red rectangle indicates that the conversion is much lower than average, that is, the game received far fewer users than it could.

On the right is how the conversion changed when the page was translated and keywords were selected in German. The rectangle in the screenshot has turned green because there are more visitors on the page and the conversion has increased by 25%.

Localization of a page is a quick and inexpensive event : in the screenshots the phrases are short, and the description, if it is long, can be partially translated. This is especially true for children's games, where the audience does not pay attention to the text, but to the screenshots.

The translation of descriptions, texts in screenshots and callouts of videos is conveniently given to translators-carriers in the Nitro professional translation service . The trick is that you do not have to run to 10-20 translators with these mini-texts: just insert the text, mark the languages ​​you need and get all the translations within a day.

When can I expect results from ASO optimization? You need to understand that ASO accelerates for a long time. To see how the conversion grows, you need to observe and constantly work on keywords and screenshots, and then you will notice the first significant results in 2-3 months.

At the same time, you cannot do ASO once and forget about it : you need to keep abreast and check what new trends appear in games in specific locales and in the world, and if new successful keywords have appeared. Full HP said that most often ASO had to be rewritten in Japan, in Korea, but in the USA they rarely change anything, since there ASO shows stable results.

How to understand that something is worth changing?Carry out A / B testing with Google: this helps to understand in which direction you need to move in the description, which icon has led more users to the page, etc. Use marketing tools that allow you to see where the game is for one or another key request. Also, with their help, you can check what chance the game will have on the desired key.

Hackathon DataMonetize: An Easy Way to Make Friends Retail and BigData

Hello, Habr! X5 Retail Group together with Russian Hackers holds an online data science hackathon in retail from June 18 to 21. The goal is to find new ideas on the use of big data in retail, which will improve the quality of customer service, optimize processes on the network, and develop and expand the business.

The X5 Retail Group has thousands of stores and tens of thousands of product lines, links in supply chains, millions of customers and innumerable transactions. All this is Big Data, and we really, really hope that the hackathon will help find new ways to use data in retail. Everything is as always - we need new and cool ideas. Under the cut - an interview with Mikhail Neverov, Head of Product in the Big Data Monetization Department at X5 Retail Group. He talks about how they work with big data in retail. Well, then - a description of the hackathon with a link to register.



A little about big data in retail with Mikhail Neverov


Let's start right away with the facts. Quite a lot of everything about the work of the company was published on our blog, but we are probably talking about the monetization department for the first time. So, the X5 has 3 retail chains and a corporate center. Within the center, there is a big data directorate, and the monetization department is already part of the directorate.

He creates products oriented to the foreign market on the basis of data accumulated by X5. Since this is a sensitive moment, I’ll explain that it is not primary data that are delivered to the foreign market, but the results of analysis and processing of these data, depersonalized as much as possible.

In fact, the monetization department is doing something like Similar Web and Yandex.Wordstat for retail. Now 4 product teams work right away in data monetization:

  • Platform for bidding.
  • Analytical portal of the supplier.
  • Targeting Automation.
  • Plus a new direction - APP-Logistics.

Retail Big Data Specifics


A huge amount of information. The amount of data generated by retail, in particular, X5 networks, is huge. This is several terabytes per day, and all this huge array of information is not structured, the data comes from several sources, which are often not interconnected.

The influence of external factors.Another very specific point for retail is that the results of the analysis of primary data can be used to form hypotheses with an eye to external factors. And there are a huge number of them. For example, a specialist considered that it takes half an hour to unload the goods. In fact, more time may be needed ... much more. So, when unloading a car with fish, sometimes trouble occurs - the pallet falls, the contents of the boxes are scattered. In this case, it takes not half an hour, but three - first to eliminate the problem, then to continue unloading. And the fish example is one of hundreds of real situations.

Novelty.By the way, big data began to be actively used in retail relatively recently. This can be judged even by the fact that our department appeared a couple of years ago, maybe a little more. And X5 Retail Group is a company that actively and universally uses technology.

Manufacturability.Now Big Data is used almost everywhere. In this area, a huge number of tools. Thanks to them, the seller is trying to more efficiently serve the buyer and offer him what he wants, and not just guess his needs, arranging a “carpet bombardment” of offers. One such solution is personalizing the offer. At the same time, we are talking here not only about the notorious SMS or VKontakte advertising, but about the complete analytics cycle, which allows not only to find who needs what to say or show, but also learn from their mistakes, evaluate the return of each ruble spent, understand why another buyer makes a purchase and analyze more global trends in consumer consumption.

In addition, now most retailers are moving towards optimization and automation of internal processes: dynamic pricing, assortment management, demand planning and forecasting, and more. Moreover, the shtetl reports that were previously in use are no longer in high esteem. Business requires a ready-made technological solution, ready to tell from the spot how to do it and give clear answers to the questions: “What will be the effect?” and "What if?" Also, over the past couple of years, capture vision (for example, computer vision for detecting voids on shelves and detecting an emerging queue) and speech recognition have been especially useful for retail, which allows real-time reception.

Examples? And please


One of the significant cases of the work of Scientists' date in retail is the analysis by our specialists of the dynamics of sales volumes of the most popular products among fans of the 2018 World Cup. There were no surprises , but the statistics were still interesting.

So, the "bestseller number 1" was water. In cities that took mundial, water sales rose by about 46%. Sochi turned out to be the leader, where turnover increased by 87%. On match days, the maximum figure was recorded in Saransk - here the sales volume grew by 160% compared to ordinary days.

In addition to water, fans bought beer. From June 14 to July 15 in those cities where the matches were held, beer turnover grew by an average of 31.8%. Sochi also became a leader - here they bought beer 64% more actively. But in St. Petersburg, growth was small - only 5.6%. On match days in Saransk, beer sales increased by 128%.

What for? The data that we receive on peak days of food consumption in the future allows us to more accurately forecast demand taking into account eventual factors.

Measure seven times, cut one -


Despite the fact that technology is being increasingly used in retail, one must take into account the fact that the industry itself is simply huge. If you ever had a question why digitalization is not going as fast as we would like, then the answer is simple - not all at once. Technological innovation should not and cannot run ahead of a steam locomotive.

For example, the category manager was given the opportunity to digitally sign everything. But this is only one direction. For digitalization to be beneficial to the entire industry, the same digital signature technology must be implemented everywhere. If one manager works with a number, and the second, from a neighboring department, works with paper and a pen, then the technology will be harmful rather than useful.

In general, the introduction of new technologies in a large company should occur gradually. The motto of retail in this regard is "not revolution, but evolution."

Project Evaluation Levels and Moments

This is the first (1) article in a series devoted to the development and use of a balanced scorecard (BSC) for monitoring the status of a project and optimizing project management in order to obtain planned results.


Different points of the project evaluation


Next, we will discuss the key points at which the project is evaluated, the levels of indicators used to evaluate it, the main stakeholders in the course of these evaluations, and the use of various evaluations for project management.


By assessment, we understand not only the simplest and most common form of assessment - financial and quantitative, but also often, no less important, not financial assessment, which allows you to assess the health of the project from various angles and identify risks and potential problems in advance.


The following assessments are used during the project life cycle and its:


  1. Assessment of expectations from the project to the start of the project;
  2. Assessment of the implementation of processes during the project;
  3. Evaluation of the implementation of plans during the project;
  4. Evaluation of the implementation of plans at the time of closure of the project;
  5. Assessment of the fulfillment of expectations from the project after the completion of the project;

Further, in detail, we will consider what and by what indicators are evaluated at each of the listed moments, who makes the assessment and what benefit can be obtained for project management.

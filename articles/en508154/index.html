As a result, after combining various lists from different sources, it was possible to collect a short list of 22 books. How to understand which of them are the most useful?



Option 1 : View book review ratings on book sites. They can be found, for example, on Litres.ru, Livelib.ru, Ozon.ru. There is also a Bookmate.com site where users mark which books they have read and make recommendations. We are interested in the number of these recommendations.

Rating of books based on user ratings of specialized sites







In general, there is some correlation in the estimates, and conclusions can be drawn from individual books. But it is worth remembering that the basis of these assessments is the opinion of only dozens of people, and sometimes units, so that the assessment remains subjective.



We continue to search for more objective metrics.



Option 2 : look at the popularity of books by the number of requests in Yandex and the number of pages on which these books are mentioned.



From WordStat we take data on direct searches of these books for the last couple of months. We look in the Yandex search for the number of pages on which the book is mentioned. Then we narrow down the search by counting the number of pages with references to books on the website of a specialized university, for example, the Higher School of Economics. Again we bring it into a single card.



Book ranking based on number of references







As you can see, the books are lined up in a new order. At the same time, there is a certain correlation between the mention of books on the university‚Äôs website and the number of pages in Yandex search. In principle, the number of references to books on the HSE website is a good parameter to determine their usefulness and relevance.



Option 3 : create a composite rating based on several metrics of both types. The choice of these metrics is our subjective decision, here you can criticize and propose your own set.



So, we chose five metrics for ourselves, sorted books by each of them. Then the top 10 in each chart were assigned 1 point. If the metric value is the same for the 10th and 11th books, we give points to both. Summarize the points and sort them in ascending order.



The metrics we stopped at: Livelib rating as the most complete, references on the HSE website, the number of requests in April, the number of pages with a book in Yandex and ... Here we decided to use another interesting parameter - the number of users who read the book on Bookmate, so as he seemed more visual than the number of positive ratings.

Eight books out of 22 were able to score more than two points out of five - we give them a "credit" and recommend them as the most popular and useful.



Briefly tell about each of them.



1. Jeff Sutherland. Scrum. revolutionary project management method







The book of the founder of Scrum has been very popular since 1995. She has survived many editions and is available in several translations. 280 pages have enough water, but there is also a clear description of the methodology invented by the author. It will be more correct to start your acquaintance with Scrum from it.

‚ÄúAt a certain time, in a certain place, with a certain small group of people, everything becomes possible.‚Äù


The book will explain how to properly manage projects faster and more efficiently, while spending less resources.



2. Kanban and "just in time" on Toyota. Management starts at the workplace.







This is a translation of training materials from Toyota specialists for workshops on the production system from the distant 1970s. As Sutherland by Scrum is a kind of "gospel", so this book has become a reference point for the Kanban approach.

‚ÄúMany people know about kanban only that it is some kind of card attached to the container to deliver parts to the assembly. In fact, it is something much more. This is a synthesis of management and philosophy, allowing us to achieve long-term success. "


In fact, there are two management methods here at once - ‚Äúkanban‚Äù and ‚Äúright on time‚Äù, which make it possible to properly build production and synchronize it with production stocks. The book will be useful as a launching pad for understanding modern Agile approaches.



3. Mike Cohn. Agile: Project Evaluation and Planning







Mike Cohn, an Agile expert, did not go into long discussions in this book, but heaped out a large mountain of facts, examples, graphs, and tips to help the reader understand Agile.

‚ÄúThe approach of many project managers can be represented as‚Äú planning, planning, planning - execution ‚Äù. The Agile approach is "planning - execution - adaptation", "planning - implementation - adaptation". The higher the project‚Äôs uncertainties, the more important the agile approach is for success. ‚Äù


The emphasis in the book is on two components of the success of any project - planning and evaluation. The book is rather big - more than 500 pages, but the time spent is worth the knowledge gained. Incidentally, this book is most often mentioned on the HSE website.



4. Jennifer Green, Andrew Stillman. Comprehending Agile







This voluminous work (450 pages) includes a description of all the main agile methodologies: Scrum, Kanban, Lean and XP (eXtremal Programming). The book is easy to read, methodologies are given somewhat superficially, in a review mode.

‚ÄúWhat people create often depends on what they focus on. The more people focus on their personal goals, rather than on the goals of the team, the less likely they will be of real value to the company. ‚Äù


For acquaintance - that‚Äôs it. The constant repetition of the same thing is intended to bring to the readers' memory the most important points.



5. Henrik Kniberg. Scrum and XP: Advanced Notes







This book is not easy to find in a Russian translation, but it is one of Scrum‚Äôs best practices in software development. Practical advice, illustrative examples - that's all we love.

‚ÄúIt turned out that it was enough just to clearly define the problem, and it is often solved by itself.‚Äù


The book is small, on the network you can find a translation made by enthusiasts from Agile Ukraine.



6. Boris Wolfson. Flexible project and product management







The main difference between this book and others is the emphasis on creating products, a look at Agile through the eyes of a product manager throughout the life of a product. Here you will find business modeling, requirements analytics, team management methods, and even risk management.

"Parkinson's Law: any work increases in volume to fill all the time allotted for it."


The book talks about three techniques: Scrum, Kanban and XP and their proper use. There is almost no water, and in general the book is more like a student's abstract.



7. Mike Cohn. Scrum: agile software development







This is a practical guide to mastering the Scrum methodology with practical examples. It is devoted to issues of flexible software development, but it will be useful to readers from other subject areas, because the principles of project management are the same.

‚ÄúScrum teams have to break the habit of thinking in the categories of‚Äú my ‚Äùtasks and‚Äú your ‚Äùtasks and get used to thinking in the categories of‚Äú our ‚Äùtasks.‚Äù


Unfortunately, the translation of the book in the version of the Williams publishing house is not very good - it is better to read the book in the original or look at other versions of the translations.



8. David Anderson. Kanban Alternative way to Agile







Closing our top is one of the key books on Kanban. True, it is not suitable for a first acquaintance: everything is chewed too deeply, there are many subtleties that will be of interest to the reader already immersed in the topic. But those who master it (and re-read it a couple of times) will be able to "join" in the kanban.

‚ÄúWhen you ask people to change, this creates fear and lowers their self-esteem, because you make it clear that their skills are no longer needed.‚Äù


It is worth noting that here Kanban is considered in the context of software development, which makes the book useful specifically for IT product specialists, developers and project managers.

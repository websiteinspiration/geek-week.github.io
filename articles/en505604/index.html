The discussion of this terminally ill issue is not my task, but no one, no one will prevent me from mentioning the intellectually honest, courageous, influential and respected person - the psychiatrist and neuropsychopharmacologist David Nutt. Until 2009, Professor Nutt chaired the UK Anti-Narcotics Board. In 2009, he published an article, which compared the risks of using ecstasy with the risks of equestrian sports; Based on statistics, Nutt showed that equestrian sport is more dangerous to health than ecstasy. Professor Nutt is one of those who urged to study the facts and approach alcohol, nicotine, other psychoactive substances rationally, without hysteria and phobia. In 2010, David Nutt founded the Independent Scientific Committee on Drugs. In the same year, The Lancet Nutt et al. Published an expert scale for the harm of psychoactive substances.. Natta's team evaluated the dangers of alcohol, tobacco, and drugs by 16 parameters. It turned out that alcohol takes first place in terms of total harm. In second place is heroin. Tobacco entered the top five. Recreational drug-friendly people often refer to the Natta scale to explain why they choose drugs rather than alcohol. Indeed, having only two options - alcohol or drugs - I would also choose something that is less harmful; it’s good that I have the option “neither one nor the other”.

David Nutt and colleagues comparedependence potential of the 20 most popular substances on a 3-point scale; heroin scored 3.0 points on this scale, benzodiazepines - 1.83, tobacco - 2.21, alcohol - 1.93. Well, these guys displayed in numbers what we always knew: psychoactive substances can be addictive if consumed. We also knew that heroin is stronger in addiction than alcohol. 3. Genes


Previously, scientific disputes were fought around two suspects: nature and education. People become dependent, for such is their nature, they said alone. People become dependent under the influence of factors of the social environment, others insisted. Nature or environment? Now, in the 21st century, scientists are still focusing on the same two suspects, both of whom are caught by the hand, so that either has changed to and : both nature and the environment are preparing the ground on which addiction grows.

One study of twins showed that the percentage of genes in the pathogenesis of dependence is 40 to 60%. Another studycalls the interval 40-70%. Almost all the studies that came to my attention indicate that the dependence is about half due to genes, and this degree of heritability is revealed in the formation of dependence on the main classes of addictive substances - stimulants, opiates, alcohol, nicotine, cannabinoids. Which genes?

The formation of severe nicotine addiction involved localized on the 15th chromosome gene cluster of nicotinic receptor subunits α5, α3 and β4 ( CHRNA5, CHRNA3, CHRNB4 ); the same cluster is involvedin the formation of alcohol, opioid and cocaine addiction. (Moreover, the words “involved”, “implicated” do not necessarily mean that the gene increases the craving for nicotine. Thus, the carriers of the CHRNA5 gene have a reduced aversion reaction during the first tests of a psychoactive substance - this is what increases the risk of addiction in such people.) Localized by 8 The chromosome gene cluster of α6 and β3 nicotinic receptor subunits ( CHRNA6, CHRNB3 ) is also guilty of nicotinism, although to a lesser extent. Finally, polymorphism in the CYP2a6 gene , which encodes an enzyme that converts nicotine to cotinine, also contributes toincrease the number of cigarettes smoked and addiction. These data were obtained as a result of a genome-wide association search (GWAS) in 80,000 people. I love modern genetics for its scale and thoroughness.

Let's move on to alcohol (calmly, this is not a call for a drink). Alcoholism is the first of the addictions in the pathogenesis of which a genetic contribution has been detected. Back in 1972, Peter Wolff noticed that many Asians have a strong reddening of the skin after drinking. Why is this happening? The thing is the metabolism of ethyl alcohol: it is oxidized mainly in the liver with the help of alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) and aldehyde dehydrogenase (ALDH). Both enzymes occur in several forms, which are encoded by different genes; in addition, there are variants (alleles) of some of these genes that encode enzymes with different biochemical characteristics. Which ADH or ALDH alleles a person carries affectson his level of alcohol consumption and the risk of alcoholism. For example, some ADH1B and ADH1C alleles encode especially active ADH enzymes, resulting in faster conversion of ethanol to acetaldehyde. These alleles reduce the risk of alcoholism. Judge for yourself: before you had time to enjoy the treasured ethanol euphoria, you began to be wriggled up from the sufferings associated with toxic acetaldehyde. ALDH2 gene variant encodes an inactive ALDH enzyme, which leads to the accumulation of acetaldehyde; people with this gene are nine times less likely to become alcoholics than carriers of other variants of the gene encoding ALDH. Thus, carriers of the ADH1B, ADH1C, and ALDH2 alleles are insured against alcoholism from birth.

But it's not just about liver enzymes. When it comes to addiction, all roads lead to the brain. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter of pleasure, but not actually a pleasant sensation, but the expectation of it. Promising us pleasure, dopamine is involved in the processes of reward and learning; learning underlies the formation of habits and addiction. Among the genetic factors leading to the breakdown of dopamine transmission, the Taq1A single nucleotide sequence in the DRD2 gene encoding the dopamine D2 receptor (though it later turned out that Taq1A is located not in DRD2 , but in the neighboring ANKK1 gene , is the most famous , but this, nevertheless, somehow affects DRD2 ). Gene polymorphismDRD3 and DRD4 , coding for the D3 and D4 receptors, respectively, are also associated with alcohol problems. In addition to alleles that affect dopamine transmission, in one large-scale study covering 275,000 people, 18 genetic variants were identified that were significantly associated with alcohol abuse and alcoholism: VRK2, DCLK2, ISL1, FTO, IGF2BP1 , etc.

Heroin is a drug with a fairly high addictive potential. But the likelihood of getting hooked on “gloomy” is much higher in individuals with the OPRM1 and OPRD1 genes .

What about marijuana? Have recently been publishedGWAS results: Danes compared genetic information taken from 2,000 people with cannabis addiction and 50,000 people without addiction; hemp dependence turned out to be associated with a mutation in the CHRNA2 gene . A larger Icelandic gene study of 5,500 individuals with cannabism (did you read that word correctly?) And 300,000 people without it confirmed the involvement of CHRNA2 in the occurrence of cannabis addiction.

4. Transcription factors and epigenetics


Our beer heroine has no money problems, you guessed it. Before getting to me, she spent a tidy sum on sequencing her precious DNA. In the future, in the era of personalized medicine, DNA analysis will enter the standard of examination for any patient. But now, in our time, doctors, in general, do not know what to do with this information. So: the patient identified six gene variants associated with alcohol dependence. It would seem that she was simply unlucky in genetic sportloto. But everything is not so simple: I found out that she has a homozygous twin sister who runs a school of figure skating, does not drink, does not smoke, does not use drugs (although everything happened in her youth) and seems to live a happy, meaningful life. It turns out that you can be a carrier of “addiction genes” and not be an alcoholic.Why is that?

Transcription is the transfer of genetic information from DNA to RNA using the RNA polymerase enzyme. Based on this information, proteins are synthesized in ribosomes (translation). Epigenetics is a science that studies such inheritance mechanisms that are associated not with genetic information, but with whether genes are active or not (on or off). If some genes are active, then they can remain as such in the next generations of cells, and even in subsequent generations of organisms. How does all this relate to addiction?

That's how. Just an hour after a single dose of cocaine, about 100 genes are activated in laboratory mice. After some time, these genes again “fade”. Some genes cease to be activated in response to repeated drug injections. But here's what is interesting: with repeated injections of psychoactive substances, the vast majority of genes become excessively active and remain so even after the introduction of surfactants. How does cocaine (or ethanol or nicotine - put your substance) do it?

All due to over-expression of transcription factors. CREB is one of them. It activates many genes, that is, makes them available for transcription. CREB rises for several hours or days, then returns to its original level. Cocaine, morphine, ethanol, tetrahydrocannabinol also increase the expression of ΔFosB. This transcription factor, unlike CREB, is already capable of long-term activation of a large number of genes. Moreover, increased expression of ΔFosB in the D1 type of middle spiny neurons of the nucleus accumbens is currently considered a necessary and sufficient condition for the neuroadaptive and behavioral changes observed during addiction. It is assumed that CREB is involved in the formation of addiction through negative reinforcement, and ΔFosB through the mechanisms of positive reinforcement.

NFκB is another transcription factor. Its expression leads to the formation of new dendritic spines. Well, that is, we are already talking about changes in the structure of the nerve cell: the cell grows spines on dendrites (and in the case of opiates, on the contrary, it loses them), which ultimately leads to remodeling of neural circuits. Moreover, not only with the introduction of psychoactive substances - NFκB is able to change the morphology of neurons also under stress and depression. Finally, MEF2 is a factor whose activity is suppressed with the introduction of cocaine, which also contributes to the formation of new dendritic spines.

Neurobiologist Eric Nestler emphasizes that there may be much more transcription factors affecting genes when using psychoactive substances than is now known, but from the aboveΔFosB alone is enough to lay the foundation for neuroplastic changes leading to addiction. ΔFosB seems to be such a molecular switch that triggers long-term cellular changes. Here it is, the key to Pandora’s box.

5. Endophenotype


Genes, epigenetics ... It's all complicated. “It's a man,” one of the readers will say. “Look, take this hypothetical heroine of yours: she is clearly some kind of unstable and evil.” Everything is decided by character. ”

And I partially agree with this. It is still a matter of epigenetic changes, but not only those that occur in the neurons of the reward system. Any of our features, any phenotypic trait is the result of the interaction of genes and the environment. And personality traits resulting from epigenetic changes, the characteristics of our nervous system, can make us more (or less) vulnerable to addiction. What are the features and features?

Behavioral patterns can vary greatly from culture to culture, from situation to situation, and also depend on gender, age, hormonal background and metabolism, related mental and physical illnesses, salary, and a croissant eaten for breakfast. Humans are complex, well-trained, and behaviorally plastic hominids. But with all the mental and behavioral diversity, human characters are more or less stable when psychometrists evaluate them using these five scales: extraversion, goodwill, good faith, neuroticism (the tendency to fall into negative experiences) and openness to experience. The Big Five model has gained recognition in the global scientific community, which is not so common when it comes to unsteady psychological abstractions.

In one studyStudying 3785 twins, scientists came to the conclusion that the associations between the Big Five traits and addiction are generally weak, but nevertheless statistically significant in relation to traits such as neurotism, goodwill and good faith. In another study , a correlation was found between the severity of the features of the Big Five and the psychoactive substance used. So, among smokers of marijuana, neurotism reached average values, among consumers of cocaine and heroin - to very high.

But perhaps it is worth looking for stronger associations not with personality traits, but with elementary features of the nervous system's response to certain signals? The totality of these features is called the endophenotype. In psychiatric genetics, the endophenotypes of schizophrenia and bipolar disorder have been little studied: for example, verbal and visual working memory is reduced in patients with schizophrenia and their families, which is associated with some alleles of the RELN gene. What if endophenotypes increase addictive vulnerability?

Exist. A group of researchers took up the studyendophenotypic traits such as positive emotionality (PEM), negative emotionality (NEM) and restraining control (CON). PEM characterizes sensitivity to reward; people with pronounced PEM are disposed to communication, generally more active, optimistic, excited, positive. PEM is modulated by the dopaminergic system, originating in the midbrain and associated with the striatum, prefrontal cortex, hypothalamus, and amygdala. NEM is a fundamental aspect of sensitivity to punishment. Sensitivity to stress, a tendency to experience anxiety, anger, depression - these are about people with severe NEM. NEM is associated with glutamatergic pathways between the ventromedial prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate gyrus, tonsil and islet. And finally, CON is the ability to restrain impulsive actions. Neural pathsproviding CON, include the right lower frontal gyrus, the pre-complementary motor zone, and their projections onto the striatum and subthalamic nucleus. Thus, certain neural circuits modulate certain endophenotypic traits. Studying how the three endophenotypic traits are related to dependence, the authors concluded that individuals with high PEM, high CON, and low NEM are less addicted, and individuals with low PEM, low CON, and high NEM are more addicted.high CON and low NEM are less susceptible to addiction, and individuals with low PEM, low CON and high NEM are less susceptible to addiction.high CON and low NEM are less susceptible to addiction, and individuals with low PEM, low CON and high NEM are less susceptible to addiction.

6. Environmental factors and stress


The moment has come when I can use the word environmental . Do you have favorite words? I like the words of the lemniscate , meritocracy , diegesis . And the environmental .

The genome is the totality of all genes. Envair (from the English environment - environment, environment) Is a combination of all environmental factors. The variety of interactions between the genome and the environment leads to the appearance of certain signs, characteristics, weak and strong personality traits. Alcohol, heroin, cocaine, nicotine, tetrahydrocannabinol are addictive - there is no doubt about it. But people are vulnerable to addiction to varying degrees. We went through genes and epigenetics, we touched on endophenotypes. Now let's dwell on the most typical environmental factors that significantly increase addictive risk.

More and more clinical, preclinical, and population-based studies are demonstrating a link between stressful circumstances and addiction. Such circumstances include: parent's death or death, parental divorce, family conflict, isolation and abandonment, betrayal by a significant loved one, emotional, physical or sexual violence (as well as observation of violent acts against another), war, natural disasters, bad economic conditions. The common thing between these options is the stress they cause. Can stress be said to increase the risk of addiction? Can.

Stress - a set of nonspecific reactions of the body in response to the effects of adverse factors (stressors). Moreover, in certain neurons of the hypothalamus, a corticotropin releasing factor (CRF) is produced, which enters the pituitary gland and stimulates the production of adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) there. Further, ACTH enters the bloodstream, reaches the adrenal glands and causes the secretion of cortisol, which, acting on receptors in various cells, prepares the body to survive “bad times”. A little stress is not harmful (and even useful), but too intense or too long stress can cause serious harm to the body. Researchaimed at revealing a clear link between stress and dependence, quite a lot has been done in recent years. With an emphasis on certain aspects, these studies generally show that stress is an undeniable risk factor for addiction, but at the same time they do not answer the question of where the critical “place” between stress and dependence is located. And only relatively recently it was shown that under stress triggered by the Wnt / β-catenin signaling pathway, morphological changes in neurons that increase the vulnerability of the nervous system to dependence are triggered. The bitter irony is that psychoactive substances do not reduce the level of stress, but in the long run they even increase it, while addicts themselves are made less and less stress-resistant and resilient.

I want to mention one factor closely related to stress: the nature of attachment with a significant adult. A targeted study of 56721 individuals revealed a significant correlation between the level of safety of interpersonal attachment and the use of psychoactive substances. Sadly, when you study hundreds of drug history and invariably find evidence of unreliable attachment among addicts with their parents, you involuntarily come to the conclusion that addiction is a disorder that occurs when a reliable attachment does not happen. It seems that we cannot live without attachment at all, and if it is not there, then even alcohol, heroin, which destroys us, is suitable, or whatever everyone gets in his way.

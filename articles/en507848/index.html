A couple of years ago we talked about how the search for a Russian translation of English articles is organized in the Anti-Plagiarism system . Naturally, one cannot do without a machine translator in an algorithm. The machine translator, of course, is based on machine learning, which, in turn, requires a very significant number of "parallel sentences", i.e. identical sentences written in two languages. A significant number are millions of offers, and the more the better. It is clear that for the Russian-English couple to find such a base (including in the public domain) is real. And what to do with those language pairs for which there cannot be too many parallel sentences in principle?







It would seem that without a large volume of training examples, it is impossible to teach a machine translation system. But the ideology of Unsupervised Learning , or "learning without a teacher," comes to the rescue .

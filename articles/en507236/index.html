We increase the effectiveness of interaction between designers and frontend developers

When the design was added to the list of key services of our outsourcing production, we decided that we did not want to work according to generally accepted standards. We began to look for a special approach to design: as efficiently as possible, as quickly as possible, as economically as possible. And we found him - just swapping designers and layout designers.

Usually, the combination of the roles of the designer and the front-end is bad. Most often it turns out like this:

Alas, not a single project as a result looks like it was originally intended. We all know that it is almost impossible to do as the designer wanted - it is an art bordering on genius. I want to share our experience of how we mastered this art.

No matter how surprising it may sound, the problem can be solved if the front-end is ‚Äúturned‚Äù into a designer, and the designer into a front-end.

A new approach to organizing the work of designers and layout designers, based on the use of Figma and Adobe XD with the API for working with layouts, quickly allowed us to:

  • Improve quality through automated visual tests of the layout and frontend;
  • Increase development speed by exporting ready-made component templates;
  • Reduce production costs by saving time.

Of course, with this approach, the load on the designer increases, but it is incomparable with saving time and labor at the front-end development stage.

We were looking for an alternative - and found!


Our team used to do pretty well with the layout, but it took too much time. However, I didn‚Äôt have to choose: a variety of layouts came to us (sometimes just .png without the source - ‚Äúmake it as you can‚Äù).

In the process, we always wanted to wedge ourselves in the work of third-party designers in order to:

  • Even at the presale stage, tell designers which ideas are feasible and which are not;
  • Layouts were validated on our side before being transferred to the front-end development phase;
  • Avoid meaningless creativity, make useful and convenient digital products.

In addition, we constantly faced discrepancies between:

  • expected and actual labor costs on the project;
  • critical revisions after editing the client.

The number of orders for design and the number of designers themselves was constantly growing, and the time for change was inexorably approaching - we had to find a way to optimize this direction.

So Adobe XD has become part of our digital story. With his arrival, the process of working with the layout has changed significantly: after the approval of the design by the client, the layout sources are temporarily transferred to layout designers for technical evaluation and corrections. All changes, if necessary, are made as delicately as possible - in order to preserve the original creative idea. Pretty soon, we switched to cloud projects (Adobe XD and Figma), where you can always find the current version of the layout, available for designers and layout designers. Thanks to this approach, we are not afraid of scaling up production and are ready for any amount of work.

Which graphic editor to choose?


To answer this question would definitely be unprofessional. Both Adobe XD and Figma have approximately equal functionality, and the number of updates in both programs is measured in hundreds.

You need to choose software, taking into account the personal work experience and the tasks that clients put before specialists. For ourselves, we decided to start honing our skills and optimize our work using Adobe XD. This decision was not final: we planned to configure all processes, fix errors, and then automate and Figma - the only difference is in the syntax.

Here are the requirements set for the component approach to the designer:

  • There should be no layers outside the components;
  • Each page should consist only of components;
  • Indentation should be the same everywhere;
  • Correct nesting and proper layer names;
  • Strict adherence to variables in the style guide.





Design Refactoring


We managed to apply the refactoring principle to digital design: as in programming, this approach allows us to fix the original layout so that it becomes neat, understandable and convenient for all specialists - and, at the same time, does not differ from the approved ‚Äúpicture‚Äù.

Of course, this is a rather lengthy process that takes up enough time and effort: we organize all color schemes, fonts, indents and other parameters so that they are the same on all pages.

These time and labor costs pay off very quickly: after refactoring, layout is easy and fast, since most of the possible problems are eliminated even at the stage of cleaning the layout.

Guidelines and components assets




To work with the layout as convenient and efficient as possible, we had to create a clear prototype system where each prototype would be standardized. This, of course, helps the assets of graphic editors.

This approach solved several pressing problems at once: designers are easily guided in accessible tools, front-end vendors do not spend time on routine analysis and correction of errors, the whole team raises the level of quality of work by saving time and money.

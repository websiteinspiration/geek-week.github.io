Four weeks of selection


There are no classic entrance exams at School 21, so you cannot prepare for admission. For four weeks, applicants will be able to try to "swim the pool."

"Pool" is, on the one hand, intensive in programming, and on the other, a unique emulation of real life. Each of us has more tasks than we can do in a day; and the pool of tasks is more than you usually can catch. This teaches you to prioritize. You have deadlines every day - it teaches you to plan your time correctly. The system checks before the first error - this teaches you attentiveness, which is one of the pillars of programming. You have to check other participants, and they - you, which teaches you to communicate briefly and essentially with strangers. We have a dismissive attitude towards instructions in our blood - ‚ÄúSchool 21‚Äù shows the importance of this element in terms of saving time to achieve results.

In a month, all these skills will become a habit, and the participant will be able to see an effect that is simply not achievable within one or even two weeks.



In my opinion, the pool is a trial period not only for applicants, but also for the School: a third of the participants leave in the first week. Someone does not fit the rhythm, someone is not ready for independence, someone is unsettled by mistakes. Those who reach the end, like 300 Spartans, only alive and seasoned in battle! One thing unites them - in spite of everything, they like to program. Of these, usually half become our members. Many of those who did not pass do not quit programming and continue their journey, but not within the framework of the School.

The pool is the period of the highest load for the entire training period. It is precisely those who dropped out at this stage who have the impression that the ‚ÄúSchool‚Äù is unbearably difficult. Often someone tries to combine this with work or study - nothing comes of it. Half of the participants here begin to program for the first time, and a third begin to master the English language, in which we have all the tasks.

Those who did not even enter, but reached the end, a completely different opinion about the "pool" - as a fantastic experience of knowing and testing oneself. It is psychologically difficult for children with excellent student syndrome - there is simply no time to double-check everything and have time to do all the tasks. But so in real life, for which an ordinary school and university do not prepare us. Therefore, with us this is also part of the educational process.

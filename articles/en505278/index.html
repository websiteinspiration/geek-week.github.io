39 abbreviations in English for beginner marketers and seoshnikov

Almost all the terms used by experts in marketing and related fields are strongly tied to the English language. Therefore, already at the stage of training a profession, you need to study them.

In our experience, it is most difficult to remember abbreviations - almost twice as much energy is spent on them. After all, you must first learn the decryption, and then the translation itself. Therefore, they collected the most common acronyms in one article.

We decided to simplify the task a little for beginners in the profession. We hope that this dictionary will be useful for beginners and junior seoshnikov and marketers.

Important!We know that many abbreviations are used in their original form, in English. But the difficulty for a beginner is that they are all pretty similar. Therefore, there is a risk of remembering incorrectly. We believe that the best way to learn them is to first understand the interpretation and translation, and then delve into the meaning and nuances.

AMA - Ask Me Anything - ext. “Ask me anything”

A page on a site where users can anonymously ask a brand or influencer questions. One of the options for the Q&A page (Questions and Answers). AMA posts can also be launched on social networks using special tools and applications for anonymous polls.

B2B - Business to Business - ext. "Business to business"

Sales for business. The type of work of an enterprise when its customers and customers are other enterprises, companies or organizations.

B2C - Business to Consumer - Add. "Business for the consumer"

Marketing activity, the purpose of which is to sell a product or service to an end user. The B2C sales strategy is characterized by a shorter sales cycle, emotional decision-making about the purchase and less close relations between the seller and the buyer than in B2B.

CMS - Content Management System - Content Management System

Script-based software that allows you to manage the contents of an online resource, change it, view and control it. The most popular CMS today: WordPress, Joomla, Drupal, MODx, 1C Bitrix.

CPC - Cost Per Click - Cost Per Click

A standard advertising model in which payment is considered for each click on an advertisement and the transition to the advertiser's website or page.

CPC = (Advertising Costs): (Number of clicks)

Example. You have placed an advertising banner on a website and received 120 clicks. You paid for advertising: 800 rubles. Applying the formula, we have CPC = 6.6 rubles. for the transition.

CPL - Cost Per Lead - The cost of receiving a potential client (lead)

A standard advertising model in which payment is considered for each lead attracted. Lead is a potential customer who reacted to the ad and left his contact information. CPL is most often used by b2b companies that need to evaluate the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

CPL = (Advertising Costs): (Number of Leads Completed)

Example. You placed a banner on the site, and received 3 applications. You spent 1200 rubles on advertising. Applying the formula, we get CPL = 400 rubles. for lead.

CPM - Cost per Mile - CPM

A standard advertising model in which payment is counted for every thousand ad impressions. Using this model, you can show an ad to as many people as possible for a relatively small budget.

CPM = (Advertising Cost) x 1000: (Number of ad impressions)

Example. You have advertised on the site and it has been shown 2,000 times. You spent 1,500 rubles on advertising. Applying the formula, we get CPM = 750 rubles. per thousand impressions.

CR - Conversion Rate - Conversion Rate

A coefficient that shows how many conversions came in per 1000 visitors. It is calculated as a percentage.

CR = (Number of conversions): (Number of visitors) x 100%

Example. 1000 visitors came to the site. Of these, 45 applications for the purchase of goods were left. Applying the formula, we obtain CR = 4.5%.

CRM - Customer Relationship Management - Customer Relationship Management

This is a system that, with the help of process automation, helps to more efficiently build a dialogue with the buyer, avoid mistakes in the work and, as a result, sell him more. CRM keeps a complete record of all communications with each client.

Here you can listen to calls, see the purchase history, create documents by template, write e-mail or sms, set a task. In general, an indispensable tool for companies that want to sell systemically, and not horribly.

CRO - Conversion Rate Optimization - Conversion Optimization

A set of actions aimed at increasing the conversion of the site. That is, one of the main functions of a marketer and a SEO.

Typically, CRO includes improving the purchase interface, creating a presentation of a product or service, analyzing and improving a sales funnel, optimizing landing pages, A / B testing.

CTA - Call to Action - Call to Action

A call motivating the user to perform an action (go to, fill out a form, call, buy, find out, etc.).

A CTA element can be a button, image, shape, or even text that calls for some action.

Register for Free or Buy Now - Classic CTAs.

CTR - Click Through Rate - Click Rate

One of the most important indicators by which to evaluate the effectiveness of contextual advertising. After all, it is he who affects the price of a click in Google Adwords and Yandex Direct. It is calculated as a percentage.

The higher the CTR, the lower the CPC.

CTR = (Number of clicks on ads): (Number of ad impressions) x 100%

Example. You launched an ad that 20,000 users saw. 800 people clicked on the banner. Applying the formula, we get CTR = 4%.

CX - Customer Experience - Customer Experience (Customer Experience)

The set of impressions that the client receives when interacting with the company.

Usually the “consumer experience” begins with an introduction to the company. Next is the beginning of interaction, ordering, payment, using a product or service, brand support. At each stage, the client comes in close contact with the company and draws his unique experience from all this. All these are important elements of competent brand management.

DA - Domain Authority

One of Google’s link metrics. This value shows how the domain (subdomain) will be ranked in search engines.

This forecast is measured in points from 1 to 100 on a logarithmic scale. That is, to increase their performance in the range from 10 to 20 is much easier than from 70 to 80.

DMCA - Digital Millennium Copyright Act - Digital Millennium Copyright Act

In fact, this is the basic law governing the protection of copyright on the Internet. Here is the full text.

A DMCA claim or DMCA Takedown Notice is a complaint filed by a claimant regarding alleged copyright infringement. Based on the results of its consideration, a decision will be made to remove a specific page or site from indexing.



FOMO - Fear of Missing Out - Fear of Lost Profit (Lost Profit Syndrome)

A marketing tactic that exploits the user's fear of missing out on something useful or important.

Actually, many advertising messages and landing pages are based on it. FOMO tools include limiting offers by time or quantity, social proof, bundles of goods and after-sales, gifts to buy, discounts, limited offers or the use of opinion leaders.

GA - Google Analytics

Free service from Google to create detailed statistics on website visitors. Alpha and omega of any marketer and a number of specialists.

HITS - Hyperlink-Induced Topic Search

One of the search ranking algorithms. Along with Page Rank.

HITS first determines the most relevant pages in response to a search query. Further, these pages are combined into a so-called root set. Further, this set increases to all pages that are associated with it, and some pages that link to it. It turns out the basic set.

Ranking is done only in this narrow subgraph, and not in the entire system.



IO - Insertion Order - Advertising Order

It contains information about the planned advertising campaign: name of the customer company, project start and end dates, individual features of advertising materials (color, size or duration), CPM (price per thousand impressions), total cost of advertising promotion, possible discounts, as well as fines and sanctions in case of failure to fulfill obligations to disseminate advertising information.

In fact, when accepting an IO, the contractor signs a full-fledged contract.

LSI - Latent Semantic Indexing - Latent Semantic Analysis

This is the so-called hidden semantic indexing, that is, the method of indexing sites in which search robots take into account the general meaning of the text, rather than the saturation of it with keywords.

That is, this is an algorithm for which SEO queries “buy cheap plastic windows in Moscow” will not work .

NPS - Net Promoter Score - Consumer Loyalty Index

A complex scheme that tries to determine one thing: how much consumers love the brand.

Its basis is a question that is often asked in companies after service. “What is the likelihood that you will advise our company to your friend or colleague?” The answer is usually placed on a scale of 1 to 10.

Next, the entire mass of answers is divided into 3 groups: critics (answered from 1 to 6), neutrals (answered 7-8), brand advocates (answered 9-10). The formula for determining NPS is as follows:

NPS = ((Number of brand advocates) - (Number of critics)): (Number of respondents) x 100%

For example, a survey of 100 people showed that among them 20 people were critics, 45 were neutral, and 35 - brand advocates. Using the formula, we get NPS = 15%.

PBN - Private Blog Network - Private website network

A number of sites that an individual (or company) directly controls and actively uses them to increase the profile of the website’s backlinks.

Refers to gray SEO. In fact, Google banned their use back in 2014. But keeping track of their availability is not easy, so some companies use this promotion tool.

However, the strategy is risky, because if a PBN is detected by a search engine, sanctions may be imposed.

PPC - Pay Per Click - Pay Per Click

An advertising payment model in which an advertiser pays for clicks on an ad.

Sometimes in Russian you can find the abbreviation “PPC” - this is not a curse word, but a tracing paper with PPC.

PR - PageRank

Search Ranking Algorithm. applies to pages linked by hyperlinks, and assigns each of them a numerical value that measures its “importance” or “authority” among other documents.

QDD - Query Deserves Diversity - ext. “The request deserves a variety”

The Google ranking algorithm that the system uses for obscure or ambiguous questions. That is, if the user writes “buy a pen”, it is not clear which one he has in mind: writing, door, window, holder or something else.

The presence and functions of this algorithm are not 100% clear. But practice shows that in case of an indefinite request, items that are not too consistent with the standard algorithm are included in the issue.

QDF - Query Deserves Freshness - Add. “The request deserves freshness”

Google ranking algorithm, which takes into account not only the weight of the page, but also the time of its publication. Fresh content is ranked higher than outdated.

Features of this algorithm have not been studied either. SEO specialists can claim it only by analyzing search results, where fresh materials are often ranked higher than old ones.

ROI - Return on Investment - Return on Investment

It shows the profitability or loss-making of a particular investment. ROI must be considered for almost every investment, including for individual advertising campaigns.
ROI = (Revenue from customers who responded to advertising): (Advertising costs)

Example. The budget for the advertising campaign in June amounted to 10,000 rubles. Advertising came from customers who made purchases of 40,000 rubles. Using the formula, ROI = 4.

Sometimes the ratio is indicated as a percentage. That is, in the example above, it will be 400%.

SEM - Search Engine Marketing - Search Engine Marketing

A set of measures aimed at increasing the position of the site in search engines using paid advertising.

If simplified, then SEM = SEO + contextual advertising.

SC - Supplementary Content

This is the name of any content that improves UX (User Experience or User Experience).

It may not serve the purpose of the page or site, but should be directly related to the meaning of the pages.

The auxiliary content as a whole can be called any elements that do not serve as a conversion of the visitor to the lead. But often these are native photos and videos, inserted posts from social networks, a header, navigation menus, comment blocks, blocks of recommended materials.

SEA - Search Engine Advertising - Contextual Advertising

Yes, this abbreviation hides the advertisement that appears on the search engine results pages. In Google and Yandex, this is usually several positions at the very top and bottom of the page, marked with the “Ad” or “Advertisement” icon.

Here, for example, a block of 4 (!) Positions in response to a request to “buy a tablet”.



SEO - Search Engine Optimization - Search Engine Optimization

This is a set of measures to increase site visibility in search engines for targeted search queries.

In short, SEO includes: analysis of search engine algorithms, demand analysis, compilation of a semantic core, external and internal website optimization, improvement of behavioral factors, analysis of results, and also a car and a small cart of tasks that you won’t be able to briefly describe.

SERM - Search Engine Reputation Management - Search Engine Reputation Management

This is a set of tools aimed at creating a positive image of the organization in the eyes of potential customers. Often these are 2 main tasks: direct work on a positive image of a brand or product (service) and processing the negative that already exists on the network.

SERM is mainly carried out by working with forums, social networks, reviews, as well as posting image and news content.

SERP - Search Engine Results Page - Search Engine Page

Actually, this is the page that the search engine gives out after entering the query into the search bar. In the Russian-speaking space, the term "search results" is more common, then in English-language guides and articles it is most often found exactly SERP.

SMM - Social Media Marketing

This is the general name for promoting goods and services on social networks that are perceived by marketing as social media.

The definition is too big to describe it briefly, so let's focus on the tasks that SMM solves:

  • PR
  • Branding
  • Trade marketing
  • Social and Internet support for marketing events
  • Bulk Advertising
  • Social promotion of a product, product or brand
  • Increase in attendance of company resources
  • Cross-promotion with non-competitive companies and brands.


And much more. In fact, this is a full-fledged marketing industry that focuses on social networks.

SMO - Social Media Optimization - Resource Optimization for Social Networks

These are events that are aimed at transforming the content of the site for its simplest use in network communities.

Most often, the following steps are taken as part of an SMO:
  • Content is aligned with the expectations and preferences of regular social media visitors.
  • The site is transformed in such a way that it conforms to the technical mechanisms of social networks (the ability to leave comments, like publications, etc.).
  • Usability of the resource improves.
  • Creating channels to easily export content. So that any publication can be expanded in social networks.


ToS - Terms of Service - Terms of Use

This is an official document in the form of an offer in which the rules for using the resource are detailed. In fact, this is the main document that governs any user actions.

With it, you can transfer the relationship of buyers of goods or services to the legal plane. This will help in case of a dispute, because even a simple visit to the site automatically means that the user agrees to the Terms of Use.

UI / UX - User Interface / User Experience - User Interface / User Experience

UI and UX are areas of design that cannot be mixed together, but that cannot exist without each other. A great product starts with UX, followed by UI inextricably.

User Experience is the process of identifying user problems and solving these problems in order to increase their satisfaction. To make it convenient for the user to use the site - so that any thought or Wishlist is immediately reflected in the functionality.

User Interface is an intermediary between a device and a person. This is not only the appearance, but also the interactivity of the product. That is, so that this convenience also looks beautiful and pleases the eye.

WP - WordPress

One of the most popular CMS for creating sites.

ZMOT - Zero Moment of Truth - Add. "Zero Moment of Truth"

The moment when the user picks up his laptop, mobile phone, or other wired device and begins to study the product or service (or potential partner) that he wants to try or buy.

That is, this is the moment of detection of need. And the seller’s task is to fit into the period of time after ZMOT and the final choice of a specific product. This is one of the tactics of getting new customers. Any tools are suitable for this.

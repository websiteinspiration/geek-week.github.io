Managed Lead Volume

This article discusses the use of Earned Volume Management with a normalized budget. In this way, all the benefits of Volume Management (EVM) are retained and privacy is created.

Background


For thousands of years, philosophers of all countries have struggled with the possibility of combining space and time. In the last century, one ensign of the Soviet Army solved this problem by ordering soldiers to dig from the fence to lunch. Then PMI described the solution to this problem in the local area - project management. So there was developed volume management (EVM), which allowed us to translate all the work into financial indicators and evaluate the planned and completed work in terms of budget.

In a nutshell, volume management measures all planned and completed work in units of the planned budget. Allows you to identify deviations caused by violation of planned dates or mismatch of cost. It is used to assess the execution rates of deadlines and cost, the planned budget, speed to completion, and many more useful indicators. This is a very useful and concise method that allows you to evaluate the current state of affairs on the project, evaluate productivity and deviations, and make predictions. For its use, no additional data is needed, standard project reporting is sufficient. The used volume management method allows you to reflect the status of the project in simple terms and discuss the status with generally accepted terms.

When it may really be necessary to use a normalized budget


In my opinion, the mastered volume method should be used on all projects, regardless of the complexity of the project or the presence of objective and subjective restrictions from the list above. Only the use of the disbursed volume method will allow us to get an objective picture of the state of affairs on the project, identify the need for changes, evaluate past and current productivity and develop forecasts for the future. In this case, simple and concise indicators and formulas are used, which are easily calculated, will be understandable to any person and easily interpreted, even by people who are not related to the project.

Sometimes, indeed, it may be necessary not to show the project budget. For example, in the case of the Contractor working under a fixed-price contract. In this case, I propose to calculate all the indicators of the utilized volume through the normalized planned budget. Below I will give recommendations on how to calculate a normalized budget in various cases.

If the data for using the accumulated volume method is not enough, you can make an additional distribution of costs, and use the volume method based on this distribution.

I can only say to the skeptics of the approaches described below that we cannot control what we cannot measure and that any approach to measurement is better than a complete lack of approach.

Normalization Options


If you wish not to use separate budget indicators in calculations and reports, I propose using a normalized budget. To exclude incorrect interpretations or the possibility of conjecture, a normalized budget should differ significantly from the real budget.

To get a normalized budget, you can use the following algorithms:

The real cost of the work is converted with a multiplier


This is the simplest method of bringing the mastered volume. We multiply or divide the cost of the work by a predetermined factor (for example, 1,000 or 1,500, for the most cunning) and then use the indicators normalized in this way. Indirect costs, in this way, are allocated to work packages or operations, in proportion to direct and get the full cost.

This approach can be used when data on labor costs are completely absent, for example, we work with a contractor under an FP type contract, either the equipment‚Äôs share in the cost of work is high, or data on labor costs come with a delay, or when there is an estimate of only the work packages of the top level or stages.

  • direct costs of a work package or operation are equal to direct labor;

Possible difficulties


Addressing Actual Indirect Costs


The regulation and distribution mechanisms quite reliably allow determining the budget and, therefore, the planned volume (PV) and the mastered volume (EV). But, when determining the actual costs (AC), a question may arise with the addressing of costs. For example, actual management costs were higher than planned, to which package of work or operation do they relate? I can offer the following options to circumvent such collisions:

  1. We identify the work package or operation that served as the main reason for the increase in actual costs, and they include all the additional costs. The approach can be used both in the presence and in the absence of data on planned labor costs. From the point of view of minimizing assumptions in the calculation results, this is the most adequate method.

In any case, such an assessment will be better than an assessment at the top-level package level and complete uncertainty regarding the current state of the project.

Using recommendations


It is much better if we use the disbursed volume method in project management. If there are good reasons not to use the accrued volume method with a real planned estimate, or if the real valuation is given at a high level, you can normalize the data and use the accrued volume method with normalized data.

The easiest way to learn English with TED videos

This is a slightly adapted cross-post * of the article. The easiest way to learn English using TED videos from my personal site (a small part at the end of the article that is not directly related to TED has been reduced).

TED videos are not only quality content across a wide range of fields of knowledge, but also one of the easiest and most interesting opportunities for learning English. All videos there are subtitled - both in English, and translated into many other languages, including Russian.

Due to the quality and variety of TED content (compared with the content of training courses specially created for learning English), it is relatively easy to keep attention, including when repeating viewing the same interesting video (repeating works better for learning a language than viewing each video once) - because the information on TED is presented in a very concentrated form and repeated viewing of the video is a natural process of in-depth study of the issue that is revealed in it.

The following shows the setup of the TED mobile application (iOS or Android) and the Youtube page with the TED video in such a way that subtitles are displayed in two languages. The output of double subtitles in the TED mobile application is configured in its options.



Now we implement all this on a desktop computer in Youtube, for example, for this video  (all TED videos are also available on the  TED Youtube channel ).

Viewing through Youtube is not as convenient as through a mobile application, but an important advantage of Youtube is the ability to view not only TED content, but also any other Youtube video with translated subtitles, for example TEDx (most TEDx videos are not available through the application and the official TED website).



A similar viewing setting through the official TED website is not possible, because there are no double subtitles (unlike a mobile application) and the transcript does not scroll automatically when watching a video (unlike Youtube).

Please note that the screenshots above are specially set to slow (75%) playback speed. As a rule, at an initial level of language proficiency, it is advisable to start a video with such a slowdown (even when using double subtitles). When the understanding of speech improves, you can start the video at 100% speed.

I think that the original English-language text is more convenient to watch through subtitles, and translation through a transcript (video decryption) - so that if you lose your understanding during viewing, you can stop for a few seconds and restore the report thread along several lines of the transcript.

In my case, it turned out that the language selection switch works only for subtitles, but does not work for transcript (video decryption) - and the transcript is always displayed in the language set in the Youtube settings (there you can configure it regardless of the global settings for the Google account language). Perhaps this is a temporary bug that will be fixed after some time. But so far it turns out that you can rearrange the original and translated text in places only by choosing the English language in the Youtube settings. As you can see, the titles and descriptions of the TED video are also translated from English, as well as subtitles. The choice of whether their original or translation will be displayed is determined by the language settings - in the mobile application - the language settings of the application, and on Youtube - the language settings of the Google account.

Single Insurance Base - System for working with insurance companies

Hello everyone! I am Yudkina Alisa, Product Owner of the SIB system at the Rosbank Dom Partner Relations Center. Today I will talk about our SIB product - the System for working with insurance companies and slightly open the backstage of the mortgage world.



If interested, welcome to cat.

If you have ever taken a mortgage, then you know that this is a long-term story. An obligatory element of a mortgage loan is an insurance policy, which is for the entire loan period (with a maximum period of up to 25 years). For the bank, this means that all this time it is necessary to maintain up-to-date information on the availability of insurance for a particular loan, the number of insured risks, the insured amount, the name of the insurance company, etc.
Previously, the data was processed by the bank's ABS system, but the exchange of information with insurance companies was carried out by e-mail and Excel. It was very inconvenient (for your understanding: the bank's portfolio consists of several tens of thousands of insurance contracts). At some point, our team realized that we could not do without an IT solution, and decided to create a System for Work with Insurance Companies (SIB).



The project began with the creation of the Personal Account of an employee of the insurance company, which was integrated with the bank‚Äôs ABS. At the first stage, it consisted of 4 tabs: main (information about the latest interactions), settings (password change and viewing accesses), insurance contracts and registries. In addition to the user interface, the System Administrator interface was also implemented, with the ability to create accounts for employees of insurance companies, as well as to control access levels.

From the first days of SIB, partners appreciated the benefits of working in the new system. A number of partners have set up integration with SIB for their part, thereby further reducing data processing time. In the first year of operation, the system worked so well that we began to think how to scale it to other painful areas.



The next stage of development was the SIB.Closing project, which included the integration of three banking systems (ABS, SIB and CRM), as well as external integration with the bank‚Äôs captive partner, Rosbank Insurance. And the customers were our colleagues from other departments involved in the coordination, preparation and maintenance of insurance in a mortgage transaction.

We set ourselves 3 goals:

  1. Lower manual processing costs
  2. Reduced response time from the insurance company (due to insurance auto underwriting on the partner side)
  3. Service for the client, as now he didn‚Äôt have to fill out a complex Excel questionnaire, but just follow the link

1 and 2 were implemented by mid-2017 and already in In September, we were able to launch a pilot with Rosbank Insurance in one of the regions where the bank operates. Over the next couple of months, we fixed bugs and, if possible, quickly eliminated them. November - December is traditionally a high season. And since January 2018, they have already worked on a new process throughout Russia, while the captive insurance company works remotely and only from one region throughout the territory through the SIB for API.

Of course, in parallel, we led the development of the remaining stages of the project. So, by mid-January, a new version of the insurance questionnaire with a more convenient design, Web2.0, was implemented.



And starting in March, we piloted the 3rd and final stage with the Rosbank Insurance API. In April, another insurance company was connected to the pilot, operating from the SIB personal account interface. For some time we still carried out preparatory work in the internal banking systems.

By summer, all bugs were successfully fixed, and from June 13, 2018, interaction on transactions with all insurance companies in all regions is carried out only through the SIB.



Initially, when we just started the project, we worked with an external provider, but gradually the development turned into an inhouse. Now the team and I continue to accompany the development of SIB on change & run tasks, and also work on other mortgage services in parallel.

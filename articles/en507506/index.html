Retrospective of creating your own cartoon

We live in an amazing time. What used to be unbelievable, today we literally "roll around under our feet." Nowadays, anyone can make their own cartoon. Animation programs simplify and speed up this process so much that even one person, being an animator, can make a real animated film.



I will gladly share the experience I have received. It will be about 2D animation, but many points are equally applicable to 3D. Who will be interested in the link to the cartoon itself at the end of the post.



All the work from the script to the final mixing of all the scenes took 9 months. When you work on a cartoon alone, the cost of production is your time (your main resource). That is why, author animation is a large number of compromises. To reduce the "project cost" will require the abandonment of many aspects - a large frame rate, from excessive detail, from cool textures, from complex movements, from frame-by-frame, from multi-character, from dialogs (voice acting), from sound design (ambient sounds), from special effects , and much more. But let's deal with the lessons learned in order.



Animation is power.In animation, you can solve any problem. When writing a script, it is tempting not to do something, because at that time you have no idea how to draw it and, moreover, to sanitize. But when you start to solve a specific problem, you find amazing and simple solutions. When writing a script, don't be afraid! Never limit the flight of your thoughts.



Animation is not the most important thing. Animation in second place. History is more important than animation. It should be interesting, worthwhile, instructive. Animation only reveals your story.



Time is stretching.As regularly happens in real animation production, the ‚Äúproject cost‚Äù rises during the production process. If you are making your first cartoon, then it will take much more time than you expected. No, even more. One of the reasons we are too optimistic about the process itself, overestimating our abilities, knowledge and skills, and they are required immediately from many areas (drawing, composition, color, animation principles, experience in timing, spacing, rhythm, frame structure, editing, effects, light and shadow, textures, etc.). Be prepared that a lack of knowledge and skills will emerge immediately in a number of areas. And that too will stretch the time.



Burnout.A consequence of the previous lesson - for such a long period you light up several times and burn out. It takes a lot of perseverance to get things done. The most effective technique is to force yourself to do small tasks. For example, you sit down at a computer and decide to make a few simple and short elements, frames. Starting to work on them, you really can limit yourself to them and turn off the computer. But often (actually very often), when you start to work, you get drawn in, inspiration is born, as a result of your work, and you do much more than you originally planned.



Speeding up skills development.Alternate, corny switch between theory (20%) and practice (80%). Do not go to either extreme. It is clear that if you confine yourself to theory, then you will never do anything. But often, many are limited to practice, the "force method" and this also reduces the speed of development. From 5 hours - study 1 hour, 4 do. In my experience, this is noticeably faster.



Soaping. Do not wash your eyes. Try to revise your scenes not too often - the eye is blurred very quickly. Get one scene and set aside for a day. The next day, you will see her with different eyes.



You always understand that the script is bad.In the process of working on your cartoon, you will have better ideas and solutions. Is it worth it to follow the originally approved plan or on the go "to re-dumplings"? My advice is to ‚Äúoverwhelm‚Äù, in the end it will turn out better!

The viewer sees differently. Telling stories is very difficult. Everything matters. The viewer will not see as you want, he will see as he sees. And from here two lessons at once:



  • Clearly control your attention, focus on the important (first of all, movement, including cameras, contrast, light / color, knocking out / contrasting from a number, etc.).

The development of the scene. The scene must develop. Do not lay out all the cards to the viewer at once. First show the lamp, then that it can glow, then that you can adjust the brightness of the glow, then that it can blink in the same measure, then an uneven beat (to the music), then that can glow in different colors, etc. Develop scenes.



Incontinence style. It‚Äôs hard to keep the style. Why is it so hard to endure it? It's simple - for six months or a year of work you cannot help but change, your skills, ideas, vision are developing - now you would have done differently, better. But you need to keep the style started a year ago so that the work is solid and does not break.



Black does not exist.Black does not exist, this is an invention of the Americans. No absolutely black color. Use very dark shades for the outline and details. The result will be better.



Improving production efficiency:



  • Banal stage-by-stage speeding up work, through cutting / moving keys, opening a project, pre-rendering).
  • Purity of structure (easy and quick to read first of all names of layers, objects, brushes, templates). If you think about an element, it means that the name for it was chosen unsuccessful.
  • Simple scene naming system.
  • High-quality planning (script, storyboard, animatic, Lists of required backgrounds, characters, props, effects, etc.). The default animation is ‚Äúin the water‚Äù - too smooth, no dynamics. Planning in the end will simply save time and raise the animation to a higher level.
  • Specialization. Do the work in stages (only backgrounds in a row or only rigs or only props). No matter what order.
  • Optimization of the number of locations, backgrounds, the number of characters - do not draw unnecessary, inconsequential, not significant, not carrying valuable information.
  • Image stylization (simplification) - conditional glare on the glasses, a limited color scheme for the entire project, etc.
  • "Skeletal approach." Throwing scenes in a rough, and then polisholishvat (ultimately more effective than licking each scene to the ideal).


Drawing backgrounds. Backgrounds need to be drawn taking into account the proposed compositing:



  • Each background element on a separate layer, so that at the stage of compositing you can move objects and use the parallax effect.
  • It is important to draw backgrounds in perspective (Direct full-face and profile are not interesting, intersections, corners, slopes, ascents are always interesting).
  • There is no equalness in the world (even identical elements must differ in details).
  • Carefully with detail (a fancy elaboration of the background can distract the viewer's attention from important events).


Rigging. Riga needs to be designed taking into account:



  • Character, features of the character (what kind of person he is, what movements he will need, in what perspectives he needs in the scenes, whether dialogues are planned) If you do not plan the rig in advance, it can significantly delay the production of the cartoon.
  • The character to draw primarily stands in the 3/4 view, in front. The most important aspect of the character. It will most often be used. If you plan to make dialogs, then you will diversify the picture 3/4 behind the "interlocutors" of the character.
  • Simplify your character as much as possible (without unnecessary details, textures) - it is much more valuable to invest time in high-quality animation, which is beautiful rigs with bad movements.
  • Name layers for quick orientation (here too).
  • The importance of setting the pivot point in the template.
  • The required style (realism, rubber), the possibilities and limitations of the technique of relocation.


Animation of peace. Characters always move - blinking, breathing, wiggling (there is no static in real life). Minor characters in the scene should move nominally. The main is bright. Right accents:



  • Blinking - not evenly (it reflects a condition, blinking speed, frequency).
  • Micro movements are visible (breathing, swinging, hair, pupils, etc.)


Special effects. Draw all special effects in separate projects. And only when you are completely satisfied with the result, transfer it to your stage. This will speed up the creation process, increase the quality of the result, you will not harm the ready-made elements in your scenes.



The policy creates new errors. You can endlessly paint scenes. In addition, fixing some errors often leads to new ones.



Well, it seems that all the main conclusions that you come to, gaining real experience. Probably the most valuable lesson for me was understanding how huge the world of animation is and how many possibilities lurk in it.

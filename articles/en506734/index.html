Transients are ...


If in a simple way, a transient is when currents and voltages in an electric network change in time due to various events, such as short circuits, circuit breakers, lightning strikes, etc. and so on. Transients - phenomena are most often temporary. In the electric power industry, they can last either nano- and microseconds (switching transistors, lightning strikes), or several minutes or hours (intersystem vibrations, electromagnetic storms).

To calculate the transient process is to find out exactly how currents and voltages change.

Transients usually flow smoothly into steady ones. In principle, this can also be considered as a continuous transient, but if the amplitude, frequency and phase of the sinusoidal signal are constant, then it is much more convenient to consider it as steady-state. Transients and Real Time


There is another area of ‚Äã‚Äãapplication of transient calculations - this is real-time calculations. If, as a rule, everyone pursues a reduction in the calculation time, then here, on the contrary, it is very important that the calculation of one second takes place in exactly one second. This is used, for example, for prototyping, testing and debugging devices designed to interact with the real world: control systems, protection, etc.

Let me explain it with an example: the power plant control system knows that it takes two seconds to react to event X of a power plant, and three to take event Y. To debut this control system, it is connected not to a real power plant, but to a so-called simulator that simulates the behavior of a power plant. The simulator real-time calculates the transients that would occur in the power plant, and behaves accordingly: it responds to event X in two seconds, and on Y - in three, regardless of the number of processor cores and clock frequency. The control system at the same time thinks that it works with a real power plant.


Left: the control system and the power plant work together for the good of the homeland.
Right: the unsuspecting control system is insidiously deceived and connected to the simulator.

Where and how to run the model?


The basis of the software for calculating transients is solver - a program that solves a system of equations. Particularly popular in the field of transients are solvers based either on the method of nodal potentials or on the method of state space. The state space method is suitable for almost any system of differential equations, and the Ohm and Kirchhoff laws are used in the method of nodal potentials, which makes it convenient for electrical systems.

The differential equations of mathematical models must be reduced to a form that is digestible for the solver, i.e., to turn them into a system of linear algebraic equations. For this, numerical integration is used. The trapezoid method is often used, and we will consider it. In real electric networks, control systems are very often used: in electric drives, in wind generators, in DC / AC converters, etc. They have a strong influence on transients, therefore they also have to be taken into account in the calculations.

The complexity of equations in control systems is theoretically limited only by the imagination of engineers: discrete fifth-order transfer functions? You are welcome. Sinus from the logarithm? Everything is already done before us.


Well, if you do not want to develop models and solvers yourself, you can use existing software products. I will give only the programs that are widely known in the narrow circles of power engineers, for compiling an exhaustive list is not an easy task. I have a background more likely to be an energy engineer than an electronics engineer, so I probably missed some popular programs with similar functionality from the electronics field. If you know something similar - share in the comments.

  • EMTP: specialized software for the calculation of transients in electrical networks. It can also be used to calculate steady processes.
  • ATP: the same
  • PSCAD: the same
  • PowerFactory: the same
  • Simulink: popular, famous, many integration methods. But for electric networks with a large number of elements, it is hardly suitable. And for prototyping - very much so.
  • SimPowerSystems: Add-on for Simulink specifically for electrical systems.
  • Hypersim: real-time calculations
  • RTDS: also real-time calculations
  • PSS / E: can in both steady state and transient
  • LTspice: with an emphasis on electronics

Half of these products are from Canada: EMTP, PSCAD, SimPowerSystems, Hypersim, RTDS. I won‚Äôt say why Canadians have such an interest in transients, but they understand them no worse than hockey.

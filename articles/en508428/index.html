The proposed classification is based on the consideration of imitators from two points of view: imitators - as structural components of the learning process, i.e. as means and methods of training that have many distinctive features (1-3 iteration). imitators - as a separate class of software hardware that has many distinctive features (4-8 iteration). 1 classification- as a structural element of the learning process. In the modern understanding, the learning process is considered as a process of interaction between the teacher and students in order to familiarize students with certain knowledge, skills, abilities and values. The structural components of the learning process are the means and methods of training. Teaching methods include the purpose of learning, the way of assimilation and the nature of the interaction between the subjects of learning, and learning tools are material objects and objects used in the educational process as carriers of educational information, an instrument of activity of the teacher and students to achieve their educational goals (i.e., implementation methods). In this sense, learning tools and methods are interdependent, which makes it possible to initially classify the concept of imitators as specific learning tools,used in certain teaching methods. Finding the areas of these definitions will allow us to classify the term more accurately.



2 classification - as a means of learning. The classifications of teaching aids used in pedagogy classify simulators as technical teaching aids (TCO) - systems, complexes, devices and equipment used to present and process information in the learning process in order to increase its effectiveness. The classification of teaching aids (like much in pedagogy) is different - depending on the underlying feature [80, -C. 402-441], however, according to the functional purpose of TSS, they are usually divided into three main classes: information, monitoring and training. Finding the field of teaching methods (i.e. the purpose of learning, the method of assimilation and the nature of the interaction of subjects of training), in which TCOs are applied, allow them to be classified according to teaching methods

5 classification- by the means and methods of interaction of the student with the simulator. In this case, interaction is understood as the processes of transferring program-controlled influences to the user's main organs of perception, as well as the processes of obtaining actions performed by the user to ensure a realistic response of the simulated environment. To implement this interaction, various methods and hardware are currently used: Based on standard input / output tools. Full or partial copy of the workplace "in hardware" (full-scale models of control panels, etc.). Based on virtual reality formation systems.







6 classification- by the method of implementing the simulation mechanism. Simulation methods are understood as a method of ensuring the generation of a model of a simulated process or equipment. Model generation can be implemented in 2 ways: software implementation; hardware and software implementation. The software implementation provides the generation of a model of a simulated process or equipment based on only a mathematical model. A hardware-software implementation provides the generation of a model of a simulated process or equipment based on a mathematical model and data obtained from real sensors.



7 classification- by the number and functions of agents. The training tasks solved with the help of a simulator can be solved by one user or may require the simultaneous operation of many users, usually performing various functions. In accordance with this, the following classification of imitators is possible: single-user; multi-user; Multiuser with an instructor. Simulators designed for use by a single agent are obviously the easiest to implement (within the framework of this classification). Such imitators are the most numerous, because most learning tasks can be successfully implemented by 1 person. Implementation of the simultaneous operation of multiple agents is necessary in cases where the task implies joint actions of agents. For example,when learning certain actions on a drilling rig, a driller, driller assistants, etc., implementing various functions, can be involved. It should also be noted that the agent in multi-user simulators is not necessarily a person, a mathematical model can also be performed as an agent, such agents are called virtual or artificial. Multiuser simulators with an instructor (usually simulators) differ from just multiuser simulators in that the instructor (the number of instructors can be different), unlike other participants in the training, does not participate in the training task, but performs completely different functions, for example: stop and restarting the simulator; preservation of any control point as initial initial conditions for subsequent training;introduction of malfunctions set by the instructor during training or introduced according to a pre-compiled scenario; change in boundary conditions during training; recording the actions of the operator and instructor with the ability to print the results; control of operator‚Äôs actions by an instructor with the possibility of intervention in process control; false alarm, dispatcher, supervisor, etc .; zooming in, etc.dispatcher, boss, etc .; zooming in, etc.dispatcher, boss, etc .; zooming in, etc.



8 - if possible, unite simulators into a single simulation system. The possibility of combining various simulators (including different manufacturers) into one complex makes it possible to classify, in accordance with which simulators can be: with the possibility of combining; without the possibility of combining. The creation of many separate, unconnected simulators, naturally gave rise to the need for a transition to large distributed simulation systems, the potential and effectiveness of which are immeasurably higher. By combining a number of existing simulators, you can get a simulation system with fundamentally new features. The following can be cited as an example, for example, there is a simulator (simulator) of a tank, aircraft, and command post. By combining these simulators, you can get a combat simulation system that can be of interest in itself,and also reuses the capabilities of individual simulators. At the same time, distributed systems should not be confused with multi-user ones, for example, a simulator can provide simultaneous work of several trainees, but cannot be connected with other simulators.



9 - if possible use in LMS-systems (support for SCORM specification). This classification is related to the practical implementation of imitators, such as: facilitating the search, review and use of educational objects (including imitators) by teachers, instructors, or automatically processes during educational programs, as well as facilitating the joint use of such objects by creating catalogs and storage facilities. Of greatest interest is the Advanced Distributed Learning (ADL) initiative group, founded in 1997 by the US Department of Defense. The result of this program was the creation of a reference model of shared objects of educational content - the SCORM specification.In fact, this model represents the integration of various standards and specifications (for example, LTSC, IMS CP) into a single content model and represents the technical infrastructure that allows the sharing of objects in a distributed learning environment. At the moment, the specification is one of the most used in the world, in fact, it is the de facto standard for the provision of electronic educational content.

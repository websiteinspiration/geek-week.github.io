Analysis: why the powerful increase in the number of users and revenue of Zoom does not indicate the success of the company after the pandemic



Image: Unsplash

Zoom video conferencing service has recently been regularly featured in news and collections of the form ‚Äúcompanies that have benefited from quarantine‚Äù. The company's revenue grew by 169% to $ 328.2 million per quarter, new users are added in millions, the value of the company's shares is growing.

However, all this does not at all mean the indispensable continuation of rapid growth after the completion of the acute phase of the pandemic. The publication Seeking Alpha has published an interesting analysis of the topic, and we present to your attention the main points of this material.

Online calls will soon be not the only way to communicate


The coronavirus pandemic and the ensuing tough quarantine measures have seriously affected the business of many companies. Particularly affected were those who were forced to close completely or could not accept customers physically. For some, however, worldwide isolation has become a positive factor. Among these companies are Zoom, whose shares last June cost $ 62.74, and in June 2020 exceeded $ 223 apiece.

But the question is, will Zoom grow as fast when quarantine is lifted, people will return to offices and be able to hold meetings live? Obviously, the fear of coronavirus, as well as other events, such as large-scale protests, will affect the demand for digital communications for quite some time.

On the other hand, in the United States, news about quarantine removal is adjacent to pictures of crowded beaches and shopping centers, and during mass protests people are not particularly worried about the virus. The isolation lasted for many months, and now everyone wants to return to normal life and communicate live.

The combination of these factors is unlikely to allow Zoom to maintain explosive growth.

The company is already overvalued


The P / E ratio (the ratio of the market value of a share to the annual earnings received per share) in the case of Zoom is already more than 6000. This means an extremely high rating. That is, investors are willing to pay very dearly for getting their piece of the pie.

There are companies with a higher P / E - for example, for Shopify this figure exceeds 1300. Such high scores indicate an inflating bubble that cannot last forever.

Competitors are not asleep


Fundamentally, Zoom offers nothing technologically unique. Video conferencing is not a new phenomenon at all; private and corporate users have long been familiar with it. As a result, this leads to serious competition, including from large players.

Google Hangouts is a similar product, and Facebook rolled out the Messenger Rooms feature with the ability to make video calls. Having Google and Facebook among its main competitors is not what any company would like. This factor also cannot be discounted.

Are there any chances for growth?


Zoom's current capitalization is approaching $ 60 billion, and the backlog for growth remains. In the second quarter of 2020, the company expects revenue of $ 495- $ 500 million. In the first quarter, revenue was $ 328.2 - and this was already a growth of 169%. It turns out that the company‚Äôs analysts are counting on its continuation and even acceleration. According to the results of the fiscal year, the company plans revenues in the range of $ 1.775 billion and $ 1.8 billion.

If these forecasts come true, Zoom will remain an extremely attractive asset even for those investors who missed the growth phase amid the coronavirus pandemic. However, it is always important to remember the fundamental risk factors that may affect such companies.

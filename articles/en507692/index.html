Development ecosystem in 2020: how programmers live in Russia and the world

Hello, Habr! At the beginning of 2020, we conducted the fourth annual survey on the state of the development ecosystem to find out how programmers live, what languages, technologies and tools they use and how the development world has changed compared to 2019. We analyzed the opinions of about 20 thousand respondents from 18 countries . The survey involved more than 2.5 thousand programmers from Russia, and this allows us to draw conclusions about how the Russian development market differs from the rest of the world. Here we share the most interesting results.



It is important to note that the survey was conducted before quarantine was introduced. It will be interesting to look at the dynamics of all indicators next year.







This year we expanded the list of questions about professional activities and added a section on lifestyle. First things first.

  • Top 5 for Russia: JavaScript (62.8%), Python (46.8%), Java (36.5%), PHP (24.5%), TypeScript (23.1%).


Interestingly, Russian programmers on average used fewer different languages ‚Äã‚Äãduring the year than developers from other countries. However, relatively new languages ‚Äã‚Äãsuch as Go , Kotlin, and Swift are used more often in Russia.



We see a similar picture in the answers regarding the study of additional languages: Russian developers are more attracted to new languages. They learn Go and Kotlin more often, and Python, Java, TypeScript and PHP less often. Top 5 languages ‚Äã‚Äãthat respondents started or continued to learn over the past 12 months:



  • in the world - Python (31%), JavaScript (26%), Java (22%), TypeScript (20%), Go (18%);
  • in Russia - JavaScript (24%), Python (23%), Go (22%), Kotlin (18%), Java (15%).

But forums and blogs are equally popular with developers around the world. Similarly, the text format of training materials remains preferred by more than half the developers, regardless of geography. Go and Kotlin

However, in general, Russian programmers are less inclined to migrate to new languages: 51% of them do not plan such a transition, for the world this figure is 45%. Database.



Top 3 DBMSs for use over the past year in the world: MySQL (66%), PostgreSQL (36%), MongoDB (35%). In Russia, PostgreSQL (61%) leads by a certain margin , MySQL accounts for 42%, MongoDB and Redis each account for 30%. Even Russians are 10 times more likely to use ClickHouse - a DBMS with Russian roots.



Hosting In Russia, they still prefer to host databases, services and applications on private servers (66%) or locally (53%). The popularity of cloud services (AWS, MS Azure, GCP, etc.) is almost 2 times lower than in the world - 27% and 53%, respectively.



Open source. Russian developers are less likely to participate in open source projects - only 41%. In the world, this figure is 52%.



Communication at work.Programmers from Russia are much less likely than their foreign counterparts to use video conferencing (Google Meet, Zoom), e-mail and calendars for communication at work, but much more often - messengers (for quick messages / calls). The preferences in the messengers are also different: Telegram is expected in the first place (Russians use it almost 9 times more often), then Slack and Skype come. In the world, the top 3 included Slack, Skype and Microsoft Teams.



Types of companies.74% of Russian developers work in IT companies, while in the world 68% of developers account for IT. It is interesting that for the world this figure has not changed since 2018, but in Russia 2 years ago more developers were involved in IT - 82%. If you look at the cut of IT-companies in which compatriots are employed, fintech and telecom stand out here - their share is significantly higher than the world average. Outside IT, Russian developers more often than their foreign colleagues work in financial and logistics companies, but less often in medicine, education and the non-profit sector.



Types of development.Russian programmers are more involved in product development - 71% in Russia compared with 63% in the world. Outsourcing development in Russia also employs more people than the world average - 22% and 13%, respectively. But those who develop products for use within their own company are one and a half times less in Russia than in the world - 30% versus 46%.



Lifestyle



This year we have added a new lifestyle section. We asked developers about their habits, ways of finding information, and participating in charity. This section was optional, but about two-thirds of the respondents chose to answer our questions. Here we also found interesting differences.



Daily routine, hobby. Russian programmers start their work day later: only half of them start working before 10 in the morning, 80% do so in the world. But the views on hobbies for all programmers, regardless of country, are common. In their free time, they like to do most of all ... what would you think? By programming!True, there are differences: if on average the developers around the world more often watch TV, socialize and watch sports, then Russians more often read, play sports and travel. And they love desktops more.



Offline Russian programmers are much more likely to go to meetups - 47% versus 34% in the world. And they use other social networks online more - Russians are much more popular with VKontakte and Instagram , while Twitter and Facebook are the leaders in the world . If you look at the sources of information as a whole, then domestic programmers use social networks, news sites, TV (almost 5 times), print media (4 times) and radio (3 times) less, but books and forums (Reddit, Stack Overflow) are addressed more often by foreign colleagues.



Artificial Intelligence. Russian developers have more confidence in artificial intelligence - 62% admit that AI will replace programmers in the future, and 8% are completely sure of this. In the global community it is 50% and 4%, respectively. 62% of Russian developers are ready to trust the decision of the judge judge, and only 49% of their foreign colleagues allow this possibility.



Charity.Views on charity also differ significantly: only 35% of domestic programmers at least somehow participate in charity projects - this is one and a half times less than in the world. But those who do this, more often as a reason, indicate a desire to help solve problems that the state does not deal with. However, the main reason for participating in charity both in Russia and in the world is the desire to make the world a better place. At the same time, Russian developers are more likely to make donations than to help professionally or work in charitable organizations.

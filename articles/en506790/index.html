Steroid Data Scenario: Introducing Decision Intelligence

What is common between defensive behavior when meeting with lions in the savannah, a responsible approach to AI management, and data warehouse design problems.



Decision intelligence is a new scientific discipline that considers all aspects of decision theory. It combines the best of the applied field of data science, social sciences and management science in a single area designed to help people improve their quality of life, improve their business and improve the world around them using data. This is the most important science of the era of artificial intelligence, which includes the skills of competent project management, strategic planning of goals, determining the optimal metrics and security systems for large-scale automation.

Decision intelligence is a discipline that allows you to turn information into the best practical action of any scale.

Let's take a look at the basic terminology and concepts. The following paragraphs can be viewed fluently (or skipped completely if the information seems too boring to you).

What does “decision” mean?


The data is beautiful, but the decisions are important. It is our decisions — our actions — that affect the world around us.

By “decision” we mean any choice between the options of any subject, so the conversation is much broader than the typical MBA dilemma (like: opening a branch of your business in London or not worth it yet).

It is our decisions — our actions — that affect the world around us.

Based on this wording, assigning a user’s photo to a label with a value, for example, a cat or a non-cat, is a decision that a computer system makes. But the decision to launch this system is pondered and made by the human leader (I hope!) Who is responsible for the project.

Who is a decision-maker (decision maker)


In our understanding, the “decision-maker” is not an interested person or investor who intervenes in the process to veto the activities of the project team, but the person responsible for the architecture of decisions and the formation of context. In other words, it is the creator of carefully formulated goals, not their destroyer.

What does decision-making mean?


Decision-making is a phrase that different disciplines use differently. And it can mean:

  • taking measures in the presence of alternative options (in this sense, both a computer and a lizard can make a decision);
  • fulfillment of the obligation (person) to make decisions, and responsibility for them. Even if a computer system can make a decision, it cannot be called a "decision maker." The system is not responsible for the results obtained - this responsibility lies entirely with the people who created it.


Make Calculations vs. make decisions


Not all events / offers are solutions. According to the terminology of decision analysis, a decision is made only once, and the resulting changes cannot be rolled back. If you can return the changes without unnecessary costs, then the decision has not yet been made.

Systematics decision intelligence


One way to get closer to studying decision intelligence is to break it down into traditional areas into quantitative aspects (mainly intersecting with applied data science) and qualitative (developed primarily by researchers in the field of social and administrative sciences).

Quality side: science of decision making


Disciplines falling into the group of qualitative aspects are traditionally called decision-making sciences - I would like everything to be called that way (alas, we do not always have what we want).

The science of decision-making deals with issues such as:

  • “How to set up decision criteria and calculate metrics?” (All disciplines)
  • “Is your chosen option motivationally compatible?” (Economy)

Data collection and data engineering


If we had facts, we would have done everything. Alas, we live in the real world and often have to work to get the treasured information. Data engineering is a complex discipline focused on providing reliable access to information on a large scale.

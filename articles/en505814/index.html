Radarr, Jackett and the bot in the cart. Download torrents in a new way



As a result, of course, streaming will finish torrents. And if in the 720 / 1080p world this, in fact, has already happened, then Blue-ray / 4k / 8k content is still held by torrents (here in Europe) and users (states).

Today, streaming wins, mainly due to convenience. Opened the app, went to the "new products" section and choose for every taste. I uploaded my process with torrents, and now it looks, at least, no worse.



Radarr is following my IMDB watchlist and RSS feeds with new releases. If what I want to see is already out - Jackett polls my torrent trackers, and qBittorent downloads the desired release. You can go do your own thing, as the movie is downloaded - I will receive a message from the bot in a group home chat. If the film has not yet been released, Radarr will view RSS news from the trackers, wait until the film comes out with the quality I need, and download it. When a 4k / blue release appears, he will update the film himself. At the same time, my simple telegram bot will keep me up to date with the events.



All this works like a clock for six months now, and other family members with a humanitarian education even use it => it's time to share the life hack with the community.

A little bit about my home platform


The heart of my home network is the Synology DS218 + NAS with up to 16 GB of RAM (the old laptop memory came in handy). US is quietly pulling a couple of virtual machines, half a dozen containers, home video surveillance, an advertisement cutter (pi-hole) and much more. This is a reliable, quiet and economical piece of iron that works 24/7 years, if you do not offend it. Unlike the home server, it has a ready-made set of applications for phones.

I set up everything described below exactly on NASA, but you can, of course, choose any other platform.

Docker significantly simplifies the maintenance of the entire farm in the long term, although it requires initial efforts to configure containers (but here I will help you).

Python scripts that you will find below can also be run in docker or even directly on NASA, but I already have ubunta. I will use it.

A few words about VPN. If copywriters or lovers of protecting children from Internet freedoms are embroiled in your threat model, you can try to raise Synology Virtual DSM inside real DSM, configure a forced VPN and play with the docker in it. There are also docker containers with OpenVPN for qBittorent / Transmission and Jackett, but you have to configure them yourself.

qBittorent


In order to remove authentication from the web face, you can add these parameters to qbittorent.conf:

WebUI\AuthSubnetWhitelist=192.168.1.0/24, 127.0.0.1/32
WebUI\AuthSubnetWhitelistEnabled=true

In the qBittorent settings, you need to set the port for incoming connections (I have 6882) and throw it inside the network on the home router. This is necessary so that you can not only download torrents, but also distribute them. Check the availability of the port from the outside here .

There are a dozen different plugins that allow you to integrate qBittorent with your desktop browser for convenience. I use Torrent control for FF.

Telegram bot


In Radarra, you can configure notifications via Telegram, but the configuration is minimal. For example, you cannot add a release size to a message template.

I wrote a small telegram bot (you can call it a bot with a stretch, because it is, by and large, a simple POST request) that can talk to qBittorent and send messages to the Telegram group chat. So far, there are only 2 types of messages: torrent added, torrent downloaded. But for six months I did not come up with anything new that should be added.

Nzb360


This is a paid phone application for managing all the mentioned facilities: it shows your Radarr card file, allows you to monitor the qBittorent queue and add new downloads through Jackett. You can easily do without it, but it is too comfortable with it. If you endure until Black Friday - the author often arranges a sale.

Modern infrastructure: problems and prospects





At the end of May, we held an online meeting on the topic “Modern infrastructure and containers: problems and prospects” . We talked about containers, Kubernetes and orchestration in principle, about the criteria for choosing the infrastructure and much more. Participants shared cases from their own practice.



The participants:



  • Evgeny Potapov, CEO of ITSumma . More than half of his clients are either migrating or want to switch to Kubernetes.
  • Dmitry Stolyarov, CTO Flant . He has 10+ years of experience working with container systems.
  • Denis Remchukov (aka Eric Oldmann), COO argotech.io, ex-RAO UES . He promised to talk about cases in the "bloody" enterprise.
  • Andrey Fedorovsky, CTO "News360.com" After the company is purchased by another player, he is responsible for a number of ML and AI projects and infrastructure.
  • Ivan Kruglov, Systems Engineer, ex–Booking.com. The same person who did a lot with Kubernetes with his own hands.


Topics:



  • Participants' insights about containers and orchestration (Docker, Kubernetes, etc.); that you tried in practice or analyzed.
  • Case: The company is building an infrastructure development plan for years. How is the decision made whether to build (or translate the current) infrastructure on containers and Coober or not?
  • Problems in the world of cloud-native, which is missing, let's imagine what will happen tomorrow.


An interesting discussion ensued, the opinions of the participants were so different and caused so many comments that I want to share them with you. There is a video for three hours , and below is a squeeze from the discussion.



Is Kubernetes Standard or Great Marketing?



“We came to him (Kubernetes. - Ed.) When no one else knew about him. We came to him even when he was not there. We wanted him before ”- Dmitry Stolyarov





Photos from Reddit.com



About 5-10 years ago, there were a huge number of tools, and there was no single standard. Every six months a new product appeared, or even more than one. First Vagrant, then Salt, Chef, Puppet, ... “and you rebuild your infrastructure every six months. You have five admins who are constantly busy copying configs, ”recalls Andrei Fedorovsky. He believes that Docker and Kubernetes "crushed" the rest. Docker has become the standard in the last five years, Kubernetes in the last two years. And this is good for the industry .



Dmitry Stolyarov and his team love Coober. They wanted such an instrument before it appeared, and came to it when no one else knew about it. At the moment, for reasons of convenience, they do not take the client if they understand that they will not implement Kubernetes in it. At the same time, according to Dmitry, the company has “a lot of gigantic success stories for remaking a terrible legacy”.



Kubernetes is not only container orchestration, it is a configuration management system with a developed API, a network component, L3 balancing and Ingress controllers, which makes it relatively easy to manage resources, scale and abstract from the lower layers of the infrastructure.



Unfortunately, in our life you have to pay for everything. And this tax is large, especially when it comes to switching companies with developed infrastructure to Kubernetes, according to Ivan Kruglov. He could freely work both in a company with a traditional infrastructure, and with Coober. The main thing is to understand the characteristics of the company and the market. But, for example, for Yevgeny Potapov, who would generalize Kubernetes to any container orchestration instrument, this is not a question.



Eugene drew an analogy with the situation in the 1990s, when object-oriented programming appeared, as a way of programming complex applications. At that time, the debate did not stop and new tools appeared that supported the PLO. Then microservices appeared as a way to get away from the monolithic concept. This, in turn, led to the emergence of containers and their management tools. “I think that we will soon come to the time when there will be no question of whether it is worth writing a microservice small application, they will write it by default with a microservice,” he said. Similarly, Docker and Kubernetes will eventually become the standard solution without the need for choice.



Base problem in stateless





Photo by Twitter: @jankolario on Unsplash



Nowadays, there are many recipes for running databases in Kubernetes. Even how to separate the part that works with the I / O disk from, conditionally, the application - part of the database. Is it possible that in the future the databases will change so much that they will be delivered in a box where one part will be orchestrated through Docker and Kubernetes, and in another part of the infrastructure, through separate software, a storage part will be provided? Will databases change as a product?



This description is similar to queue management, but the requirements for reliability and synchronization of information in traditional databases are much higher, says Andrei. Cache hit ratio in normal databases is kept at 99%. If worker goes down, a new one starts, and the cache is “warmed up” from scratch. Until the cache is warmed up, worker runs slowly, which means that you cannot load the user load on it. While there is no user load, the cache does not heat up. It's a vicious circle.



Dmitry fundamentally disagrees - quorums and sharding solve the problem. But Andrei insists that the solution is not suitable for everyone. In some situations, a quorum is suitable, but it gives an additional load on the network. NoSQL database is not suitable in all cases.



The participants of the meeting were divided into two camps.



Denis and Andrei argue that everything that is written to disk — bases and stuff — cannot be done in the current ecosystem of Coober. It is not possible to maintain the integrity and consistency of productive data in Kubernetes. This is a fundamental feature. Solution: hybrid infrastructure.



Even modern cloud native databases, such as MongoDB and Cassandra, or message queues like Kafka or RabbitMQ, require persistent data storage outside of Kubernetes.



Eugene objects: “The bases in Kubera are near Russian injuries, or near-enterprise trauma, which is connected with the fact that there is no Cloud Adoption in Russia.” Small or medium-sized companies in the West - this is Cloud. Amazon RDS is easier to use than messing with Kubernetes yourself. In Russia, they use Coober “on-premise” and transfer bases to it when they try to get rid of the zoo.



Dmitry also did not agree with the statement that no bases should be kept in Kubernetes: “The base is different. And if you shove a giant relational base - then by no means. If you shove something small and cloud native, which is mentally prepared for a half-ephemeral life, everything will be fine. ” Dmitry also mentioned that the database management tools are not ready for either Docker or Kuber, so there are great difficulties.



Ivan, in turn, is confident that even if we ignore the concepts of stateful and stateless, the ecosystem of enterprise solutions in Kubernetes is not yet ready. With Coober, it is difficult to support the requirements of the legislative and regulatory bodies. For example, it is impossible to make a decision to provide identity, where strict guarantees of server identification are required, up to the hardware that is inserted into the servers. This area is developing, but so far there is no solution.

The participants failed to agree, therefore, in this part of the conclusions will not follow. Let's give a couple of practical examples.



Case 1. Cybersecurity of the “mega-regulator” with bases outside of Coober



In the case of a developed cybersecurity system, the use of containers and orchestration allows you to fight off attacks and intrusions. For example, in one mega-regulator, Denis and his team implemented a bunch of an orchestrator with a trained SIEM service that analyzes logs in real time and determines the process of attack, hacking, or failure. In the event of an attack, an attempt to put something in, or when an ransomware virus invades, it lifts containers with applications faster through the orchestra than they become infected, or faster than an attacker attacks them.



Case 2. Partial Relocation of Booking.com Databases to Kubernetes



At Booking.com, the main database — MySQL with asynchronous replication — has a master and a whole hierarchy of slaves. By the time Ivan left the company, a project was launched to transfer slaves that can be "shot off" with some damage.



In addition to the main base, there is a Cassandra installation with a self-recorded orchestration, which was written before Coober went to the mainstream. There are no problems in this regard, but it has persistent to local SSDs. Remote repositories, even within the same data center, are not used due to the high latency problem.



The third class of databases is the search service Booking.com, where each node of the service is a database. Attempts to transfer the search service to Coober failed, because each node is 60-80 GB of local storage, which are difficult to “raise” and “warm up”.



As a result, the search engine in Kubernetes was not transferred, and Ivan does not think that there will be new attempts in the near future. The MySQL database was moved halfway: only Slaves that are not afraid to “shoot”. Cassandra "took root" perfectly.



The choice of infrastructure as a task without a common solution





Photo by Manuel Geissinger from Pexels



Suppose we have a new company, or a company where part of the infrastructure is built in the old way. It builds an infrastructure development plan for years. How is the decision made whether to build infrastructure on containers and Coober or not?



Companies that fight for nanoseconds are excluded from the discussion. Healthy conservatism pays off for reasons of reliability, but nevertheless, there are companies that should consider new approaches.



Ivan: “I would certainly start a company in cloud now, simply because it’s faster,” although it’s not necessarily cheaper. With the development of venture capitalism, startups with money have no big problems, and the main task is to conquer the market.



Ivan holds the view thatthe development of the current infrastructure is the criterion of choice . If in the past there were serious investments, and it works, then there is no point in remaking. If the infrastructure is not developed, and there are problems with tools, security and monitoring, then it makes sense to look at a distributed infrastructure.



The tax will have to be paid in any case, and Ivan would pay the one that allowed him to pay less in the future. “ Because it’s just because I’m traveling in a train that others are moving, I’ll travel much further than if I get on another train that I have to throw fuel on myself.“- says Ivan. When the company is new, and the latency requirements are tens of milliseconds, then Ivan would look in the direction of the "operators" in which classic databases are "wrapped" today. They raise a replication chain, which switches itself in case of failover, etc. ...



For a small company with a pair of servers in Coober, it makes no sense, says Andrey. But if she plans to grow to hundreds of servers or more, then automation and a resource management system are needed. 90% of cases justify the cost. Moreover, regardless of the level of load and resources. Everything from startups to large companies with a millionth audience, it makes sense to gradually look towards products for container orchestration. “Yes, this is really the future,” Andrey is sure.



Denis identified two main criteria -scalability and stability of work. He will choose the tools that are best suited for this task. “It can be on the knee the collected noun, and on it Nutanix Community Edition. This may be the second line in the form of an application on Kuber with a database on the backend, which is replicated and has the specified RTO and RPO parameters ”(recovery time / point objectives - approx ).



Eugene outlined a possible problem with the staff. At the moment, there are not many highly qualified specialists in the market who are versed in the guts. Indeed, if the selected technology is old, it is difficult to hire someone other than older, bored and tired people. While other participants believe that this is a matter of training.

If you raise the question of choice: launch a small company on the Public Cloud with bases in Amazon RDS or “on premise” with bases in Kubernetes, then despite some drawbacks, Amazon RDS became the choice of participants.



Since the majority of mitap listeners are not from a “bloody” enterprise, distributed decisions are what we should strive for. Storage systems must be distributed, reliable, and create latency, measured in units of milliseconds, a maximum of tens , - summed up Andrew.



Kubernetes Use Assessment



Listener Anton Zhbankov asked a trap to Kubernetes apologists: how was the feasibility study chosen and carried out? Why Kubernetes, why not virtual machines, for example?





Photo by Tatyana Eremina on Unsplash



Dmitry and Ivan answered it. In both cases, trial and error, a sequence of decisions was made, as a result of which both participants came to Kubernetes. Now the business begins to develop software on its own, which makes sense to transfer to Coober. We are not talking about classic third-party systems, such as 1C. Kubernetes helps when developers need to make releases quickly, with non-stop Continuous Improvement.



Andrey’s team tried to make a scalable cluster based on virtual machines. Nodes fell like dominoes, which sometimes led to the fall of the cluster. “Theoretically, you can finish and support with your hands, but dreary. And if there is a solution on the market that allows you to work out of the box, then we are happy to go to it. And we switched as a result. ”- says Andrei.



Standards for such an analysis and calculation are available, but no one will say how true they are on real hardware in operation. For calculations, it is also important to understand each instrument and ecosystem, but this is not possible.



What awaits us





Photo by Drew Beamer on Unsplash



As technologies develop, more and more disparate pieces appear, and then a phase transition occurs, a vendor appears that killed enough “dough” so that everything connects together in a single tool.



Don't you think that the moment will come when the tool appears, what Ubuntu has become for the Linux world? Perhaps a single instrument of containerization and orchestration will include Coober. With it, it’s easy to build on-premise clouds.



Ivan gave the answer: “Google is building Anthos right now - this is their package offer, which deploys the cloud and includes Kuber, Service Mesh, monitoring, all the bindings that are needed for microservices in on-premise. We are almost in the future. ”



Denis also mentioned Nutanix and VMWare with the vRealize Suite product, which can do this without containerization.



Dmitry shared his opinion that reducing “pain” and reducing taxes are two areas where improvements can be expected.

humanID:





Linux.com talks about a new project designed to increase the security and comfort of working on the Internet. Every day, billions of people use social accounts, such as Facebook Login and the like, to access applications over the Internet. The main disadvantage of this system is the inability to distinguish a real user from a bot, the publication writes. The non-profit organization humanID, a recipient of the Harvard University Social Impact Fund, came up with an innovative idea: to develop an anonymous login with one click, which serves as an alternative to social input. "With the help of humanID, everyone can use the services without giving up privacy and not selling their data. Botnets are automatically excluded, while applications can easily block intruders and trolls, creating more civic digital communities , ‚Äùsays Bastian Purrer, co-founder of humanID.

Two hours and 3.5 dollars. How I made a simple site with visualization of the distribution of coronavirus

It all started with the fact that I could not find a normal visualization of the data on the distribution of coronavirus. Rather, there was one site showing the dynamics on the map and metrics for China, but this is exactly what I was least interested in, I am interested in the global picture. To be honest, this virus scares me.

Plan


Well, this is the simplest task, I thought:

  1. We are looking for an open API on the WHO website.
  2. We are writing a parser.
  3. We raise a little servo with a base somewhere in the free tier of Amazon.
  4. We buy a domain, there should not be difficulty in choosing.
  5. Set the flask, wrap d3js for graphs, screw letsencrypt there for greater importance.

Reality


Something went wrong:

  1. There is no open API on the WHO site.
  2. You can‚Äôt write a normal parser, WHO uploads the data in PDF, the format changes, and spending more than one evening on creating a dashboard was not in my plans.
  3. It turns out that I already spent my free tier on some experiments.
  4. Almost all coronavirus and coronavirus 2020 domains are already taken, who needs them at all?
  5. Given the preceding paragraphs, I definitely won‚Äôt get on with one evening, setting up flask and the base for it ...

Decision


We can say that the solution turned out to be really nocode and fully meet my expectations:

  1. The data is collected in a small plate in Google Sheets.

  2. Datastudio created a simple dashboard.


  3. The entire site - one html file in 42 (not so special) lines, half of which is metadata of any markup.

  4. Instead of certificate and google analytics - cloudflare.
  5. Instead of connecting mail for feedback - a link to Twitter.

findings

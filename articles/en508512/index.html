How we made a VR game about space crash

Stages, errors, conclusions



I came to BeaversBrothers a year ago. I was called when the company launched a new service - the development of VR / AR solutions for business. To develop technologies and understand our capabilities, we decided to make a virtual game. After all, the game has everything: a fascinating world, a vivid storyline that captivates the script, interactive with the user, a complex technical implementation. Therefore, such a pilot was doubly interesting to us.



We have been developing the game for half a year. This was the first team project on virtual reality, and we stepped on all the rakes that we could step on. As a result, we got tremendous experience, worked out all the possible mistakes, but still unreleased the game Astro Collapse in the Oculus store .



In this article, I will talk about the main stages of creating a VR game and give practical recommendations to developers and their customers. Do not make our mistakes, and you will finish the game in 3 months, not half a year.



About the game



Astro Collapse is a single-player space shooter in virtual reality. A giant shower of asteroids is approaching Earth. Human civilization is on the verge of destruction. People urgently evacuated to shuttles and leave the planet. You are a shooter on a warship that accompanies shuttles and clears their path from asteroids. You need to listen to the commands of the captain of the ship and destroy asteroids from an armor-piercing machine gun, a powerful plasma gun and a laser gun.





Purpose of the game: save as many ships as possible and protect the planet



Search for an idea



When we started working on the project, we did not have an explosive concept. We just wanted to make a virtual game to gain experience. The first three ideas came to me by chance.



Tubing



In my Instagram feed, I saw how friends ride the ‚Äúbagels‚Äù from the hills. And I thought, why not launch players on them from the top of a virtual mountain ?!





On the first test of the game, our copywriter lost his balance and almost fell.



Karting



In honor of February 23, girls from the company rented go-karts for us. When I rushed along the highway, I decided that it would be great to drive in the virtual world as well. So the second idea came.





The prototype of the game was simple mechanics: go around the boxes and not crash into the walls.



Saving the earth



It‚Äôs quite simple here. I read the news on the Internet and noticed that now they talk a lot about space: Ilon Mask, life on Mars, the origin of the universe. This is where the story of Astro Collapse began.





The first prototype of a space shooter: at first it was slow and boring.



As a result, we spent 3 weeks developing all the prototypes. Due to the fact that we did not have one big idea, we had to compose quick solutions on the go. We made prototypes without knowing the audience of the game and whether they would be interesting. As a result, two ideas and two weeks spent on them went nowhere. And time is money.



Before you make a game, think about what it will be and for whom. Create a prototype of the game for the idea in which you are sure.


Game script



In the first versions of the game, the mechanics were very simple. To escape, the player needs to shoot asteroids flying on his ship. The game is divided into levels. The higher the level, the more asteroids attack the ship.



We tested the script inside our company and found out that it is very boring.







Our editor is testing the first version of the game:



Before developing a game, come up with an interesting scenario, so as not to waste time and money on alterations.


We decided to develop the plot and add additional objects so that the player does not feel alone in the virtual world. On this and stopped. That was a big mistake. We did not think of testing the first version on children and adults not immersed in the product. We made a second prototype of the game, based only on the opinion of the team.



Thousands of asteroids fly to Earth. In a few minutes they will reach the goal. It is necessary to evacuate people and try to protect the planet. Asteroids need to be shot so that evacuation ships do not fall under them.





Scenes from the second prototype of the game



We exhaled. In our opinion, the game turned out exciting and interesting! But not so simple ...



Smashing testing on kids and gamers



To verify the success of our game, we decided to conduct the last test before publishing. For this, the children of our employees, their friends and their acquaintances were invited to the office. The participants were from different audiences - this guaranteed the objectivity of testing. We were sure that everyone would like the game, and if there were any comments, then they would be small. Already the first tester dispelled our pink dreams and made a small span.



The players did not understand what was happening: who to shoot at, who the enemy was, what kind of ships they were passing by. Everyone asked: ‚ÄúHow to get another weapon and why there are no bonuses?‚Äù. Children and adults reacted differently to the game.



Adults wanted a measured game: unhurried shooting, less head movement, clear instructions on what to do and who to shoot.





Adult phrases during testing: Why such fast asteroids? Why are there so many? And what's that? Who's that? What is this for?



Children did without a plot. They did not care what caused cosmic chaos. They simply destroyed everything that moves and stands still. They said that they want even more asteroids, ships, and movements. The most important thing that they did not have was the boss at the end of the game.





Children's Phrases: Why are there so few asteroids? Where is the boss asteroid? Is it an explosion?



We did not receive completely negative reviews and no one said: ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt like it at all, redo it‚Äù. But we realized how many details we did not take into account. The most important flaw - people did not understand the plot of the game and their goal.



In the third version of the game, we added broadcasting: orders from the commander, screams of people, sounds of chaos and panic. So we were able to immerse players in the atmosphere.



To tell a story and introduce a person to the plot, we made an introductory video that talks about what happened and what remains to be done in the game.





The game trailer



also added a training mode in which the player learns how to shoot, who are the enemies and allies, how to get a superweapon. The plot and goals of the player became clear. A powerful final chord appeared - a huge boss asteroid.



Now, not only we were satisfied with the result, but also the people who played the game for the first time.



Test the game for the target audience from the very first version so that at the end of the project you don‚Äôt have to start all over again.


The main mistake in the end



The project did not have a manager from the very beginning. It appeared when we tested the first version of the game. Before him, development was slow, we often went into the wilds and lost our spirit. But with the advent of the manager, the situation has changed. He organized testing, established communication within the team, monitored the timing and quality of the project. We released the game 2 months after its arrival.



A project should always have an evangelist manager who will manage the development and keep the team up to date.


To summarize



What is important to know when you are developing a VR game:



  1. First come up with an idea that you believe in. Only then implement.
  2. Think over the scenario of the game and test it on the target audience.
  3. Conduct extensive testing of the game from the very first prototype.
  4. Assign a responsible project manager so that the team does not relax and does not lose focus.


These four simple tips can help you cut budgets and development time by half, or even triple.

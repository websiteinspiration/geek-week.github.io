Why do green parrots live in Germany?







After thirty years I have lived in Ukraine, everyday life in Germany appears in a completely new light. And regardless of the reasons that prompt people to move to another country, I think everyone has gone through several stages of destruction and formation, awareness and forgetting. This is understandable and logical, because any person perceives the world through the prism of his own consciousness.



My parents invested a lot in me, developing conscious critical thinking. Therefore, I can ask well-formulated questions and, as a rule, I am able to find answers to them. I note that many methodological teaching aids have been developed, including The Art of Asking the Right Questions, the authors of which note that any deductive or inductive thinking is still based on knowledge. Any question, even the simplest, can be asked only after receiving a sufficient amount of knowledge. Therefore, having arrived in a foreign country and having no idea about it, you are trying to build on the everyday experience that you have received in your own country. But this does not work, because for those questions that you would easily find answers in your home country, you will not find the answers "in a distant country" ...



What did I know about Germany while in Ukraine? Actually NOTHING. This is not an understatement. I believe that in order to learn something about the country, one must live there. Do you like a state and want to move there to live? Then try to pose at least one critical question, and you will understand how vague and confused your fabrications based on fragmentary information are. For example, to a simple question, whether or not to do the Haftpflichtversicherung (the so-called insurance in case you or your child accidentally harm someone else’s property), you will answer “yes, do it”. People, especially those who have children, first come to Germany, do the Haftpflichtversicherung. Imagine that your 7-year-old son walked along the sidewalk next to you and scratched someone else's car. It is clear that insurance should cover everything - for this it is done.But what and how to say it right in an insurance company? During the scratching, did the child walk 1.5-2 meters away from you or did you hold his hand? In this case, the question "How to answer the insurer?" already critical, because in one case they will pay for you, and in the second you will pay for yourself. And, no matter how surprising it may sound, but you will be forced to pay if you held the child’s hand during scratching! This is the “German" logic. It is unprofitable and sometimes tiresome, but I began to pose, albeit small, but critical "questions in German." In German, not in the sense of linguistic knowledge, but in the sense that, while in Germany, they can be answered.



Last year, in February, I went to the Universitätsklinikum Mannheim on a case. This is a beautiful old building located on the banks of the Rhine River. Having entered one of the buildings where I had an appointment, I felt at home — a building with high ceilings of 3 or 4 meters, wooden heavy doors painted with white oil paint. Even in Ukraine, it seemed to me that no one had ever peeled off the old paint layer, but painted a new layer on top of the old one - and because of this, the wooden heavy doors became even heavier. In the hall there was an old toothless piano, on which it was impossible for a person to even play a dog waltz, and who could play on it - these were sunbeams, piercing a damp, despite the dry winter, hall. In principle, at this time of the year everything is napping and even not so many people are in the clinic. But still, the sounds of something or someone violated the winter silence.These were GREEN PARROTS (Indian ringed parrot).









I used to plan everything in advance (it wasn’t connected with moving to Germany - I was always like that), find out information about historical aspects and sights in advance, but green parrots - it was a huge surprise for me! At first, it was thought that this was probably one of the types of bumps (crossbills). But I did not observe conifers, exclusively deciduous. And the song is very professional - the shishkar will not pull. I had to rummage around on the Internet, ask people, finding out what made such a pampered royal bird, whose birthplace is India, to wander and settle in Germany.









Some scientists fear that parrots could disrupt the local ecosystem and displace, for example, owls and bats. But! From a subjective point of view: watching these slightly arrogant birds, you can always feel the breath of spring, and they definitely guarantee a smile and good mood. And returning to an objective view of things: parrots in Germany appeared quite a long time ago (about 40 years ago) and no negative influence on the shift of the ecological niche has yet been proven. In general, it is probably worth saying that green parrots have found their niche without endangering or crowding out local species.



Local residents, however, complain about the bad nature of parrots. Not only do they scream loudly, so if you park a car under a tree where a parrot feeds, then a cone or any other fruit can fly into the car. Explaining to the parrot that this is bad form is pointless, just like hoping that living in Manheim you will not encounter these harms and their tricks. You can not even drive a car, but ride a bicycle, but even then a parrot can get you, for example, tearing off the insulation from houses in order to build a nest.

In general, in Germany, this bird can be found not only in Manheim, but also in Cologne, Geldelberg, Bonn, Düssenldorf.Currently, green parrots in Germany are being studied by scientists Michael Brown and Michael Wink / Institut für Pharmazie und Molekulare Biotechnologie (IPMB). Since 2006, they have been ringing couples of parrots and using the local population (conducting explanatory work on how to read the ornithological ring), they are investigating the question of where the Heidelberg parrots winter (near Manheim).



We all love animals, but try again to ask another critical question: “As the owner of the house, whose facade is constantly peeled by parrots, can I fence my own facade with a grid?”. Haha There it was! There are certain standards (you have never noticed that in Germany all houses are of the same color, height and roof are the same color), and you have to endure, from year to year, close up the holes that arise. For the city hall, it’s easier to pay the owner to restore the facade than to spoil PERFECTIONISM with an ugly grid.









And how do they still fight in German with the green robbers? The first is the construction of houses (birdhouses) by parrots so that the birds do not want to steal part of the facade of the house, the second - in the birdhouses themselves - the replacement of real eggs with artificial ones (to prevent the reproductive success of the hooligan population). Some townspeople even propose to trim the wings of parrots. But this idea did not find the proper response among the majority of the population, because there is a saying: "Born to crawl - can not fly." Only here it is the other way round: the one born to fly cannot crawl. Moreover, what consequences this “clipping” and the associated “crawl” will bring, no one knows. I learned from a pair of bird watchers that in the 1970s, in Berlin, in the Dahlem-Zelendorf area, there was also a small group of green parrots,but they chose an unsuccessful neighborhood next to a hawk, a hawk, who preferred to hunt unfamiliar green birds. And within 3-4 years, the parrots were mercilessly exterminated in this area. Just like pigeons. Because of this, in some areas of Berlin there are no pigeons at all. Therefore, if we already consider a radical decrease in the population of green sneakers, then some residents are considering the option of invasion by predators.

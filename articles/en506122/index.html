Adapters for CF cards to the back of the system unit are very popular, where they are mounted instead of the plugs.


But they have two drawbacks:
1. The IDE cable may not be long enough to reach both the back wall and the optical drive ‚Äî and you often only have one IDE channel.

I immediately refused the option to cut a hole in the case, I decided to think up something in the 3.5 "compartment. But I didn‚Äôt really like how it would look externally, because I decided to go the other way - gut the 3.5 "drive and hide the adapter inside. A little work with a screwdriver and pliers, a little hot glue and double-sided tape - and the "drive" was ready.
The LED lights up during disk activity synchronously with the case.



From the disadvantages of my solution:
1) The IDE tail is attached so that the second device cannot be hung on it. You can decide by replacing this tail with an IDE extender mom-dad, just to bring dad out. 300 rubles for ali, maybe I'll order it.
2) The door hangs, its spring does not press anymore. In principle, it is not very scary - it normally hangs under its weight when the card is not inserted.

It was a bad idea to eject a memory card at the touch of a button, but there would have to greatly complicate the design with a lever system.
4) For some reason, here I have 12 volts short for the case - maybe the adapter is defective, maybe when assembling something, it scratched somewhere unsuccessfully. But, since 12 volts is not needed here, I just did not eventually connect this contact.

Appearance quite suits me, let all drives come out multi-colored. Black, however, stands out too much - I will change it later.

Memory


I wanted to put 32 + 32 + 8, but the glitches of the module itself and the motherboard jokes intervened.
One of the 32-megabyte modules with 486 works at half the capacity - it is normal for the entire capacity at the pentium.
A motherboard in two adjacent slots accepts only the same modules. That is, if I put slots 32 and 8 in 1 and 2, then at boot I see 64 and a memory error. If you put 8 and 32, then I will see 16. 3 and 4 slots work similarly. So in fact, I can use different modules only in 1 and 3 slots, and I have to leave 2 and 4 free for now. For now, I just put one 32 megabyte module. And so good for 486.

In which cities of Russia there is no quarantine for visitors


Green - you can safely go, red - you need to prepare in advance (for example, a pass or application form), black - there is quarantine or self-isolation for visitors, blue - there are nuances. Image: Maxim Yaskevich, my colleague in Tutu.

Which cities do not need to be quarantined for 14 days if you want to travel by train or fly by plane to visit friends or relatives. Well, or on a business trip, but there is usually a little more clear situation before the decision to travel. Actual on June 5 and will be approximately until June 8, 2020.

You can walk freely


This is a list of regions of the Russian Federation where quarantine is canceled, there are no observations and other difficulties for visitors, no passes are needed. Some of them need to wear masks and gloves.

  • Republic of Bashkortostan
  • Bryansk region
  • The Republic of Ingushetia
  • Kaluga region
  • Kursk region
  • Leningrad region
  • Novosibirsk region
  • St. Petersburg
  • Sverdlovsk region
  • Tver region
  • Chelyabinsk region
  • Moscow region
  • Karachay-Cherkess Republic
  • Republic of Adygea

Need to prepare in advance


In these cities there is no observation, but you need to get a pass, report about yourself or fill out a questionnaire at the entrance.

  • Arkhangelsk region (arrivals from the regions must be informed about themselves and fill out the form )
  • Kabardino-Balkarian Republic (arrivals from the regions must be informed about themselves)
  • Republic of Kalmykia (arrivals from the regions must be informed about themselves)
  • Moscow (to get around the city you need a pass )
  • Khanty-Mansiysk Autonomous Okrug (a pass is required . Visitors from Bashkortostan, Dagestan and Yakutia must have a certificate of the epidemiological environment)
  • Chechen Republic (need a pass)

Self-isolation for visitors


And this is a list of cities in which you need to quarantine 14 days in observance or self-isolation.

  • Altai Republic - report about yourself, 14 days in self-isolation
  • Altai Territory - under 65, self-isolation 14 days, if you are from Moscow - either self-isolation or an observatory.
  • Amur Region - 14 days at home
  • Astrakhan region - 14 days at home
  • Belgorod region - 14 days at home or the presence of a certificate confirming the absence of COVID-19.
  • The Republic of Buryatia - 14 days at home or at the observatory.
  • Vladimir region - notify and sit at home for 14 days.
  • Volgograd region - again two weeks at home
  • Vologda Oblast - Until July 1, they ask to observe the self-isolation of all visitors, testing is proposed.
  • Voronezh region - self-isolation for guests from Moscow and St. Petersburg.
  • Republic of Dagestan - report about itself and in quarantine.

What are the stages of a custom project


I divide the work on the first release of an external project into five stages:

  1. Introductory stage.
  2. Preparatory stage.
  3. Stage of development.
  4. Stage of completion of the project or module.
  5. Stage of support.

For all subsequent releases, items two through five remain relevant.

1. Introductory stage


Stage Result:

  • Signed framework agreement with the customer
  • Synchronized understanding of goals and objectives
  • Formed basis for a single team with the customer on the entire horizon of cooperation

Step 1: Find out what the customer wants


It is necessary to receive answers from the customer to the following questions:

  • What does he want to do?
  • Why does he want to do this?
  • Who does he want to do this for?
  • What technologies does he want to do this on?
  • How much time does he have to create this?
  • What are the boundaries of the creation budget?
  • What kind of customer experiences?
  • What does the customer attribute to bottlenecks?
  • What are the hardware / performance / user device requirements?
  • How does the customer see the delivery of the project?
  • How does the customer see the support of the project upon its completion?
  • What are the boundaries of the escort budget?
  • Is it possible to put a project in our portfolio?

Step 2: Agree with the customer flow on which you will work with him


It is necessary to tell the customer the whole path of work on the project.

Personally, I am retelling what I am writing about in this article.

This can be done in different ways: orally, text, presentation, somehow. Try to determine who the customer is - visual, audio, kinesthetic or discrete, and, depending on this, choose a way to present your flow.

Further, this flow needs to be adjusted together with the customer so that both sides are comfortable.

How is the project cost formed?

Each team member has an hour cost.

The customer is given a price list that indicates the cost of an hour for each specialist. The cost of an hour in practice is determined by the market, because margin management is through cost control.

Thus, the cost of the project / stage / task is determined by the cost of an hour of work of a specific specialist and estimate for his work.

How to calculate the cost and cost of an hour is an occasion for a separate article.

Step 3: Introduce the customer to the team


You can face or online. Our team and customer team are present at the meeting.

I myself talk about who we will be responsible for, and then I give the customer team the opportunity to ask questions to my team members directly.

Step 4: Sign the master contract


Briefly about what kind of agreement this is: A

framework agreement.

This agreement reflects the basic rights and obligations of the parties.

That is, the entire ‚Äúbureaucracy‚Äù is sewn into this agreement.

Moreover, everything related to the content part, that is, specific tasks / deadlines / costs, is packaged in separate mini-contracts, called tasks (applications), which are ‚Äúglued‚Äù to the framework agreement.

Thus, from the point of view of the bureaucracy, flexibility appears in the decomposition of the project into tasks / timelines / costs.

Hint

I am in favor of not having more than a month of tasks in the assignment to the contract. Let's say if there is a stage for 3 months - we beat it into 3 sub-stages, each lasting a month.

Thus, in the case of a problem customer, we do not risk losing money more than 1 month of work.

The customer also remains in the black - he does not risk all the money at once -> he often sees the intermediate results of work -> he sleeps calmer.

2. Preparatory phase


Stage Result:

  • Formed raw materials for developers
  • Timeline (Schedule)
  • The cost of each product by module
  • Signed task to the framework agreement on the development of the module / task / stage

This stage acts as the foundation for all subsequent stages. This makes it the most important.

Preparation is the most creative and unpredictable process due to the fact that there is grinding in with the customer and a lot of interaction with him. And the customers are all different.

Step 1: Get Ready for the Creative Process


Analyst, UX / UI designer and QA, holding hands, communicate with the customer, collect all critical information for them.

The analyst and the UX / UI designer give their estimates on the way out.

Project Manager oversees the process.

Why is there a QA?

QA is the person who is responsible for quality. The quality of not only the system, but also the quality of requirements, wireframes and layouts. These three entities need to be tested for comprehensibility, completeness, consistency, and adequacy. This means that from the very beginning he needs to keep an eye on the fact that, at least, the analyst and designer do not miss anything when communicating with the customer. And as a maximum - to validate and supplement the customer, "test" those introductory ones that he gives.

Project Manager takes these estimates, verifies that the team has not forgotten anything, has not mixed up that the estimates are reasonable, based on the experience with the team, lays a buffer and receives the final estimate.

Based on estimates, Project Manager generates a timeline and cost.

Then there is communication with the customer, bidding for estimates / deadlines / cost, adjustments in the introductory for the analyst and designer.

If everything is OK, a task for a master contract is created for this stage.

An example of a task for this stage can be found at the bottom of the article.

Step 4: Conduct an assessment of labor costs, time and cost, and formulate a development task


In most cases, having raw materials for a module or a pair of modules - you can begin to evaluate them.
That is, you do not need to wait for the availability of raw materials for all modules.

Within the framework of the directions (frontend / backend), the work should be evaluated modulo by the leads or, in their absence, by specific performers.

  1. Developers give estimates to their tasks

  3. Based on the experience with the team, Project Manager lays down buffers and receives the final estimate, the timing and cost of the module. If there is no experience with the team, you should lay buffers based on the complexity of the tasks, the degree of validity of the estimate by the performer, and your own experience.

Notes

Ideally, a story behind a story manages to move only in dreams. This is normal. It is important to be aware of how many lines are simultaneously comfortable to keep in RAM for you and your team.

From the point of view of the bureaucracy, the task within the framework of the contract most likely will be sewn up the module = group of stores. However, nothing prevents them from passing their story as a story.
Earlier, get feedback from the customer - there will be less fuss when you close the task.

Infrastructure


Remember: the fact that the application opens and works well in the browser does not mean that it is ready for production.

In production, you need to be able to monitor the status of your application. And in case of problems, have the tools to treat these problems.

In prod, the application must be controlled, fault-tolerant and protected.

For this we need (basic):

  • properly organized access
  • error collection system
  • monitoring system, health checks, alerts
  • log collection system
  • backups

And all this horror in the organization is sometimes more difficult to develop the products themselves.

Blood Written Rules


  1. First of all, do integrations. You never know at what step in the integration a plug will happen
  2. Conduct retrospectives. Not often needed. It is necessary not rarely. And don't forget to fix the problems found during the flashbacks. And not just write them in meeting notes.
  3. Keep track of luggage from deficiencies / bugs and small things. Straight story after story never manages to move. There will always be several at work. It is important to ensure that technical debt, unfinished features and bugs do not exceed the critical line. If they pass - to be in trouble. Listen to the team. Keep track of its effectiveness.

For those who are interested in judicial practice regarding the collection of personal data of users on various portals of state organizations, as well as in the provision of various state services, I ask under the cat:



I kept it for myself, I realized that I needed to share this information.



In short, that is, the cassation ruling of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation in favor of the subject of personal data on the issue of demanding consent to the processing of personal data when rendering public servants.



The story itself is abstract:



  1. Applicant - an individual applied for a public service to a government agency of a constituent entity of the Russian Federation.
  2. According to the existing administrative regulations, together with the application for the provision of public services, the applicant had to give his consent to the processing of personal data .
  3. However, the applicant refused to give his consent , in particular, he was afraid of the transfer of information to third parties.
  4. The applicant was denied the provision of public services.
  5. The applicant sued the state authority.
  6. The first instance sided with the applicant.
  7. The appeal sided with the government agency.
  8. The Armed Forces of the Russian Federation sided with the Applicant.


From the definition of the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation in fact:



  • Consent to the provision of personal data is voluntary
  • All data for the provision of public services were indicated in the application, and according to the Federal Law of July 27, 2010 N 210-, state governments do not have the right to demand consent and documents that are unnecessary for the provision of public service.

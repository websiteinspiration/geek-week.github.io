Turbo and AMP - or website acceleration?

Website speed is an important ranking factor, especially on the mobile Internet. To speed up the loading of pages on phones and tablets, you can use Turbo technology from Yandex and Google AMP. However, they have too many cons.



Turbo and AMP - or website acceleration?









A modern site should be fast. According to Google statistics, if a resource is loaded for more than 3 seconds, approximately 50% of users leave it. And in the interval from 2 to 5 seconds of loading, the conversion drops by almost 10 times.



Low speed is also a negative ranking factor. Google and Yandex pessimize pages with low speed and a high percentage of bounce rates. For search engines, user comfort is important.



About how to control performance, read the material "Why and how to check the speed of loading a site."



Mobilegeddon here and now



Performance is especially significant on the mobile Internet. Smartphone and tablet users are most demanding on the speed at which pages open. According to the same Google, a slowdown from 1 to 10 seconds increases the failure rate by 123%.



Slow download speeds annoy mobile users more than ads and non-playable videos.



Google



If 10 years ago the mobile Internet audience was not so large, then with the development of technology it has grown. And today, at least half of site visitors are gadget users.



Seeing the trend, Google in 2005 announced the principle of Mobile First Index. Sites that are not adapted for mobile devices, began to decline in the issuance - and in the desktop version, too. Thus began the era of MobileGeaddon.



Faster Mobile Pages



Adaptation for mobile devices took a long time and with a creak. For 10 years, by 2015, not all site owners managed to configure them to work with smartphones. The situation was complicated by the low speed of the Internet connection in the mobile network.



Google decided to radically solve the problem. The company offered developers and website owners the technology of fast loading - Accelerated Mobile Pages, abbreviated as AMP.



What is Google AMP?



Accelerated Mobile Pages are simplified versions of pages. They use 10 times less data and open faster than 1 second, even with a slow Internet connection.









Google



AMPs Why AMPs are so fast:



  • They are written in a special HTML language with a limited number of AMP tags.
  • Own library of scripts allows to load different page elements asynchronously.
  • Google stores and plays AMP pages on its own servers.


In fact, AMP is the simplest constructor from which you can build lightweight web pages. But it does not have most of the functionality of ordinary sites: no animation, no filters, no dynamic changes.



Google is actively ‚Äúcompleting‚Äù and promoting its technology. AMP pages are shown above the others in the search and are marked with lightning.



Yandex Turbo Pages



Accelerated mobile pages appeared in Yandex in 2017. They got the prefix "Turbo" - by the name of the technology of fast loading in the Opera browser.









Yandex Turbo Pages



The principle of operation of Turbo pages is the same as that of AMP. To create them, a set of lightweight templates is used, and the content is cached on Yandex servers. This allows you to almost instantly open sites without going to them.



Turbo pages load 15 times faster than the originals.



Initially, the technology was intended for news resources. But then Yandex began to connect everyone. The search engine prefers its own accelerated pages: they appear higher in the search results and are marked with a rocket icon.



Benefits of Accelerated Pages



Both technologies have the same advantages:



  • almost instant loading pages;
  •   Correct display on any mobile devices;
  •   priority in search results;
  • Highlighting in search results using icons
  • lower bounce rate.


Search engines try to encourage webmasters to use quick pages to make search more convenient for people. To this end, updates are regularly rolled out for Turbo and AMP. 



But, despite the bonuses when ranking, site owners are reluctant to connect accelerated pages. Technology is not for everyone.



Why you should not connect Turbo or AMP



When quick pages first appeared, many thought that they had a future. In reality, both AMP and Turbo were ‚Äúsharpened‚Äù to solve a rather narrow range of tasks. Lightweight, primitive in their device and trimmed in functionality, pages are well suited for displaying text with a picture. But no more than that.



When quick pages are needed



Accelerated mobile pages are a good solution when you need to deliver content as quickly and easily as possible.



Which sites are suitable for quick pages:



  • news feeds;
  •   informational resources;
  •   blogs
  •   online magazines;
  •  some directories;
  • spacing pages.


The technology may be suitable for a store with a small number of positions or as an alternative to a light version of the site - especially when it has mainly mobile traffic. However, for most commercial web projects, it is not applicable.



AMP and Turbo - what's the catch?



Accelerated pages in their current form cannot replace the usual mobile version of a modern corporate resource, online store, or even a business card site that involves active interaction with users.

Technology disadvantages:



1.       Template design



All accelerated pages are ‚Äúassembled‚Äù from a limited set of typical elements. Therefore, visually they are so similar to each other. The visitor‚Äôs eyes have nothing to cling to, the site does not leave any impressions and is not remembered. This is especially annoying when you seriously spent on the design and usability of the main version.



2.       "Alien" urls



Quick pages open on Yandex and Google links. This does not just cause image damage, but directly harms SEO promotion. After all, despite the fact that analytics systems count traffic from such pages, in fact, there are no visitors or an increase in the link mass. Third-party resources link to Google and Yandex URLs. Moreover, in order to ensure high speed surfing on key sections of the site, you will have to create new AMP and Turbo pages.



3.       Lean functionality



Although Google and Yandex are constantly developing accelerated pages, their functionality will always be limited. It is being cut intentionally - in order to prevent a slowdown in content loading. You can add a quote, a YouTube video, or an online map. But many useful and interesting features that attract users do not work.



4.       Limited monetization.



If the site makes money on advertising, with the creation of quick pages, revenue from it will be limited. It is possible that advertisements will also be shown more often due to an increase in the flow of visitors, however, according to Yandex rules, for example, on a Turbo page it is allowed to place no more than 2-3 blocks with advertising. Whereas in the regular version you can show a dozen ads.



Another minus of accelerated pages is the difficulty with maintenance. Having connected Turbo and AMP, you will have to follow them, as well as the main site and the mobile version. This may require additional costs.



Finally, you will not be able to fully control your own project by betting on fast pages. After all, content management on them occurs from the search engine.



When speed is not important



Performance is not the only indicator of website quality. The web resource should be relevant to the user request and solve the tasks of the visitor. On a high-quality website, a person gains positive experience - emotionally perceives design, uses the available functionality, receives information and closes his needs.









Speed ‚Äã‚Äãis not the main indicator of the quality of a site.



There are studies showing that users are less critical in relation to sites that satisfy their needs. Of great importance is the motivation of the person.



When people complain about the speed of a site, the problem is often different.



User Interface Engineering



Therefore, it makes no sense to chase a high download speed to the detriment of beauty, information content and interactivity. It is important to find a balance between all the components of a high-quality Internet project.



Optimum download speed



It should be understood that the concept of "download speed" includes several components:



  • server response time;
  • time to receive the first byte (Time To First Byte, TTFB);
  • Content loading
  • loading the visual part.


The optimal speed for the search robot and for the user may vary. For SEO, the Time To First Byte metric is important in the first place. Whereas a person evaluates the speed of loading the visual component.



A reasonable TTFB is 200-300 ms. The total page load time, from the point of view of the user, should be 3-5 seconds. These figures should be guided in the work to speed up the site.



Acceleration as a Method



Google AMP and Turbo Yandex technologies solve one single task - instant performance. This is to the detriment of SEO, advertising and user comfort.



Accelerated mobile pages are not suitable for most commercial Internet projects. And setting them up is not always easy, and maintenance requires additional resources.  



It is much more correct to work on optimizing an existing mobile or adaptive version of a site. Its acceleration will improve behavioral parameters and increase positions in search results. It benefits both you and your audience.

VR when training staff. The real pros and cons

For 20 years I have been developing training programs for training specialists. During this time, he used a lot of VR devices. There were eMagin Z800 3D Visor, Razer Hydra, Barco stereo projection system with a silver rear projection screen, the first Oculus, gloves, OptiTrack, etc. And each time everything ended either with the termination of the production of equipment or simply with ‚Äúexperiments‚Äù, often the capabilities of VR equipment did not allow ‚Äúserious‚Äù use in training personnel.









For example, already history, the eMagin z800 did not allow the person who first put it on to work for more than 5 minutes. Usually dizziness and other unpleasant conditions began. The image quality was also not high, the student had to spend considerable time trying to understand what exactly he sees.















An example of what the user saw.



What has changed now? A lot of things. Customers require support for VR helmets, not for the image, but for the real learning process. The latest developments of Oculus and HTC Vive also begin to work in training centers. We can say that there is a little confusion with the API, but this is no longer a problem either, because there are lots of examples.



Now a little about the ‚Äútheoretical‚Äù pluses and possibilities of VR -

Virtual reality is a model three-dimensional (3D) environment created by computer tools and realistically responding to interaction with users, and the system for creating virtual reality is a system that provides generation of a reality model in accordance with mathematical a model of this reality using software.



The bottom line is that when the programmed controlled influences are applied to the user's main organs of perception (visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory), as well as while providing a realistic response of the simulated environment to the actions performed, the effect of the user's personal participation in the observed virtual environment appears.



The main difference between virtual reality systems and other technologies (video, 2D, 3D graphics, etc.) is that VR provides the effect of personal presence and personal participation of the user (i.e. the user does not feel the difference between actions in reality and actions, performed in a VR system). VR systems are capable of implementing a fundamentally new and very effective way of transmitting information with a deep, at the subconscious level, degree of impact on the user.



The depth of information consolidation is several times superior to traditional methods. This and other qualities of VR systems are of great importance in creating simulators.

That's what they expect from VR.



Now practice.



1. Purpose



I‚Äôll clarify that I consider the use of VR only for staff training. In other words, we are interested in systematic training, i.e. ensuring the assimilation of a certain combination of skills, abilities and procedures. Tasks - to ensure the conformity of the acquired materials or skills to the requirements of the upcoming work, the effectiveness of the educational process, and also to ensure that the stereotypes learned during training are successfully transferred to the conditions of real work.



2. Select the desired content



The level of correspondence of the synthesized image and sound to the original is an important factor on which the effectiveness of the simulator as a whole depends. Real equipment is rarely silent. Very often, the sound carries a lot of information about the operation of the equipment or ongoing processes. A change in the sound picture often indicates an accident. The synthesized image of any object, part or process must be recognizable. Failure to comply with these requirements can lead to a loss of user time in trying to understand what he sees and hears, which significantly reduces the effectiveness of training.















We select a simulator for performing regular technological operations and a simulator for personnel actions in the event of an emergency.



3. Choose the hardware



HTC Vive + additional trackers Vive Tracker + gloves Noitom Hi5 VR Glove.



4. We put an experiment in the training center. The group of trainees (of different ages) is divided into two parts. The first is trained first on a PC without VR, then on a VR helmet. The second part of the group, respectively, is the other way around.



5. Compare the effectiveness of learning using VR with a classic keyboard / mouse.



How to compare?



The quantitative justification for assessing the effectiveness of the use of simulators from the point of view of improving the quality of training causes considerable difficulties.



Most existing studies associate the effectiveness of electronic educational resources (which include simulators) with how much the student remembers (this can be easily measured). Often the following characteristics stand out:



  • temporary (time taken to complete the action, operations, reaction time, time taken to correct the error, etc.);
  • high-speed (labor productivity, reaction rate, movement, etc. - the reciprocal of time);
  • accuracy (error value in terms of physical quantities (millimeters, angles, etc.), the number of errors, the probability of error, the probability of an exact reaction, action, etc.);
  • informational (the amount of memorized material, processed information, the amount of perception, etc.).


Other factors are noted, such as the development of creative abilities, professional intuition, etc., but there is no consensus on the assessment of these factors, which is the reason for the difficulties in determining the quantitative assessment. On the issue of the effectiveness of perception and memorization of information, there is a large similarity of views. With the increasing popularity of imitators, one can expect the appearance of research in the field of pedagogy. The method is not perfect, because uses ‚Äúpedagogical scales‚Äù and ‚Äúcompetency matrices,‚Äù that is, dimensions that do not have any economic equivalent or dependence. Other shortcomings are indicated, such as taking into account the knowledge / skills achieved during the training process, i.e. transferred to the training conditions, and not to the actual working conditions of the specialist, etc.(Efficiency should be assessed as the result of transferring skills from training conditions to the conditions of real work).



Another approach is Adequacy as a measure of efficiency, that is, the degree of similarity between a real or an object or a process imitated using a simulator. This method focuses on the question ‚Äúhow accurately does the simulator reproduce real equipment and processes‚Äù, that is, the main advantage of this method is to take into account the following set of factors that characterize the simulator as a learning tool:



  • the level of correspondence (similarity) of the synthesized image to the original;
  • the level of conformity of the synthesized sound environment to the original;
  • level of conformity of control mechanisms to the original;
  • the level of compliance of the mechanisms of environmental influences (temperature, pressure, etc.);
  • the adequacy and universality of the mathematical model (behavior of objects), etc.


This method has a rather interesting variety - adequacy from the point of view of sensory processes and perception, that is, the question shifts from ‚Äúhow exactly is the equipment and processes simulated?‚Äù to the question ‚Äúhow much is the difference in perception between training on the simulator and training on real equipment?‚Äù, that is, the emphasis is shifting from equipment to the student. To assess the adequacy (or similarity) of perception, at the moment, expensive and complex studies are necessary, because many physiological indicators must be taken into account:



  • eye-tracking (tracking the path of the eye);
  • a change in blood chemistry;
  • registration of electrical activity of the brain, etc.


As the main drawback of the method is the need to use an expert approach in the process of setting factor weights, as well as the boundaries of factors at the stage of developing requirements for the created simulator.



In general, this method is of considerable interest, but is quite difficult to implement. If the material will be of interest to readers, I will definitely write an article about the neural interface.

As a result, a new approach was chosen to assess the effectiveness of the simulator, the distinguishing feature of which is to consider the simulator not only as a technical training tool, but also as a tool (tools and methods) for risk management. With this setting, efficiency can be defined as the predicted reduction of risks (losses) of the enterprise as a result of training personnel on the simulator. Thus, the effectiveness can be determined by the following ratio: the



effect of the use of simulators is the predicted risk reduction depending on the costs

Effect = (AB) / C, where



A is the expected risk (loss) taking into account the current value of the probability of the human factor (for example, rubles);

B- The expected risk (loss), taking into account the decrease in the probability of the human factor (due to the use of simulators);

C- The costs of creating (or purchasing) and using simulators in the training process.

* the probabilities of risks A and B contain a period (period) of time - the time of the experiment.



6. To summarize (for the standard we take a standard PC without VR)



Factor:



  • temporary (time taken to complete the action, operations, reaction time, time spent on fixing the error, etc.) - 140% (VR is 40% better)
  • high-speed (labor productivity, reaction rate, movement, etc. - the inverse of time) - 90% (VR is 10% worse);

  • accuracy (error value in terms of physical quantities (millimeters, angles, etc.), the number of errors, the probability of error, the probability of an exact reaction, action, etc.) - 120% (VR is 20% better)
  • informational (the volume of memorized material, processed information, the volume of perception - 170% (VR is 70% better)
  • predicted risk reduction - 140% (VR is 40% better)


The results show a clear victory for VR. Of the minuses, one can point out the following - several trainees, nevertheless, complained of dizziness and fatigue (FPS did not sag) while working in the helmet, some of the trainees noted slight dizziness and fatigue after training.

From Outsourcing to Development (Part 2)

In a previous article , I talked about the background to the creation of Veliam and the decision to distribute it through the SaaS system. In this article, I will talk about what had to be done so that the product would not become local, but public. About how they began to spread and what problems they encountered.

Planning


The current server side for users was on Linux. Almost every organization has Windows servers, which cannot be said about Linux. The main strength of Veliam is its remote connections to servers and network equipment behind NAT. But this functionality was very rigidly tied to the fact that the router should have been a Mikrotik. And this obviously would not satisfy many. I first started thinking about adding router support to the most common vendors. But I realized that this is an endless race with an extension of the list of supported firms. Moreover, those already supported can have a different set of commands for changing NAT rules from model to model. VPN was the only way out of the situation.

Since we decided to distribute the product, but not as open source, it was no longer possible to include various libraries with open licenses such as the GPL. This is generally a separate issue, after deciding to sell the product, I had to sort through half of the libraries due to the fact that they were GPL. When they wrote for themselves, it was normal. But not suitable for distribution. The first VPN that comes to mind is OpenVPN. But he is GPL. There was also an option to use the Japanese SoftEther VPN. His license allowed him to be included in his product. After a couple of days of various tests on how to integrate it in such a way that the user does not need to configure anything and know about SoftEther VPN - we got a prototype. Everything was as it should. But, for some reason, this scheme confused us anyway, and we eventually abandoned it.But naturally they refused after they came up with another option. As a result, they did everything on regular TCP connections. Some of the connections work through the coordinator, some directly through the Nat Hole Punching (NHP) technology, which was also implemented on Free Pascal. I must say that I have never even heard of NHP before. And it could not have crossed my mind that you can connect 2 network devices, both of which are located behind NAT directly. I studied the topic, understood the principle of work and sat down to write. Conceived, the user is connected with one click to the desired device for NAT via RDP, SSH or Winbox without entering passwords and setting up a VPN. The required list of objects is sent to the input, and what kind of requests should be made, and in response I receive data. And all this in multithreaded mode. Fine.

Distribution start


From the very beginning, we understood that we needed a small flow of users of the system so that they would test the product in combat mode and give some first feedback. Several purchased posts in the VK have borne fruit. Send the first registration.

Here it must be said that entering the market when your company does not have a famous name, and at the same time providing agentless monitoring functionality in which you need to enter accounts from your servers and workstations, is very difficult. It scares so many people. From the very beginning, we understood that there would be problems with this and were prepared for this both technically and morally. All remote connections, despite the fact that RDP and SSH are encrypted by default, are additionally encrypted with our software using the AES standard. All data from local servers is transmitted to the cloud via HTTPS. Accounts are stored in encrypted form. Encryption keys for all subsystems are individual for all clients. For remote connections, session encryption keys are generally used.

All we can do in this situation so that people can be calmer is to be as open as possible, work on security and not get tired of answering people to their questions.

For many, the convenience and functionality of software outweigh the fear, and they register. Some individuals in the published posts in VK wrote that this software cannot be used because This is a collection of their passwords and generally a no-name company. I must say that this opinion was not one person. Many simply don‚Äôt understand that when they install other proprietary software on a server that works as a service, they also have full rights in the system and they don‚Äôt need accounting in order to do something illegal (it‚Äôs clear that you can change the user from whom the service is started, but this way, you can enter any account). In fact, people's concerns are understandable. Installing software on the server is a common thing, but entering an account is already a little scary and intimate, since a good half of people have one password for all services,And to do a separate account even for the test is lazy. But at the moment there are a huge number of services that people trust their credentials and not only. And we strive to become one of them.

I already wrote above that after it was decided to make the product publicly available, and not just for my own use, I had to work hard and look for replacements for some modules that did not allow myself to be included in our product. But after the release, a very unpleasant thing accidentally appeared. The Veliam Server, which was on the client side, included the MariaDB DBMS. And she is licensed under the GPL. The GPL license assumes that the software must be open source, and if MariaDB, which has this license, is included in the composition of our product, then our product must also be under this license. But fortunately, the purpose of this license is open source, and not the punishment in court of those who accidentally make a mistake. If the copyright holder has a complaint, he will notify the violator in writing and he must eliminate the violation within 30 days.We found our mistake ourselves and did not receive letters and immediately began to consider options for how to solve the problem. The solution was obvious - the transition to SQLite. This database has no licensing restrictions. Most modern browsers use SQLite, and a bunch of other programs. I found information on the Internet that SQLite is considered the most common DBMS in the world, just because of browsers, but did not look for proofs, so this is inaccurate information. This becomes a non-trivial task when there are several hundred servers installed on clients with MariaDB and data in it. Some MariaDB features are not available in SQLite. Well, for example, in the code used queries like

Select * FROM `table` WHERE `id`>1000 FOR UPDATE

This design makes not only a selection from the table, but also blocks the row data. And a few more designs also had to be rewritten. But besides the fact that I had to rewrite a lot of requests, I also had to come up with a mechanism that, when updating Veliam Server, the client will port all the data to a new DBMS and delete the old one. Also, transactions did not work in SQLite and this was a real problem. But after reading the expanses of the World Wide Web, I easily found that transactions in SQLite can be enabled by sending a simple command when connecting

PRAGMA journal_mode=WAL;

As a result, the task is completed and now the server part of the clients runs on SQLite. We did not notice any changes in the operation of the system.

New HelpDesk


From the internal version to the SaaS version, it was necessary to port the HelpDesk system, but with some changes. The first thing I wanted to do was integrate with the client‚Äôs domain in terms of transparent user authorization in the system. Now the user, to go to HelpDesk and leave a request in the system, simply clicks on the shortcut on the desktop and the browser opens. The user does not enter any credentials. The module for Apache SSPI, which is part of Veliam Server, automatically authorizes the user under a domain account. To leave a request in the system when a user is outside the corporate network, he clicks on the button and a link arrives in his mail, through which he logs in to HelpDesk without passwords. If the user is turned off or deleted in the domain, then the account in HelpDesk will also stop working.Thus, the system administrator does not need to monitor the accounts in the domain and in HelpDesk himself. The employee quit - disconnected the account in the domain and that‚Äôs all, he won‚Äôt log into the system not from the corporate network, not from the link. For this integration to work, the system administrator needs to make one GPO, whichAdds an internal site to the intranet zone and throws a shortcut to all users on the desktop .

The second thing that we consider essential for HelpDesk systems, in any case for ourselves, is to connect to the applicant directly from the application with one click. Moreover, connections must be made if the system administrator is on a different network. For outsourcing, this is a must, for regular system administrators it is also often very necessary. There are already several products that do the job of remote connections. And we decided to make integration for them. Now we have done the integration for VNC, and in the future we plan to add Radmin and TeamViewer. Using our network transport for remote connections to the infrastructure, we made VNC connect to remote workstations behind NAT. The same will be with Radmin. Now, in order to connect to the user,you just need to press the "connect to the applicant" button in the application itself. The VNC client opens and connects to the applicant, regardless of whether you are on the same network or sitting at home in slippers. Previously, the system administrator, using GPO, must install everything on the VNC Server workstations.

Now we ourselves are moving to the new HelpDesk and are using integration with the domain and VNC. It is very convenient for us. Now we can not pay for TeamViewer, which we used for more than three years to work our support team.

What are we planning to do next?


When we released the product, we did not make any paid tariffs, but simply limited the free tariff to 50 monitoring objects. Five dozen network devices and servers should be enough for everyone, we thought. And then requests to increase the limit began to come. To say that we were a little shocked is to say nothing. Are companies with such a number of servers interested in our software? We expanded the limit for those who made such requests for free. In response to their request, we asked some of them why they needed so much, did they really have such a large number of servers and network equipment. And it turned out that system administrators began to use the system in a way that we had not planned at all. Everything turned out to be simple - not only servers, but also workstations began to monitor with our software. Hence there are many requests for expanding limits.Now we have already introduced paid tariffs and the limits can be expanded independently.

Servers almost always work with either storage or local disks in a RAID array. And we originally made a product for them. And SMART monitoring was not interesting for this task. But taking into account the fact that people adapted the software for monitoring workstations, there were requests for the implementation of SMART monitoring. Soon we will realize it.

With the advent of the Veliam Connector, it became unnecessary to deploy a VPN server on a corporate network, or do RDGW, or just forward ports to the necessary machines for connecting via RDP. Many people use our system only for these remote connections. Veliam Connector is only for Windows, and some users of companies connect from home laptops running MacOS to workstations or terminals on the corporate network. And it turns out that the system administrator is forced, due to several users, to return to the issue of forwarding or VPN anyway. Therefore, now we are already finishing making a version of Veliam Connector for MacOS. Users of their favorite apple technology will also have the opportunity to connect to the corporate infrastructure with one click.

I really like the fact that having a large number of system users does not have to puzzle what people need and what will be more convenient. they write their wishes themselves, so there are a lot of development plans for the near future.

In parallel, we are planning to start translating the system into English and distributing it abroad. We do not yet know how we will distribute the product outside our country, we are looking for options. Perhaps more on that later there will be a separate article. Perhaps someone who has read this article will be able to suggest the desired vector, well, or he himself knows and knows how to do it and will offer his services. We will be grateful for your help.

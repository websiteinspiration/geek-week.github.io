Sites, go to IPv6, two

On the eighteenth of September last year, Belarusians were pleased with the unexpected decree No. 350 . Among other bureaucracy there was a particularly interesting paragraph:
6. Internet service providers are required to:
...
from January 1, 2020, when rendering services for placing information systems and (or) information resources on the Internet, addressing using technology that provides full support for Internet protocols of versions 4 and 6 by network devices;
...
That is, from January 1, all sites hosted in Belarus should be able to address via IPv6.

And, thus, Belarus became the first country in which IPv6 was enshrined in law.
In the news, slogans about the next technological breakthrough came together, but ... let's see how successful the administrative and administrative implementation of IPv6 was.

To begin with, we examine "common" resources. Unfortunately, we did not find a list of all BY domain domains in the public domain, so we‚Äôll make several representative samples and check the sites for AAAA records.

It should be noted here that not all sites in the BY zone are required to host in Belarus.
In accordance with the Decree of the President of the Republic of Belarus dated 01.02.2010 No. 60 ‚ÄúOn measures to improve the use of the national segment of the Internet‚Äù and the Decree of the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Belarus dated 29.04.2010 No. 644 ‚ÄúOn some issues of improving the use of the national segment of the global Internet network‚Äù information networks, systems and resources of the national segment of the Internet located on the territory of the Republic of Belarus are subject to registration in the State Register. But this is required only if activities are carried out using the website to sell goods, perform work, and provide services in the territory of the Republic of Belarus.

Therefore, the result was very surprising, in these lists I expected to see no more than 10% of resources with AAAA records, but it turned out that the average site owners turned out to be quite conscious.

Further analyzes will be more precise. And the resources in these analyzes are either state structures, or fall under Decree 60 and Resolution 644, that is, they must be placed in the Republic of Belarus, and can take advantage of the capabilities of the 350th decree.

The first one is based on a list of operatorswho issued licenses for activities in the field of communications. They are the providers of Internet services, and they certainly should use IPv6 without any orders. There are fewer positions in the sample than licensees, since some organizations simply do not have sites, some received several licenses, some have domains outside the BY zone, and others simply ceased to exist.

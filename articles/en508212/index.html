Cohort analysis in email marketing and other areas

The better you understand your customers, the more effective marketing strategies you use. To find out who is in the contact database, segmentation is often used: by gender, age, purchased goods. And this is good.



It is important not to forget that if gender is the only thing that unites people in a segment, then these are very different people, because segmentation on one basis does not take into account different stages of interaction with the company. For example, a new customer is not the same as a loyal customer, regardless of gender.



To assess the behavior of the client along his entire path of interaction with the company, a cohort analysis is used, which as a result will help:



  • personalize communications;
  • prevent customer churn;
  • Optimize your advertising costs.


What is cohort analysis and cohorts



Cohort is a group of users united by common features and a time interval. For example, users who register with the service in the same week. They performed the same action in one interval. It is the time distribution that distinguishes cohorts from segments.



Cohort analysis is one of the most popular analyzes in marketing and product analytics. It shows the change in key indicators over time in the context of each cohort. Thus, it becomes possible, depending on when the user took the action, to track the achievement of specific goals: conversion, ROI, CAC, LTV, Retention Rate - and understand the premises. For example, you see that users of the fourth week convert better than users of the first. And you know that in the third week you introduced onboarding. So, onboarding helps to better convert users.

Cohort Analysis in Email Marketing



Many companies use generalized statistics when evaluating email marketing: indicators of openness, clickability, unsubscribing for each month or for a separate letter. But such an assessment is not able to answer the question of who interacts with the newsletters and how. One option is to segment users. Good, but not enough, as both “new” and “old” subscribers, who behave differently, and their brand expectations differ, can fall into one segment. To obtain more detailed information, it is necessary to use cohort analysis.



Using it you can:



  • to track the dynamics of interaction between users who signed up at different times;
  • identify the most active cohorts;
  • test hypotheses in cohorts;
  • build more personalized communications;
  • determine the lifetime of subscribers;
  • start reactivation on time.


If you add additional segmentation to the cohort analysis, the information will be more detailed, and you can get much more useful insights.



I’ll show you an example of how to use the basic method of constructing a cohort analysis without additional segmentation. This is enough to understand the principle of construction.



An example of building a basic cohort analysis



Formulation of the problem



In this example, the purpose of the cohort analysis is to track the interaction of subscribers with newsletters based on Click Rate (number of clicks).



Parameter Definition



The analysis will be based on the following indicators.



  1. The sign of cohort formation is the date of subscription.
  2. The size of the cohort is a month.
  3. The time interval is from January 2018 to November 2019.
  4. The key indicator is CR.


Further work steps will depend on the parameter values.



Initial data



In Table 1 we collect information about subscribers:



  • Subscriber ID (email can be an ID);
  • date of subscription.






In Table 2 - information about interactions (one line - one action):



  • Subscriber ID
  • date of action;
  • mailing in which the action is committed.






The original table already displays a unique number of clicks. If your source data contains non-unique values, remove duplicate rows from the set before starting work. They are formed if a person, for example, double-clicked on one link, and since some platforms give all the data that they have, two identical rows will appear in the table - the difference will only be in the time of these events.



Work algorithm



  1. We group users by the date of subscription, taking into account the size of the cohort. We get the number of subscribers in each cohort.
  2. We combine table 2 with table 1, using as a key a common field - subscriber ID.
  3. We group the table obtained from point 2 into the fields “date of completion of action” and “date of subscription” and calculate the total number of clicks for such a pair.
  4. We calculate for each pair the value of the key indicator.


Results Analysis



The results are presented in the form of a matrix, where the rows are the cohorts, the columns are the months, the values ​​are the key indicator (CR).







For example, for users who registered in February 2018, the CR percentage in the same month was 2.11%, but in March 2018 they clicked much more actively - 14.20%.

Other applications



Cohort analysis is a universal method that allows you to analyze various information. Most often, cohort analysis is used for:



  • evaluation of the effectiveness of advertising channels;
  • evaluation of the results of A / B testing;
  • formation of a portrait of an ideal client;
  • optimization of the sales cycle in the SaaS business;
  • analysis of the effectiveness of mobile applications;
  • definitions of LTV.


Build Tools



The simplest



To build a cohort analysis, you can use Excel, Google Sheets and the like. The availability of these tools is a major advantage. Among the shortcomings, one can highlight the limited size of the source data, the inability to quickly recalculate when changing parameters, the lack of auto-update, and others.



Tools with Appropriate Functionality



Some advertising and analytical services have built-in functionality for building cohort analysis. The advantage of this method is that the user only needs to select the parameters, and the cohort analysis report will be quickly built.



The disadvantages of this approach include:



  • platform-limited set of parameters;
  • the need to use this system for data collection, which means for tracking analytics and for advertising.


Such systems include:



  • Google analytics
  • Yandex.Metrica (AppMetrica)
  • Adjust
  • Roistat
  • Kissmetrics
  • other




Universal tools



The most universal tools for building cohort analysis are platforms for business analysis, for example, Power BI. With their help, you can:



  • get data for analysis from any service
  • choose the options that interest you
  • set up auto-update.

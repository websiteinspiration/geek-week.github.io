Omnichannel in customer service: what to look for and how to do everything right?

The introduction of omnichannelism is the key to fastidious customers. We at CTI have been working with this topic for a long time and see how gradually it becomes a key strategy in marketing - according to our data, already three quarters of customers, if they want to interact with the brand, are ready to do this both online and offline.


Properly organized and maintained omnichannel interaction with customers is now a necessary basis for building a customer-oriented strategy that supports the creation of high-quality goods and services, which will allow businesses to survive in the era of the economy of emotions and a paradigm shift in customer service.
We tried to make a small memo on what and how to take into account, introducing omnichannel in our marketing processes.


Omni-Channel Implementation Approaches


Omni-channel marketing is aimed at collecting, remembering, understanding and using consumer behavior at all points of contact with the brand, regardless of the channel, whether online or offline. In fact, omnichannelity forms a competitive advantage of a company or brand in the eyes of consumers, which is expressed in the following:


  • access to information about the brand and its content in a form convenient for the client and channel;
  • the ability to receive personal and situationally interesting offers at any time and place;
  • prompt resolution of issues related to the purchase, delivery and installation;
  • clear, simple and effective technical and service support.

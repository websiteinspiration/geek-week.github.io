Life hack for bits

Good Zen, comrades.

I decided somehow to simplify my part of the musical life, namely, to automate the selection of samples for music (or, rather, for the “beats”).

The result I creatively decided to call a Kaleidophone. By analogy with a kaleidoscope, which creates endless new drawings from existing objects.


To get the result faster, simplify our music requirements.

We will build our track from three components:

  • Percussion
  • Bass
  • Melody

And we will compare the components in two ways:

  • Key
  • Pace

In general, with these "rules" the music will already be quite harmonious.


What do we need for everything to work? Sources - different samples for each of the three components. And a program that will source these randomly combine and show the result.

The algorithm is as follows:

  • Choose a random number from 50 to 180 (tempo; there may well be other boundaries, for good, you need to set the minimum and maximum rates of the available audio samples)
  • Next, select a random letter from A to G (tonality)
  • With a 40% probability, add to the letter # (finer tone adjustment)
  • 50% m (, — , — )
  • (, drums, bass melody)

Of the improvements planned and unplanned, it is quite possible to add a smarter pace choice (not random between A and B, but go through all folders and dynamically select frames). You can also use not three tracks, but more. On FX, on effects. You can generate MIDI tunes, feed them to “instruments”, apply some randomly selected filters and effects too - and as a result we get an almost endless procedural radio.

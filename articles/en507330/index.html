Ransomware - A New Way to Organize Data Leaks

Data leaks are a sore point for the security service. And now, when most work from home, the danger of leaks is much higher. That is why well-known cybercriminal groups pay increased attention to outdated and insufficiently protected remote access protocols. And, interestingly, more and more data leaks today are associated with Ransomware. How, why and how - read under the cut.







To begin with, the development and distribution of ransomware is a very profitable criminal business in itself. For example, the US FBI estimates that the Sodinokibi group earned approximately $ 1 million per month over the past year. And the attackers who used Ryuk received even more - at the beginning of the group's activities, their income amounted to $ 3 million per month. So it is not surprising that many directors for information security (CISO) report ransomware as one of the five main risks to business.

In the second half of May, 20% more ransomware programs were blocked worldwide than usual. After a slight decline now, in June, we are again seeing an increase in activity. And there are several reasons for this.



Get on the victim‚Äôs computer



Protection technologies are developing, and attackers have to change their tactics somewhat in order to get to a specific system. Ransomware targeted attacks continue to be spread by well-designed phishing emails (this is not without social engineering). Recently, however, malware developers have paid much attention to remote employees. To attack them, you can find poorly protected remote access services, such as RDP, or VPN servers with vulnerabilities.

This is what they are doing. Even dark ransomware-as-a-service services have appeared on the darknet that provide everything you need to attack the selected organization or person.



Attackers are looking for any way to penetrate the corporate network and expand the spectrum of attacks. So, attempts to infect networks of service providers have become a popular trend. As cloud services are only gaining popularity today, the infection of a popular service allows you to attack dozens or even hundreds of victims at once.



In the case of hacking web-based security management consoles or backups, attackers can disable protection, delete backups, and ensure that their malware is deployed throughout the organization. By the way, that is why experts recommend carefully protecting all service accounts using multi-factor authentication. For example, all Acronis cloud services allow you to set double protection, because if a password is compromised, attackers can nullify all the benefits of using an integrated cyber protection system.



Extend attack spectrum



When the cherished goal is reached, and the malware is already inside the corporate network, quite typical tactics are usually used for further distribution. Attackers study the situation and try to overcome the barriers that are created within the company to counter threats. This part of the attack can occur in manual mode (because if they have already hit the net, it means bait on the hook!). Well-known tools are used for this, such as PowerShell, WMI PsExec, as well as the newer Cobalt Strike emulator and other utilities. Some criminal groups intentionally attack password managers to penetrate deeper into the corporate network. And malware such as Ragnar was recently spotted in the completely closed image of the VirtualBox virtual machine,which helps to hide the presence of extraneous software on the machine.



Thus, getting into the corporate network, malware tries to check the user's access level and apply the stolen passwords. Utilities like Mimikatz and Bloodhound & Co. Help crack domain administrator accounts. And only when the attacker considers the distribution possibilities exhausted, the ransomware program is downloaded directly to the client systems.



Ransomware as a cover



Given the severity of the threat of data loss, every year more and more companies implement the so-called ‚ÄúDisaster recovery plan‚Äù. Because of this, they don‚Äôt really care about encrypted data, and in the event of an Ransomware attack they do not start collecting the ransom, but start the recovery process. But the attackers do not sleep. Under the guise of Ransomware, massive data theft occurs. The first to massively use such tactics began Maze back in 2019, although other groups periodically combined attacks. Now, at least Sodinokibi, Netfilm, Nemty, Netwalker, Ragnar, Psya, DoppelPaymer, CLOP, AKO and Sekhmet are engaged in data theft along with encryption.



Sometimes, attackers manage to pump out dozens of terabytes of data from the company that could be detected by network monitoring tools (if they were installed and configured). After all, most often data transfer is just using FTP, Putty, WinSCP or PowerShell scripts. To overcome DLP and network monitoring systems, data can be encrypted or sent as an archive with a password, and this is a new challenge for security services that need to check outgoing traffic for the presence of such files.



A study of the behavior of infostealers shows that attackers do not collect everything in a row - they are interested in financial reports, customer databases, personal data of employees and customers, contracts, records, legal documents. Malicious software scans disks for any information that could theoretically be used to blackmail.

If such an attack is successful, usually the attackers publish a small teaser, showing several documents in confirmation that the data has leaked from the organization. And some groups publish in general the entire data set on their website if the time for payment of the ransom has already passed. To avoid blocking and provide wide coverage, data is published, including on the TOR network.



Another way to monetize is by selling data. For example, Sodinokibi recently announced open auctions, in which the data goes to the highest bidder. The starting price of such trading is $ 50-100K, depending on the quality and content of the data. For example, a set of 10,000 records of cash flows, confidential business data, and scanned driver‚Äôs licenses were sold for as low as $ 100,000. And for $ 50,000 you could buy more than 20,000 financial documents plus three databases with accounting files and client data.



Sites that publish leaks are very different. It can be a simple page on which everything stolen is simply laid out, and there are also more complex structures with sections and the possibility of buying. But the main thing is that they all serve the same goal - to increase the chances of attackers to receive real money. If this business model shows good results for attackers, there is no doubt that there will be even more such sites, and the theft and monetization techniques for corporate data will be expanded further.

What to do with new attacks



The main challenge for security services under these conditions is that recently, more and more incidents related to Ransomware are simply a method of distracting from data theft. Attackers no longer rely solely on server encryption. On the contrary, the main goal is to organize a leak while you are struggling with cryptographers.



Thus, the use of a backup system alone, even with a good recovery plan, is not enough to counter multi-layer threats. No, of course, backups cannot be avoided either, because attackers will definitely try to encrypt something and ask for a ransom. It is more likely that now every attack using Ransomware should be considered as a reason for a comprehensive analysis of traffic and launch an investigation of a possible attack. And you should also think about additional security features that could:



  • Quickly detect attacks and analyze atypical network activity using AI
  • Recover systems instantly in the event of a zero-day Ransomware attack so you can monitor network activity

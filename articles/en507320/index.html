Software as a "bun": access to what services give their employees the company



In recent years, competition for technical personnel has seriously intensified. Salary itself has long ceased to be the only factor that candidates pay attention to. According to surveys, for 57% of them, the presence of additional bonuses (benefits) is very important in assessing the future place of work.

Beautiful offices, ping-pong tables and free IT lunches are taken for granted. In search of new ways to attract employees (especially engineers!), Companies begin to offer them free access to subscription services. I examined the existing proposals and compiled a list of such resources.

Savings Help: Branch and Even


According to statistics, 55% of US residents live from paycheck to paycheck - and this does not apply only to those who earn a little. There is also evidence that over 30% of employers associate lateness, absenteeism, delays in projects with the fact that employees have to earn extra money somewhere.

In order to help employees better plan their finances, some companies use services like Even and Branch. The principle of their work is similar - in Even the application connects to the company‚Äôs accounting department, and optional employees can allow access to their bank account, and in the case of Branch, an employee creates a virtual wallet to which the saved money is transferred.

Then they can set the percentage of salary that will be set aside for savings. In addition, if apps are allowed to analyze spending, they can show recommendations for reducing revenue - for example, through more careful subscription management.



Meditation Apps: Headspace and Calm


According to studies , more than half of all people say that they experience stress at work to one degree or another.

Also, for several years in the USA, applications for meditation and mindfulness have been actively developing. Today they are increasingly used in the corporate segment - companies realized that calmer and more peaceful employees work better and achieve better results, and the overall working environment is improving.

You can find many stories in the media about how companies give their employees premium access to the popular Headspace and Calm meditation applications.



Improving Written English: Grammarly and Linguix


In the period of mass remote work, companies are actively working to improve communication. Despite the surge in demand for video conferencing, email and text in general remain the main ways of working communication. Many companies buy Grammarly service licenses for their employees - it corrects errors and helps make the text more readable.

In addition, many company employees need not just error correction, but also language training. For example, this is true for engineers who work remotely for American / European companies or move to work in a new country. Companies that want such employees to quickly integrate into working communication and, over time, begin to write better, use the Linguix service. It not only shows errors, but also offers options for replacing words with synonyms and offers detailed descriptions of popular errors.



Business plans of such services allow companies to save - savings per license per month compared to the regular version for private customers is tens of percent.

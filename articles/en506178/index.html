Automation - The Future of Testing

Cyps predicts that ‚Äúthe future of software testing is almost fully automated test cases. Automation is great, but if you automate the wrong area, you will not run into problems, ‚Äùhe adds.

On the other hand, experts such as Yakel, Dunn, Ors and Honda believe that it is necessary to be careful in automation. According to Yakel, there are many views on translating testing into full automation, "but you must understand that not every thing you do can be automated." You still need manual interaction with people, especially users. A lot of unrealistic expectations are gathered around automation, but balance must be maintained.

Dunn notes that testers should be careful with excessive automation, citing traps that are easy to fall into. The first trap is that you can automate everything, and the second trap is that you supposedly do quality testing by writing a lot of automated tests. But excessive automation can actually reduce efficiency due to the high level of service. In turn, this can in turn lead to lower quality tests.

‚ÄúUsing smart tools to perform simple tasks will allow testers to spend more time and energy on more effective types of testing,‚Äù concludes Honda.

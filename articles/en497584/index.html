It all started with the Quantified self movement. His philosophy is that you can influence something only when you can measure it. Accordingly, you can improve your health if you monitor its characteristics. The basis was the number of steps per day and weight. Around this concept, Fitbit and Withings appeared, who were ready to offer breakthrough solutions for the QS audience.

Nikolai ‚ÄúCrazy Robots‚Äù Belousov bought Withings smart floor scales in 2010 to lose weight. Useful information when you are trying to get rid of adipose tissue seriously, and not from water and muscles. How does it even work


Picooc smart scales work on the same principle as Withings and Fitbit: the already mentioned bio-impedance analysis. Sensors are built into the weighing platform. When the user steps on them, the device passes an imperceptible electrical impulse through the feet. Tissue resistance is the basis for calculating the percentage of fat, lean mass and body water content.



The remaining indicators are displayed in the Picooc cloud. The cloud and the application for him are the pride of the company, researchers from the University of Chicago and Chinese nutritionists worked on its algorithms. The Picooc application is constantly evolving and adapting, and thanks to new users, its accuracy and functionality are growing. Big data is a serious business.



Picooc obtained a medical device license in China. According to independent tests, the measurement results are close to bio-impedance analysis performed in a clinical setting. In this case, the scales measure the composition of the lower body and the application extrapolates the results, which does not prevent the data from being relevant.

Now the scales and the Picooc app count more than 10 indicators. They are divided into current, ideal (based on gender, height, age and ethnicity) and control (how much you need to dial / reset):

  • Body weight (kg / ideal / control)
  • Muscle (% / kg / ideal / control)
  • Internal Fat Index
  • Basic metabolic rate (current / target / control)
  • Water in the body (%)
  • Protein (%)
  • Bone mass
  • Skeletal muscle (%)
  • Body mass index
  • Metabolic age
  • Lean body mass
  • Body rating
  • Body type



The same algorithms analyze the body and the changes occurring in it. The output is graphs with useful explanations and text tips. Libra will warn if a person continues to get fat after the holidays, and also recognize bad diets. They will praise in advance if the user began to grow muscles due to physical exertion, and are well versed in borderline situations. For the gadget, of course.



The app has a sports beta developed for athletes and athletes. It takes into account the difference between a regular and a trained body and allows you to quickly adjust your workouts to achieve results most effectively.



Picooc scales help you understand the tricks of a healthy lifestyle: analyze the body and give specific advice. The gadget does not replace a doctor; if you need expert opinion - contact a specialist. But in other cases, the tips from the application can and should be guided.

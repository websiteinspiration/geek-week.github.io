Using spreadsheets and computing software for Newsvendor modeling

Two widely used modeling tools in supply chain management are spreadsheets (usually Excel) and math programs (such as Matlab or Python). In this article, they are compared using the Newsvendor (news vendor) model as the basis for logistic models in inventory management. Simulation models are useful for finding approximate solutions when the problem is mathematically complex and does not provide an analytical solution.

The problem of newspaper


Newsvendor is one of the most widely used models in the science of inventory management. It well describes a situation of uncertain demand when too small an order leads to lost profits and deficits, and an excessive order is fraught with losses from markdowns and inventory costs. For example, if a newsboy buys few newspapers for resale, he will not only lose part of his income, but may cause dissatisfaction of his customers (which can be translated into money as a loss of future income). However, surplus newspapers at the end of the day will lead to the delivery of waste paper at a price below cost. The Newsvendor model, formerly known as the newsboy problem (Newsboy - the name was probably changed due to political correctness) is the basis of many theoretical works in the field of logistics for solving inventory management problems with variable demand.Although the model was originally intended for ‚Äúfashionable‚Äù products with a short life cycle (single-period model), Newsvendor was adapted to multi-period inventory management tasks and formed the basis of popular control methods such as order-up-to and reorder-point order-quantity (points of placement of a new order).

I made a comparison between spreadsheets and programming to determine the strengths and weaknesses of each tool in complex logistics models, of which Newsvendor is an example.

Simulation: why and how


The question of the modeling method is important for students and novice researchers in the field of logistics. Separate models were developed in Excel and Matlab - both are available for download .

Mathematical models in business may have a solution for simplified equations. For example, a predictable (deterministic) demand EOQ model can be easily used without simulation. Unfortunately, many realistic models in supply chain management are too complex to find an analytical solution. Numerical models are useful for modeling when the exact solution is too difficult to find: the system of equations is too complex. Simulation allows you to try different options to make sure that the approximate solution is reliable: stable in case of errors in the input data or changes in the environment. Newsvendor allows you to get a simple analytical solution for the optimal order size in its classic form, so a simulation model may not be required.However, many modifications of Newsvendor for one and several periods, which more closely correspond to the reality of the business, do not allow analytically obtaining solutions for an optimal order. The simulation method is popular in practice because it allows you to use a basic model, such as Newsvendor, with relative ease to find approximate solutions and demonstrate their reliability.



The Excel model (hereinafter referred to as the Excel), available as an additional file in this article , is based on a combination of methods described in the popular Newsvendor spreadsheet modeling tutorials. The advantage of Excel is the convenience of setting order ranges for novice users and observing how the expected profit chart responds to changes in key parameters. The model in Excel uses the built-in basic functions of Excel version 2007 and higher without the need for add-ons.

Newsvendor model algorithm also presentedfor Matlab (it can be used in open Octave). Matlab is chosen as a simplified programming language. Unlike Python, which requires the import of specialized libraries such as NumPy, Matlab has a large number of built-in functions that are ready to use and do not need additional configuration. Matlab code is perhaps the easiest to learn for beginners among programming languages, even compared to Python. Using Matlab can be faster for beginners compared to Python for many tasks. Moreover, Matlab is compatible with Octave open source, like Python. In my experience, it is easier to start using it for students with minimal programming skills.

The results show that Excel is clearly superior to other tools in terms of recalculation speed for a single simulation session. Moreover, spreadsheet models are more understandable for managers to understand and use, even if these models are acceptable for small and medium-sized enterprises only. However, for larger tasks, the spreadsheet becomes cumbersome (too large and slow) and it will be difficult to integrate into other IT / network systems, so mathematical software like Octave will become more convenient. For very large problems with hundreds of thousands of variables used by some companies, the use of specialized software becomes necessary.

Excel can be of much greater value as a workbook. In essence, a ‚Äúwhat if‚Äù analysis can be useful for studying Newsvendor while understanding the application of the modeling method at the university. For example, a lecturer may give instructions to change the demand parameter and ask students what will happen to the expected profit if demand volatility becomes lower in the next period. When studying Newsvendor, it is valuable to be able to change key parameters and immediately observe changes in optimal profit and order.

The results in this article are preliminary. In the comparison, we used the classic Newsvendor model with a normal distribution and a limited range of parameters for modeling. As an improvement, a more comprehensive comparison with multi-period models, different distribution of demand, a wide range of input data for further verification and expansion of the results would be desirable.

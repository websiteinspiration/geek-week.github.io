Features of remote work - based on internal meeting

In our experience, remote workers with the most diverse living conditions face exactly the same problems - the body gets tired of sitting in the same position, their relatives do not understand them, children or pets are constantly distracting, and the background for all this is a strange feeling of loneliness (although it would seem where he comes from in isolation with his family).

Let's figure out how to handle this all.

I focus on the fact that in this post we are talking about full-fledged remote work, and not about freelance. However, many tips can also be useful to freelancers.

The principle of building a work schedule


The first thing to do on a remote site is to decide on a schedule.

The schedule usually depends on the work team, so it makes no sense to talk about particulars. It is important that the schedule must be spoken out at the start, choosing an interaction scheme that suits everyone. It‚Äôs convenient for someone to start at 10 am Moscow time, someone in Siberia would like to get out 4 hours earlier, and for someone specifics require certain hours of presence online. You can almost always come to a comfortable compromise.

It is more convenient when the schedule is stable. This helps to focus on business on time.
It is customary for our team to indicate hours of work and lunch in Slack status, setting ‚Äúaway‚Äù and warning if it is necessary to move away at this time ( we already wrote about other principles of internal communications ).

In distant work, force majeure happens when you turn off the electricity or the Internet. In this case, we also have internal regulations: it is necessary by phone or SMS to notify the one who can report the problem to the whole team - the operational or technical director.

Tools


From the point of view of tools for remote work, a laptop and stable Internet are enough. Is it worth it to confine yourself to a laptop - a question of ergonomics. It is more convenient to have either an external keyboard or an additional monitor (or maybe both).

As for the Internet, it is better to have some kind of fallback. This is a standard risk for remote work. Few of the Maksiltek residents keep a redundant wired Internet channel at home, but many have routers for connecting via a mobile network (with a SIM card and a tariff with a traffic margin - recently, operators have begun to prevent simple sharing of the Internet from a smartphone to a laptop). Some have a kind of "plan B" - at least until recently. Not far from the place of permanent work, an office or coworking was explored, where you can quickly move in an emergency in 20-30 minutes. Unfortunately, now such a safety net is not possible in all cities.

Workplace


Most of the Maksilektovets work from home - an apartment or a separate residential building. Not everyone has the conditions to set aside a separate office, but we focus all beginners on the fact that the workspace is important. If at home people will constantly walk by you, ask about something, sensible work will not work.

Under normal conditions, if the work does not work out at home, we recommend looking in the direction of a rented office or coworking. But given the circumstances, we give a few examples of how our colleagues solve this problem without leaving home:

  • The head of the testing department works on the balcony, since the region of his residence allows him to occupy this space even in winter. So you can concentrate behind a closed door, despite a small child.
  • One of our front-end workers moves around the apartment after the sun - in the morning he works from a room facing the east, and towards the evening he moves to the kitchen, whose windows face west. This way of ‚Äúchanging the scenery‚Äù is suitable for those who find it difficult to work and live in one location.
  • Until recently, the host of the blog on Habr√© supported two jobs at home in order to move from corner to corner in accordance with the routine of family life. When children sleep during the day, you can work from the kitchen, and when awake, from the workplace in the bedroom.

The workplace should be organized so that you are comfortable and comfortable, otherwise productivity suffers. We already wrote about how to technically organize this convenience .

Which is safer - PIN Online or PIN Offline?

With the advent of microprocessor payment cards on the market, along with a method that has been well known for a long time to verify the PIN Online card holder , when the PIN value is verified by the card issuer on its host, the PIN Offline method has been widely used .







The essence of the PIN Offline method is that the card issuer delegates the verification of the PIN to his card. The card itself checks the value of the PIN entered by the user on the terminal device, comparing it with the reference value, securely stored in the payment application of the card.

Despite the fact that both verification methods have been used in parallel for 15 years now, sometimes you still hear questions: which method provides higher security when processing operations - PIN Online or PIN Offline? And in general, is it possible for the issuer (the bank that issued the card) to do with only one of the indicated methods for checking IDUs? For example, using the PIN Online method. Obviously, from the issuer's point of view, this method is simpler than the PIN Offline method when implementing card personalization procedures, changing the PIN by the card holder, controlling the limit on the number of attempts to enter incorrect PIN values, since in this case the above procedures are performed only on the issuer's host and do not require additional actions on the side of the card payment application.

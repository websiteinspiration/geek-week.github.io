Analysis and design are two things: identifying the problem that we are trying to solve, and evaluating our proposed solution in terms of whether it solves this problem or not. It really is what these things are, not something else.

We must solve problems. We should not cut features. Solve the problem! There is nothing about features ... What is a feature? A feature is just an attribute of something. This is a shiny chrome handle on something. This is not the purpose of the machine.

There is no guarantee if you make a list of features, even if it is received from the client, that he will solve their own problem, or that he will solve any problem, or that the features, when you combine them, will not create a ton of other problems. Thus, programming and writing code does not boil down to a list of features.

In particular, features offered by users, despite all their efforts to satisfy themselves, are often not good ideas. You have to get to the bottom of the matter and find out what their problem is, and how best to solve it, and then reconcile it with what they ask.

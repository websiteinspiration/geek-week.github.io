SpatialChat parties became popular during the pandemic - and they are unlikely to end after cove

I am an absolute homebody, sociophobe and a hater of company parties. The same I would have thrown everything now and burst into the most huge noisy crowd that I could find. You really begin to appreciate things only when you are deprived of them. Strange year, very strange.

Covid made us play live chat, and even the fact that we have been trying to reinvent social networks over the past 20 years seems to have not worked out to the end. Something is missing in the correspondence, video calling is quickly tiring, it is already sick of the tape of friends with cats.

But in the spring of this year, an unusual thing spawned - SpatialChat. This is a virtual space where dozens of people go through video communications, and can literally move inside as they want. To approach one people, to another, to accumulate in compasses, to watch vidos together - like for real. The space can be arbitrarily divided into tables, dance floors, bar counters, toilets, smoking rooms - anything. It seems that in no virtual party there have been so many triggers that cause a feeling of communication offline.

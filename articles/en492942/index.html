Ontol (= most useful) about remote work [compilation of 100+ articles]

“If you are not ashamed of the first version of the product, you entered the market too late.”



Hello everyone, I pulled for a long time, and so I decided to put not even an MVP, but an idea that I am currently working on. It crystallized a year ago, following the results of 7 years of writing on Habré.

Many have heard about the pyramid "data-information-knowledge-wisdom".
It is interesting that the number of projects about data is several million, about information - hundreds of thousands, about knowledge - hundreds, but in a good way, tens, about wisdom - I have not met a single one.

For a year I look at from 10,000 to 50,000 texts in Russian and English. Texts from the category of "wisdom" are sometimes found on Habré and in the blog of Paul Graham, and to systematically capture (and later create) "black swans" among the texts, I came up with the project " Ontol.org ". Now we are openly sawing a prototype of MVP and in parallel I am doing some work manually. Who cares - register on Ontol.org , as soon as we roll out the MVP, we invite you to a closed beta.

Ideally, Ontol is a selection of the top 10 coolest materials on a super important topic (multidimensionally ranked by the community and experts), so that in 1 hour a person can get acquainted with the most useful materials of mankind on this topic. (+ top100 longlist of less important materials for further study)

In the meantime, a very crude selection of the most useful materials about remote work on Habré ( UPD+ vc.ru). (anyone who has links to English-language materials or from other resources - write in the comments.)

Now the selection is raw (done in 3 hours, 200 posts viewed). Ideally, Ontol on each topic should be done from 10 to 100 hours (view from 2,000 to 50,000 posts) and upgrade for a lifetime :).

Here's what it will look like in the end (without interactivity / mechanic for now): ontol.org/magisterludi/remote

At the moment, ranking by Habr rating (but this is a very primitive approximation), very soon we will screw the multidimensional social / crowdsourcing ranking method so that Awesome exactly "get" into the request. You can help to rank by indicating your top 5 most interesting articles in the comments.

How to choose a UPS

I am glad to see everyone and want to share my observations and experience in the field of UPS.

What is a UPS, we all know. For what they are needed, it seems to be also clear (in any case, I want to believe in it).

And so the hour came when we need to buy a UPS, and here the headache begins.

Which to choose?
What to look at?

  • Brand Name?
  • A manufacturer with a century of history?
  • Number of installations?
  • Cost?
  • Does a colleague have the same?



Let's get it right.

Brand fame


At the word UPS, we immediately recall the company APC, this abbreviation is strongly associated with UPS, especially in Russia. And really, who does not know this?



Or the Legrand company, among other things, founded in 1860. And APC was founded in 1981. APC launched its first UPS in 1984, which it developed after the cessation of state funding for solar energy development. The Legrand company released its UPS after 2010, and that is because in 2010 it bought the MetaSystem company.

The APC company fellows - certainly, only until 2007 APC company did not let out 3-phase powerful UPSs. Moreover, APC has never released a 3-phase UPS. In 2006, APC was acquired by Schneider Electric, an equally respected company founded in France in 1836. At the beginning of its journey, the company was called Schneider and exclusively engaged in the production of weapons for the French army and for export to the flesh until the end of World War II. Only in the post-war period was it reoriented towards the electrical market, adding Electric to the name. (an interesting fact: Many artillery pieces of the Russian Empire were purchased from Schneider)

So, Schneider Electric acquired APC in 2006, with the firm intention to cross efforts with MGE, which was also successfully acquired in 2007 - thereby firmly occupying a niche in the market. But the European Antimonopoly Committee did not approve the deal (at least something the Europeans did not approve of the topic) and split the MGE company between two buyers. The 3-phase direction went to Schneider Electric and was integrated into the APC by Schneider Electric product line, and the EATON (founded in 1911 in the USA and originally specializing in the production of automobile axles for trucks) took over the single-phase direction, which also took advantage of it and bought Powerware, teamed up shortly before with Best Power, with their powerful 3-phase systems.

I really like the history of companies, I can talk about it for a long time and selflessly, sorting through the bright pages of the history of the brands PowerCom, CE + T, Riello, Socomec, Newave - sold at ABB, Delta and Huawei are a little aloof - their story needs to be told separately, without forgetting Kehua.

Let's finish with the story and summarize.

It is worth noting 2 of our possible selection factors:

  • Brand fame
  • A manufacturer with 100 years of history

Because it is absolutely not important! The UPS itself, in the form we are used to seeing them, appeared quite recently the end of the 90s and the beginning of the 2000s and for this small period of time. And during this short period of time, almost everyone in the market managed to resell and re-buy each other. Fundamentally not developing a technology, but only integrating it into its product line.

Did UPS beat before the 80s? Yes there were, only they were not called that. They were mechanical as well known as 3-machine converters and they are more likely the progenitors of modern diesel dynamic UPSs. In any case, the principles are the same. Therefore, the statements of the DDIBP manufacturers about the uniqueness and innovativeness of their developments are not completely true.

We pass to the following.

Number of installations


The number of installations - a good factor, it shows confidence in the equipment (as the manufacturers explain to us) I'll tell you honestly - IT DOES NOT SHOW ANYTHING, except for the successful work of the sales department, but you can always look at what you buy in the natural habitat, you can even listen to the local operation service about how this machine works seamlessly at their facility - curious, but no more. Because you are dragged into the subjective side of the assessment and the subjective way of choosing.

Cost


I will say succinctly - one of the most important factors, but it has nothing to do with the price of the purchased equipment, which is announced to you by the seller.

So what to rely on?

Where to look?



At one time, I found myself in this situation more than once. And each decision was like a sapper‚Äôs action on a minefield. But in data centers it is not customary to do so. The principle of "Dementia and courage" does not bring results in the long run.

Thinking about this, I came to a very simple conclusion - an ideal UPS does not exist and a good UPS does not exist either. They are all the same! (this is not a literal definition, all the same, the development departments are not in vain eating their bread and red caviar from vendors) You just need to choose the UPS that suits you right here and now.

And then what to be guided by, you ask?

Now I will try to bring you to this.

Recently I studied at the Uptime Institute and there was one phrase in the prologue to the course:



‚ÄúStarting the path, you must clearly understand where you want to be‚Äù

I was amazed at the accuracy and capacity of this phrase, which gives a methodology for solving all possible problems.

Returning to the UPS, what is important for us, as for future owners of this beautiful fake? As little as possible to experience problems with our UPS.

And the first thing to look at is the service.. Is a specific UPS service available in your area, how many companies can do it, how much it costs. How many specialists on specific equipment in the area where our UPS will be. Does that Service Partner have enough qualified engineers with whom you plan to contract?

Example:

One famous Italian brand, or rather, Russian OEM of a famous Italian brand. A good price for a UPS, many people sell it, a lot of installations, including those in St. Petersburg, but there are no engineers who can repair it, really take it apart, find the fault and fix it. And then adjust correctly and optimally for specific conditions and place of work after the "specialists" in Poland.

I suffered a lot, sorting through service companies, I was angry and spoke in every possible way negatively about this Russian OEM of the Italian brand, about the Italian brand itself and about all those involved in it. But I found a service partner who had all the necessary competencies and a sufficient number of engineers, which allowed me to completely close the problems with this UPS. Now I think these UPSs are completely reliable, but my opinion is not important. I might not find this service partner and would continue to be indignant. But first things first.

The second thing you need to pay attention to is spare parts and consumables . Namely, the cost, availability, condition of sale of delivery. It is very important!

Example:

One famous Chinese brand. He supplied me with his UPS. New UPS model. The first copy imported into Russia. NDP ends, the car surrenders and is idling for the weekend. On Monday, adjusters arrive at the car, which turned off with an error and abandoned the exit. After a couple of phone calls and 14 hours of waiting, the volume of spare parts arrives, sufficient to collect another 2 UPS. Comes immediately to the object.

You will say that this is not an honest example, such as the guarantee of a vendor in the process of commissioning or carrier (we found out later when we considered who owed how much and to whom) but I do not agree with you. As an illustration, another case with a NDP of a famous Finnish brand (at that time Finnish) spare parts, namely motherboards for one UPS, waited about a month.

Urgent purchase of component boards that are not included in the list of consumables of the respected Swiss division of the American brand may not be completed in 2 months. Tested by personal example.

From here, we smoothly proceed to the following - this is the presence of a brand representation in the country, the availability of representation, an understandable and transparent guarantee policy. After all, it is the representation of the brand or vendor (how anyone likes it) that will be your arbiter, your knowledge base, your training center, your technical support and your guide to the world of the brand.

Example:

The first described example with an Italian brand. It so happened that this brand does not have a representative office in Russia, which is why it is necessary to solve a lot of issues with their equipment in a very strange way. Which might not have arisen if it were in Russia. Including with the service. Because one of the most important functions of a representative office is to roll out the company's technical policy, including for service programs.



To summarize, I want to draw your close attention to answering these simple questions about UPS. And making sure that all these questions received answers that suit you. You can say for sure that you will not have problems with specific equipment. More precisely, they will be and a lot, but you can always solve them quickly and efficiently!

Pay attention to it, choose the equipment wisely!

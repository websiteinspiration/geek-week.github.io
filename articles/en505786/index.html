5 classic art techniques to create a good presentation

We consider painting and architecture techniques that will help improve presentation design.


We are used to looking for inspiration in cases on Behance and Dribbble. But you can be inspired not only by the work of contemporaries. Design developed under the influence of art, so life hacks from Da Vinci or Klimt will be no less useful.

How is design different from art? It serves applied tasks: to attract attention and convey information to people.

How is design similar to art? It uses the visual channel of perception. Even when creating a presentation, you need to understand how color, shape and composition work.

The masterpieces of world art are the best helpers in this, and we decided to tell you about the rules of painting and architecture that are useful to the designer.

Tectonics: making a clear structure


Structural clarity is an important principle of modern design, which was first formulated by the ancient Greeks. They strove for order in everything. With a cursory glance at the ancient temple, it is immediately clear how it is built and what parts it consists of. This property is called tectonics.



Tectonics is a distinctive principle of ancient Greek architecture, which is also loved by many modern architects. Due to the clarity of the construction, the facade looks reliable and stable. Also, such buildings are convenient for people: their shape, inputs and outputs are understandable and predictable.

Visual communications are often compared with architecture. Presentation, as a building, must be strong, convincing and consistent.

The basis of tectonics is the division of the whole into parts . Each facade element is clearly visible. A good presentation is the same. There are groups of slides of the same subject, there is each separate slide and information blocks located on this slide. The longer the narrative, the more structural units are required.

A good presentation, like a Greek building, is predictable . In European culture, we are used to reading from top to bottom and from left to right - this is how the viewer's eyes will slide on the slide with the text. It is necessary that the blocks of information be consistent and obey a single logic, and not hang randomly on the screen.

Finally, the functionality of the parts is important.. In the Greek temple, every little thing in its place. And accents play an important role in design. Markers, icons and frames help to attract the viewer's attention or show the connection of one element with another.

Grisaille: looking for tonal contrasts


If there is a lot of information, the slides will be complex in structure. To avoid confusion, it is important to observe tonal contrast: the ratio of dark and light spots on the screen. Artists from the Middle Ages for this purpose made monochrome sketches - this technique is called grisaille.

Grisaille helps to identify the main tonal contrasts in order to preserve them in a full color image.



It is the tone that helps us distinguish the boundaries of objects . We understand without problems what is depicted in black and white paintings or photographs. Color determines the mood, but it is secondary in the perception of figures in space.

Often web designers start work with black and white prototypes. This may be relevant when creating presentations to avoid mistakes.

Sfumato: emphasize the unity of the elements


We talked about the division of information into structural units, but the relationship of the elements with each other is equally important: especially in cases where it is necessary to reflect the corporate identity of the company.

Sfumato is a pictorial technique, which consists in softening the contours in order to show a single air environment. That is how Leonardo Da Vinci painted his paintings. Smooth color transitions create a sense of community of space and, therefore, harmony.



There must be unity in the design of presentations. The most obvious sfumato analogy in design is gradients.


Alt: Color difference helps visually separate text blocks. Soft transitions make the slide homogeneous and harmonious.

Of course, the designer is not alive with gradients. Let's look at the sfumato idea more broadly: the purpose of this technique isachieve visual unity . To do this, select fonts, color combinations and graphic techniques that are suitable for each other.

Negative technique: we attach importance to the background


When creating a presentation, we move the headers, blocks of text, photos and icons across the screen. Background is a relatively static thing that is easy to forget about. But even sections of the slide where there is no content affect the overall impression.

Negative technique is the term watercolor painters. Due to the lack of white paint, they are forced to leave light objects intact and use white sections of the sheet as full parts of the composition. For example, like white flowers in the following picture:



Areas on a piece of paper or on the screen where there are no images are called negative space. Usually they are perceived on the slides as the background, over which the figures are superimposed: photographs, pictures and blocks of text. But to make the visual accompaniment more interesting, you can, imitating watercolors, turn parts of the background into shapes. This is a popular technique in corporate identity and advertising design that works well in visual communications.


Alt: On this slide, a piece of white background is brought to the forefront and overlaps part of the female face. This makes the photo more interesting, attracts increased attention to it.

Negative space allows you to add creativity to the design, while remaining concise - for presentations, this is a valuable trick.

Of course, the background does not always turn into figures: most often it remains behind. But at the same time, the background is always part of the composition , because an empty spot has its own tone, color, size and shape. We work with negative space without even realizing it when we create any design.


Alt: We see a clear color picture thanks to the white background. In addition, white background triangles connect the composition: without them, the text just hung in the air.

In most cases, the design looks good when the background sections are relatively evenly distributed between the shapes. The presentation needs places where the viewer will concentrate, and empty areas where the view will rest. If the voids are too small, the slide will seem crowded, it will be difficult to navigate in the information. If the voids are large, the eye falls into these holes, and the viewer is distracted from the content.

Ornamentality: taking care of beauty


Since ancient times, people have sought to surround themselves with beauty: it is enough to recall any decorative and applied art. Different peoples had their own ornaments - simple repeating patterns that are relatively easy to reproduce on any media. In the Art Nouveau era, the fusion of classical art with decorative art began, and ornamentalism is especially characteristic of this trend.

Visual communications value minimalism and functionality. But presentations without beautiful details will make people bored. Designers are looking for a middle ground and often use the principle of ornamentality - a repetition of simple decorative elements .

Repeating frames, shapes or markers set the desired mood, but do not attract attention. The logic of the ornament is simple and understandable, so that the viewer will think about the content of the slide without being distracted by artistic delights.


Alt: The left side of the presentation is based on repeating squares overlapping each other. These elements set the mood for the slide, but do not conflict with the text - due to simple repeating forms

So, the principle of ornamentalism teaches us that templates with simple decorative elements that will be reproduced many times are good for presentations. They help revitalize the text without scattering the viewer's attention.

findings


  1. Before you begin, imagine yourself an architect. Come up with a clear presentation design and divide the information into structural units.
  2. Make a monochrome sketch of the slides - in a graphics editor, on a piece of paper, or at least mentally. Highlight blocks of information using contrasts of light and dark.
  3. Create a single visual environment to link elements on a slide with each other. Smooth transitions, the unity of the color scheme or graphic techniques can help with this.
  4. Remember that it is important not only the location of the pictures and text in relation to each other, but also the ratio of voids between them. Use the background as part of the composition.
  5. People hardly perceive dry information. A good presentation is emotional. Use an ornament to design your slides - a simple pattern that will be repeated throughout the presentation.

How did it happen? Here we turn briefly to theory. In accordance with Shannon's theorem, the access point's bandwidth is determined by (a) the number of spatial streams, (b) the bandwidth and ¬© the signal-to-noise ratio. In Huawei, modifications in comparison with previous products were made on all three points. So, our TDs are able to form up to 12 spatial streams - one and a half times more than the top models of other vendors. In addition, they can support eight spatial streams with a width of 160 MHz versus, at best, eight streams of 80 MHz with competitors. Finally, thanks to Smart Antenna technology, our access points demonstrate significantly greater tolerance to interference and a higher level of RSSI at the client‚Äôs reception.



At the end of 2019, our colleagues from Tel Aviv received the highest award within the company precisely because they managed to achieve a signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) higher than that of another well-known American manufacturer on a Wi-Fi 802.11ax-enabled chip. The result was achieved both through the use of new materials, and with the help of a more advanced algorithmic base, wired into the processor. Hence the other advantages of Wi-Fi 6 "in the interpretation of Huawei." In particular, a multi-user MIMO mechanism has been implemented, thanks to which up to eight spatial streams can be allocated per user; MU-MIMO is designed to use the entire antenna resource of the access point in transmitting information to clients. Of course, eight threads at once will not be shipped under any smartphone,but for a laptop of the latest generation or a VR-complex for industrial use - completely.







Thus, with 16 spatial streams at the physical level, it is possible to take the 10 Gbit / s bar per point. At the level of application traffic, the efficiency of the data transmission medium will be 78‚Äì80%, or about 8 Gb / s. Let us make a reservation, this is true in the case of the operation of 160 MHz channels. Of course, Wi-Fi 6 is designed first and foremost for mass connections, and if there are dozens of them, then each separately taken will not be so sky-high speed.

Full optical slice











Increasingly, we are building campuses according to the new model - IP + POL , and do not obey the dictates of the whims of the technological fashion. The previously prevailing approach, in which, deploying the network infrastructure in the building, we pulled the optics to the floor, and then did the wiring with copper, imposed severe restrictions on the architecture. It is enough that, if necessary, an upgrade had to change almost the entire environment at the floor level. The material itself, copper, is also not ideal: both in terms of throughput, and in terms of life cycle, and in terms of further development of the environment. Of course, copper was understandable to everyone and made it possible to create simple network solutions quickly and inexpensively. However, in 2020, copper loses to optics in terms of total cost of ownership and potential to upgrade the network.



The superiority of optics is especially pronounced when it is necessary to lay on the long life cycle of the infrastructure (and evaluate the costs of it for a long time), as well as when a serious evolution awaits it. For example, it is required that 4K cameras and 8K TVs or other high-resolution digital signage are constantly functioning in the environment. In such situations, the most reasonable solution would be to use a fully optical - using optical switches - network. Previously, a small number of end terminals - optical network units (ONU) - served as a stop factor when opting for such a campus building model. Currently, not only user machines assume the ability to connect via terminals to the optical network. A transceiver that works with the POL network is inserted into the same Wi-Fi point,and we get wireless service through a high-speed optical network.



Thus, it is possible to fully implement Wi-Fi 6 with little blood: to establish an IP + POL network, connect Wi-Fi to it and quietly increase productivity. The only thing, in the case of Wi-Fi-points, local power is required. Otherwise, nothing prevents us from bringing the network to 10 or 50 Gb / s.







Deployment of fully optical networks is advisable in a variety of cases. For example, it is difficult for them to imagine an alternative in old houses with long spans. If you have never rebuilt a building in the center of Moscow, then, believe me, you are very lucky: usually all the cable passages in such buildings are clogged, and sometimes you have to do everything from scratch to organize a local network in your mind. In the case of the POL solution, you can lay the optical cable, separate it with splitters and create a modern network.



The same applies to educational institutions with buildings of old architecture, hotel complexes and huge buildings, including airports.

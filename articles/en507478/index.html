11 reasons that your site is poor

Now every self-respecting company should have its own website. Sometimes this boils down to absurdity: a network of two or three car washes within the same city buys a domain, finds designers and programmers, and cuts a personal landing page. Amid all this, the number of pseudo-designers and pseudo-programmers is growing, who, after the first more or less beautiful project, run on freelance and create sites that look, to put it mildly, not very.



This article is a kind of memo for managers, with which you can either monitor the work of already hired designers and programmers, or understand that the site needs to be updated. It is worth reading to novice draftsmen and coders.



1. The main page is much more beautiful than others



This means that the site was created to get out and the main budget was poured into the main page. In this case, it is better to either land the main page to the level of the others (this will be a stupid decision), or invest a little more and finish the other sections.



Agree, is it inconvenient after opening a minimalistic main page to go to the page "Examples of work" or "About us" with the design of the dinosaur era?



2. The style of the site does not correspond to the scope of the company



Wavy squiggles, flowing animations, moving figures - all this, of course, is beautiful, but not for a construction company or a center for scientific research.



It is important to preserve the features of your chosen industry: somewhere, you need to emphasize stability and calmness, somewhere - dynamism and modernity.

3. Wrong colors







Red is not suitable for the consent button, and green is clearly inappropriate for warning. We think this is obvious, but sometimes such errors occur.



4. Lots of interactive pop-ups







Interactive is beautiful and progressive, but in moderation. And pop-ups are still crap that no user likes. It is better to replace them with blocks in the structure of the site. A minimalistic and modern logo will certainly distinguish the company from competitors. But you need to understand that the site should be easy to use and maximize the style of not only the logo, but also the company itself. Therefore, after updating the logo, it is better to do a redesign of the site.

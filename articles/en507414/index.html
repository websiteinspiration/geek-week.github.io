Technical documentation in software development: who, why, when and how describes the project





Hello! My name is Dasha Grigoryeva, I am a technical writer at 65apps. We are developing complex mobile solutions, and my task is to prepare technical documentation for projects. Very often, the role of a technical writer is underestimated in a company (not ours, of course). Therefore, I want to tell you why the developer company needs such a specialist, what is a technical project, how does it differ from TK and why is it all necessary for the development of a mobile application.



Communicating with a large number of companies, I still meet with the firm conviction that writing technical specifications for software development is a waste of time and money. We at 65apps think differently. Therefore, I will give a few arguments in favor of the technical documentation and tell you who writes it and how.



Technical documentation allows you to assess the cost of development and agree on the functionality of the future system. In the event of a dispute about the cost and timing of the development of a particular feature, it can become a certain guarantee for the customer. On the other hand, if there is a need for the development of the application, the documentation will facilitate the refinement process and give a clear understanding of whether it is possible to integrate new functionality into the existing system.



Another example is government organizations or organizations whose activities are limited or subject to laws and supervisory authorities. They are obliged to carry out software development in accordance with all the rules and in compliance with all standards. In such projects, technical documentation prepared in accordance with GOSTs is a necessary condition.

And of course, well-written and up-to-date documentation is necessary so that each participant in the development process can access documents if questions arise regarding a specific task or the entire system as a whole.

Typically, the development of TK is the analyst. It is he who communicates with the customer / interested party and identifies his requirements. In difficult cases, a project manager, developers, testers and other specialists can be involved in the process. The more complex the project, the more documentation requirements. Serious customers from large corporations and the public sector require the preparation of documentation in accordance with GOSTs, and this task is very time-consuming, requiring attention to details and constant involvement in the process. And this is not only TK, but also a technical project that fully describes all the solutions, approaches and methodologies used in the development. GOSTs also apply to it. The preparation of such documentation should be carried out by a specialist - technical writer.



For large projects, having a technical writer on staff is a must. Development of solutions for large customers can last a year or more, this is a fairly long time. During this time, there are a sufficient number of changes that provoke documentation updates. In addition, any long-term software development involves development. In this case, the current technical documentation will reduce the risks laid down in the work of performers.



An exception may be made for representatives of the public sector. It is not necessary for such organizations to have in their staff specialists of the appropriate profile, since when the need arises, you can always turn to professionals who will perform their work qualitatively.



Who is a technical writer



There is no strictly formulated list of duties of a technical writer: each company forms it itself, and sometimes entrusts such a specialist with additional tasks. Therefore, it is sometimes difficult to even determine the type of activity of a technical writer. In our company, such a specialist draws up the documentation necessary for further software development.



For this, he collects information and materials from project participants and documents these data according to customer requirements, including in accordance with GOSTs. We are talking about GOST 19 "Unified system of program documentation" and GOST 34 "Information technology. Set of standards for automated systems". Also, a technical writer monitors the relevance of technical information, if necessary, on long and complex projects.



What technical documentation does the developer need



Consider the process of system development ideal from the point of view of GOST.

It all starts with the requirements of the customer or interested parties, which must be identified and must be recorded in the ‚ÄúTerms of Reference‚Äù document.



The terms of reference is a document regulating business goals, a general description of the system, the amount of work, the boundaries of the project, as well as the procedure for development, evaluation and acceptance. This document answers the question ‚Äúwhat needs to be done?‚Äù and is actually a statement of the problem.



The analyst draws up the TOR and coordinates it with all interested parties. After all the requirements are assembled and approved, you can begin to prototype the future system and develop software.



At this stage, the development of layouts, scenarios, architecture, etc. begins. Since we are talking about the reference development process, all this should be described in the technical design, which also uses some of the information from the TOR.



A technical project is a collection of documents that describe and justify all approaches, methods, architectural and technical solutions used to create a system. For example, a technical project includes interface mock-ups, description of protocols for integration with related systems and equipment, user scripts, description of the algorithm and their formation, structure of servers and databases, as well as other requirements for the system and its interaction with other external systems.

that's not all: there are many standards for writing technical documentation, and for each country they have their own. In domestic standards, there is a separate GOST for the creation of automated work and for a software product. For example, a technical project in accordance with GOST 19 ‚ÄúUnified system of program documentation‚Äù may include the following list of works:



  • clarification of the structure of input and output data,
  • development of an algorithm for solving the problem,
  • determination of the presentation of input and output data,
  • definition of semantics and syntax of a language,
  • program structure development,
  • final definition of hardware configuration,
  • development of an action plan for the development and implementation of programs,
  • development of an explanatory note,
  • coordination and approval of the technical design.


Accordingly, the technical design answers the question ‚Äúhow to do it?‚Äù and it is he who is the technical writer.



Now that you have a clear understanding of the architecture, functionality and design of the project, you can proceed to the development of the system and its testing.



At the testing stage, in addition to checking the system‚Äôs operability, it also checks that all the requirements recorded in the documents are fulfilled, which allows to resolve disputes with the customer.



When to compile technical documentation



Above, I described the ideal process of creating software, but sometimes some documents of a technical project are made up after the development stage.



There are projects in which it is important to have complete documentation before starting work. This applies to solutions with high security requirements, compliance with legislation, etc. For example, mobile applications for banks. In this case, it is important to first think over all the details of the system (information security of customers and the bank itself, compliance with laws). This will take more time, but will avoid financial and reputational risks.



If the development follows the Agile methodology, then it makes no sense to prescribe all the documentation before starting work. If it is important for the customer to work on sprints and control the development process, the documentation can be added in parallel with the main process.

In our company, both options are possible - we can adapt to the conditions and wishes of the customer.



Today, a technical writer is not the most common specialty, but for a serious developer company, such an employee is no less important than, for example, an analyst.

Technical documentation is necessarily developed for state customers, it is also necessary for long-term projects, on which contractors are more likely to change. It makes sense to create a technical project for the know-how of technologies and projects of high complexity - the QA department will need documentation to distinguish between bugs and features.

Comparison of cold calls and correspondence: How to get 221 sales per month using one of the methods

I would like to share my case, where we were able to compare the results from two close methods: “cold” calls and correspondence. We will tell you how we achieved automation of the process, what “pitfalls” we encountered, and what conclusions we came to based on the numbers. The most valuable experience with which we share with you.

Let’s explain right away. Given the situation amid a pandemic in the world, many businesses are faced with a lack of customers. And we were no exception. For a law firm specializing in the registration of trademarks around the world, the main audience is the United States and Germany. Our customers are people who trade on Amazon and pay attention to protecting their products from copying.



And at some point, this entire audience simply began to disappear from our field of vision. Applications were terminated. Sales fell. What to do? We must again arm ourselves with schemes to attract leads. We decided to start with cold calls.

What problems have you encountered since the start with “cold” calls and correspondence?




We will not stretch, but let’s say right away that all the known disadvantages of the first method made themselves felt during the week of testing.

Problems:

  • Base of numbers

Finding a truly decent base of phone numbers is unrealistic. Just take note. Our managers constantly spent extra time on dialing non-working numbers.

  • Negative perception

A well-known fact - everyone is annoyed by “cold” calls. No matter how good your script is. Given the big blows to many businesses due to COVID, people began to receive calls even with a big negative, which directly affected further sales.

  • High starting costs

Do not forget that effective dialing to databases requires significant expenses for the organization of telephony. Even the purchase of a noise canceling headset unpleasantly “bit” our budget.

After 2 weeks, we came to the decision that the “time-to-result” relationship does not justify telephony, so we tried to switch to cold correspondence on Linkedin. Why did you choose this social network? Everything is very simple. It was there that our segment of the business audience was located.



And what do you think? Did we manage to get better results immediately in comparison with calls?

No.

And here, after 7 days of testing, we saw a low conversion in response. The numbers were even worse. The problem of such correspondence became immediately apparent - people respond for a long time or even lose our messages. Correspondence is much easier to close and ignore, as opposed to calling your number.

But we also noticed 2 positive points:

  1. The people who responded are much easier to show the value of our product and offer. For many objections or questions, just drop the link with the video / article / presentation. There is less aggression, but interest in self-study of the material is much higher.
  2. If a person answers positively in correspondence, it is much easier to transfer him to the category of “hot” lead. Those who conditionally said: “I am interested,” easily agreed to go on to a telephone conversation in the future.

Namely 2 of these factors have become decisive in decision making. We need to continue with cold correspondence, as well as make efforts to eliminate problems and increase conversion.

What problems are you talking about?


The first is a huge waste of time adding friends and sending messages of the same type. Our managers were tired and made offensive "mistakes." Template messages in the style of "Welcome, Anna ..." were easily sent to Alexei, Leonid, Viktor and others.

How to solve this? Everything is pretty simple.

We immediately came to the decision that we need to automate the entire sending of template messages through the LinkAdd.in promotion service. We considered other offers, but they all worked in a semi-automatic mode and required the hiring of a separate person who managed the mailings. LinkAdd costs a little more, but it allows you to send mailings on full autopilot, and we saved about $ 400 / month on the salary of an additional employee.

The tool worked according to a simple principle: once asked the target audience - then the text of the first message is adjusted and sent individually for each. Managers can calmly work only with those who have already answered.

Forgot about the routine.

But then a second problem arose. From one account, you can send only up to 3000 invites per month. We wanted to scale and this figure does not suit us. The solution is to register an additional 9 accounts. But here there were some difficulties.

  • With a constant exit and switching from accounts, Linkedin can simply give a lock, thinking about hacking accounts.
  • Managers have to clear the browser history, switch VPNs and do additional futile actions.
  • Only potential customers suffer from this. The person who answered the first account waits almost 24 hours for the manager to go through a circle of 10 accounts and return to the first again.

Important: By measuring the time tracker, we saw that we spend 3 hours a day not on correspondence with users, but on routine switching between accounts.

Because of these additional problems, we already wanted to look for a third alternative, but something interesting happened. The LinkAdd.in service itself sent us a letter and offered to take part in testing its new product - a CRM system, in which the manager can conduct correspondence from all 10 of our Linkedin accounts from a personal account.

Friendly all office say: “Let's try”




According to the results of the first weeks, we were faced with unpleasant bugs. The product was frankly "damp", but the fact that those. support quickly fixed everything. Somewhere in 10-12 days of testing, we already forgot about errors in the work of CRM.

  • Now only 1 manager was responsible for everything
  • He led at the same time 10 accounts
  • Response time to all messages - up to 20 minutes
  • A nice bonus - at work he began to have free time

Test Results for May 2020



And now we come to the main thing. What are the results of the “cold” correspondence?

  • From 10 accounts we sent 25 421 invites
  • Of these 25,421 invites, 6,900 users confirmed the request and received the first automatic message
  • 1,173 users answered positively to the first message. We consider them to be the leaders, adding to ourselves in AmoCRM
  • 527 people in correspondence provided their telephones and switched to talking calls. The rest continued messaging

For a month of working with the use of “cold” correspondence, we entered into 163 telephone sales and 58 in chat. We have made for ourselves an unambiguous choice in favor of this method.

Automated messages are much more efficient than calls, which require both financial costs at the start and a lot of time to call.

We got a fully automated tool that generates leads and does not make managers think about dismissing because of “this routine.”

Our unambiguous choice is “cold” correspondence on Linkedin with their subsequent withdrawal to a call or correspondence in a convenient messenger.

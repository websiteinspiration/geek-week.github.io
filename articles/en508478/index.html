The use of simulators can significantly reduce the cost of training in general, by reducing the cost of training, certification and certification, etc. Studying the cost of buying and installing educational equipment, as well as studying the costs of its operation, allowed us to establish the following factors:



  • Savings due to reduced costs for the purchase of equipment, installation and configuration (initial costs). This is not only the purchase of new equipment, but also the regular updating of obsolete equipment, expanding the range of equipment, etc.
  • Savings by reducing the cost of operating equipment (electricity consumption, fuels and lubricants, consumables, equipment repair and maintenance, etc.).
  • Savings due to the possibility of replication (copying) of simulators for several workplaces (laboratories, training centers, etc.).






Do not forget about the additional costs associated with putting the equipment into operation, for example, conducting a laboratory workshop in order to study the testing methods of compressor equipment are characterized by additional economic costs, such as:



  • the compressor must be installed in a separate building;
  • work associated with high gas pressure is a significant hazard, which requires the installation of special protective equipment;
  • in case of erroneous actions, equipment breakdown is possible;
  • Installation and adjustment of the compressor must be carried out by highly qualified personnel, etc.


Also in the calculations are taken into account and compared operating costs. In the case of the above example (compressor), a significant difference can be noted between the costs of operating real equipment and simulators. For example, the energy consumption of a compressor is 33 kW, a PC with a simulator is 300 watts. If we consider a larger object, for example, a drilling rig, the difference will be even greater, because on average, the cost of drilling is more than 20-40 thousand rubles per 1 meter, from which we can draw conclusions about the cost of operation.

"Streaming" training - reducing time costs



When using real equipment, as a rule, it is difficult to provide high-quality training for many specialists in a short time. If the number of samples of educational equipment is small, a situation arises when one student (or the teacher / instructor himself) does the work, the rest are forced to wait in line. In fact, this leads either to "stretching" of the training time (when forming the queue) or to a decrease in the quality of training (one performs actions - the others observe).



The ability to copy (duplicate) simulators to many computers allows you to train a whole group of specialists at the same time, while each specialist performs the work individually.



The possibility of training specialists before the construction of a new technological facility



In the case of the construction of a new production facility or the whole complex, in most cases, staff training is necessary (especially in the case of the use of new types of structures, technologies, etc.). Training of specialists after the construction of the facility (that is, at the finished facility) entails a ‚Äúdelay‚Äù in the timing of the commissioning of the facility, and is also a possible cause of accidents (due to the "poor knowledge" of the facility). This ability of imitators is difficult enough to overestimate. A quantitative assessment of the effectiveness of using simulators, taking into account the possibility of training specialists even before the construction of a new technological facility, can be defined as the economic income of the enterprise by reducing the time it takes to launch a particular facility into operation. This is a very valuable quality of simulators,especially for training centers of developing enterprises.







Improving the quality of training



Improving the quality of training when using simulators consists of the following set of factors:



  • Conducting more work in the same time.
  • Ensuring individual work of students.
  • The ability to visually observe the internal structure of the equipment being studied, micro and macro objects and processes, fast or slow technological and natural processes or phenomena.
  • The possibility of visual observation of abstract concepts or concepts (for example, visualization of the accumulation of fatigue damage), etc.
  • Ability to change equipment configuration and environmental parameters.
  • Assessment of the possible consequences of alternative conditions and activities.
  • Interest in imitators, lack of responsibility and danger, the ability to ‚Äúexperiment‚Äù.
  • The ability to use simulators in the independent work of students.
  • The possibility of objective quality control of training.


Conducting more work. The ability of simulators to ‚Äúaccelerate time‚Äù allows you to complete training faster, i.e. the use of simulators allows the implementation of more laboratory, practical work, training, etc. As a result, time can be used more efficiently.



Increase in the share of individual work of students. The problem is more typical for malware / secondary vocational education. Individual work is largely distinguished by the perception and storage of information. According to Haskett consulting inc. (HCI): "People remember 20% of what they see, 40% of what they see and hear, and 70% of what they see, hear, and do." In other words, improving the quality of training when using simulators arises due to an increase in the efficiency of perception of information (an increase in% of information storage). When using real equipment for training, it is quite difficult to ensure individual work with the equipment of each student. As a rule, one person does the work (manages the equipment), the others record, for example, the readings of devices, not always understanding the essence of the process.The reasons for this situation are understandable - the inability to provide equipment to each student, lack of time for each student to perform work individually. In turn, the use of simulators allows, in most cases, the individual performance of work by each student in the presence of an appropriate number of computers.







The ability to visually observe the internal structure of the equipment being studied, micro and macro objects and processes, fast or slow technological and natural processes or phenomena. When using simulators, it is possible to carry out work with a demonstration of phenomena and processes that are not observed on real equipment due to high danger or significant technical difficulty.



The possibility of visual observation of abstract concepts or concepts (for example, visualization of the accumulation of fatigue damage), etc. This ability of imitators provides fundamentally new opportunities for training and can significantly contribute to understanding information, which also significantly affects the quality of training.



Ability to change equipment configuration and environmental parameters. For example, conducting a laboratory workshop to study the influence of various factors on the efficiency of a gearbox using a simulator allows you to study the effect on oil viscosity of the type of bearings, the degree of manufacturing accuracy, gear ratio, etc. As a rule, the implementation of such capabilities using real equipment Despite the great cognitive interest, it is hampered by significant difficulties in the technical plan. Change in environmental parameters, such as barometric pressure, temperature, relative humidity, etc. It is also a difficult task that can be solved using a simulator.Improving the quality of training, taking into account this factor, is achieved due to a better systematization of knowledge and understanding of more dependencies.







Assessment of the possible consequences of alternative actions and alternative methods in solving assigned tasks. When using simulators, students can, if necessary, experiment, which causes additional interest and incentive to learn. This indirectly increases the effectiveness of training. This is also facilitated by the initial interest of specialists in imitators, the lack of responsibility and danger. This is explained by an elementary ‚Äúcuriosity‚Äù, a desire to experiment and a desire for research. Even for experienced staff, the answer to the question ‚Äúwhat happens if‚Äù is often of great interest. In general, these factors increase the motivation for learning, which, as you know, significantly affects the quality of training.



The use of imitators in the students' independent work also provides additional advantages and opportunities to improve the quality of education. The use of simulators as elements of courses in LMS - systems (distance learning systems) allows you to launch simulators from home, work, hostel, and any place where there is access to the Internet. This possibility of using imitators in ‚Äúindependent‚Äù training of employees and students represents significant advantages in solving the issue of improving the quality of training.



The possibility of objective quality control of training. The use of imitators makes it possible to objectively determine and accurately assess the volume of knowledge, skills and abilities, both in combination or individually (only knowledge or only skills). In addition to the objective control of training, the use of simulators allows you to identify changes in the course of training, issues requiring additional study, etc.

Despite an a priori understanding of the effectiveness of the considered complex of factors, the quantitative justification for assessing the effectiveness of using simulators from the point of view of improving the quality of training causes considerable difficulties. Most existing studies associate the effectiveness of electronic educational resources (which include simulators) with how much the student remembers (this can be easily measured). In the studies cited in the book Novikov D.A. (Patterns of iterative learning. M.: Institute for Management Problems of the Russian Academy of Sciences, 1998. - 77 p.) The following characteristics are also highlighted:



  • temporary (time taken to complete the action, operations, reaction time, time taken to correct the error, etc.);
  • high-speed (labor productivity, reaction rate, movement, etc. - the reciprocal of time);
  • accuracy (error value in terms of physical quantities (millimeters, angles, etc.), the number of errors, the probability of error, the probability of an exact reaction, action, etc.);
  • informational (the amount of memorized material, processed information, the amount of perception, etc.).


Other factors are noted, such as the development of creative abilities, professional intuition, etc., but there is no consensus on the assessment of these factors, which is the reason for the difficulties in determining the quantitative assessment. On the issue of the effectiveness of perception and memorization of information, there is a large similarity of views.

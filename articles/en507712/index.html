Startup Nautilus Data Technologies is preparing to launch a new data center





In the data center industry, work continues despite the crisis. For example, the startup Nautilus Data Technologies recently announced its intention to launch a new floating DC. About Nautilus Data Technologies became known several years ago, when the company announced plans to develop a floating data center. This seemed to be another fix idea that would never be realized. But no, in 2015 the company began work on its first data center, Eli M. Its floating base was launched 30 kilometers from San Francisco . The capacity of the DC was 8 MW, and the capacity was 800 server racks.



The startup has previously received about $ 36 million investment from various partners. Now the largest investor has invested in it- Orion Energy Partners. She invested $ 100 million in floating data centers. The funds will go to expand the capabilities of data centers, create additional facilities, new research, etc.





Double Deck Data Center from Nautilus Data Technologies with a Modular Structure



Why do we need floating data centers? Their main advantage is mobility. So, if any company has a need for additional resources, it can moor such a data center to the shore, in the region where it works, and quickly obtain the necessary resources. Investors who have invested in the company plan to create several such data centers at once, placing them in the port of Singapore. There is no way to build a data center on land here - there is a banal lack of free space, the building density is very high. But on the coast - please. According to the developers, a full-fledged floating data center can be deployed in about six months.



The company‚Äôs representatives also say that the mobility of the data center makes it possible to quickly set sail from the coast if a problem arises in the region - floods, fires, local conflicts, etc.



It should be understood that this is not an autonomous DC, it needs the appropriate infrastructure to work - communication channels, a power grid, etc. Such an object will not be able to work in the middle of the ocean. But it can be transported to almost any region where you can get by water - the ocean, sea or navigable river.





The appearance of the new data center



The positive point here is the cooling system. It is water, and to create it, you do not need to deploy a complex system for supplying and discharging water. The coolant is always at hand. It is dialed directly from the ocean or sea (through special hatches located below the waterline of the floating base), it is slightly cleaned and used for cooling. Next, the heated water is poured back into the sea or ocean. Due to the fact that water does not need to be pumped through pipelines from far away, the energy consumption of DCs is lower than that of a standard facility of similar capacity. At the test data center of the company, PUE was 1.045, at a real facility it was slightly higher - 1.15. According to calculations made by environmental experts, the negative impact on the environment will be minimal. Local and especially global ecosystems will not be affected.









This is how the cooling system for servers based on heat exchangers in the back door of the server rack (manufacturer ColdLogik) looks like.



As for the new DC, it has already received the name Stockton I. Construction is underway in the port of Stockton in northern California. According to the plan, the data center will be launched in late 2020. Nautilus Data Technologies is building another facility at the Limerick docks in Ireland. The cost of creating an Irish DC is $ 35 million. According to the developers, the energy efficiency of floating data centers is 80% higher than usual, in addition, the density of racks in such facilities is several times higher than in standard DCs. Capital costs are reduced up to 30% compared with the same indicator for standard DC.

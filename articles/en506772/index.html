To create or refuse - a test of ideas for strength

How does an IT start-up and any new task in an IT project begin? With ideas and questions to yourself


To create a wow, just inspiration is not enough. It is important to be confident in yourself and in your idea. The topic is not new, but occasionally you have to deal with frustrated developers and businessmen. Disappointment usually arises due to a fire that goes out by the middle of the project. There are common reasons for this: a lack of understanding of what and for whom we are doing, and then regret about the time spent.

I highly recommend reading this material to those who want to order development of startups, but doubt their decision, and those who are spinning in the field of IT over the creation and development of projects. First, we go through the first pages of the results and select a list of working solutions (if at least one works at all!) - there may be one, but there may be dozens.

Pursuing the development of the product and finding that the ‚Äúfeature‚Äù has been repeatedly implemented by other developers, it is urgent to include it in the composition of your application. It may turn out that the opportunity was realized, but it simply turned out to be not in demand, and perhaps not yet popular. In any case, this is an occasion to note the need for development and move on to the next step of the checklist.

What problem do I want to solve and for whom?


If the idea to create something has come, then the final product should be in demand. But were questions asked: will the product solve the problem and is it a problem at all? In this case, it is better to be tough.

Often we try to solve problems that are not really them, or we find inconveniences that we are ready to eliminate, but these inconveniences are visible only to us and to a limited circle of people. When creating or improving a product, you need to verify that the real problem is being solved.

I offer a basic list of questions sufficient to test the hypothesis of the existence of a problem:

Is this created to eliminate the loss of time or money?
If the solution is in no way connected with this, then the circle of your users will most likely be very limited. Immediately I want to stop the indignation: entertaining IT projects (for example, games) solve the problem of losing time. Instead of losing it uselessly, dying from boredom, people lose it by doing business. Therefore, it is not necessary that the decision directly concerns time and money, but it is important to draw it to the fact that these losses will be eliminated.

Are there any alternative ways to solve the invented problem?
If there are alternative methods, then they are used and up to now, everyone was happy with it. You need to understand the reason for the consumer to refuse the current algorithm and switch to a new one. To do this, you need more questions, the answers to which will help to understand future promotion methods:
- If there is an alternative solution, then what will be better than the new?
- Why will they choose the created product, and not do it as before?

And who has this problem?
This is the last and main question. It so happens that the problem seems to be a problem only for a limited circle of persons or only for one person - himself.
To test the hypothesis of the existence of a problem, decide on the circle of people who may need the created solution, select the target audience and call, write and ask. Important! When interviewing, do not try to sell, use the phrases: "Is it interesting ...", "And how do you feel about ...", "What if we offer you to participate in testing for free ..." and others.
If you find a response among your friends, acquaintances or potential customers - among any possible users, then the target audience is found, and something really necessary is created. Otherwise, it seems that a local problem is being solved - this is normal if you have not planned a large number of end users. But if you were planning, something seems to have gone wrong and you should not make a useless product.



Time, money and rock and roll


Any automation idea has the goal of capturing users. Getting users - making a profit.



Before starting development, evaluate the profitability of the idea, what budget it can bring to a new or developing project.

For new projects, we estimate the expected number of users in the first 6 months, and for developing projects, their expected growth in general over the same period. We are building a plan with minimal sales and with minimal cost. We evaluate the development costs: both monetary and time resources (this is a separate big topic, most importantly keep in mind a combination of words - the minimum version of the product).

The time to create the minimum version should not exceed 4-6 months - this is the excusable period when no one has time to start doing the same, and the inspiration will be still strong. Before starting development, it will be necessary to go through the stage of describing the minimum version of the project in order to understand the scope at this stage. This is a step in the formation of technical specifications (just don‚Äôt think about GOST - we don‚Äôt need it).

The time of financial payback and the first profit should not be longer than the development time, because it will have to be a plus in order to develop. Budgeting an IT project is a separate art form. There are different types of projects and different ways of earning. For example, projects "for yourself", where you need to automate some internal processes of the organization in order to benefit from the implementation of the solution, and not from selling it to anyone. There are applications for a one-time sale and implementation, there are applications for subscription. Too many things, the main thing is to find a way to roughly estimate income.

If the costs and return on investment are satisfactory, then the checklist has been passed and confidence should become clearly greater, and desire is brighter!

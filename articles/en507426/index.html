Who else helped the pandemic: Avast antivirus developer capitalization doubled





Image: Pexels



Earlier in our blog, we wrote about companies that had the coronavirus pandemic play into their hands ( one , two ). As a rule, they included developers of services with which people were forced to interact during isolation: from video calls to streaming and online games.



The massive transition to remote work had a serious impact on the field of information security. For cybercriminals, new opportunities for conducting cyber attacks have opened up, and companies are seriously thinking about protecting the home infrastructure of their employees. This trend was used by some manufacturers of security products - for example, the capitalization of the Avast antivirus developer has almost doubled since the start of isolation .



Why Avast is growing



In the last year, Avast shares did not grow much or even decrease in price, however, since about mid-March, with a massive transition to and an increase in information security threats, securities began to rise in price. As a result, by the summer, the capitalization of the Prague-based company almost doubled.



The success of the company contributed to its inclusion in the European stock exchange index FTSE 100 - it contains ‚Äúblue chips‚Äù, that is, the most liquid and reliable shares of the London stock exchange.



In recent months, cybercriminals have been actively trying to exploit the shortcomings of the new digital reality, in which millions of people were suddenly not in the office, but at home, working remotely. As a result, the popularity of attacks aimed at stealing data or blocking resources for ransom is growing.



Not growth, but recovery



The current growth of Avast is interesting in that in essence it is a restoration of positions that were previously lost. At the end of last year, the antivirus developer was at the center of the scandal surrounding user privacy. At that time, many media outlets reported that Jumpshot, an Avast-based analytics company, was selling anti-virus user data to its customers.



Then Avast shares collapsed by more than 13%, the company suffered up to $ 25 million in losses and was forced to close Jumpsoft. Many users removed the antivirus in protest.

Introduction


In practice, as the main metric capable of calculating and comparing risks for various financial investments is a measure $ inline $ var $ inline $(Value at Risk). In RiskMetricks, compiled by JPMorgan in 1996, this measure is the main one for assessing market risks. Since then, many banks and financial institutions have begun to follow this technical document to assess the risks of their own investments, and this value has informally become the main measure of market risk.


Let us approach the definition of this value from afar.

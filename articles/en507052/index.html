The coolest Data Scientist does not waste time on statistics



I recently found out that one of my good friends received a doctorate in statistics, while he never even thought about the question: what is the use of statistics? Oh my God. If you don’t know why, then you don’t know whether you need it. And since it’s not obvious to the professors, we’ll see what I can do.

Statistics is the science of how to make decisions in the face of uncertainty. How can you think? Follow standard actions or follow the path of a priori beliefs. But what if your mind is undecided? What if you have not yet developed any opinion on this?

Just be guided by your assumptions (this is such a fancy way of saying that “the best guess is based on your knowledge”). How to do it? Look at the data, and say what's there. This is called analytics (or data-mining), and if you've ever used spreadsheets, you probably already answered the question. The fact is that your instinct will help make the right assessment and, accordingly, a choice. Therefore, any need for complex mathematical calculations simply disappears.

“But I could be wrong!” Of course, a mistake cannot be ruled out, which means falling into uncertainty. There is no such mathematics in the world thanks to which it would be possible to come to a confident answer. Your best guess may turn out to be erroneous, but nevertheless, since this is your best guess, against the background of it all the others are wrong and even more likely wrong.

So, no matter how much data you have, just follow your intuition.

“Wait, but I need to know if I have enough data?” - Truth? And enough for what?

Slow down for a moment and imagine that you choose between blue and orange hats. If you really do not care what the color will be, and the data is added in favor of the orange copy, then, taking in the end a blue hat, you will look like a complete madman. Even if only 3 points are gained. Yes, even if it says only 0.0000000000001% for orange. With what fright then blue ?! Take an orange hat and don’t suffer anymore with math.



If the color of the hat is not important to you, and in favor of the orange copy of the arguments it is gaining more, then there is no way to find such mathematics that would eventually point you to the blue hat. Mathematics is not magic to you, and it does not violate common sense.

The only option, why would you end up taking a blue hat - you initially liked it better. Then you might wonder how strong the arguments for orange are. In other words, is there enough data to change the decision. Well, how can I tell you? Welcome to the statistics.



In the first row of the table, the data does not contradict your initial preferences, statistical calculations are not needed. You can make a decision immediately. If the arguments do not fit your preference, you can turn to statistical calculations to see if this evidence can make you change your mind.

Statistics is the science of how to change your decisions.

So, if you are faced with uncertainty (for example, “Will this machine learning system work with the data received tomorrow?”) And the solutions are not the same (for example, “We probably shouldn't run it if it doesn't work”) , then you came to the right place: statistics will come in handy.

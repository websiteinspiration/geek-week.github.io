Who are you, an IT analyst?

Not all IT developers are, right?

It seems clear, but how often it is this stereotype that has to be debunked ...

Thanks to my work to develop the Novosibirsk IT analytics community, I have to periodically present my specialty.

Present to very different people, often far from the world of technology. But every time I fall into a stupor - what to talk about, if everything is clear?



Today I am structuring background information about the specialty of IT analytics. I will tell you about the most common subspecies.

I do not plan to cover all the details - the main task is to give an idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe specialty . Less important is to help with clues where to look for future analytics.

In a broad sense, an IT analyst is a specialist who collects and draws conclusions based on data . The analytics workflow consists of collection, processing, and conclusion. The result of the work helps to answer the question - what does the customer want?

Depending on the type of data, IT analysts can be divided into subspecies.



Below is a brief description of the tasks and skills of various IT analysts.
Before moving on to the description, I note two key points. First, the requirements are different from the business sector and the specific company. Secondly, this is not an exhaustive list, but includes all the popular subspecies of analysts.

So, let's begin:



Description


Tasks - analysis and optimization of business processes. Such an analyst knows well the processes of his company (or the direction with which he works). Understands best practices, competitors and business bottlenecks. Applies his knowledge to improve processes. Skills - knowledge of the subject area; requirements management; charting a business process; communication skills; quality research, etc. Data is a business process. Business Analyst - a specialist in improving business processes. Technical knowledge for this role is not the most important thing, knowledge of processes or the ability to quickly understand them is much more important. You need to be able to listen, ask the right questions and isolate user problems.








Description


Tasks - translation of business requirements into a technical language. He knows how the developed system and software in general are arranged. Can read the code.
Skills - technical knowledge; software architecture; technical documentation; system design and interaction, etc.
Data is the device of the system under development.

System analyst - translator of business wishes on a technical plane. He understands the technical limitations, the nuances of the system and the difficulties encountered by programmers. However, he can talk with business, understand their requirements and immediately see the contradictions.

Data Analyst is a specialist in quantitative research. Scrupulously checks for changes in trends, reports when something goes wrong and explores large amounts of data. Probably wants, and will surely become, a date a Sainthist.

Tasks - to understand what site visitors want. Good in web analytics systems (Google Analytics and Yandex.Metrica). Draws conclusions about visitor behavior, problem areas and growth points. Improves the web page and user experience.
Skills - the ability to work with web analytics systems, data interpretation, conducting A / B tests, etc.
Data - information about working with Web pages.

Web-analyst - will say for sure what is happening with the site. Tracks the actions of visitors, the use of buttons, achievement of goals. Understands interaction bottlenecks. Draws conclusions about the success of new pages and updates to existing ones.

UX-analyst - will find out what actions cause difficulties for the audience or are not obvious to it. It will help you understand how to simplify the interaction between the user and the system.

To summarize, you have to throw a fly in the ointment and emphasize once again - the division of subspecies of IT analysts is rather arbitrary . Names vary from company to company. It is worth looking at the description of the requirements and clarifying the expectations at the interview.

Nevertheless, whatever type of data you would like to work with, what specifics you would not choose - the more tools of different analysts you can use, the more valuable you are in the market and the easier it is to adapt.

The problem with extrusion? That way

So you know what a real universal fakap is? Recently, I completely felt it in my own skin. I hope the text will be useful to beginner 3D printers, as the problem is not unique, but finding a solution was not easy, and even I, an old atheist, almost believed in miracles. Sorry for the presentation, since I'm not a writer or a blogger, but a simple engineer - a firmware engineer.

In addition, the text is designed for people with minimal experience with 3D printing.

Solely for the reason not to inflate the article and not to explain the purpose of the various details and slang words. I'll start with the background. A little less than a year ago I bought myself an inexpensive 3D printer. One of the most popular (not as advertisements, but it would be clear what they mean) - Ender 3. The assembly was simple, there are many recommendations on the Web. Of course, assembling it according to the instructions - I would have got a non-working unit, but having previously “smoked the manuals”, I managed to perform the first test print of 5 with a plus for such a simple machine! Everything was fine until a certain point. But then it began ...

The printer was needed for a specific task - printing custom cases for various small-scale electronic crafts. After playing enough with ready-made models and providing my daughter with various animals and little men, I mastered the “top” of FreeCAD and started doing useful things. Everything was going well ... By and large, the press was launched “from the foot”, set and went to sleep. Unless the first layer controlled. But the first “bells” appeared.

Somewhere part of the layer will fall out, somewhere plastic will burn. He calibrated the table “on paper”, sinned on plastic and nozzle, changed settings in the slicer, plastics and nozzles - the problems disappeared. Till…
At one “fine” moment I was faced with an “insoluble” problem. The end of March, a smooth transfer to udalenka (I successfully moved from the Moscow Region to Minsk 2 years earlier), I decided to assemble a separate computer for work from what it was. In zagashnikah lay a good motherboard, processor, a pair of monitors and 2 cases. One is a huge full ATX, the other is barebone. I decided to assemble in a small one, so that I take up less space. The case is non-standard. With a cradle on 1 CD drive and a 3.5 inch hard drive. But I found a small 3x 2.5 SATA SSD, for the system and one is enough “more than” and 1x for 1Tb 2.5 HDD.

All this "living creatures" was housed in a large building on a makeshift adapter from old bank cards. But, since the printer has already appeared, it would be nice to use it. He created a model holder, put it on print and went about his business. When I looked, how it prints - I saw only noodles ... From that moment, the repair path, 2 months long, went. I will try to paint on the shelves what helped and what didn’t and what was the root cause.

Step 1. Setting up commercials {prevention is always useful}


Once again I set up the table "on a piece of paper", launched it - the plastic does not stick. Changed settings, plastics - without result. Instead of sticking to the table, it bends to the nozzle. When I double-checked the nozzle clearance settings, I found out that the rollers were loose and the table had a backlash of almost 5 mm at the edges. It is strange how he typed before that ... It seemed that the reason was found. Adjusted. But it was almost no better. I printed the part with grief in half, but the quality left much to be desired:



Clear gaps in the press are visible.



And here are the bundles ...

Step 2. A song about Ze [e] de {not bad when everything goes like clockwork}


The first thing that came to mind was the problem in the Z-axis drive. And all the tips on the forums on a problem similar to mine pointed to this. Dismantled, checked the ride. The nut bit a thread and found a “bug” in a pair of rollers. When moving without a drive screw, the rail “X” slightly stuck on 1 revolution of the roller. I ordered a drive nut, rollers (spare parts are always good, but that was not the point). By the method of combinatorics and some kind of mother, he distributed the axle rollers over the loads so that the defective had minimal effort. "Biting" was gone. Cleaned and lubricated the drive screw. Adjusted the position of his engine. As a result, a light touch of the finger is enough to lower the carriage down. The result is negative.

Step 3. Slicer {tuning, but not a solution to the problem}


Updated the slicer. Moreover, in the new release there was just an improvement related precisely to print quality for my printer. Played with the flow and speed, layer thickness, etc. Even successfully printed a few simple small cases and parts. But the settings were frankly wild: flow - + 10%, speed - -5%. Considering that after the assembly an excellent print was obtained on the “default” settings. The problem of printing complex parts has not disappeared ...



Based on this article: https://habr.com/en/post/494058/ I made a tooth brushing timer for my daughter. On it I am preparing a separate description. Spoiler: eight-legged controller, firmware - 119 assembler commands.

Step 3. The extruder {and happiness was so close ...}


Logic tells you, since there are no problems with geometry and axis drives, there must be something with a pitch. He removed the filament feed gear and cleaned it. No visible wear is observed. He returned it to its place, shifting it a little along the overhang to even out wear. The result is 0. Oh, if I looked a little to the right, I would save a month of nerves and $ 200.

Step 4. Checking the BD drivers {no comment}


Excellent geometry, no problems with the mechanics. At the time of the first build, everything was much worse. A bunch of studied materials, improvements and settings, and the result is zero. I do not believe in miracles, electronics remained. Moreover, forum users often complain about burnt drivers. It is clear that a completely burnt shoulder of the bridge can be detected “by eye” immediately, I thought about a “floating” malfunction. After all, the plastic lay down normally, then - omissions, the thread is thinned, hair and other delights. I ordered an improved control board on ARM, but in order not to waste time, I decided to check the existing one. He took off the board, the Z-axis motor as an inductive load, assembled a stand on the table, set the hot-end temperature with a resistor so that the extruder could be twisted. I looked at the oscillograms on the SD coils. All perfectly.He heated the driver with a hairdryer (a defect in a crystal or a weld-in is sometimes manifested), mechanically acted on the driver ICs, cooled propane with butane (gas for lighters). Note. It cools quite well to -20, it's not necessary to buy an expensive freezer for this. All perfectly. Failures are not visible!

Step 5. Updating the “firmware” {and I almost believed in a miracle and conspiracy theory}


No, I’m sure that miracles do not happen. There are bugs, glitches, tricks, lack of information, subjective perception of reality, in the end. But not miracles! But how to explain that with good mechanics and electronics, printing behaves extremely strangely? The Chinese made a bookmark in FW to additionally clam with the boards? It is done elementarily, but, I think, they would have long ago discovered and a white fluffy fox would have come to the brand. A bug in the firmware? Everything can be, only somehow strange it appeared.

Lost settings in EEPROM or "plugging" FLASH *? Most likely. Well, since the board is on the table, nothing prevents it from being reflashed. Updated to the "vanilla" Marlin 1.1.9, collected everything back. Result - a miracle did not happen.

Step 6. Wash the "throat" {keep your head in the cold, and the hot end is clean, I recommend doing it immediately after buying the printer}


A gift of "heaven" was a link on one of the forums on the video from the manufacturer. There, they honestly admit that not all hotnds are equally useful, that is, well-assembled.

The Teflon tube is not fully pressed in, the cut is not even. The consequence is corking and the extruder cannot properly advance the plastic. Here it is - my case!

Abnormal flow and retract values ​​all converge. Despite the late Saturday evening and a visit to the brasserie, I decided to check. Right Guana there is immeasurable, down to the remnants of the plastic that was printed a month ago (the same green holder). Very likely to win! I cleaned the “throat”, cut the tube straight, pressed it all the way in, also fixed the second end of the tube more tightly, many use electrical tape, but I wound a little wire 0.7, which allows you to freely unscrew the holder:



And here is the “killed” end of the tube, it’s clear that crookedly cropped:



The model is cut into layers with default settings. Print ... The rim has gone, the contour has gone, there are no snot during retract. Hooray!!! Victory!!! Already going to write an article so that others do not suffer, but ... A complete fiasco. Thinning and tearing of plastic, printing gaps. Already ready to give up. Wife encourages, but throw him out, buy a new one! I can’t do that. There must be a reason. But where ??? Reasonable (and censored) thoughts were gone ...

Step 7. Calibration of the feed {with good mechanics, most likely it will not be needed, if only after replacing the drive parts or flashing}


In the instructions for upgrading to “vanilla” Marlin, it was said that for the factory printer, all the settings are perfect, but the accuracy of the plastic feed may go a bit. Okay, we’ll calibrate. He pulled out the plastic, warmed up the hot-end (in the firmware the lock does not allow moving the “E” to the cold one). He unscrewed the tube from the feed mechanism, cut the plastic flush and carried out an extrusion command of 300mm. I have a ruler of 350. Extrusion lasts long enough, at the end I measured the length of the filament that came out and I could not believe my eyes 226mm. I understand the difference in 3-5%, but not in 25%! I'll try another plastic ...

Note. I saw how the flow is regulated by the marks on the bar marked with a marker, But with cutting, IHMO, more precisely.

Step 8. Victory!


At first I checked for PETG, I decided to change it to PLA. The first one I overheated a little during drying, attributed the inadequate spread to possible sticking on the coil. Circumcision, extrusion. And nothing. Plastic is not fed at all. The drive axis rotates slowly, but the screws show that there is movement. I looked, the bar went wrong between the gear and the pinch roller. Okay, right. Something a little dangling movie. It’s necessary to tighten. A fig! Here he is the culprit of all my troubles: The



lever of the pressure roller cracked. At the same time, the clamping force remained sufficient for partial advancement of the bar. Traces of the gear remained and the pinch roller rotated.

Accordingly, all games with the adjustment of the clamping spring were unsuccessful. Slips of the rod were invisible to the eye, and the crack could not be seen on the assembled mechanism. All! Engineer Ecstasy Received! The lever was glued, an aluminum mechanism was ordered, just in case a spare was printed (if it breaks, while I wait for a new one). Here is a link to the model . Of course, it’s unpleasant to spend so much time and money because of such a trifle, but it helped me to plunge much deeper into the processes of printing and catching glitches.

Ampere Announces Altra Max 128-core ARM Processor





A few months ago, California-based Ampere introduced the industry's first 80-core Ampere Altra ARM processor . This chip on a 64-bit architecture is designed for installation in server hardware, it is not for the consumer market. Now the company has expanded the range of processors, supplementing it with a 128-core model .



Altra Max is compatible with other Altra models and can be installed in servers where there are already processors of the company of the same line. All of it is based on the design of ARM Neoverse N1, it also lies at the heart of AWS Graviton2 processors from AWS. Ampere said that the new processors will increase the productivity of equipment, which is what cloud service providers are interested in.



As for the characteristics, the novelty is equipped with 128 physical cores (without multithreading technology). The L1 cache for instructions and data is 64 KB, L2 is 1 MB, L3 is 32 MB for each core. The core clock frequency is 3.0 GHz.







Supported eight-channel RAM ECC DDR4-3200, the maximum amount of RAM - 4 TB. The 128-line PCI Express 4 interface is also supported. If you install two processors on one motherboard, the number of supported lines will be 192. It is known that 32 lines are used for exchanging data between sockets. The entire Altra range is manufactured using the 7nm process technology from TSMC.



Depending on the model, the energy consumption of the chips is from 45 to 250 watts. The 80-core Ampere Altra has a TDP of 210 watts.







Developers propose using a processor for server applications, including data analytics, neural networks, databases, border computing, and cloud applications. Especially for the needs of machine learning, Ampere implemented hardware support for the data formats FP16 (half precision numbers) and INT8 (single-byte representation of an integer). AES and SHA-256 hash hardware acceleration is provided.





Benchmark for 80-core ARM processor Ampere Altra



According to manufacturers, server-side ARM processors are more efficient than X86, largely due to reduced power consumption. Therefore, it is possible to increase the density of equipment with these processors in the data center, while improving the specific productivity, energy consumption and heat dissipation.



Now the Altra line includes 12 processors; processors with the number of cores from 32 to 80, frequencies from 1.7 to 3.3 GHz and heat packages from 45 to 250 Watts have already gone on sale . Prices have not yet been announced.



It is known that several partners became interested in the new product, including Gigabyte, Wiwynn, Cloudflare, Genymobile, NVIDIA, Equinix and smaller companies. Mass production of new processors will begin in 2021, so that the start of sales can be expected around the same time.

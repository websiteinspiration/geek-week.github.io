AI in radiology




Numerous medical imaging data is stored in abundance in small local systems. But what if you use deep learning by uploading data to the cloud and ‚Äúfeeding‚Äù their AI? Machines and algorithms can effectively interpret visualization data, revealing patterns and anomalies.

The most obvious use case: radiologist / radiologist assistant, involved in the identification and localization of suspicious skin lesions, lesions, tumors, internal hemorrhages, brain lesions, etc. The computer works faster and more accurately, and therefore is able to produce specific data about the disease a few seconds after processing the information. Man cannot do that.

There is another point. Highly qualified specialists are expensive, and there is a huge demand for them. They experience serious pressure, literally bogging down in the data streams that are streaming down on them from all sides. According to this article , such a specialist should issue a diagnosis every 3-4 seconds. Machine intelligence can enhance the skills of an ordinary specialist, helping him to understand difficult situations. Thus reducing the number of false diagnoses and saving lives.

The identification of rare or difficult to diagnose diseases often depends on the experience of the doctor, as well as the degree of ‚Äúneglect‚Äù of the disease. Simply put, until the sore climbs out, it may not be recognized. Having trained a computer on large data sets containing raw images and many forms of pathologies associated with certain diseases, it is possible to improve the quality of diagnosis and the number of detected diseases. AIDOC startup is developing this idea . 

AIs can improve the quality of medical facilities by automating the time-consuming and responsible part of the work of doctors. Using computer algorithms, you can also monitor the effectiveness of treatment and the quality of the operation performed, to predict the rate of recovery of the body.

A good example of this technology is the Microsoft InnerEye project . He suggests using ML techniques to segment and identify tumors using 3D x-rays. This can help in the precise planning of surgery, navigation, and the effective formation of tumor contours for planning radiation therapy.

It should also be noted that MRI and other modern imaging systems used for early detection of cancer work with ML. Algorithms help with advanced image analysis. For example, perform prostate segmentation or combine several different images (for example, ultrasound, CT and MRI) to get a more accurate picture. Machine intelligence is also capable of recognizing oncology during routine medical procedures and even surgical intervention (it often happens that during another operation another malignant tumor goes unnoticed).

AI in pathology




Pathological diagnosis involves examining a tissue section under a microscope. Using Deep Learning to train the image recognition algorithm in combination with human experience will provide more accurate diagnostics. Pixel-level analysis of digital photographs can help detect pathological changes that the human eye can easily miss. And this will provide a more effective diagnosis.

Such technology is developed, for example, at Harvard Medical School. The algorithm uses speech and image recognition technology to recognize images with pathologies and trains computers to distinguish between cancerous and noncancerous lesions. The combination of this algorithm with human performance resulted in an accuracy of 99.5%. 

Machine Learning and Medical Science


In various medical facilities, petabytes of data are generated. This data, unfortunately, is usually randomly scattered and unstructured. This in no way reproached the doctors. They have to treat not so much how to report on the treatment. However, chaos greatly interferes with the planning and global monitoring of the health of a particular country or the world as a whole.

An additional complication is that, unlike standard business data, patient data does not lend itself well to simple statistical modeling and analytics. A powerful AI-enabled cloud platform with access to medical databases is able to efficiently analyze mixed information (for example, blood pathology, genetic characteristics, x-rays, medical history). She (theoretically) is able to analyze input data and reveal hidden patterns that are not visible due to the overly large amount of medical information.

Interpreted AI models and distributed machine learning systems are great for these tasks. They will allow not only the effective development of medical science, finding new patterns and racial / gender / age characteristics of people, but also the formation of more accurate data on the health status of the population in specific regions.

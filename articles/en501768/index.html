Application examples


Quality control during casting aluminum



Source: 3d-scantech.com

Let's get acquainted with the work of KSCAN20 in the foundry at the stage of control of finished aluminum parts. An example is available on the Scantech website . In the production of household appliances, electronics, and engineering, casting from aluminum is widespread. Injection molding is a multi-level process with a number of features. For the timely detection of errors in the production process, it is important to know the dimensions of the parts and the deviation parameters. Otherwise, the products will not meet the requirements. The task of quality control is to provide for and avoid deformation of the object. For this, it is necessary to collect data on the stiffness, structure, and surface roughness. At this stage, the 3D scanner does an excellent job.

Creating a 3D model using KSCAN takes only 16 minutes: from setting markers to complete information output. Reducing the operating time compared to other devices is due to the use of fewer markers.

ThinkSmart Car Repair




ThinkSmart from Vietnam has created a system for the production of spare body parts for vehicle repairs. At the first stage, KSCAN and 3D SLA printing technology are used to obtain a digital model of the desired part. At the second stage, a finished object from ABS plastic is printed on a 3D printer.


Source: thinksmart.com

The combination of SLA and the KSCAN 3D scanner ensures high-precision printing, while ABS features guarantee reliability, strength and high temperature resistance of the part.

The cost of parts manufactured using this technology is significantly lower than with aluminum casting. Therefore, when releasing small batches of parts, a significant reduction in costs can be achieved.

Stepper motor control with Simatic S7-1200 with a limited number of pulse outputs

At the end of last year, I was approached by one company that provides comprehensive solutions for grain crops with a proposal to consider the automation project of a small grain sampling system. A feature of this project was that design solutions and actuators were already developed and implemented in hardware. Without going into details of the technological process of sampling, we can say that the goal of automation is to control the mechanical valves of the grain-air flow, start a screw mixer for uniformity of samples, control the electric motors of air turbines, process control signals of the operator and sensors of some steps of operations. The valves and mixer were designed to be driven by stepper motors.



Earlier, it was decided to build an automation system based on the single-board microcomputer Orange pi plus 2e and the Arduino Nano microcontroller. For these boards, applications were found for another similar project, but this is another story.

The rules of aerodrome flight support are described in great detail in regulatory documents. Most of them are still the beginning of the 90s. More modern amendments have changed the standards not very much. Despite the development of technology, many things worked out over the years and written in blood, excuse me for the pathos, remained. Still one of the main tools of the airdrome - head, legs, eyes.

A very important milestone in the establishment of the rules of aerodrome support was the disasterTu-154 aircraft at the Omsk airport in 1984. Then, in conditions of poor visibility, the aircraft received permission to land when cleaning machines were operating on the strip (without radio tapping stations and without flashing beacons). Two of them had kerosene tanks of 7.5 tons each. 178 dead. Then the basic rules were developed when working on the runway, the requirements for cars performing work, maintaining radio communications, interaction between services that provide flights. But the human factor remains the main cause of the accident, and the Falcon 50 disaster in Vnukovo in 2014 only confirms this hackneyed truth.

Autumn and winter at the airport


In autumn and winter (as in the rest of the time) there are flight lanes at the aerodrome (these are runways and end braking lanes so that the sides roll out less), the airfield is the place where the lanes, taxiways, aprons and different platforms, curbs transitions from artificial turf to the ground (they are fortified so that nothing gets into the engines due to jet erosion of the ground), the apron is a place for passengers to pick up and drop off, load and unload luggage, mail and cargo, and for other types of services. There is also a CTA, which is also a control point: the coordinates of the airfield on the map. As a rule, at single-lane aerodromes, the CTA is located in the geometric center of the strip.

The task is to maintain constant readiness of the airfield for aircraft flights. In winter, the aerodrome service cleans snow and ice on stripes, taxiways, aprons and intra-airport roads, areas of radio beacon landing systems. At least four times during the 12-hour shift, the runway is inspected; at least once per shift, the friction coefficient (KSS) on the runway is measured, both in summer and winter. Additional inspections and measurements are made when the weather conditions change and at the request of the Tower (there are ATM controllers sitting there).

Inspection of the runway is carried out by car by the shift supervisor of the aerodrome service. He estimates on the runway, taxiway, aprons:

  • adhesion coefficient;
  • the presence, type and thickness of precipitation;
  • condition and quality of surface cleaning;
  • state and visibility of daytime and portable markings.

On the planned part of the airfield:

  • dimensions of LP cleared of snow;
  • the value of the slope of the pairing of the cleaned part of the medicinal product with virgin snow
  • soil density and surface evenness.

Based on the results of the inspection, the head of the shift by radio reports to the HSE controller the coefficient of adhesion on the runway and makes an entry in the logbook of the state of the airfield (the form of the logbook is determined by federal aviation regulations) of the following form:
‚ÄúInspection of the runway, taxiways, aprons. Runway nnL / nnR in places dry snow up to 3 mm, Ksts 0.4 / 0.40 / 0.40. Runway nnL / nnR is snowed up to 3 mm, Ksts 0.39 / 0.39 / 0.39. The aprons are snowy, sometimes slippery. The airfield is prepared for flights. ‚Äù


In the journal, for each such entry, the shift supervisor of the aerodrome service, the senior aviation chief of the aerodrome, and the director of operations of the ATM are signed.

The main parameter of the readiness of the airfield to receive aircraft is the coefficient of adhesion of coatings on the runway, which should be at least 0.3. The adhesion coefficient is a dimensionless quantity expressing the ratio of the adhesion force between two bodies to the normal pressure force pressing these two bodies against each other, or the adhesion characteristic between the aircraft pneumatics and the runway surface under various conditions. Cohesion coefficient is measured on third of the runway, first on one side of the axis of the runway, then on the second. The measured values ‚Äã‚Äãin thirds are reduced to the arithmetic mean and entered into the logbook of the state of the airfield. The worse the meteorological situation, the more often the adhesion coefficient is measured. Based on the values ‚Äã‚Äãof the coefficient of adhesion, thickness of precipitation, weather forecast, the shift supervisor of the aerodrome service decideswhether coating treatment with anti-icing reagents and mechanical cleaning are needed. With an adhesion ratio of less than 0.3, the aerodrome closes.

The Tower dispatcher reports the value of the coefficient of adhesion to the crews of the aircraft, the crew, in turn, can independently decide on landing, taking into account, among other things, lateral wind. Many airlines by internal documents establish their normative values ‚Äã‚ÄãKSC, in which the pilot must decide to go to the landing or go to the emergency.

So, it charged the snowfall. How is it decided what to clean first? At each aerodrome, a cleaning sequence plan (winter maintenance plan) is developed, in which the sequence of cleaning the aerodrome elements is established. First of all, cleaning usually includes a runway complex (i.e., runway, main taxiway, all or some connecting taxiways in agreement with the HSE, the unpaved part of the flight strip adjacent to the runway in width, which depends on the category of RMS), preparation of the CRM and timing zones, main aprons. Second stage: the second runway complex, if there is one at the airport, the remaining aprons in the same volume. The third stage: cleaning the soil part of the flight strip adjacent to its ends, expanding the cleaning of the soil part of the flight strip along the runway (the width of cleaning again depends on the PMC category), cleaning the aprons of the aprons, planning snow shafts,cleaning of access roads to radio facilities and intra-airport roads. We pick up the tails and wait for the next snowfall. Or we start from the first stage, if the snow does not stop.

Coating is cleaned in two main ways: mechanical and chemical. Anti-icing reagents are used both to prevent the formation of ice, and to clean coatings from already formed deposits and ice. At the aerodrome, only certified reagents are allowed. The certification check should confirm that anti-icing reagents will not cause corrosion of the delicate materials of which the aircraft are made. Ecology, let's say, comes with a much lower priority.

On the runway, such cars go with a Christmas tree or a pig with a small overlap (0.8 meters approximately). Ice forecasting


In order to prevent runway closure due to a decrease in adhesion coefficient, the shift supervisor should treat the runway with anti-icing reagents before it becomes covered with ice. The ice forecasting technique was developed back in the USSR. When forecasting, a number of parameters and their values ‚Äã‚Äãare taken into account:

  • air temperature - from 1 to minus 5 ¬∞ ;
  • relative humidity - from 86 to 98%;
  • dew point deficit - from 0 to minus 7 ¬∞ ;
  • the difference in temperature between the air and the surface of the coating is from 2 to 4 ¬∞ C;
  • the temperature difference between the coating surface and the dew point is from 0 to minus 2 ¬∞ C.

Now these parameters are used in the development of ice warning systems on runways. Weather stations and surface condition sensors for artificial coatings are installed on the runway. Using the software, the probability of ice formation on the runway is predicted, which helps to change the aerodrome service in time to prepare for changing weather conditions and proactively treat the runway with anti-ice reagents.

Emergency repairs


In winter, it is impossible to lay asphalt or repair the chip cleavage with cement concrete. However, if a defect has formed that could lead to an accident, it must be repaired. For emergency repairs of artificial coatings at low temperatures, special materials are used that cure during mechanical sealing without heating and work well at low temperatures. Such a patch will perfectly last until the summer, when it will be replaced with the original material of the existing coating.

Spring and summer


As always, maintaining the operational qualities of airfield coatings is required: the frictional properties of the runway coatings, maintaining the visibility of the markings of the runway, MRD, RD, aprons, preserving the design geometric parameters, shapes and sizes of the aerodrome elements. If translated into Russian, it means that all the elements of the airfield must be kept in such a condition that it can receive and release aircraft of a certain type, while the aircraft should not be damaged, and the pilot should not get lost at the airport. Such a short wording conceals a huge amount of work and huge budgets.

It all starts with the design: it is at this moment that the physical (coating strength) and geometric characteristics (length, width, curvature radii) of the future elements of the airfield are laid. And the task of AO is to preserve them.

Coating Repair


During the winter, our artificial surfaces in places worn out, and the soil sections of the airfield sank and flooded, the marking was erased. Typically, in March, coating troubleshooting and inspection of the drainage network are carried out. Airfield service engineers take their feet in their hands and walk, armed with a paper scheme, bypass the runway, taxiway, aprons. The detected chips, cracks, cavities, shells, peeling and their sizes are noted on the diagrams. Information about defects is usually transferred to the schemes in a graphical editor so that you can see the dynamics of the destruction of coatings and their general condition. Next, a defect analysis is carried out and a repair plan is drawn up for the upcoming spring-summer period. In the same way soil sections of the airfield, wells, ditches are inspected. Plans are being made for backfilling, waterlogging, land reclamation, ditch cleaning,repair existing collectors and laying new ones, replacing wells. The maintenance of soil elements is a very important and not always simple task. Any flooding of the soil can lead to flooding of the runway or apron base, and this is a threat to reduce the strength of the coating or its destruction.

All summer, part of spring and autumn repairs are carried out:

  • cleaning and sealing of seams on runways and aprons, if the coatings are cement-concrete;
  • jointing and sealing joints and cracks on asphalt pavements;
  • repair of asphalt and cement concrete coatings, including overhaul;
  • adding wells and soil subsidence;
  • restoration of hatches lost during the winter;
  • laying new and repairing existing collectors;
  • repair of intra-airport roads.

It would seem that everything is like on ordinary roads, only materials should be allowed for use at airfields, and the quality of work should not be as usual on all roads of the country.

The repair task is issued to the shift daily in the morning. The work, depending on their volume, is carried out either by the airport's aerodrome service, which has the necessary specialists and workers, or by contractors, if the volume of work does not allow them to be performed on their own. Most often, the airport has an asphalt paver, a milling machine, an ice rink, and every little thing like a seam cutter, a bitumen boiler, a seam filler, compressors, a vibrating plate.

For repairs, the strip is closed. At aerodromes with high intensity, this is not easy, because then you need to either change the schedule for the closing period so as not to create massive delays and overload the dispatchers, or work only at night. In recent years, materials have appeared that allow you to change the stove in the strip overnight and in the morning to return the strip to operation. Yes, it is very expensive, but changing the schedule due to repairs at some point becomes more expensive. On aprons, it‚Äôs easier for the time of repair to block the parking lot or part of the taxiing route so as not to damage the aircraft during work.

Inspections are repeated in the fall. Defects formed during the summer are repaired. The need for future summer repairs, land reclamation, and laying of collectors is identified. This information underlies the budget for next year.

Marking


Aprons are streaked with red, yellow, white lines. Runway - white. Coating labeling requirements are established by the Federal Aviation Regulations. You can also refer to the ICAO documents (Appendix 14 to the Convention on International Civil Aviation, Volume I), but the ICAO requirements are slightly different from the Russian ones, but you can understand the markings on foreign airfields. In markers everything is regulated - from the thickness of the line to its shape and color.

The yellow lines mark the taxiing routes of the aircraft, the red lines indicate the parking (maintenance) of the aircraft, and the white lines on the platforms indicate the routes of special vehicles. All runway markings are white. In order for the yellow taxiing lines on concrete surfaces to look more contrast, they are framed in black.

Previously, the parking place was designated only in the form of an octagon, in everyday life called an envelope, where only one aircraft was installed:


1 - taxiing axis of the aircraft;
2 - aircraft parking number;
3 - contour of the aircraft service area;
4 - ways of movement of special vehicles;
5 - T-shaped stop sign for special vehicles;
6 - T-shaped sign of the place where the aircraft stops;
7 - the dividing axis of the path of special vehicles;
8 - permission sign for entry and exit of special vehicles.

At foreign aerodromes, parking lots have long been combined into service areas for which you can put, for example, one wide-body aircraft or two narrow-body (two - wide, four - narrow, etc.). In the Russian Federation, the alignment was legalized only in 2015, although it was used earlier at some airfields.

Every spring and autumn, the markings are updated. If the marking scheme has changed, the old marking is painted over in black / gray or removed by the demarcator, and a new one is applied.

The processing of schemes is done as follows: first, a scheme is developed in conjunction with the traffic service of the State Corporation for ATM, ground services of the airport. If the aircraft is new to the aerodrome, then the airline is asked for the size of the aircraft (wing span, length, location of the landing gear, turning radius). Safety intervals are checked. After coordination with all interested services, the scheme is approved and the aerodrome service begins marking. The scheme is carried out in kind manually using a tape measure, curvimeter, chalk and rope. Such high technology. Not all aerodrome services can afford surveying equipment and related software. So the summer of the aerodrome service engineer takes place in the pose of a drinking horse. At the same time, the accuracy of marking should be quite high, because the safe intervals between the MSs,by taxiing, turning radii should be verified and should not be less than those established by regulatory documents and aircraft characteristics. Making large tolerances is also not welcome: aprons are not rubber. Accuracy of marking is a guarantee of taxiing safety and the absence of collisions on the apron. In any incident involving a collision of two aircraft or aircraft with other obstacles, the commission of inquiry will first check all distances, and if any of them is less than the standard, the blame for the collision will fall on the aerodrome service, even if the pilot did not steer by markup.Accuracy of marking is a guarantee of taxiing safety and the absence of collisions on the apron. Marking updates are easier. The team of the operator of the marking apparatus and a pair of workers under the guidance of the master systematically passes the platforms, taxiways and runways in the spring and autumn, updating the existing markings.

In the remaining sections of the soil elements of the airfield, the height of the grass is selected based on the specific conditions of each aerodrome, including the ornithological situation, that is, the type of bird that does not mind nesting at the airfield. Mowing takes place almost continuously all summer: we have finished the first pass - we need to start the second one already, and so - all summer. Large sections of soil elements of the airfield are mowed by wide-sweeping airfield mowers, the remaining areas by various modifications of the mounted, as well as manual brush cutters.

Runway cleaning from rubber deposits. When braking the aircraft on the runway, rubber bands from pneumatics remain. Over time, their area and thickness become such that the adhesion coefficient decreases, the plane slows down worse. Therefore, the strip of rubber layers is cleaned with a frequency that depends on the intensity of flights.

Impregnation with hydrophobic materials . In order for concrete coatings not to wear out ahead of time from the use of anti-icing chemicals, they are impregnated with special water-repellent compounds certified for use at aerodromes. It is advisable to do this once every two years.

Daily runway and apron inspections and cleaning. The end of winter does not mean that it is possible to abandon inspections of the airfield and measure the coefficient of adhesion. Inspections are carried out four times per shift and at the request of the Tower manager in order to detect foreign objects on the runway: the sealer that has flown out of the seam, the part that has fallen off the plane, a fragment of concrete - everything can cause an accident. The coefficient of adhesion is measured at least once per shift, as well as after rain. Aprons are also constantly inspected. Runways and aprons are periodically swept away from dust and dirt.

Harmonization


Strips of air approaches should be free from obstacles. Now look at the satellite images of your nearest airfield and see how free they are from buildings and what is the height of these buildings.

The area around the airfields is increasingly being built up. Building affects flight safety: there is an increased risk of collision with an obstacle, interference is generated in the operation of the PMC (for example, failures in the timing work), the ornithological situation is deteriorating (large flat roofs attract birds), and visibility is deteriorating.

In 2017, the definition of the aerodrome territory was revised. Now it has been divided into subzones, each of which imposes its own restrictions on the use of land around the airfield: high-altitude, radio-technical, ornithological, industrial, sanitary and epidemiological. The idea was to, after the development and approval of the aerodrome territory, introduce all restrictions on the general plans and the rules of land use and development of settlements. Then anyone who wanted to get land for building could immediately see all the existing restrictions and did not run around all the nearby airfields to coordinate the construction. Good intentions, but the rules for the development and establishment of the near-aerodrome territory were written so that since 2017, only one GA airport in the Russian Federation has established a near-aerodrome territory.

Ornithological provision


Not all airports have a unit that deals with ornithological support for flights. Often these functions are performed by an aerodrome service specialist along with their main responsibilities. The main task of such a specialist is to ensure that the birds stay away from the runway. A variety of tools and equipment are used to scare away birds - from disco balls and flares to bioacoustic installations and birds of prey. However, it is worth noting that the bird gets used to the noise, and to the roar, and to the electronic cries of cell makers warning about the danger. The troubled grandmother wakes up at three in the morning from the noise of an airplane flying 10 kilometers from her and writes a complaint, and the lapwing will nest and take out chicks 50 meters from the edge of the runway, returning to their home airfield from year to year.

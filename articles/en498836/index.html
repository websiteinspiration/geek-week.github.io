How do video games bring billions?




The first major commercial success in the industry occurred in the 1980s. Many of this period is called the "golden age of arcade games." When famous classic arcades such as Pac-man and Space Invaders appeared all over America , the owners of the money were rowing with a shovel. Moreover, in the literal sense - each toy installed on the machine brought a real real coin.

Today, there are two revenue models commonly used by video game publishers: the traditional pay-per-game ( P2P ) model and the more modern free-play model ( F2P ).

For most of the industry‚Äôs modern history, the P2P model acted by default. The developer bears the costs of producing his games, and it is logical that he sells them to consumers in order to recover costs and make a profit.

According to the F2P model, the developer, in fact, distributes his games for free. If players do not want to pay, they do not pay. And the developer runs the risk of being in the red.

League of Legends


League of Legends , one of the most popular video games in the world, is a successful pioneer in the F2P model.

When the developer of Riot Games chose this model (F2P) for his game, he took a big risk. At that time, the model has not yet established itself as suitable for this genre. The main source of income was the sale of so-called ‚Äúskins‚Äù of characters (character skins).

To some extent, the incentive to spend money on the game comes from its longevity - League of Legends recently turned 11 years old. Instead of creating a new world, the developer is constantly improving the existing one, and each iteration is called a new "season".

If an ordinary P2P game is a kind of feature film, League of Legends is more of a lengthy television show. Since 2009, we know the same League of Legends, and for example, for the same period there were 11 different Call of Duty games.

As more and more franchises develop successfully in the form of ‚Äúfree‚Äù games, it becomes obvious that the F2P model will become the main driver of further industry growth. Higher availability of F2P games is also crucial for the rapid development of eSports.

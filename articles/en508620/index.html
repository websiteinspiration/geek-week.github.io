Escaping Boredom - ETL Processes

At the end of winter and the beginning of spring, it became possible to work with the Apache NiFi streaming data delivery tool, new to me. When studying the instrument, the feeling that in addition to the official documentation, the materials for dummies, with practical examples, would not be out of place all the time.







After completing the task, I decided to try to facilitate entry into the world of NiFi.







Background almost unrelated to the article



In February of this year, a proposal was received to develop a prototype system for processing data from various sources. Sources do not provide information at the same time, in different formats. It was necessary to convert it into a single format, collect information from sources in one structure and transfer it to processing. During processing, additional directories from internal databases for enrichment were used. After processing, the data had to be recorded in two databases and a daily report was generated.

The task fit into the concept of ETL . The search for ETL implementations has led to a fairly large number of ETL systems that allow you to build processing processes. Including opensource system.







Apache NiFi was chosen for luck. The prototype was built and delivered to the customer.







Initially, the customer wanted a monolithic application, and he considered the use of NiFi simply as a prototyping tool (I read it somewhere). But after meeting in the vicinity - NiFi remained in the product.







And now the story itself



After the delivery of work to the customer, interest in ETL did not disappear. In April, self-isolation occurred with non-working days and free time needed to be spent with benefit.







When I began to deal with Apache NiFi, it turned out that more or less detailed information is only available on the project website. Russian articles are in many ways just translations of the introductory parts of official documentation. The main drawback is that it is often not clear in what format the parameters should be entered. Google saves, but not always.

Transform nature into decoration with 3D scanning



Jewelry Making with the EinScan Pro 2x 3D Scanner


When asked about the areas in which 3D scanning is used, most people will not hesitate to answer that such equipment is most often used in industry - in metrology and reverse engineering.

More recently, the above industries were almost the only areas of application of 3D scanning, but the situation has changed dramatically in recent years. Modern 3D scanners like the EinScan Pro 2X, proved that the technology of three-dimensional scanning has a huge variety of applications, ranging from small amateur projects to highly professional reverse engineering. The line of 3D scanners EinScan Pro 2X has allowed new users to significantly reduce the initial costs associated with the introduction of 3D scanning and modeling in production, as a result of which you can see an unprecedented increase in creative applications for these technologies.

Due to the fact that modern 3D scanners have become more affordable and easier to maintain, many areas of business that previously could not introduce 3D scanners into production due to the high cost of equipment now actively use three-dimensional scanning technologies to improve business processes. Schnider & Hammer AG is one of the adherents of 3D scanning, and today we will consider one unusual project that this company was able to implement with the help of the EinScan Pro 2X scanner.

A few words about Schnider & Hammer AG


Founded in 1993, Schnider & Hammer specializes in traditional jewelry making for over 25 years, but has always shown interest in modern technologies and their application in-house.

From the very beginning of its existence, the company worked with 3D-design and rapid production tools to deliver quality products to the market as soon as possible. Masters of the company create unusual and surprisingly beautiful jewelry, such as an impressive collection of Weissenstein bracelets.

One of the projects of the jewelry house was the creation of jewelry that would imitate nature, exactly repeating its beauty and character. The surfaces of trees, stones and other natural objects are inimitable, delightful patterns for jewelry, but the implementation of these designs using traditional methods of jewelry production was too complicated. Simply put - forms that freely exist in nature are very difficult to reproduce manually in the workshop.

Choosing the Right 3D Scanner


After much deliberation, Schnider & Hammer decided to purchase a 3D scanner. The craftsmen were interested in this technology and knew that a professional 3D scanner would help to overcome the problem that the manufacturer faced when creating nature-inspired designs. There are many 3D scanners on the market, but not every model meets the specific requirements of the company. The EinScan Pro 2X scanner turned out to be exactly the solution that the master jewelers were looking for: a professional-grade 3D scanner with low initial costs. In addition, the EinScan Pro 2X is very convenient for an inexperienced user and can be used in a variety of business fields, due to its versatility.

Using the EinScan Pro 2X 3D Scanner in Jewelry Production


The biggest problem with Schnider & Hammer when using the EinScan scanner was that the models for scanning were provided by nature itself, that is, they were outdoors. All optical 3D scanners use light to examine an object and build its three-dimensional model, which means that scanning outdoors is a huge problem, regardless of the type of scanner. The company also needed a way to power the scanner outdoors, because all scanners needed an external power source.

The scanner quickly collects data, so that within a few minutes a file from the EinScan software can be sent to a 3D modeling program. The finished digital model is characterized by a high level of detail, reflecting even the smallest irregularities and grooves on the surface of the tree bark. This level of detail can only be created by nature, and it is this property that the Schnider & Hammer brand plans to include in its jewelry collections. The scan result fully reflects the natural original and can be modified and edited using CAD software.

Jewelry or any other items whose creation was inspired by nature, are often not able to convey the beauty, naturalness and energy of the natural prototype. 3D scanning provides a safe way to recreate and digitize the surrounding world, while the multi-functional 3D scanner EinScan Pro 2X allows you to use 3D scanning technology with ease and without high initial investments.

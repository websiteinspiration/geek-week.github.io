How Signal crypto messenger successfully resists wiretapping by US authorities



Against the backdrop of events in the US, the popularity of the Signal secure messenger has grown sharply - from 6,000 to 26,000 downloads per day . This program implements strong cryptography and end-to-end encryption, it is distributed with open source code and works on well-known cryptographic protocols (unlike proprietary MProto ).

But now Signal developers are facing a new threat. The US government is trying to compromise the protection of the messenger and again gain access to the correspondence of users. In 2016, they managed to get a subpoena to remove the correspondence of one user, but because of end-to-end encryption, there was nothing to remove .

The only data that Signal can provide at the request of government services or on the subpoena is only the data that it stores about the user. It:

  • Unix account creation date
  • date of last use in Unix time.

And it's all. No contacts, group information or profile information.


User dossier that Signal provided upon request. Screenshot from court documents

‚ÄúWe developed Signal in such a way that personal data was stored by customers, not ours,‚Äù the developers write . - Signal uses end-to-end encryption, so we never have access to the content of the messages you send. They are visible only to you and the intended recipients. However, Signal also applies this design philosophy to the rest of your data. ‚Äù

Unlike any other popular messengers, Signal does not have access to contacts, social graph, group data, group status information, profile name, profile avatar, location data, search history and so on. There are no trackers, ads, or analytics code in the program code. All information is encrypted, except for the date the account was created and the date it was last used, as shown in the screenshot.


Shot from a lecture by Moxy Marlinspike at the Chaos Computer Club information security conference in December 2019 with a demonstration of the credentials of his personal Signal account

‚ÄúSince we created Signal in order to completely avoid the storage of any confidential information, I can go on stage in front of thousands of people and publicly publish all my account information without revealing anything other than how long I have installed Signal (with since the last time I changed the phone) and the last date I used it (by the way today), ‚Äùsays Moxie Marlinspike, a well-known hacker, cryptanarchist and main program developer.

The main weakness of Signal and similar messengers is the binding to a specific phone number. For example, if an outsider joins a certain group, then he will see the phone numbers of all members of this group. This technique was used by Hong Kong police to identify the protesters in WhatsApp, Signal and Telegram-.

On the other hand, the attacker will not receive the rest of the account data even upon judicial request.

‚ÄúIf you ask the CEO of any other major communications platform to publicly publish your credentials from your platform, he won‚Äôt do that,‚Äù Moxie writes. ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt blame them - this is a lot of data that is inconvenient to share with you, - but this raises the question of how comfortable it is to share the same data with them [the authorities].‚Äù

Any American company is obliged to comply with the rules of American law and provide data at the request of the court, whether WhatsApp or Signal. But in the case of Signal, she simply has nothing to show.

In some countries, after leaks from WhatsApp, even the military and politicians are transferred to the mandatory use of Signal and Wickr crypto messengers. For example, such rules are set for the fighters of the 82nd Airborne Division of the US Army and for the communication of members of the conservative party of Great Britain . So the presence of a Signal on the phone is not a sign of suspicious activity, but rather a sign of common sense.

The lack of personal data in the ownership of the company is the only strategy that ensures the security of this data, the creators of Signal are sure. Accordingly, trust in any company does not come down to trusting the safety of our personal data, but trusting their engineering capabilities and know-how for developing software that will not allow the transfer of personal data from the user and ensures that they are stored only by him and no one else not available, including the company itself.

Signal is a non-profit organization 501 (c) (3), so the software has been and will remain free and free. ‚ÄúOur mission is to increase privacy on the Internet, so we publish our technology and share knowledge in order to encourage other companies to use it in their own products and services,‚Äù writes Moxie Marlinspike. Today, Signal protocol employs several other messengers, although they have not implemented a strict refusal to collect personal information about users and continue to store contacts, conversations, photos and other data for each account on their servers. For example, WhatsApp also uses the secure Signal protocol, but it transfers the user's social graph to the parent company Facebook.The same applies to the commercial companies Telegram FZ LLC and Telegram Messenger Inc. In the case of Signal, onlyThe truncated hash of each phone number from the contact list. Such a hash cannot be directly tampered with, although there is no question of complete secrecy.

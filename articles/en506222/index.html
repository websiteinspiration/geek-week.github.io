Checklists: try not to miss the details

On the very day when the product development process begins, you are already behind schedule and do not fit into the budget.
Don Norman

How often do we encounter situations when, having learned something new and useful, after a while we forget about it without having time to put knowledge into practice?


One day, while reading a chapter in Don Norman's book ‚ÄúDesign of Habitual Things‚Äù about checklists, I realized that simply creating notes and a rare review of them is not the most effective practice. Therefore, I decided to create for myself check lists that I could use in my work, whether planning a new project from scratch or routine closing of another task.


Who is this article for?


Fortunately, I managed to be in different roles, so the checklists cover several areas. Somewhere deeper, somewhere less.


Many of these tips in one form or another have met me repeatedly in various sources.


A significant part of the points are universal and applicable to many industries and professions, but the focus is still on working on web projects from the moment of the idea to the moment it will be implemented.


Also, depending on the size of the projects, some points may be inadequate or not applicable.

Work with ideas


  • Generate many ideas at once. Do not get hung up on one or two ideas.


  • Be creative no matter the circumstances. Do not criticize ideas, neither yours nor others. Even crazy ideas, which often seem obviously wrong, may contain some kind of creative thought. Separate the stages of generation and stages of evaluation of ideas. Later it can be removed and used at the last stage, when you will choose the best idea. Do not give up ideas too soon.

Intel NUC Chaco Canyon - Works Where You Need It



From contests about Intel NUC, we are moving smoothly to the announcements of NUC innovations. However, Chaco Canyon cannot be called a 100% new NUC series - the first information about it appeared back last fall. But only now we know everything about Chaco Canyon, including the cost and the date of appearance on sale. Moreover, this time has already come.

The Chaco Canyon series (which currently includes only one NUC8CCHKR PC model ) is noteworthy for two reasons. Firstly, this is one of the most compact representatives of the NUC class - the height of its case is only slightly more than 3 cm. Secondly, passive cooling of the processor is used here. That is, the computer turned out not only small, but also absolutely silent. This was made possible by using the cost-effective Intel Celeron N3350 processor with a TDP of only 6 watts.

Chaco Canyon can be useful where high performance is not required, but compactness and invisibility are necessary.

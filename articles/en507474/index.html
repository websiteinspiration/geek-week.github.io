Mosquito extermination

Summer has come and the mosquito has come. Of course, preparing for their destruction is already too late, but better late than never.



Introductory.



  1. A typical Russian mosquito can fly 100 meters, and certain species fly by smell up to 5 kilometers that a shark. This means that in order for you to have no mosquitoes, mosquitoes should not be in a radius of 100 meters.
  2. A mosquito breeds every 5 days. Therefore, if less than 1/5 of the mosquito population is destroyed per day, then the methods will be useless.
  3. Mosquitoes need a lot of water. They live near wetlands, or where there is water. In areas, they usually fly out in the evening after a hot day, when dew falls - to get drunk.

Usually these are different devices based on an ultraviolet lamp and a blocking generator with a grid. A blocking generator generates 1000 volts per grid, which leads to electric shock to any insect that approaches it.



An ultraviolet lamp is designed to attract mosquitoes.



In practice, alas, I have not seen working UV lamps. None of them ATTRACTED mosquitoes.

Combined technical methods



We are talking about mosquito traps like Mosquito Killer or Aero.







The principle of the trap is as follows.



  1. The trap warms up to 36 degrees, which attracts mosquitoes.
  2. The trap generates CO2, which attracts mosquitoes.
  3. The trap also sprays an attractant (nonatal, octinenol).

CO2 is sprayed from a cylinder with CO2, or by igniting propane.



With an interval of several minutes, CO2 is released. After the CO2 emission, a powerful fan is turned on, which sucks mosquitoes into the mosquito receiver, in which they die from dehydration during the night.



I recommend using CO2-based systems rather than propane, as safer.

My choice is the Russian development of the Aero Mosquito Elimination System. Surprisingly - made very soundly. No worse than with AliExpress ¬©.



The range is about 10 meters. Putting a few traps along the borders of the site or next to green spaces is a good choice.



The effect of the trap in the form of a decrease in the mosquito population becomes noticeable after 3-4 weeks of use. But do not forget about:



  1. radius of action
  2. breeding rate.


Turn on the trap (they are usually with a timer), you need it when the mosquitoes begin to fly (5 pm), and turn off the clock at 5 am when the mosquitoes stop flying. You can keep it always on, but then you will refill the CO2 cylinder every 3 days.



Important: mosquitoes do not fly in high winds and the trap is useless. Of course, during the rain, too, it is of little use.



Unfortunately, these traps are not equipped with either a humidity sensor or a wind sensor. When cold (10 degrees) mosquitoes do not fly - the trap is useless.

Organizational Methods



Mosquitoes usually live in the grass. There are a lot of them. Therefore, do not be lazy - mow the grass more often. Not only is the mowed lawn beautiful, but there are also fewer mosquitoes.



However, you need to mow everything around - not just your lawn. As I wrote earlier, mosquitoes fly within a radius of 100 meters. Therefore, the lawn of the neighbors should be mowed. The same applies to spraying.



Mosquito nets help the house well. Usually they put frame cheap meshes. They are not comfortable, and where to put them in the winter? It is recommended to use roll nets. They give fewer gaps, and are generally more effective than frame ones. And in winter they can be minimized.

Please note that the mesh should be with sufficient cells so as not to let pass not only adult mosquitoes, but also newly hatched ones (they are usually several times smaller).







Ponds, birds and insects



Special bacteria that destroy mosquito larvae (larvae) have long been bred. They are recommended for settlement in ponds / swamps. Google it.



There are fish that reduce the mosquito population.

The worst enemies of mosquitoes are dragonflies, swallows and swifts.



Basements: a noble nursery of mosquitoes. In basements, the installation of dehumidifiers is recommended. Not only do mosquitoes not live in dry air, but also a dry basement - protection against mold and other unpleasant moments.



The dehumidifier is better to install with MECHANICAL and not electronic control. Because electronic ones are usually reset when there is a power outage, which means you have to turn it on manually. I have a mechanical dehumidifier MASTER DH-711 for 4 years already in the basement and I do not even touch it.







In the dehumidifier, you need to remove the container for collecting water, connect a pipe instead and drain directly into the ground - drill a meter and a half earth, insert the 100th gray pipe there, and cover it. Lower the drain pipe into the pipe.

If we talk about more recent projects, then in February this year, the FNTE team introduced an energy-efficient air conditioning system for historic buildings that need special conditions. It does not require the installation of massive technological boxes that spoil the facades of architectural monuments. The principle of operation of the installation resembles a refrigerator. In summer, hot air enters the heat exchanger, where it gives part of the energy to the coolant - water. It goes to the evaporator of the heat pump, which transfers heat along the freon circuit to the condenser (it is installed on the hood). In winter, energy is exchanged in reverse order.

That is, at almost any temperature, we can "take" heat from the air that is discharged into the street and transfer it to what is pumped into the premises. In this case, very little energy is consumed, and quite a lot of heat is given. It is in the winter, and in the summer - it works in the opposite way, cooling the air coming from the street â€

- Sergey Muraveinikov , Head of the Inventors' Team

According to the developers, air conditioning usually takes about 60% of the energy consumed by the building. The new system will save up to 80% of this volume.

Three ways to combine ground on a printed circuit board

Often when designing printed circuit boards, a situation arises when it is necessary to short-circuit two circuits on a circuit board without connecting them in a circuit. On the circuit, the circuits are not directly connected, so that it is more convenient for the circuitry to perceive the circuit and work with it. Often seek to divide the land of various circuit blocks. Or divide the power supply system of the board into different branches and set each branch separately, and then remove the adjustment resistors, replacing them with a short-circuit trace.

In OrCAD / Allegro, you can connect two different circuits in at least three different ways:

  1. Through the through hole.
  2. Through static polygons.
  3. Through special seats.

Consider all three cases:

Through the through hole

To short the two circuits through the through hole, you must select any through hole on the PCB that belongs to one of the chains that you want to connect.


Selecting the command for assigning and editing the properties of an object

Next, right-click the mouse and select the command to edit properties of the vias - Property Edit from the drop-down list. After this action, the user will appear in a new dialog box, which will allow you to assign a new NET_SHORT property to the vias. The value of this property must match the name of the circuit with which it is necessary to carry out the closure.


Assigning a new NET_SHORT property to an object

After the user clicks on Apply, and then on OK, he will see that the polygon has ceased to flow around the hole and passes through it. In other words, the chains are shorted.



View of the hole and polygon before and after assigning the Net_Short property.

Through static polygons

This method is very simple. Its essence is to draw two static polygons belonging to two different chains, and pushing the boundaries of these polygons onto each other. Thus, shorting the circuits at the desired point on the circuit board is obtained.


Shorting 2 chains using polygons

Through special seats

The essence of this method is to create a footprint similar to a resistor or capacitor. However, its sites can be located on any layer of the printed circuit board: both internal and external. And the sites will be shorted together by a small polygon or a segment of the conductive line. It is more convenient to short-circuit the polygon, since it can be located on a certain subclass and included as needed. Those. when the board is adjusted using shorting resistors, this polygon is disabled. When the device is developed and launched into mass production, the layer with short polygons is included in the Gerber files.

Also, the user does not need to remember all the coordinates where one circuit is shorted to another. Since the connection occurs through the footprints, they can be called in a certain way and their coordinates will always be present in the report on the components of the printed circuit board.
In order to create such a component, it is enough to take any footprint from a resistor or capacitor, call it a unique name and place it on the developed circuit board.

Then you need to edit the pads by choosing Tools ‚Üí Padstack ‚Üí Modify Design Padstack. After activating the command, the desired contact pad is selected.


Select CP for editing

In the pad editor, you need to switch the pad type to Die Pad, and then select the desired layer on the Design Layers tab.


Changing the type of pads


Change the layer on which the pads will now be located

After changing the layer, select the File ‚Üí Update to design and exit command in the pads editor window.


Element KPs have moved to the layer selected by the user.

The component pads will be moved to the layer selected by the user.

To realize a closure, you can resize the pads so that they intersect. This will be the easiest way out. You can also draw a connecting polygon.

How to choose the right power for your UPS. We analyze the example of Eaton



Reliable protection of computers, performance and long life of uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) depend on the properly selected capacity of the UPS in relation to the load. In this post, we will consider simple rules for selecting a UPS for power - they will help both save the budget and remain confident that these devices will provide protection in the event of a sudden power outage or blackout.

Load calculation for UPS power selection


When selecting a UPS, operate with three values:

  1. load power
  2. UPS rated power
  3. required battery life of the UPS.

These are the main parameters, but there are still nuances, and we will also talk about this below.

With the determination of the load power, everything is relatively simple - the power of all devices that are planned to be connected to one UPS is summed (usually this is a group of devices located next to each other). Then, the received numbers are summarized for all UPSs serving such groups of devices. The power consumed by monitors, a printer, speakers, a router, an external drive, etc., can be found on the device labels. For a PC or server, the power indicated on the power supply is taken.

Experts will say that this is a very approximate calculation of the load, since in different modes the power consumption of each device can differ significantly from that indicated on the labels or in the specifications for the power supplies. This will be absolutely true, but they will agree that in this way power is determined ‚Äúby the upper limit of consumption‚Äù. If the actual load power as a result is lower than calculated, then nothing bad will happen.

Next comes the first nuance - it is associated with the rated power of the UPS: Usually it is indicated in volt-amperes (VA) and is displayed as numbers in the model name of the UPS. For example, the Eaton 5P 850 UPS model has a rated power of 850 VA. In this case, the load power is calculated in watts (W), since it is in watts that the power supplies of computers, monitors and other IT devices are marked. The next parameter, the value of which should be known before choosing the UPS power, is the desired UPS operating time in battery mode. The catalog for each UPS model provides an estimated battery life at a load of 50% and 70% of rated power.

Usually, it takes 5 minutes to correctly shut down the operating system on computers, especially if you automate this process using the UPS monitoring and management software, either from a third-party or from a UPS manufacturer (for example, Eaton Intelligent Power Manager). However, if you need much more time to maintain computers, you should choose more powerful UPS models or even buy and install additional external batteries. Such external batteries are available for UPS models operating in the corporate sector.

Monitoring and managing shutdown loads


After the calculations are done, the UPS is purchased and the load is connected, it is advisable to monitor the actual level of load during operation. This can be done using the UPS service display or using remote monitoring software. Based on these observations made at different operating modes of the loads, we can finally determine whether the UPS power is correctly selected for the protected devices.


Screenshot of UPS management software. Source: Eaton

For remote load monitoring, Eaton offers companies the proprietary Intelligent Power Manager (IPM) uninterruptible power system management software .

The basic version for ten UPS is available free of charge, a paid license is required to control more sources. IPM provides remote control of the corporate infrastructure of guaranteed energy supply from any computer using the web interface. In addition to physical servers, IPM supports power management of virtual machines - you can automatically shut down the VMware, HyperV, RedHat KVM, and Xen hypervisors.

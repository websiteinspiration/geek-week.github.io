How to increase the completeness of massive online courses

The period of self-isolation led to the explosion of the popularity of educational online platforms. Well- known MOOC providers reported record inflows of traffic and students, and many large companies launched their own anti-crisis courses . But it is worth recognizing that the real success indicator of any educational project is not the number of applicants, but the percentage of graduates .

There are some problems with the MOOC format.

Dmitry Zatsepin, the director of the Aktion-ICFER educational business, talks about the reach factors of mass online courses, as well as his experience with Aktion products.


Photo NeONBRAND Unsplash.com

Freedom versus Completion


Various studies show that about 85% of students in the average MOOC course drop out of school, and only some projects can boast 40% completion. Students leave classical educational structures much less often, but it is worth recognizing that a direct comparison of online schools with universities will be inappropriate, since the MOOC concept gives much more freedom - for example, the ability to switch between courses.

The flexibility and availability of MOOC solutions has a downside. Low entry threshold allows you to sign up for an almost unlimited number of classes. The fact of the recording in this case is only an indicator of the interest of the audience. Specialists from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the University of Murcia (University of Murcia, Spain) studied the statistics of online courses taught by their colleagues from MIT and Harvard at the EdX site. It turned out that more than half of the students enrolled in the MOOC do not touch educational materials.

MOOC completeness is associated, in particular, with those for whom the course is intended. Educational programs for a wide audience and beginners are often only a reflection of our long-term ambitions. A huge number of students subscribe to these classes. For example, at Udemy, more than 6 million users study Excel and 1.5 million learn to trade stocks - of course, not all of the signatories will complete these courses.

Highly specialized programs will have higher reach rates. For example, Harvard Business School online courses for entrepreneurs and accountants complete about 85%students. A similar pattern is observed for Aktion courses with 84% completion. High indicators in both cases are due to the fact that students receive in-depth specialized knowledge that they need to work. Moreover, the qualification and salary of a specialist and even the success of the company in which he works can depend on the successful assimilation of materials.

In the case of MOOC, designed for a wide audience, users have fewer external incentives to take the course to the end - this knowledge may not be necessary for students to work, there is no urgency in studying the topic - therefore, students often cover procrastination. In 2014, Stanford specialists conducted a survey among students who enrolled in university MOOC, but did not finish the program. More than 70% of them named the main cause of failure it is the desire to postpone classes for later.

But procrastination is not the only reason for the low percentage of reach. Of the entire course program, the listener may be interested in only a few individual topics. After studying them, he sees no reason to move on. At Coursera they say that this behavior is inherent in most of their students - they look for information pointwise and listen only to the lectures they need.

To avoid this situation, course developers can tailor them to individual student requirements. We at Aktion offer the user to take an entrance test to determine their level of knowledge on the topic. After that, we get the opportunity to adapt the course to the listener's request, so that all the information is relevant for him, and he does not quit training in the middle.

However, if we talk about specialized MOOC courses, in some cases they are even more valued than diplomas of higher educational institutions.

Another factor affecting the completion of courses is their cost, according to teachers at the Harvard Business School. The high price plays the role of a filter - only those who really need professional knowledge buy access to the program. For example, a Harvard course in economics, accounting, and management costs more than two thousand dollars . Users who paid this amount are motivated to complete the training.

The most expensive courses for CEOs are MBA programs. And the audience that pays more money is the most demanding in terms of the quality of knowledge. Knowledge, not a document, is their priority.

We organized free anti-crisis courses, but this practice is not wide. It was our step towards assistance, our reaction to what is happening, to the pandemic and its consequences for workers.

But the appointment of a high price per course does not guarantee growth in completion. Consider what else can be done to increase this figure.

Update content more often


To launch a successful MOOC, it‚Äôs not enough to upload presentations and lectures to some new platform. Content must be adapted to the habits of the audience and the features of online sites. These questions were asked during the development of the first mass online courses at MIT. To solve them, the leader of one of the courses, biologist and geneticist Eric Lander (Eric Lander), even hired a whole team of programmers, videographers and specialists in the development of educational materials. However, over the past time, MIT has not been able to develop a universal approach to organizing mass online courses - their developers continue to regularly review and supplement the content of training programs.

One reason for updating course content more often is the principle of asynchronous communication specific to MOOC. If in a traditional university setting you can conduct a lesson and forget about it, then the MOOC material is available to students for a long time after publication - it becomes easy to ‚Äúcatch‚Äù the author on the fact that his words are no longer relevant. In such a situation, confidence in the rest of the course content and in the platform itself falls.

If the course consists of only videos and slides, this can ‚Äúlull‚Äù the listener, inspire him with a false sense of understanding - and in the end the material will be easily forgotten. As an alternative format, search engine developers for free online Class Central courses offerrecord screencasts. And we at Aktion focus on an accessible and structured presentation of material. Our courses often raise issues of legislation and other topics that are potentially difficult to grasp - so that students do not have problems with their assimilation, we have developed a special system: it covers video content and texts, charts, and diagrams.

Help students with navigation


Whatever your content, its perception directly depends on the user interface and the opportunities that it provides to the audience. Bad UX, coupled with a non-intuitive UI, can discourage any desire to learn. According to Adobe, 38% of users will close a site or application if they don‚Äôt like the interface.


Photo Fabian Grohs Unsplash.com

Students come to the course with certain expectations. They are due to the most common UX patterns for web and mobile applications. The faster students understand the interface, the faster they will go to the core of the educational process. The developer of the MOOC course will also benefit from a competent UI. He will receive fewer questions related to the functionality of the service, and the freed up time can be spent on improving the content.

We have our own powerful bureau with UX-specialists, sometimes we hire third-party developers, therefore products and solutions are constantly being improved and modernized. Continuous work is carried out both with paid and with free sites.

- Dmitry Zatsepin

In addition to the generally accepted UI patterns, it is worth remembering about game and interactive elements like various indicators of educational progress. Research confirms their effectiveness - gamification helps to improve the quality of education. Gamification evangelist and co-founder of Dopamine creative agency Gabe Zichermann notes that the game-based learning format helps students memorize information 40% more efficiently than classical methods. Students become more interested in learning, which in turn increases the completeness of the MOOC.

In a broad sense, the term UX describes the user‚Äôs experience of working with an online resource. The definition includes not only interaction with the graphical interface, but also the understandability of all instructions. In MOOCs that do not take UX factors into account, chaos may ensue. For example, in 2013, participants in one of the Coursera courses complained that the site did not provide detailed instructions for the assignments. Users were asked to join in groups, but were not explained how to do this. Forums filled the threads where students tried to figure out what to do next. Many students called the course organization ‚Äúa mess‚Äù (a mess).

If users cannot figure out how the educational process is organized, they will abandon training. To avoid this, it is important to orient them in time. One possible practice is to offer a short demo course that talks about the key components of the learning platform.

Establish communication


Communication on MOOC platforms can be divided into two types: ‚Äúuser-users‚Äù and ‚Äúuser-mentor‚Äù.

Online courses for a wide audience cannot provide the same level of involvement in the educational process as, for example, a university. One of the reasons experts call the lack of live communication between students. Various chat rooms and forums become a solution to the problem, where students freely exchange experiences, discuss tasks and help each other. The authors of the courses benefit from such lively communication. They get the opportunity to monitor what students liked and did not like, so that in the future they could modify the training program and improve the user experience.

In some cases, the process of collective assessment of work helps to engage students in online communication. As a result, students get the opportunity to further consolidate knowledge or find errors on their own in their decision. However, in this case, the communication "user-mentor" may be practically absent, and this format is usually used in the framework of courses that do not imply the acquisition of highly specialized, professional skills.

The contract for the development of the site in terms of project management (theory + sample)

A development contract that forms the correct interaction between the customer and the contractor, covers risks and regulates all stages of work is a rather difficult thing. We built our 2 years, collecting feedback from customers on the one hand and the project team on the other. Stratosphere is a web integrator specializing in e-commerce, b2b and digital transformation. Accordingly, the entire article will be further written on the example of web development.

Small business often refers to the contract as a ‚Äúpiece of paper‚Äù and formality. From the seller of trailers in the region it is quite possible to hear that ‚Äúyes what is your agreement to me, the main thing is that I said a word‚Äù.

Big business, on the contrary, understands the significance of the contract, but checks it with the help of full-time lawyers, as a rule, not versed in the development. For them, development looks something like "take the site from the warehouse and ship it to the buyer." They do not understand the essence and are triggered by familiar words like ‚Äúterm‚Äù, ‚Äúfine‚Äù, etc.

A separate category is those who are trying to insert into the contract clauses on which you are initially to blame, in order to manipulate them later.

Of course, all three approaches are wrong.

We in the studio are considering a development contract in terms of PMBOK.

PMBOK (Project management body of knowledge) - a set of knowledge on project management. It is universal and, in fact, suitable for anything: from developing a site to building a bridge.

From the point of view of PMBOK, a contract is a document that authorizes a project, allocates resources for it, appoints those responsible, determines the rules for managing requirements, risks, and communications.

Requirements management


The contract establishes in what order and by what documents the requirements for the project are regulated. Requirements can be formed as

  • Specifications
  • Project architecture
  • Prototypes
  • Technical task
  • Tickets with additional requirements arising in the process

As a rule, at the stage of signing the contract there is only a part of this - for example, only the specification.

The contract establishes that analytics is carried out within it, the architecture of the project is created, then a prototype is built on its basis, and then the technical task.

From the point of view of legislation (Art. 703, clause 3 of the Civil Code of the Russian Federation), everything that is not specified directly in the contract is done at the discretion of the contractor.

Accordingly, the requirements for the project must be necessarily tied to the contract, the procedure for their influence on the execution of work is fixed.

Edits are a separate part of the requirements. From the point of view of contract work, revision is bringing the work in line with previously agreed requirements. But in the design, as a rule, the edits assume that the work is done according to the requirements, but after the execution they have expanded / changed (here, repaint, here change, and make the man in the illustration less bearded). This is an established practice and it is imperative to fix in the contract how many iterations of such edits are permissible.

Communication management


The contract should regulate the communication of the parties, protecting both parties as much as possible from chaotic communications and unrecorded obligations. For example, it would be unreasonable to gather the whole team every day for a meeting if the time for it is not fixed and not taken into account in payment.

What can be fixed


  • Ticket system (address, service)
  • Setting tasks only within the ticket
  • Evaluation of tasks only on the basis of information contained in the ticket
  • Mandatory call and meeting report to the ticket

It is important to limit the number of communications so that the project does not begin to consume more resources than is allocated to it.

Here it is important to connect communications with requirements. At what point the set Ticket becomes a task that must be completed, how it affects the timing and cost, in what form it is agreed.

The contract also fixes the procedure for coordinating the results of work. For example, we ask you to sign mock-ups and prototypes, because there are times when the customer manager agreed with us one design option, and with his manager another.

Resource management


Resources are allocated to the project. In the case of software product development, this is usually the time of a specialist.

This means that the contract should fix how much time and which specialists will be allocated, how it is fixed and what will happen if the resource is exceeded, depending on the reasons for this excess.

For example, the merger, the contractor did not meet certain labor costs due to an incorrect assessment, the task is being completed at his expense. And if due to incorrect data from the customer, then at the expense of the customer.

Management of risks


In a development project, for example, an online store, there is a set of risks that a contract must necessarily fix.

We will analyze some of these risks.



The contractor did not allocate the resource on time and was late with the work.

This is one of the simplest and most understandable risks. In this case, the contract determines whether there are fines and at what point they occur. He also determines at what time delay the deadline for the work becomes irrelevant and is no longer paid.

The customer did not provide the materials necessary for work on time.

Access to services for integration, upload files from corporate ERP, etc. At the same time, the idle employees' time is ‚Äúburned out" and it incurs losses. The contract determines the procedure in this case. For example, a late fee may be levied, or the task is surrendered to demo data, or the task is excluded from the contract and done separately, and the project is surrendered without it.

Work has lost relevance and the project should be minimized

In this case, the contract should regulate the conditions for the divergence of the parties - whether they will complete the current stage and in what volume, how and in what order it is paid.

The work has been completed, but the customer does not participate in the acceptance or does not get in touch.

In this case, the contract regulates the procedure - for example, the act is sent to the contact address or via EDI and the absence of a response in due time means the signing of the act.

The project has lost its working capacity.

The contract regulates in which cases it is the responsibility of the customer, and in which it is the contractor. For example, the Contractor‚Äôs responsibility may not be

  • Content
  • Integration
  • Third Party Intervention in the Code
  • Change access to the site
  • Hosting crash

The term of work falls on a state weekend.

It is reasonable if the work on the weekend is not agreed, to fix the period in working days.

The data provided is incorrect.

For example, the Customer provided the upload files from the ERP, the Contractor integrated them into the site, and the Customer then changed the format of the downloaded data.
The contract should regulate in this case the payment for additional work, or the delivery of the project to the data case.

Summary of conclusions

An agreement is a basic document authorizing a project. It captures all the interactions of the parties: from requirements to risks. How well designed it is depends on how easy the project will be and how friendly the work of the customer and the contractor will be.

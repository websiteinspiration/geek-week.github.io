Until 2007: The Perfect Chain of Events



Until 2007, a series of circumstances eventually gave birth to what is today known as DevOps.



Lean manufacturing has already established itself as the best practice. Also known as the Toyota Production System , lean manufacturing seeks to optimize processes in the production hall. (By the way, Toyota leadership was originally inspired by the original assembly line methods introduced by Ford Motor Company.) Continuous improvement is a mantra for lean manufacturing. In practice, the following paths are constantly evaluated:



  1. Maintaining stock levels of raw materials and finished products at a minimum . Lean manufacturing means the minimum amount of stocks of raw materials for the production of goods and the minimum number of finished products awaiting distribution by orders or shipment.
  2. Minimizing the order queue . Ideally, if the orders received immediately go to the completed state. The key metric for lean manufacturing will always be the time from order to delivery.
  3. Maximizing the efficiency of the production process . Reorganization of processes and improved automation are combined to produce goods as quickly as possible. Each production site along the entire path (cutting, welding, assembly, testing, etc.) is evaluated for inefficiency.

2007-2008: Frustrated Belgian



Belgian consultant, project manager and agile practitioner Patrick Debois accepted an appointment from the Belgian government to help with the migration of data centers. In particular, he was engaged in certification and availability testing. His responsibilities required him to coordinate actions and build relationships between software development teams and server, database and network maintenance teams. His disappointment at the lack of cohesion and the walls that share the methods of development and operation, have annoyed him. Striving for the best soon led Debois to action.

In 2008, at an Agile conference in Toronto, Andrew Schaefer proposed moderating a specially designed informal meeting to discuss Agile Infrastructure.". And only one person came to discuss the topic: Patrick Debois. Their discussion and exchange of ideas advanced the Agile system administration concept. In the same year, Debois and Schaefer formed the moderately successful Agile Systems Administrator group at Google.

With the growing number of supporters, DevOpsDays was first held in the United States of America in Mountain View, California, immediately after the annual Velocity Conference. Fast forward to 2018: Over 30 DevOpsDays conferences planned, including dozens in the United States.

DevOps for the future



It is worth describing DevOps rather as a journey, or perhaps aspiration, rather than as a final destination. DevOps, like lean manufacturing, is committed to continuous improvement, increased productivity and efficiency, and even continuous deployment. Automated tools for supporting DevOps continue to evolve.



Much has been achieved since the creation of DevOps in the last decade, and we expect to see even more in 2018 and in the future.

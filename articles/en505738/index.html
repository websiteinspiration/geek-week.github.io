A bit about periodograms of time series

Hello, Habr!


Today I want to talk about the periodogram and one of its possible applications in the field of time series analysis. With its help, you can determine how well the constant, seasonal and random component stood out, as well as make general conclusions about the structure of the time series. In the article, I propose to look at how a periodogram is built and to analyze model and real examples. To all interested - welcome under cat.


Bit of theory


A time series is a sequence of data measured at some, often equal, time intervals. In the classical case, it is assumed that the time series consists of three components: trend (T_n), seasonal (S_n) and random (E_n). Classical models of time series:

in the additive case and

in the multiplicative.


Whatever the goal in the study of time series, it is often desirable at the very beginning to try to separate and analyze each component. I will explain with a few examples why this might be necessary. Knowing the trend or tendency of a series, it is possible to make predictions several points ahead and talk about the nature of the ongoing process in any long-term perspective. Knowing the nature of the seasonal component, it is possible to formulate various hypotheses, as well as adjust the forecast depending on the nature of seasonality. A random variable can be investigated to understand how much the process under study is subject to random influences, and try to exclude this random part when constructing an approximation or forecast. For the selection and analysis of each part, special statistical techniques and methods are used.

2020 Digital Breakout Online Hackathon: How It Was





Four days ago, the second hackathon of the large-scale Digital Breakthrough competition ended. This year the competition was held online and included three events: two hackathons and a championship. I managed to participate in the second online hackathon, and in this article I want to share my experience.



Disclaimer: everything described in the article reflects the personal experience of the author and may not coincide with the opinion of other participants.



For the past three years I have been a regular at various IT meetings and conferences, but I was wary of hackathons. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãcreating a cool application in two days seemed impossible to me, the risk of not being able to cope was enormous, and the profit was doubtful. Yes, and the series on the weekend will not see themselves. However, the friends who participated in the hackathons enthusiastically talked about how they had a great time, so in the end the curiosity began to outweigh the doubts, and the last straw was the Reksoft post, after reading which I decided to register for the Digital Breakthrough online hackathon No. 2. Actually, I am writing this post for those who doubt and hope that it will help to make a decision. I must say right away that reality has exceeded all my expectations.



Team search



The first task to be dealt with after registration is finding a team. At Digital Breakout, the team search process was organized very conveniently: a list of teams to which you could apply was available in your account. You could immediately see what composition in each team and what competencies its participants have. Teams could also see a list of your competencies, which were indicated during registration, and decide whether you fit them or not. I do frontend, so I applied to teams where there is no frontend. The organizers also made a telegram chat, where they could find the team using the hashtag. It took me two days and five attempts to find a team: the first 4 teams after a short ‚Äúinterview‚Äù, in which I talked about my skills and experience, preferred other front-endors to me.



Cases



Five cases were available at the hackathon for the solution, which the team had to prioritize. The earlier the team ranks the cases, the higher the likelihood that the team will drop the case that is selected as the first priority. Our team was lucky: we got the case that we selected first - the development of a corporate portal for Gazprombank, in which employees could offer their ideas, discuss topics of interest to them, and take surveys. Finally, the choice of case for our team became known to us two days before the start of the hackathon. Until that moment, I studied the technology that I would have to work with if we had the second priority case we selected. I didn‚Äôt prepare for the other cases, because I was too lazydid not have enough time. In the VKontakte group of the Digital Project, video discussions of all cases that took place on the eve of the RIF were available: it was possible to get acquainted with the case-holder companies and understand the problems of the case. There were other useful materials - for example, a master class on conducting presentations in an online format. In general, I believe that the organizers did everything possible so that the teams could properly prepare for the hackathon. After the start of the hackathon, which was supposed to last 36 hours, materials on the case, in which the problems and features of the case were described in detail, were available in the personal account. The organizers also created a separate telegram chat for each case, in which it was possible to ask questions to experts, and published a video tour of the checkpoint system, which I will discuss later.



Event program



After the hackathon started, a schedule of three checkpoints appeared in your account, which was a discussion of intermediate results with experts. Each team was assisted by three experts: a functional expert (helps with immersion in the subject matter of the case and the industry), a technical expert (helps with the code) and a tracker (controls the achievement of the team‚Äôs goals, helps with the product). The teams also had the opportunity to speak on the pitch: presenting their product to an expert, after which the expert could ask questions and give feedback. During the hackathon, various entertainment activities were carried out - a competition for the best online business card of the team, online charging, online meditation. Since I wrote code continuously, I was not able to participate in the last two.



By the first checkpoint, we managed to make a mobile and desktop design of our application, which I proudly showed to experts. The tracker praised our design, but said that this is not enough, because a good design is not a reason to use the application. In addition, this does not make us any better than other teams - we will just be another team that made the web application. Honestly, at that moment I even felt a little resentment towards the tracker: the task of the case is to create a web application where employees can put forward their ideas, we have done our job well, what else is needed? Then I realized that I thought too much as a performer, and the task of the hackathon is to come up with a unique approach to solving the problem. I asked the tracker what could motivate him to use the application, and he replied that it would be important for him,if he could continue to follow how his ideas develop and are implemented. We realized that this is a cool idea, and implemented it in our application. On the following checkpoints, the tracker helped us with the presentation. Presentation is the most important part of working at the hackathon: it‚Äôs not enough to make a cool product, you still need to convey its key values ‚Äã‚Äãto the jury. The tracker helped us make the presentation in such a way as to emphasize the details that emphasize our decision. I presented this presentation on the pitch, the expert praised our idea, but said that it‚Äôs a pity that you can‚Äôt create groups to work on initiatives. We picked up this idea and in a couple of hours implemented it in design and in code. In total, by the time of protection, our team had a backend and prototypes of a web application and a mobile application ready,in which all the main declared features were implemented (we did not manage to make secondary features in 36 hours). The defense was a three-minute presentation, during which the team talked about its decision. No questions were asked about the defense. A few hours after the defense announced a short list of teams that were in the top ten, and then three prizes. Our team took sixth place.



What did you like in the hackathon



Most of all in the hackathon I liked the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãpumping my skills as a product developer. My team and I also liked the high-quality organization of the hackathon - we did not notice any problems and overlaps, the communication between the team and the experts was very well organized. Acquaintance and the opportunity to work with new people, exchange opinions and experience became a pleasant bonus. We have received many positive impressions and are going to participate in the following hackathons.

So why doesn't the developer want to understand your business?



In search of meaning and purpose



The goal of any business is the same - to benefit other people. For this purpose they pay with fiat money and social capital.



My experience is that the developer often does not see the benefits that bring other people.



Therefore, it is difficult for him to evaluate his contribution - he sees only the code and money for this code.



Accordingly, his motivation (to rewrite everything on RUST) is based on "writing a top code", and not "releasing features (a synonym for" benefit ") for users."



Explain



Typically, firms have different departments: marketing, tech support, analytics, sales, strategy. And far from everyone, there is a semi-autonomous reservation called the ‚Äúdevelopment department‚Äù, which they communicate with through a special diplomat-translator (PM, Team Lead).



In practice, it is not customary to allow a developer to a client or a business. The segregation, isolation of developers from business processes does not allow them to better understand the tasks. Sometimes it is justified, sometimes not.



This is exactly what apartheid separates developers from those to whom they benefit. Over time, developers are increasingly moving away from business reality in a country of clean development. Which often ignores simple human joys and pains.



Recommendation



So that your developers do not turn into autistic specialists who speak a mixture of matan and Haskell, gradually introduce them to customers and business. It is important that the developer from time to time hear directly from the customer ‚Äúthank you, this is fire!‚Äù and "well, EPT, this is no good."

How to achieve this



I got it like this:



  1. Sometimes I drive the developer to meetings with the business.
  2. From time to time I invite you to call customers.
  3. At the end of each month, I arrange a meeting where I tell you what indicators have been achieved and what benefits have been brought.
  4. I give the opportunity to the client / business to work directly on the task with the programmer.


It is important to note! I am always opposed so that someone other than a responsible person could set tasks for developers directly, but the points above do not contradict this rule.



All this, of course, occurs during business hours and is indicated as the personal tasks of the developer in the task tracker. Such activities should be done at least 1-2 per week, and for beginners it is possible more often.



Let the developer know that he can enjoy more than just paying or writing code. But also from the realization of the benefits that it brings to other people. From the inevitable buzz and pain that are present when solving business problems together with other capable people.


Yes, there are self-contained comrades who do not want to interact with anyone. They even communicate with colleagues with difficulty. And this is not about shyness, it is about aggressive isolation, sweet misanthropy. Useful character trait, but not for collaboration on complex projects.

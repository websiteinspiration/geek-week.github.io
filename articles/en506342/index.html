How an automation system solves the problems of a sports club

The income of most sports clubs depends on state subsidies and sponsorship. Independent financial activity is not always successful. Half-empty stands, low ticket sales and paraphernalia are common problems. At the same time, the club can conduct vigorous marketing activities and spend considerable money on attracting people to the hall.

The Automated Fan Management System (FRM) helps to increase sales: it is a convenient tool for researching the audience, for establishing contact with each fan and increasing his loyalty. In addition, using FRM reduces reporting costs.

In the FRM system, you can work with directories and reports, select information on certain parameters using filters, and upload it as a file. For example, you can select data for preferential categories of fans or information about all tickets sold for the season.



How is FRM created?


The first thing we do during cooperation with a sports organization is to conduct marketing research. We analyze the history of sales, statistics, the audience of groups in social networks, site data, database of phone numbers. Pictures of people in the stands and a special program based on artificial intelligence technologies help us determine the gender and age composition of the audience. Phone numbers - geography of orders. Sales volumes - the demand for certain events, certain places in the stands.



To work on a process automation project for the Perm Parma basketball club Parma, we also conducted a survey among fans (more than five hundred people took part in it) and several in-depth interviews, both with club staff and fans. People told what they think about the club, why they come or don‚Äôt come to the matches, what they would like to see or receive. We examined fans' hobbies to understand: what does a basketball game compete with in the sphere of personal and family leisure? And, of course, we were interested in the media resources that they use: sites, radio, television channels, print media. The results of such a study help to better understand the audience of the sports club and decide what to do next. Based on the results, the creation of an automated system for working with fans may be recommended.



The second step is to identify the sources of information needed for FRM. Any sports club already has a lot of information about its fans - it remains to collect, organize and use it wisely. Sources of data for FRM can be: a ticket and subscription sales system, a mobile application, attributes store sales data, a website, social networks, a closed fan club, and voucher distribution data. While working with sources, an analysis of the completeness and purity of the data is carried out. Some of the information may not be needed for FRM, and some may not be enough for the tasks. Some data does not exist in its pure form, but it can be obtained from what it is: for example, calculate the average cost of a ticket in a season or calculate the total amount spent by a fan to attend matches.



Next, the data warehouse is designed: its structure, data processing rules are thought out, a web interface is created, reports are generated. In FRM, which we developed for Parma, the administrative interface allows the club employee to work with all received and stored data, create selections, upload their result as a report file, make corrections and upload new data.

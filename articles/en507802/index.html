Cybersecurity and the pandemic: 5 major threats to business





The worldwide spread of the COVID-19 pandemic is coming to naught (or at least I want to believe it). But the impact that she had on the whole world, primarily on business, will be felt for a very long time. If we talk about cybersecurity, then not even all aspects of the impact of the epidemic are still analyzed and understood.



Cybercriminals during the pandemic developed frenzied activity. So, in early June, Dmitry Medvedev, Deputy Chairman of the Security Council of the Russian Federation, spoke about the 85% increase in cybercrime in the Russian Federation. Over the past few months, the number of cases of such crimes has exceeded 80 thousand. More than half are classified as severe and especially severe. But what caused such activity of cybercriminals?



According to experts , several factors of influence are now operating at once.





Some companies, sending employees to work remotely, provide them with corporate equipment, software, digital keys and the like tools that make it possible to work from home without any problems. First of all, without problems in terms of information security, because corporations care about protecting their data.



But not all companies have such opportunities. Most employees working from home use their own hardware, software, and other tools. The IT infrastructure was being rebuilt very quickly, and the main objective of the business was the uninterrupted business and organizational processes, while the requirements of information security were not priority. Accordingly, the number of potential victims of virtual scammers has increased many times over in a short time.



Criminals use common tools like phishing, malware, etc. Nothing new here. But all this is used many times more actively than before. According to Trend Micro, the number of malicious URLs associated with COVID-19 has increased by an order of magnitude in just two months.







In addition, new versions of malware have appeared that infect victims' devices. Mailing lists have generally become a gold mine for cybercriminals, because now users are willing to read the news, and do not follow safety precautions when opening emails with interesting news. It is clear that many mailings are formed by cybercriminals.







The situation has reached a critical level, so the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Protection Agency The United States (CISA) issued a series of recommendations that said: "Cybercriminals can take advantage of the general concern about COVID-19 and put in place phishing attacks and misinformation of users."



Late detection of cyberattacks and delayed response



Many companies have an information security department, whose employees ensure that cybercriminals are not able to compromise hardware and software, as well as steal valuable information. But because of the pandemic, information security experts, as well as representatives of other specialties, were sent to work home. Someone stayed in the office, but this is rather the exception.



Not always the whole range of corporate information security tools is available to employees who work from home. Accordingly, the detection of cyber attacks is becoming more complicated, as is the timely response to them. And cybercriminals are making more successful attacks than before. Successful attacks are also committed against government organizations. So, according to BloombergLast week, the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services was targeted for cyber attacks.



According to experts , now companies should pay much more attention to the development of attacks within the network, regularly checking to see if they were previously attacked. Due to a shift in focus in the near future, the key factors for successful protection will be constant monitoring of information security incidents, in-depth analysis of network traffic and a retrospective analysis of network events.



Work from unsafe places



This paragraph is not about houses and apartments. Not in all countries and regions, people have everything they need for comfortable online work. In most cases, they forget about this, saying only about the need to deploy the information security perimeter of the house.



Millions of people do not have the Internet or even computers at home, but they need to work remotely. Accordingly, these people go to Internet cafes when it comes to the complete absence of computer equipment or to public places with free internet. Everything has been said about protecting information when connecting to public WiFi networks, so we won’t repeat it. But now this problem has become much more urgent than before.



In particular, because the self-isolation mode is being removed or has already been removed in many regions. But companies are in no hurry to return people to offices. And this means that a wave of people wishing to work from cafes, fast food chains and other public places where there are no cyber defense tools at all is coming.



Mass layoffs



This is another not too obvious factor, which, nevertheless, has a powerful effect on the increase in the number of cases of cybercrime. It is about reducing staff, including representatives of information technology. Someone is looking for a new place, but for someone it is easier to use their own knowledge and experience in the field of IT for personal gain. Accordingly, the army of cybercriminals is replenished, and the number of acts committed by them is increasing.



The economic crisis intensifies the problem, which began to develop almost simultaneously with the pandemic and largely thanks to it.



An even bigger problem is the actions of cybercriminals in relation to people who have lost their jobs. Now a large number of false job sites are spreading over the network, hundreds of thousands of fake job offers are being sent. People who need it open letters with similar offers and infect their devices, as a result of which attackers seize control of them, receiving various kinds of information, including access to bank accounts.



In addition, people, seeking to save money, are looking for inexpensive or completely free offers from different companies. An example is a free subscription to video services or online courses. Cybercriminals have proved themselves here. So, in the spring began sending messages to Facebook Messenger about free access to Netflix Premium for 2 months. Users who received and opened it unwittingly shared with Facebook scammers access data, and also sent this message to their contacts. And this is just one example, in fact, there are thousands of them.



Return to offices







As mentioned above, during a pandemic, the infrastructure of companies was rebuilt in such a way as to quickly switch to a remote mode of operation. Now, when the regime of self-isolation is softened, the reverse process is happening - employees are returning to offices.



Accordingly, it is necessary to rebuild IT and information security processes again. Companies need to protect the perimeter and core of the infrastructure again, eliminate temporary solutions, check employee equipment and constantly monitor all of these processes. The same focus of information security needs to be shifted again, returning to the previous threats that were characteristic of certain business sectors.



It is necessary to remove mass remote access, in case the company returns to its previous mode of operation, monitor and fix information security problems, including changing accesses, installing corporate software, etc. The situation becomes more complicated if some of the employees are transferred to normal operation, and some remain in remote locations - in this case, it is necessary to adapt the IT and information security infrastructure to new conditions. Already now it is necessary to develop realistic and reliable plans for quitting quarantine.



In general, all these problems are not unsolvable, but the pandemic posed a number of very unusual challenges for the information security industry. Moreover, it is not at all a fact that these problems, once solved, will not come back again in the near future. So the business needs to rethink risk management, information security policies and much more in order to remain effective and not suffer too much if the situation repeats.



An example of a successful solution is the case of the Trustbank Joint-Stock Bank (Trust Bank Trust) , which is one of the largest banks in the Republic of Uzbekistan. Even before the pandemic, the bank organized VPN channels for communication with 12 remote branches based on Zyxel gateways. After the introduction of a self-isolation regime in the country, this infrastructure began to be used to organize remote access for employees.



Timur Dzhumabaev, director of the bank’s security department, commented on the situation: “With Zyxel, we realized our goals: we acquired a quality product and did not overpay. As a bank, we love to count our money. Special thanks to the fact that in the conditions of the worldwide coronavirus pandemic, almost the entire bank was transferred to remote work and thanks to the Zyxel USG1900 firewall and IP SEC VPN licenses, the bank employee quietly works from home using Zyxel’s software - ZyWall SecuExtender. ”

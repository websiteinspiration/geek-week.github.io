Making an Arduino Toroidal Coil Winding Machine





Translation from the Electric DIY Lab website



Hello everyone, I present to you an Arduino-based machine for winding toroidal coils made by me. The machine automatically winds the wire and turns the toroid. As an interface, I used an encoder and a 16 √ó 2 LCD screen. The user can enter parameters such as coil diameter, number of revolutions and winding angle.



In this article I will tell you how to build this car and give details of its work.





Everything is described in detail in the video - you can watch it or read the article.



Accessories



The list of components for self-assembly:

  • Arduino nano
  • A4988 stepper motor driver
  • Encoder
  • 16 √ó 2 I2C LCD
  • Nema 17 stepper motor
  • 1000 RPM engine
  • Belt
  • L293D
  • Aluminum profile 20 √ó 20

Build Details



Winding ring











I made a ring from 12 mm plywood. The outer diameter is 145 mm, the inner is 122 mm. There is a recess 43 mm long and 5 mm deep for the coil.



In the ring, I made one cut and a lock to open it. Opening the lock, we place a toroidal coil inside the ring.



Also, the ring has a recess on the outside, 8 mm wide and 4 mm deep, in which a belt 6 mm wide is placed.



Coil







Coil for copper wire that I have machined from a nylon rod. All sizes are shown in the picture.



The material was chosen because nylon, firstly, is lighter than aluminum, and secondly, it is easy to grind on the machine. In addition, when the machine is running, it does not hesitate so much.



Machine body























The case is also made of 12 mm plywood. Three guide rollers are fixed on it, placed approximately 120 ¬∞ from each other.



Rollers are made of 626Z bearings, nuts and bolts. Our wooden winding ring will rotate on them.



The upper part of the ring leans back, and after closing it is clamped with the wing nut. Having thrown away this part, we install the ring inside the machine. Having returned it to its place, it is necessary to press the roller to it so that it goes into the groove.



Toroid Holder Rollers







This is a roller that rotates the coil, and at the same time holds it. I turned them from a nylon rod on my mini lathe . All sizes are shown in the photo.



I supplied the rollers with foam rubber tape, it holds the reel well and it does not slip. It is important to use wing nuts to secure the guides - the usual ones are removed from vibration.







I put the top and bottom of each roller on the flange bearing.



Stepper motor mount







So I fixed the stepper motor, NEMA17. It rotates the coil, which allows you to automatically wind the wire around its entire circumference and does not require manual rotation.



DC motor























This motor rotates the winding ring. I used the Orange Jhonson 12v Dc Motor 300 RPM. I advise you to take the engine to 600 RPM or 1000 RPM.



The belt is 600 mm long and 6 mm wide. The motor holder attached to the aluminum profile is also made of plywood.



Infrared sensor















I used a sensor from SeedStudio . It sends a signal to the Arduino interrupt processing contact - this way the Arduino can count the number of ring revolutions.



I mounted the sensor on an aluminum profile so that the ring lock also works with the reflective surface to which the sensor responds.



This sensor generates 2 signals for one rotation of the ring - when the tree is replaced by metal, the signal changes from low voltage to high, and then vice versa. The interrupt handler logs two state changes. Therefore, to calculate the real number of turns, I had to divide the number of operations in half.



Apparatus base











The base is also made of 12 mm plywood, has dimensions of 300x200 mm. Four rubber feet will hold the car firmly and well, and help to avoid unnecessary vibration.



To install the components, I mounted an aluminum profile on the base. I love it for flexibility in use. All components can be easily mounted on the profile and moved along it. Allows easy alignment of components relative to each other.



Controller housing











The box is printed on a 3D printer, and a board, LCD display and encoder are installed inside. The case gives a professional look to the whole project, and also provides convenient adjustment of the device. The case is fixed to the base with a metal bracket.

Menu navigation



The LCD is used to display information, and the encoder is used for input.







Welcome screen first.







On the second screen, you need to enter the outer diameter of the coil - the device supports coils of different diameters.







On the third screen you need to enter the number of turns.







On the fourth screen you need to enter the angle of coverage of the coil. 360 ¬∞ means that the entire coil will be covered with wire. 720 ¬∞ means that the coil will be wrapped with wire twice in a circle.







On the 5th screen, you can check all the input data before starting the machine. If everything is correct, click on the encoder and the car starts.







The 6th screen shows the number of turns in real time.







The 7th screen appears when finished.

Universe D Shellfish



Agents live in a two-dimensional space, which is folded into a torus so that it does not have edges and agents can always move without bumping into obstacles (Fig. 1).





Figure 1. Thor. To obtain a sweep, the torus is cut along the selected circles and rotated on a plane in the form of a rectangle.



All space available for agents is divided into hexagonal cells (Fig. 2). Their world looks like a honeycomb. When modeling, I used a world size of 100 √ó 100 cells (total area of ‚Äã‚Äã10,000 cells). No more than two agents can be located in one cell at a time. If an agent crosses one of the borders of a rectangle during movement, then it returns to the rectangle from the opposite side, as if it were crawling along a donut.



In addition to agents in the cells there is a nutrient resource - cake , which agents can eat. Food is generated in cells in accordance with a special algorithm.



Food for D Shellfish



Each cell can contain an arbitrary number of whole cake (Fig. 2). In one time step, the agent can eat one cake , this will replenish its energy reserves by 50 J - the energy capacity of the food.





Figure 2. An example of the location of a serving of food in space. Gray cells do not contain food, dark blue cells contain 1 cake , yellow cells contain 15 cake .



The amount of food in space is constantly changing. There are mechanisms of degradation and proliferation of food in space, which are necessary to maintain a stationary amount of food in space. These mechanisms act as a factor limiting the availability of resources and at the same time ensuring the variability of the environment. The amount of food varies due to the following processes:



  • decreases when eaten by agents;
  • decreases due to degradation of the cake portion ;
  • increases as a result of "growth";
  • increases when agents die;
  • increases with successful attacks of some agents on others, as a result of which cake falls into space .


The average total amount of food in space depends on the population density of the agents. When the density of filling the space with agents is less than one agent per cell (1 Dr / cell ), the stationary density of food in space is 20 cake / cell (or 1000 J / cell in energy units). But if there are more than one agent per cell, the stationary amount of food in space that can be maintained in space will be reduced. And when the agents completely fill the space (2 Dr / cell ), the stationary amount of food becomes zero (Fig. 3).





Figure 3. The dependence of the stationary amount of food on the density of agents in space



If the amount of food becomes less than the specified stationary value, then at the end of the current time step in space will be generated 50% of the current lack of food. The required amount of food is generated in portions in a random place, a random volume (but not less than 250 cake ). Such portions are generated until the required amount of food is laid out in space.



If the amount of food exceeds a predetermined stationary value, then at the end of the current time step, the amount of food in randomly selected groups of adjacent cells will be reduced. The group consists of 19 cells (Fig. 4). The number of such groups is chosen so that the excess of food decreases by 10%. This can be interpreted as the inability to maintain an excessive amount of food in space, as a result of which it degrades. Over time, the amount of food in space will tend to a given stationary value.



Such an algorithm for generating food allows preventing overpopulation.



Device D Shell



All agents have the same structure. The difference is only in the values ‚Äã‚Äãof the parameters of their neural network. In this model, each agent is represented by the following set of characteristics:



  • the name of the agent, unique throughout the simulation, a natural number;
  • the agent‚Äôs energy supply, which is expressed as an integer J ;
  • cell number in which the agent is located;
  • the age of the agent, a natural number that at the end of each time step increases by one;
  • agent generation, natural number. At the birth of an agent, this parameter is set one more than the generation of one of the parents (of the two, the parent who has this characteristic more is selected);


Each agent has three chromosomes, two of which determine the parameters of its neural network:



  1. Chromosome WEIGHTS - matrix of weights of neurons of the hidden layer;
  2. Chromosome BIAS - displacement vector of neurons of the hidden layer;
  3. Chromosome COLOR - defines three components of the color of the agent - red, green, blue. These three chromosomes are inherited during reproduction.


Agents have four groups of receptors that transmit information from the environment and the environment to the input layers of the neural network of agents.



  • the presence of food in neighboring cells (19 receptors) - allow the agent to receive information about the amount of food next to it (Fig. 4);
  • the presence of agents in neighboring cells (19 receptors) - allow the agent to receive information about the number of other agents near it (Fig. 4);
  • colors of the neighboring agent (3 receptors) and intrinsic color (3 receptors) - allow the agent to receive information about the color of the second agent in the cell, if there is one and about its color;
  • the current energy reserve of the agent (1 receptor);




Figure 4. The layout of the cells that are visible to the agent is shown by a purple circle in the center. An agent can take a step into each of these cells. From 1st to 6th, the step length is equal to one cell. From 7th to 18th - a step of two cells in length.



The outputs of the neural network are connected to a group of effectors , the state of which determines the action that the agent will perform at the current time step. Agents can do one of six things: pass , step 1 , step 2 , eat , attack , sex .



Pass - inaction - the agent does not perform any actions, remaining in place. This action is always successful.



Step 1 , Step 2- the agent makes an attempt to step into one of the 18 neighboring cells (Fig. 4). Step 1 - step one cell long. Step 2 is a two-cell step. If there is free space in the cell selected by the agent, then the action is successful and the agent moves to a new place, otherwise the action is not successful and the agent remains in the old place.



Eat - an attempt to eat food in the current cell. If there is food in the cell, the operation was successful and the agent consumes 1 cake and increases its supply of energy 50 J .



Attack - an attempt to hit the agent in the current cell. If there is a second one in the cell with the agent, then the action is successful and the neighboring agent loses 150 J and 3 cake falls into the cell .



Sex - the agent is trying to mate with the agent in the current cell. Mating will be successful only if there is another agent in the cell, and it also performs the Sex action . In this case, both parent agents lose 1/3 of their energy supply (in addition to the energy costs for the functioning of the neural network and the execution of the action, see Table 1). A newborn agent receives 1/3 of the energy supply from each parent. The action will be successful if the newborn receives enough energy to survive - more than 500 J , otherwise he will die immediately. The third condition for the success of the Sexis the choice of the parent agent (which gave the majority of the energy - the female) of the neighboring cell with free space to accommodate the newborn agent. If there is no free space in this cell, then the Sex action is considered unsuccessful, and the newborn dies. In the case of the death of a newborn, its energy reserve is converted into food and placed in a cage with parents.

The cheapest in terms of energy consumption is the Pass . The amount of energy spent on inaction ( e 0 ) is determined by the size of the agent‚Äôs neural network. The more neurons in the hidden layer, the greater the energy cost for the functioning of the neural network. These energy losses occur in any other selected action. The dependence of the background energy consumption on the number of neurons will limit the growth of computational costs without a noticeable increase in the fitness of agents.



When the energy reserve of the agent becomes less than 500 J - the agent dies, and the remainder of its energy is released into the cell that it occupied.



Energy model of the universe D shell



Model D shell is open, it has a flow of energy that serves as the driving force of processes in the universe of D shell. It enters the space in the form of food that is eaten by agents. Agents perform actions, spending energy. In fig. 5 shows a diagram of the energy fluxes in the model D shell.





Figure 5. Scheme of energy flows in model D shell.



Food enters the space from the outside of the system with a food in flow . There are also two internal flows replenishing food in space: harm , dead. The first of them is caused by energy losses by agents who were attacked by a neighboring agent. The energy lost by them falls out in the form of an equivalent amount of cake in the cell with the injured agent. The second stream is food that appears in the cell after the death of the agent, the amount of which is equivalent to the remainder of the agent's energy before death.



Food from space is consumed by agents who successfully complete the Eat action - the eating stream . The energy spent on performing the actions leaves the system - the stream used . The second way that energy leaves the system is the flow of spoiled food , which is associated with a degrading excess of food in space.



Natural selection



In the D universe, Rakosh operates natural selection.



For existence, agents need to consume food to replenish their energy supply, which is constantly consumed. Agents that eat more efficiently can last longer and therefore leave more offspring. The effectiveness of reproduction is determined by the frequency of the Sex action and the amount of energy that parents transmit to their offspring. The actions of agents are determined in addition to the information received by them as well as the parameters of their neural network. Which are inherited during reproduction, while they are subject to random changes - mutations.



The action of these factors leads to the fact that different agents have different probability of surviving and leaving offspring.

Without registration and SMS: “socially significant” sites and a list of the Ministry of Digital Affairs

Round table, Saturday, June 6, 2020, 12:00 Moscow time . Broadcast .

Against the backdrop of coronavirus stories, a relatively fresh - and generally thanks to the coronavirus and received sudden support - idea of ​​the Ministry of Communications and Digital Development, also the Ministry of Digital, on the adoption of a list of “socially significant Internet resources”, access to which providers should provide the Russians for free.

At the same time say that the list raised questions- it does not say anything. Not only is the actual content of the list ambiguous - it also included large commercial online stores (obviously, Russians have money to buy them when they no longer have to pay for communication), and some frankly strange sites, for example, a site selling scientific work, and much more, the need for which is doubtful to every Russian (by the way, Habr also entered!).

Even worse, even the technical implementation of this access by the providers raises a lot of questions - the list simply contains a list of domain names, sometimes with subdomains, sometimes without. The issues of mapping them to IP addresses, capturing the freed up names, using them for proxying, including foreign traffic, and much, much more are left out of the brackets - as a result, a potentially positive idea can have a devastating effect comparable to the lists of Roskomnadzor.

To understand what is going on and where to wait for the trick, as well as to collect different opinions about this initiative, tomorrow at 12:00 we will hold a round table broadcast on YouTube (recording at the end will be available at the same link).

  • What was the list of sites supposed to be - and how did it turn out?
  • What is the difference between the bill of the Ministry of Finance and the experiment of the Ministry of Finance?
  • The whole range of technical problems with the list - and can they be solved?

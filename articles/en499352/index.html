Rating Comment Filter


For ten years of reading Habr, I have developed the habit of viewing the most highly rated comments on the article I have read. Sometimes they come across information more useful than the article itself, but there is not always a desire to view conditional 100+ comments. Of course, a high score can be a sign of both a good comment and a hype one, but if I read an article that is far from my area of ‚Äã‚Äãfamiliarity, reading the comments with the highest ratings is enough to read the general point.

A long time ago I already wrote a user script that simplifies the search for comments, and in connection with quarantine I got the hand to comb it a bit and arrange it as extensions for the Google Chrome and Mozilla Firefox browsers. The extension displays statistics for all ratings in the comments. By clicking on one of the positive ratings, all comments with a rating equal to or higher are highlighted, and when clicking on a negative rating, equal or lower. A second click on the rating removes the filtering and returns the default comment tree.



The plugin can be useful to active readers of Habr who want to filter the information flow to save their time. The project is open source, I will be glad to suggestions and comments. github.com/ptol/habr-comments

Podcasts as corporate media: how to make money on them and what metrics to use

We used to listen to the radio, now we listen to podcasts.

A podcast is an audio blog. It differs from the radio in the absence of live broadcasting - the material is recorded in advance, so users can listen to it at any convenient time. And from audio books - in that it is easier and cheaper to create. Thanks to this, podcasts are increasingly using companies as corporate media.

In the article I’ll tell you why podcasts are gaining popularity, which companies have already taken their place in this market, whether the podcast can become an independent media channel, how to measure its effectiveness and how you can make money on it.

Why podcasts are gaining popularity


Podcasts were first discussed in the early 2000s, in 2005 they appeared on iTunes, and in 2014, after the success of the podcast with journalistic investigations by Serial, they began to be perceived as a serious tool for content marketing. According to the international auditing company Deloitte, listed on rbc.ru, in 2020 the global podcast market will grow by 30% and reach $ 1.1 billion. And such growth will continue in the future.

It is convenient to listen to podcasts in the background at any time - during cleaning, playing sports or on the way to work. According to the latest researchTIBURON Research 59% of women surveyed listen to podcasts during household chores. Another advantage of podcasts is the short format: in 20-30 minutes you can listen to the finished “work”. The same study noted that the optimal podcast length is 24 minutes.

Tatyana Kotova, editor and screenwriter of educational projects, listens to “Arzamas” and “The Naked Digger”

“Podcasts are a powerful thing for self-development on the road: you go, for example, on the subway and listen to a podcast about American literature. It is interesting".

In addition, podcasts easily adapt to current topics and listener requests. At the same time, topics can be absolutely any, and eminent experts and ordinary people can participate in them. 

Maria Savotikova, development manager, listens to “It happened”, “It's not easy”, “Norm” and others

“I love podcasts for the opportunity to easily select the appropriate topics and listen to various interesting stories while walking or traveling on the subway. I also love the stories of different people - it is interesting to listen to how someone shares experience on topics that are important to me. ”

Maria Elsenbach, editor, listens to “Rosenthal and Guildenstern” and others.

“It’s convenient to combine podcasts with household chores - and you’re not bored, and you don’t bother much, and learn new things. The optimal duration is 40 minutes, it will fit perfectly into your affairs and will not be delayed. And podcasts are an ideal option in case you don’t understand a topic, but want to enter it without special efforts: insanely different things - from Oscar ceremonies a hundred years ago to the use of the word sex in the modern world - are discussed in plain language. "

From the point of view of creating podcasts are cheaper than audio books - there is no need to buy expensive equipment, organize high-quality sound, attract a lot of people, read, edit, record and finalize. RBC claims that for the creation of 20 hours of audiobooks you will have to pay from $ 3 thousand to $ 15 thousand. To start a podcast, you only need a microphone and an Internet connection. 

In addition, there are now many convenient services for podcasting. Right in them you can create, distribute, analyze and monetize your podcasts. 

Sergey Kaplichny, content marketer, author of the podcast "Development"

“You can start with an absolute zero. I even recommend that you do not buy anything at the start, especially if the podcast is an experiment for you and it is not yet clear what will come of it. You can rent a studio, they are now inexpensive, and record your episode there for 1,500 rubles. Or even a few episodes. Write it down, run it, see how relevant it is to your audience, how interesting it is, how well you do it. And if the response is good, then you can already spend money on a microphone, special software, and more. The entry threshold for something unprofessional is as low as possible. ”

Polina Nakrainikova, editor-in-chief, Lifehacker

“At first, our podcast team consisted of only a sound engineer and a host - she independently composed the scripts, organized the process, and recorded audio. In May 2019, we decided to increase the volume and make the “Lifehacker Podcast” daily: this is how the second host appeared on the team. By the winter of 2020, we realized that if we want faster and more informed growth, we will need an organizer, a podcast editor who will be responsible for launching and managing new projects. With it, we launched the podcast “Spent” about reasonable spending and ultrashort, “Lifehack” - three-minute tips about everything in the world.

Now we are again looking for new people in the team: the demand for podcasts is growing, in addition, we just really like working on them. Our main expenses are employee salaries, and additional expenses are the purchase of music and jingles and placement on some sites like SoundCloud. ”

What Russian companies do podcasts


Podcasts take over the world: According to a study by Edison Research and Nielsen, in April 2020, there were more than 1 million podcasts from 30 million episodes in the United States. But in Russia this market is not yet so developed: for example, in 2019, there were only 2,550 podcasts and 50,172 episodes on VKontakte . Nevertheless, Lifehacker, Medusa, Yandex, Kinopoisk and other well-known Russian companies already have podcasts. At the same time, it is wrong to think that podcasts are always about media and media - even companies with an immediate product can successfully exist in this market. For example, the English language school Don't Speak has its podcast, the Tinkoff bank has two podcasts, Evotor has two podcasts, the Invitro company made a special podcast of 5 issues.

Can a podcast become an independent media channel for a company


The podcast market in Russia has not yet formed and is similar to YouTube in 2008: it has recently appeared, few people know about it - in December 2019, the podcast audience in our country was 5.1 million people, it is inconvenient to use it - there is still no single platform with podcasts . Therefore, to say that the podcast right now can become an independent media channel for the company is not necessary. Most likely, this approach is a long-term and long-term work.

Maria Elsenbach, editor, listens to Rosenthal and Guildenstern.

“The main disadvantage of podcasts is the lack of a single platform for hosting. I listen to podcasts through Google. Podcasts, but not everything is available. Someone, for example, publishes podcasts in Yandex.Music or SoundCloud. I would like to collect them in one place. "


In 2019, Brand Analytics found out that most often the Russian-speaking audience listens to podcasts on YouTube, Soundcloud.com, Apple Podcasts and VKontakte. The problem with the first two services is that they are not at all associated with podcasts. The global problem of podcasts is the lack of a single service for listening.

A more successful strategy now is to promote podcasts on sites that already have their own audience. For example, Medusa and Arzamas podcasts convert their audience from the site. 

Polina Nakrainikova, Lifehacker’s editor-in-chief “

Now a podcast alone is not enough: to win the maximum number of audiences, it is better for a company to be present on several platforms at once.

Sergey Kaplichny, content marketer, author of the podcast "Development"

“Podcasts in Russia are a new format that is not suitable for everyone. People doubt: “Why listen to audio without video?” or "I’ll read something faster on this subject than I will listen." Therefore, while a podcast as an independent media is difficult. I would recommend acting in conjunction with other content marketing channels. But it’s quite possible that in a year, for example, the situation will change and good examples will appear where exactly podcasts will be the only media channel for any company. ” 

At the same time, podcasts have great potential: according to IAB Russia forecasts, the Russian audience of podcasts will actively grow and reach 10.5 million a month in December 2020, and in December 2024 - 27.9 million people. Therefore, abandoning the idea of ​​launching your own podcast is not worth it.

Podcast metrics


In order for the podcast to find its listener, it must be uploaded to the hosting - a special service that allows users to post information on the Internet. After downloading, the hosts themselves distribute podcasts to applications and sites where they can be found and listened to, and then get statistics for the authors.


This is how the process of distributing podcasts across different applications and services looks like. Source: Netology Course “ Creating Podcasts: From Idea to Launch ”

This mechanism hides the key problem of collecting podcast analytics - different hosting companies may have a different set of statistics about downloaded podcasts and generally view statistics differently, and some sites, such as VKontakte or Yandex.Music, do not give their metrics to hosting at all. Podcasts need to collect analytics directly in these services. Sometimes podcasts listen without an internet connection, which also makes collecting analytics difficult.

In general, podcasts may have the following metrics:

  • Auditions - How many people turned on the podcast
  • Listening - how many listeners have listened to the podcast to the end. Such a matrix is, for example, VKontakte and Apple;
  • number of listeners / subscribers;
  • concentration while listening - attention and actions of the audience during and after listening;
  • audience ratings and reviews;
  • downloads.

How to make money on podcasts


In 2020, there are two main ways to make money on podcasts: advertising from direct advertisers and crowdfunding - donations from listeners.

Advertising in podcasts is a promising direction for Russia, which is already successfully developing in the United States: according to IAB, in 2018, American companies spent $ 479.1 million on advertising, and in 2019 they should have spent 40% more.
But for now, the main problem with monetizing podcasts through direct advertising is the distrust of advertisers in this content format. Due to scattered statistics and the lack of a unified podcast site, advertisers do not fully understand what podcasts are and how advertising is arranged in them, and podcasters do not know how to evaluate an advertising place and make an advertisement well.

But there are pluses: podcasts are developing rapidly, and their audience is usually very high-quality - young people with an active lifestyle prefer the audio format. In addition, you can target thematically - listeners in most cases choose podcasts specifically for topics of interest to them - and get into a specific context. 

Polina Nakrainikova, editor-in-chief of Lifehacker “

It's not only the number of audience, but also its quality: listeners of podcasts are the very core that we are striving to build up. It’s no joke - a person is ready to spend twenty minutes to an hour at Lifehacker: it’s quite a lot, because we really appreciate every listener. ”


Statistics from Brand Analytics for 2019 showsthat young people aged 25–34 years are more likely to listen to podcasts in Russia.

Another interesting nuance: since podcasts are often listened to during some other affairs, they usually don’t rewind advertisements in them - it’s just not as convenient to do as when watching a video.

Podcast crowdfunding is like volunteering from listeners, which can be of any size and any regularity. Thanks to donations, listeners can get early access to releases or unique content, mentioning a name on the air, souvenirs, merchandise and other similar buns, and may not receive anything - it depends on the decision of the authors of the podcast.

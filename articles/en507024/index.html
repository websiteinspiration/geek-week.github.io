How to Become a Data Scientist Without Online Courses



This is just my opinion. If you have counterarguments, please write them in the comments.

Want to become data scientists?

I met more than 50 data scientists and worked with a couple more personally.

Here I will tell how these people came to Data Science. No online courses.

1. Solve the problem with machine learning


Select a real problem and solve it with machine learning.

This is not easy to do, as there is no ready-made action plan. But regardless of whether you succeed in this matter or not, thanks to the experience and history gained, you can subsequently sell yourself much more expensive.

Here are examples of tasks that you can try to solve:

  • Fake News Recognition
  • Forecasting the cost of housing in your area
  • Selection of pets depending on the lifestyle of potential owners

If your solver works (or even almost works), create a user interface that you can use and publish it on Hacker News or Product Hunt.

Add a line called ‚ÄúData Scientist‚Äù to the summary. If it turned out to solve the problem of machine learning, nobody would care that it was just a one-man show.

Now you have something to tell at the interview, and such a story will have much more weight than a certificate of completion of online courses.

2. Find a Mentor - Artificial Intelligence Expert


Build relationships with an experienced person who can provide AI-based solutions for the problems you are currently working on.

This is how I managed to break into the science of data.

As a software engineer, a startup accelerator allocated my company PhD an AI project for a few hours a week.

Every week we discussed problems and possible solutions, I tried to implement them, then we analyzed the failures and repeated again. After 6 months of work, we were able to solve several problems, the knowledge gained became an invaluable experience.

To find new mentors in data science, I did the following.

  • Posted on LinkedIn to data researchers in his city
  • Invited them to drink coffee
  • Started a conversation about a specific problem, told how I would like to solve it and listened to the opinion of the interlocutor
  • Then I continued to work with the results

3. Take a Machine Learning Internship


Get a temporary job with a small salary, but the opportunity to participate in a serious project to implement AI.

The former machine learning trainee developer at my startup is now interning as a data engineer on Facebook, and will probably soon become their full-time employee.

This path is not for everyone, and works much better if you are still young or are finishing school. Not everyone is ready to quit work and become a trainee, but you may be able to find a remote job or part-time internship.

It is imperative that your resume contains a reference to working with AI.

4. Get started with the Data Scene at your current job.


Find out how the company you are currently working in can use AI to solve the problem - and solve it.

Perhaps during the working day you have no time to do this. But with strong motivation, work in the evenings or on weekends. Then imagine the result of your labors.

If you work in a small company, no one will mind that you are trying to bring more benefits. If your project is really valuable, it is likely that you will add it to everyday duties.

Add this project to your resume later and try updating the job title.

5. Organize a bootcamp on Data Science


Visit the paid bootcamp for Data Science.

It costs money, and not all bootcamps are the same, but I know at least 10 people who came to Data Science after the bootcamp, and everyone has large, respected companies.

The best bootcamps are accepted only by doctors of science, so it is quite possible that the success of the candidate depends on a biased attitude (bootcamps are accepted by students who, in their opinion, have reached heights in any case).

What are the benefits of bootcamps?

  • Participants are engaged in consulting real companies.
  • Graduates communicate with companies looking for employees
  • There is training

However, not every graduate gets a job.

6. Become a software engineer first


I wrote about it here .

As long as the data researchers solve the problems associated with the code, they will continue to closely intersect with software engineering.

Having gained experience as a software engineer, start looking for a job in the field of data science, for which you will need the same technical features (the same database, language, framework, software packages).

If you manage to check most of the requirements for a candidate, your chances of getting an interview will increase significantly.

There are other advantages to becoming a software engineer first.

  • Provide a decent income
  • Work for companies that hire data researchers
  • Create a universal technical past
  • Prove that you can do this type of work

...

7. Before you get a job, get a doctorate or master's degree


Do you have 2 years or 6 years to unlearn? I personally do not.

But most of my acquaintances, data researchers, have taken this path.

They are either:

  • received a master's degree in AI related field
  • defended a PhD in the technical field (not necessarily close to AI)

I would not recommend going back to school (university) to study Data Science. But if you are currently in a school, you can continue your education and get a degree in AI. Oddly enough, people with higher education receive the highest AI salaries .

Despite the fact that getting classical diplomas is long and expensive, they are more trusted than online certificates.

What about online courses?


Yes, online courses take place BE. But not in order to get a job.

The advantages of the courses are that you study what you have a gap in and analyze in detail specific methods.

But on the other hand, the courses give a sense of accomplishment, without forcing you to follow the path of greatest resistance.

I would rather find the problem first, and then learn how to solve it by taking online courses.

Why bim managers are in demand


I have identified four reasons:

  • Around the world started using BIM
  • In Russia, most work completely without BIM
  • Soon everyone in Russia wants BIM
  • Bim managers are few

Around the world started using BIM


In the UK in 2011, only 10% of companies used BIM. In 2019, their number increased to 70%. It is written in the UK National BIM Report . The rest of the world is on the same trend.


BIM helps save money and time. Because it is used by most companies in the UK, USA and Singapore.

Soon everyone in Russia wants BIM


After 2021, the state will accept projects only in BIM. Take a look at the map below. For six years now, the process of transition to BIM technologies has been going on.



No construction company wants to lose such a large customer. Russian companies are likely to do everything possible to switch to BIM. Therefore, in the coming years they will seek and hire bim managers. But there's a problem.

Bim managers are few


No university prepares bim managers. Those that are working now have learned everything themselves. Received a building education, worked with drawings and at a construction site, mastered Revit, Dynamo, NavisWorks.

I went to hh.ru and found out that there are only 8-11 people in the bim-manager position in Russia.

How to become a bim manager


To become a bim manager, in my experience, you need four things:

  • To be able and like to program
  • Know Revit A to Z
  • Be able to explain difficult in the most accessible language
  • Experience in construction and with drawings

I started programming at the school desk. In the 7th grade, he began to write HTML sites and created servers on his computer for multi-player games. I wanted to figure out something complex and incomprehensible for myself. It was interesting to look for answers to questions on how to do all this yourself without YouTube.

I started to learn Revit in college.

When I was asked to draw a coursework by hand, I studied AutoCAD and made a coursework in it. To do by hand was laziness. But my abilities were not appreciated: I got a deuce and found out who the conservatives are.

When classmates began to order term papers, I stopped working in AutoCAD. Reading the specifications manually was unbearable. I learned Revit and started doing everything there.

I learned to explain the difficult in the most accessible language when I sold term papers to students. They did not understand that I did this to them in Revit. I had to spend hours explaining how to open a drawing in AutoCAD and how to protect it in front of teachers.

I got a suitable work experience like this.

First I went to work on the construction site of construction and installation works on monolithic works. There I coordinated the work of the workers, handed over the work to the customer and took concrete at night.

Then I worked as a VET engineer. There I did executive documentation. Counting bricks by hand was too lazy. Therefore, I used Revit.

After I tried to work as a design engineer. There I created drawings of the KZh brand. Somehow I tried to convince the management to start using BIM. Twisted at the temple, they say, we do not need.

How to get to work


I posted my resume hh. He wrote that he worked there and there, did so-and-so there, attached work and added that he was ready to work for food if I worked with BIM specialists.

A day later, I was invited to an interview. I came to the office. There, designers began to communicate with me: they asked me to show my work and asked about working experience. The conversation went without manipulation. And then there was a check: I was asked on the example of my work, how I created families, what is the logic of their construction, whether I can work in Dynamo.

Everything went not so smoothly. When I was asked to demonstrate the work on numbering the premises, the program issued an error. I fixed it right there. This surprised the interlocutor and they immediately took me as a bim manager. They gave a salary of 30 000 ‚ÇΩ and a place in the office.

Dispatcher of the future: how will his role in the service company change?

To date, the best practices of the Field Service Management methodology and digital transformation of service processes are formed mainly in the West - thanks to the developed service industry and the active introduction of advanced software. This allows market leaders in IT service solutions to predict future development trends in the service business: how will automation affect traditional roles in the company and which will significantly reduce the cost of service?

Russian companies are just beginning to take this path. Therefore, those who are able to adopt the world's leading experience in automation of service companies first are the future leaders of their industry in Russia and the CIS countries. The functionality of modern FSM-class IT platforms optimizes the processes for which non-digitalized companies spend up to 60% of their dispatchers working hours, losing not only in finances, but also in the quality of services. Future dispatcher


Modern advances in technology are already leading to the evolution of the role of the dispatcher and service organizations are expanding the scope of dispatchers. Technology makes dispatchers more efficient, allows them to use unique capabilities to solve the problems of their customers, and service organizations get more benefit from the work of their employees.

In the past, the work of the dispatcher was to periodically assign tasks to technicians working remotely in the field, while the transparency of the processes left much to be desired. The results ranged from dispatcher to dispatcher, it was difficult to reconcile them with the business goals of the company, and they did not scale.

With the help of intelligent tools, the Future Dispatcher offers clients comprehensive services for the most efficient and quickest result. He answers the initial service request, looks at the facility data and the history of the service itself. Then it provides remote processing of the request by a team of experts to immediately fix the problem without leaving the facility (for example, when there is remote access to equipment or the ability to conduct preliminary diagnostics through the personnel present at the facility).

If this is not possible, the dispatcher must make sure that all the tools, materials and information on the application are prepared for the technician before leaving the site. Also, time and conditions must be agreed in advance with the client. The dispatcher manages the entire process and works closely with the customer to determine the urgency of his request, thereby adding an important component - human contact, which allows the organization to provide high-quality service.

Dispatcher role today


The role of the dispatcher is vital and ultimately determines the difference between a specific service organization and its competitors. Service organizations that do not invest in digital transformation and do not prioritize customer service risk losing market competition and losing market share.

Dispatchers are employees who are responsible for the interaction of two parties - the client and the contractor. Without dispatchers appointing the right most suitable employee for each job, technicians would not have the chance to do everything possible to work effectively on request.

Dispatchers play an important role not only in the planning process, but also in the customer service chain. At a tactical level, their work requires operational interaction with the finance department, the client department and the HR service, as well as with engineers and quality specialists.

Future Dispatcher: Competent - Informed - Effective


The dispatcher of the future manages all internal and external activities of the local service chain, guided by the goal of satisfying the needs of customers throughout the life cycle of their asset (equipment or other facility).

From the time a customer purchases an asset, installs it, maintains it, monitors it until the end of its service life (that is, replace or decommission it), the dispatcher is at every step of the way.

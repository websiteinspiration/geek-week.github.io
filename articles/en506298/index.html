If you cut one of them, you can see what these artifacts consist of. Inside the sealed plastic container is a winding of thin wire. Its rows are laid out with insulating paper, which is impregnated with paraffin. They are flooded with all the internal volume.



On one of the ferrite halves we wind the primary winding. It consists of fifty turns of a stranded wire of the type littsendrat 1 mm thick. Each core is covered with an insulating varnish. We rewind everything with adhesive tape, insert the high-voltage windings into the halves of the ferrite and actually everything. This is what the heart of our high voltage discharges looks like. It is certainly not ideal, since in the future, during prolonged use, the iron bracket that tightens the ferrite will heat up.

In the design, the high voltage windings look to meet each other, and their thin "earth" wires are connected together. The high-voltage ends here are built up with a high-voltage wire so that Zeus does not get out and breaks into the control board or somewhere else. It was a manual on how to assemble a high-voltage transformer for Jacob's ladder.

This option has the right to life, but the only drawback of this performance is a small capacity. At the electrodes, the arc extends a maximum of three centimeters and then breaks. Now, someone may ask why they need to feed the circuit from the 220 network, if you can assemble a simple ZVS oscillator that needs only 12 volts.Yes, in principle, you can! A power supply unit alone will take up more space than my entire balcony in Khrushchev. And this is silent about the hellish overheating of the ferrite core after several minutes of operation.



A generator operating from a network 220 allows you to place everything on a single board, from a surge protector to power switches. All components here are not scarce and easily fit in one hand.

Let's take a look at the circuit diagram of the device.At the power input, several protections are provided here: a fuse, a varistor that limits possible high-voltage emissions to the network and a thermistor that limits the charge current of a fairly powerful capacitor, it is needed so that the fuse does not burn out when the circuit is plugged into the outlet. The generator here is built on the basis of the driver chip IR2153, which controls the power transistors.

It was originally planned to use high-voltage film capacitors in the circuit. But then the choice fell on non-induction capacitors of the MKPH brand with a capacity of 0.33 microfarads. They are used in induction cookers. Power switches are designed for a voltage of 600 volts and a current of about 20 amperes. Marking 20N60.

Now our task is to connect all the details according to the scheme.A few hours of work in the tracer program and at the output we get a fairly powerful compact generator. You can etch the board yourself, or you will turn to specialists with this task .



To build this generator, a circuit is not needed, since it indicates where and what element should be located. We install transistors on the radiator. The board provides seats for different capacitors. The thermistor heats up during operation, it must be placed as high as possible.

In general, we solder everything in its place and the work on assembling the generator can be considered exhaustive. Let's pay attention to the missing resistor near the IR2153 chip. Instead, you need to install a tuning resistor of 50 kOhm. This is necessary to further adjust the resonance.



It is unacceptable to plug the generator directly into a socket! I recommend using ballast in the form of a light bulb connected in series with the circuit. If during the setup process it breaks the keys and a short circuit appears on the line, the light will flash brightly and prevent traffic jams in the apartment. Type security and all that.



Before you run the circuit to the full extent, it must be configured. First you need to power up the IR2153 driver and make sure that the control signals are sent to the keys. From an external power supply, we supply 15 volts to it. We connect the probes of the digital oscilloscope to the gates of the power transistors. The amplitude of the control pulses will be equal to the supply voltage of the driver microcircuit. She has a zener diode inside it for 15 volts, because this amplitude is the edge.

The IR2153 chip controls the keys pretty tricky. Between the opening of the first and second transistors there is a pause of 1.2 Œºs, it is called "Dead Time". The fact is that the keys must work on the load in turn. If current flows through both keys at the same time, they will explode quite effectively, as they will cause a short circuit to the network. Frequency adjustment here is carried out by a subscript resistor. The frequency adjustment range starts at 20 kilohertz and ends at about 220 kilohertz. Quite a wide range for our purposes.



We connect a high-voltage transformer, do not hesitate to clamp the wires well.
An important point, during the operation of such devices, high-voltage wires should not hang in the air and God forbid somehow touching the elements of the generator board.

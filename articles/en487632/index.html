In open space, employees get tired faster , complain of migraines and memory problems . This is a fact, and all these difficulties have one source - the incessant office noise.

The price of bad sounds

The participants received a task that had to be completed in noisy conditions. Everyone coped with the task, but the introverts worked more slowly, complaining of fatigue and inability to concentrate. Extroverts, on the contrary, thought worse in silence.

How to stay crazy with noise


Despite the depressing disease statistics, employee complaints and general opinion about open space, such offices continue to be popular. So, to avoid such an experience will be difficult. But "shut" from the noise will help a variety of (sometimes openly strange) gadgets - from helmets and masks to a kind of " blinkers ", which simultaneously reduce the load on peripheral vision and muffle external sounds. It is worth recognizing that most of them are expensive and not always effective. Ear plugs and headphones are a more popular way to protect against noise. However, not everything is so simple here either - scientists do not have an unequivocal answer to the question whether music increases productivity during intellectual work or, on the contrary, is distracting.

If vigorous rhythms only make you nervous, try turning on the special soundtrack in the headphones. For example, pink noise , which can even help restore organic damage to the ear. Those for whom this option for some reason does not fit, you can return to nature - and try to work under the sound of rain or sea waves.

Headphones, gadgets and new materialshelp to cope with the noise "on an individual basis", but there is a possibility that the fashion for open space will sooner or later decline. Already, some employers are trying to look for alternatives to beautiful, but uncomfortable offices. Sometimes they try to diversify the open floor plan with plants and book shelves - so that employees can "hide" in emerging niches. Another alternative to open space is working from home, and some companies, like Pixar, generally move to spaces with individual offices and open common areas for "non-working" communication.

A geological model of an Arizona crater located in the Arizona desert, USA

About 50 thousand years ago, a 50-meter iron-nickel meteorite crashed into the Arizona desert at a speed of 12.8 - 20 km / s. The explosion that occurred was three times more powerful than the explosion from the fall of the Tunguska meteorite and formed a crater 1.2 km in diameter with a depth of 229 m. In geology, this structure is called impact. The crater located in the desert is perfectly visible in satellite images (the black "blot" directly above the crater is a parking lot for curious tourists, the highway approaches it):

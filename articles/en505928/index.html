Why functional programming is so complicated

I started reading articles and series “Introduction to Functional Programming”, “Introduction to Category Theory” and even “Introduction to Lambda Calculus” several times. Moreover, both Russian and English. Each time the impression was very similar: firstly, a lot of new incomprehensible words; secondly, many new definitions that arise out of nowhere; thirdly, it is completely unclear how to use it.


Perhaps the most incomprehensible and furious was Category Theory. I got used to it only with the third approach. The first two times I honestly read everything, it seems I understood, but because she had no connection with real life, then a week later she safely completely weathered.


Attempts to use the concepts studied somehow in the work were broken up by a complete misunderstanding of how to apply the acquired deep knowledge. After all, I remind you that the FP paradigm (somewhere more convenient, somewhere not very, but) can be used in almost any language, it is not necessary to study the conditional Haskell for this.


To whom this article


This article is for programmers who have long wanted to understand Functional Programming, tried to read something on this topic and leaned against the wall "so what, this damn crap is such, and why complicate everything so much !?". Therefore, in this article I will try to answer the question “why did they come up with this”, without bumping into technical jungle. Today I will prompt such a “Robert Kiosaki from functional programming”, which not only teaches you financial functional literacy, but also motivates you to develop it in yourself.

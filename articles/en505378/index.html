My first article on Habré will be devoted to contact points - they are often not looked at comprehensively and do not even suspect how many of them are in the company.

And I'll start with two stories


History # 1. About the application. I wanted to order sneakers on one very popular site, where a lot of shoes are sold. Previously, it was possible to order several sizes through the application, try on in the store and buy the pair you liked. Now their stores are closed, purchases through the application. First you choose the size (with shoes you can guess, but you can not), then you pay, then the shoes are brought home. What if it doesn’t? Make a refund? Wait another week? Order three pairs of shoes, pay, then return two that did not fit? .. I decided to figure it out and write a letter. There is no chatbot or online consultant in the application - I wrote in the section “Appeals”, described the problem, asked what is the best way to proceed. The answer came (quote) "Please specify your delivery city." And there is no field for an answer. Physically. Wrote a second appeal,where she paid attention to it - they did not answer me. The company spends a lot of money on marketing, advertising, teaches sellers, and the fields for responding to the appeal in the application are simply absent as a given. The application is an obvious point of contact between a company and a client. Our contact failed. A trifle? No. I didn’t buy sneakers.

#2. About the guard. The other day I went to a pharmacy to buy a hand cream. Pharmacy is a large and famous network. In a large room, I was the only buyer. The cream that I was looking for was not there, so I had to choose a new one. And choosing a new one is a whole story. Therefore, I began to look at the products of various companies when I noticed that the guard literally walks behind me. I'm to the rack - he is behind me. I don’t want to buy the cream, I wanted to cringe and run away. But without a cream in any way - took the child. I stand at the checkout. I share with the sellers my impressions of the guard. And they answered - yes, they constantly complain about him, he is always like that. Guys! This is a pharmacy. This is a very delicate place - people come here not only for cream. For medicines, remedies for baldness and impotence, means for depilation and much more than others.No one wants to share such intimate things with the guards. Which, unfortunately, are also points of contact. Will I return to this pharmacy? No thanks.

These are two stories about how different the points of contact are and that there are no trifles in relation to the client .

Why is this in IT? Yes, maybe the question is not technical, but purely marketing, but it seems to me that it’s important for product experts, project managers who work on both sides to remember that even in complex IT development decisions are made by people, not robots (for now) . And everything that is relevant about contact points for a pharmacy or shoe store is also relevant for the IT segment.

What are contact points?


These are all “places” where customers come across a company or product online, offline, over the phone, or in any other way.

According to marketer Igor Mann, these can be whole groups:

  1. company contact points (website, signboard, documents, advertising, printing and much more);
  2. product contact points: packaging, layout, design, name, barcode, user manual, warranty card;
  3. service contact points: name, presentation, customer reviews, cases, publications;
  4. employee contact points: height, build, smile, neat look, hairstyle, uniform, badge, posture, speech patterns.

They can be controlled and not, priority and not.

“Zero” contact points


These are three blocks:

  • positioning
  • brand book company
  • company and product description

These are basic things, must have, without which it is impossible to work with other things. They determine what message you are delivering to customers and how this message looks. It makes no sense to rush to write in the media and social networks, to develop mentions about yourself before they are finalized - an understanding of the product will be fragmented, chaotic, and its recognition due to a deficiency in the visual part will be low.
Try to remember a different tagline from Volvo than “The safest car in the world.”
Or another corporate color or logo from Sberbank.

The result was a fairly extensive list - 53 points . Surely we still forgot something.

This is 53 points where customers come across us and create their impression of us as a company and a product.

After that, we determined which points are the most priority for us, which we can control, and which ones we can influence, and began to work out each of them. It's like parsing a cabinet.

Practicing some contact points takes a couple of minutes - for example, a signature in corporate mail. Some (for example, we restarted the site as the main point after the product itself) - several months.

How to work with contact points?


  1. Think about all the places where a client may come across your company, product, employees.
  2. Record the entire list by group.
  3. Sort the contact points from the most important to the least important or those you need to think about in the future.
  4. Briefly describe the state of the point. If you need to refine it, write what exactly needs to be done.
  5. Assign contact points.
  6. Schedule an update schedule. For example, once every six months - a global audit, and check up forms on the site or call the call center “through the eyes of the client” - once a week.

In conclusion, I want to remind you that there is a very important rule in marketing.
“Attracting a new customer can cost a company six times more than retaining and increasing the loyalty of an existing one . ” (Strauss, El-Ansary and Frost, 2006)
If your phone does not work, sellers are inattentive, the application crashes, and the company does not look reliable for the client - new customers will not come, and old ones may not remain, no matter how technological the product is. Make sure this does not happen.

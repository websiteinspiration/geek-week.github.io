It is high time to use longitudinal instability to increase the maneuverability of cordless aerobatic aircraft models. Since the use of longitudinal instability in natural aviation entails very sophisticated automation, aeronautical simulators, including professional ones, seem to have the impression of the insolubility of this problem in cord models. Partly, and for this reason, the design of professional cordless aerobatic models stopped at the level of piston aviation of the late 40s.

However, the problem has recently been solved by very simple means that do not complicate the cord management system and increase the mass of the model. The new technical solution provides for the use of the front horizontal plumage or most of its area in the vane mode for horizontal flight and round figures. Moreover, the vane surface has no contact with the control system. And at the corners of square and triangular figures, i.e. at maximum deviations of the control knob, direct thorn or reverse thorn, mounted on the control system thrust, pushes the lever of the vane surface and deflects it to the maximum possible angle. The distance between the spikes is such that the control surface can freely feather while performing forward and reverse round shapes. As a resultduring horizontal flight and the execution of round figures, the focus on the angle of attack of the model is behind the center of mass and the model is statically stable overload. And at the corners of square and triangular figures, the focus of the model is shifted forward, it is ahead of the center of mass and the model becomes statically unstable. Unlike natural aviation, where the aircraft is constantly statically unstable and its instability is neutralized by an artificial stability system; the model is permanently statically stable with short-term exceptions for making corners of square and triangular figures. Thus, longitudinal instability is used to increase the maneuverability of the model at the corners of square and triangular figures, which become sharper, and therefore look more advantageous in comparison with the existing models.

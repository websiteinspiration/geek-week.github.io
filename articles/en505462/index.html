Peripheral computing based on mini and micro DPCs is a growing global trend, the so-called Edge Computing. The trend is clear and logical: the movement of computing to the place of creation of the primary information is a direct result of the digitalization of the business: the data should be as close as possible to the customer. This market (peripheral computing), according to Gartner, is growing at an average of 29.7% per year and will increase by almost five times by 2023 - up to $ 4.6 billion. Who may need this? Those who need unified solutions that can be quickly and inexpensively implemented and scaled up in regional branches, where a fast response of information systems is required regardless of the quality of communication channels, for example, to remote branches of a bank or an oil concern. Most of the objects of oil and gas production (wells, for example) are significantly removed from the central offices, and due to the narrowness of the communication channels, enterprises need to process a large amount of data directly at the place of their receipt.

The ability to locally process and aggregate data is an important, but the only factor of interest in this product. MicroCODs are most often used if the organization does not have the ability (or desire) to use the services of a commercial data center or build its own. Far from all, for various reasons, they are ready to choose between their own and those of others, between long-term projects for the construction of a data center and public clouds.

MicroCOD is an alternative accessible to many, which avoids the long-term and expensive construction of its own data center, while maintaining full control over the infrastructure. Commercial structures, industrial enterprises, and government services have an interest in micro-data centers. The main motive is the typification of the solution. It is suitable for those who want to get the result quickly and for adequate money - without design and construction work, without preliminary preparation of the premises and getting it into ownership.

And here the following query arises: the product is one, and the motivation for its acquisition may be different. How to satisfy customers with different needs in one solution? 1.5 years after the start of sales, we clearly see two equal requests: one of them - to reduce the cost of the product, the other - to increase reliability by increasing battery life and redundancy.

Idea



In January 2019, I started reading 2–5 books a month. After a dozen works of art, I realized that I like books on business and self-improvement more. So at some point I read about the habits of successful people and sleep - and began to get up at 5 in the morning. By the way, I wrote an article about this .



My book list included Getting Things Done, Eat That Frog, Willpower, and Atomic Habits. All four are excellent stories about the impact of cognitive psychology on motivation and productivity. I thought, they say, what the hell is not joking - and decided to consistently try out all the tips from the books. And every time something went wrong.



Each time I found myself either with a giant list of tasks that pressured me psychologically (I just increased the amount of work), or with a cloud of tasks that I would never have started. The projects “sport”, “self-development”, “reading” and “hobbies” conveyed greetings. It was especially disappointing when I had both long lists of tasks and a ton of tasks that I would never have started.



But I'm stubborn, so I tried to figure out what had gone wrong. As a result, I collected lists of rules from all methodologies, walked through each of the points and rated it on a scale from “positively affects productivity” to “harms productivity”. He selected only those that helped to be effective. Removed items that either did not give anything or worsened the situation.



For example, experimentally, I got rid of the concept of "projects". It turned out that when there are no folders where you can hide the task, it runs! Now it’s obvious to me, but I don’t understand why it is not obvious to the same adherents of GTD.



As a result, I came to a simple list of rules, thereby 20% of the restrictions, which give 80% improvement in productivity. And he described a number of these rules, first in an article about personal productivity - and then he tried to apply it in the companies with which I work to manage programmers. I had to slightly modify and supplement the rules, but even there, this system worked perfectly.

What's next?



To date, Tudurant has approximately 5,000 registrations and 2,000 active users. As you may have noticed above, small numbers do not bother me, you always need to start your journey somewhere. Before that, I managed to grow Voysi from the first 100 added chats to more than 1,100,000 connected chats.



I conduct the entire development of Tudurant in Tudurant itself, with duplication of some tasks in the public Trello table, so that users will roughly understand what to expect from a future service. Literally every task for developing Tudurant lies with me, like a task in Tudurant. And it's cool - I myself use the service that I’m developing.



The following cool innovations that I have in my work are delegation, epics and templates. I try to focus not on the functions that competitors have (I believe that they all look the wrong way), but on functions that will directly improve user productivity.



Delegation is when a user can add other “trusted” users, which can throw tasks to his / her inbox and see the status of these tasks with one click of a button.



Epic- This is a certain element of gamification. Tudurant already has test “ranks” of productivity (completed 1000 tasks, received a badge) and hashtags (a way to organize tasks into projects without hiding tasks). I decided to combine them with the “epic” function, when you can select a specific hashtag and purpose on it (for example, complete 100 tasks with the “sport” hashtag this year) and see clear progress on the goal.



Quests - so that you can create the same linear lists for training programs (push out 20 times, pull up 10 times, jump 5 times every 2 days), share them and open access only to a certain circle of users (for example, who will buy the program from someone on the side) . I clicked on a button, selected a date, and your list of tasks was supplemented with all the necessary tasks from training.



In general, the development of Tudurant is based on user feedback. Anyone can write me or in our Telegram chat “Tuduranta” any offer or report any bug. If the proposal is tolerable and fits into the methodology, I immediately add to myself the task of doing this.

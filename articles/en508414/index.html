Introduction: Which companies hire remotely and who they need.



The first step to success in finding remote work: to understand which companies in general today hire remote engineers to work continuously. There are such companies, and they do not need freelancers - Upwork exists for such tasks - but people in a team for a long time. What kind of employers are these?



Before quarantine, most often remote workers were hired by companies that could be called startups. This is not necessarily a very small company, it may be already formed, existing for several years, but still flexible and actively developing business.



Typically, these companies are divided into two categories - those that hire remote employees for some specific reason, and completely remote companies. The latter include, for example, Basecamp, or Wordpress developers from Automattic. To better understand their culture, you should read more about it. For this, the interviews of the founders and, for example, media research on the distribution of salaries by geography (here is such material about Automattic ) are well suited .







Distribution of Automattic employees: there are people from Russia



At the same time, recently, including due to the coronavirus pandemic, the so-called FAANG companies, that is, technological giants, are beginning to hire more actively remote employees. So, starting in July, Facebook plans to actively hire remote employees .



All this increases the chances of finding a good remote position. However, you need to understand that in the case of remote IT professionals, companies usually require quite experienced people.



According to research, the average annual earnings of developers who work remotely is $ 139,081, and those who worked (before the pandemic) in the office - only $ 111,321. This fact was also confirmed during my conversation with Nikita Lvov, a backend engineer in European American startup Fjor.

- Nikita Lvov, Fjor

The problem with such lists is that it is not always clear who compiles them, how relevant the information is, there are questions to its accuracy and completeness. For example, a company may be listed, but there are no vacancies, then you need to google them separately, there may not be a salary or all requirements - for example, often even for remote positions, American companies are looking for employees within the United States. All this can lead to a large investment of time with a not very large exhaust in the form of scheduled interviews.



A more advanced way is to use data from specialized recruitment agencies. Today in Russia there are companies that directly specialize in finding engineers for foreign companies - with the possibility of moving to or remotely. For example, here are a few remote jobs from one recruiting agency:

The advantage of such "agency" vacancies is that they almost always indicate the salary, and the information as a whole is much more accurate.

Recently, some agencies have simplified the search even more. For example, a special telegram bot g-mate (@ g_jobbot ) was recently launched .



He can write the desired job parameters - salary, udalenka or relocation, technology, etc. After that, the bot will begin to send job descriptions directly to the chat. You can immediately ask a question that will get a real person, a consultant from the agency. As a result, very quickly you can not only find the vacancies that are suitable for the required parameters, but also get information about how many stages of the interview will be, who the hiring manager is and many other useful details.

Conclusion: tips for engineers + useful links



An important point: you are looking for a job, not a freelance, so the employer will evaluate the so-called soft skills. How well you communicate, openness and ability to work in a team is important in the case of remote-vacancies. It is necessary to formulate the correct expectations and, for example, study the principles of hiring and building teams of foreign companies. They often publish such information on the site. For example, here's a Zapier startup talking about culture in his remote team, and here are the principles of Amazon employees.



Among other long-playing tasks for everyone who wants to get a job in a foreign company - whether moving or remotely - is learning English. To find a good job, you do not need an ideal level, but the better you speak and understand, the easier it will be to go through interviews and join the work.

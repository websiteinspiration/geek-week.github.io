HCI: turnkey solutions for building a flexible corporate IT infrastructure

In IT there is such a thing as End User Computing - computing for end users. How, where and how can such decisions help, what should they be? According to today's requirements, employees would like to be able to work safely from any device, anywhere. Technological aspects account for up to 30% of incentives for employees involved, according to a Forrester report (Employee Index). This is a significant factor in attracting and retaining qualified personnel.

Computer systems, collectively called EUCs, help reduce costs and simplify desktop management.



For example, you can centrally install new applications, manage updates, and grant rights to users. And this applies not only to PCs, but also to other user devices with which they can access corporate applications and data anywhere. In particular, the concept of BYOD can be implemented.

Employees today are becoming more mobile. They work remotely, on different projects, from different countries, time zones and organizations. Services created by vendors are designed to provide flexibility in responding to changing needs of employees.


PC: the disadvantages of the traditional model.

Ideally, these services allow you to provide users with the necessary resources, including without deploying and operating their own IT infrastructure (in the case of a cloud model), increase or decrease their volume on demand, connect new users with a few clicks or using the API, or delete them . Administrators have the ability to manage users, applications, images, and policies much easier.

Company data is not stored on user devices, and you can control access to them in detail. Strict regulatory companies choose EUC to meet regulatory requirements for the financial industry, retail, healthcare, government, etc.

In the traditional model, desktop management, as a rule, is a very difficult task. Moreover, it is inefficient and expensive. Adding new client systems can be time consuming. Not to mention the fact that managing and maintaining such an environment with the growth of the PC fleet is becoming increasingly difficult. Another problem is updating this park. 67% of respondents plan to change corporate PCs at least every three years, according to a Forrester report (Analytics Global Business Technographics Infrastructure). Meanwhile, users, wherever they are, need access to their applications and files.

To solve this problem, IT departments are increasingly thinking about EUC - a set of technologies used to deliver desktops, to manage and protect desktop computers, applications and data.


According to the survey data , the main users of EUC are healthcare, the financial industry and the public sector.

How to eliminate unnecessary complexity when deploying an EUC? Today, vendors offer ready-to-deploy EUC solutions, in particular, hardware and software systems for VDI (Virtual Desktop Infrastructure) based on Citrix and VMware software. As an alternative, the DaaS cloud service (Desktop as a Service) is also offered.

Initially, the incentives for the introduction of VDI were the reduction in the cost of the workplace in large organizations and the requirements of information security. IT departments also had to ensure that end users did not experience performance issues when switching from physical workstations to virtual ones. The balance between cost and performance has become one of the most difficult tasks in providing users with efficient VDI platforms.

Meanwhile, the unification of virtual workstation software means saving on maintenance and preventing unauthorized downloads of applications or malicious programs thanks to controlled boot images. In addition, this solution can be scaled to support several hundred or even thousands of users. It is suitable for a wide range of VDI deployment scenarios and employee categories - from typical office application users to 3D rendering specialists.


According to the forecast of Maximize Market Research, in the coming years, the average annual growth rate of the global VDI market will exceed 11%, and by 2024 its volume will reach $ 14.6 billion.

The industry offers hyperconverged systems as one of the most efficient and easy-to-use platforms for deploying VDI. In particular, such a solution for VDI was developed by Nutanix and Lenovo.

Hyperconverged Infrastructure for VDI


Hyper-Converged Infrastructure (HCI) is the next evolution of data center equipment. This modular solution combines servers, storage systems, network components and virtualization software, which is responsible for creating a resource pool and their distribution, and a software-defined approach gives hyper-converged systems such properties as high flexibility and scalability of corporate IT infrastructure. VDI is one of the main use cases for HCI.
According to IDC, the growth of investment in hyper-converged infrastructure in the next five years will be more than 70%.

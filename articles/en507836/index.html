Successful launch. 44th in 2020. 15th from China. BeiDou Navigation Satellite

Vyacheslav Ermolin, June 23, 2020







Mission



Launch of the third generation BeiDou-3 navigation satellite for the Chinese navigation system. The launch completes the formation of the regular strength of the BeiDou navigation system (BDS). The satellite was launched into a geo-transitional orbit by the Chang Zheng-3B / G3 launch vehicle from the Sichan Cosmodrome.







Motto: “There is a total quantity. Global Services in Three Steps. ”



Start time and place



June 23, 2020 at 01:43:04 UTC. Xichang Cosmodrome (XSLC). Starting complex-2 (LC-2).



Launch vehicle



Chang Zheng-3B / G3. Three-stage launch vehicle for launching heavy loads into geostationary orbit. Load capacity of more than 5,000 kg per GPO .



Payload



Beidou-3 GEO-3. Third generation navigation satellite for geostationary orbit. The third unit in the series. Weight 4 600 kg.



Orbit



45807 / 2020-040A. Target Orbit - GEO. Geo-transition orbit 218 x 35 784 km with an inclination of 28.411 °, orbital period 631.38 min.



Interesting



  • The 15th launch of China this year. Two start up emergency.
  • The 336th launch of the Chang Zheng launch vehicle (The Great Campaign).
  • 17th launch of Chang Zheng-3B / G3 (Y68) launch vehicle. No accidents.
  • 55th launch of BeiDou satellite navigation system (BDS).
  • There are now 49 BeiDou satellites in orbit. 44 is used for its intended purpose. Not used for their intended purpose 5. The Beidou-2 and Beidou-3 satellites are used to provide services.
  • The staff of BeiDou consists of 35 devices: 27 satellites on MEO, 5 satellites on GEO and 3 satellites on IGSO.
  • There is the possibility of regional transmission of short text messages via Beidou satellites.
  • The cost of launching the launch vehicle is about $ 70 million.
  • The cost of withdrawing 1 kg of payload on LEO is at least $ 6,000.

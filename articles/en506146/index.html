What does a game analyst do and how to become one

Game analyst is a special profession that lies at the junction of game design and product analytics. Such specialists can work with amazing metrics - joy, reaction to mechanics, behavioral patterns. They help game developers make decisions based on data, not intuition.

If you want to work in game dev, pay attention to game analytics - this is a great way to become useful and make good money without a technical background.

We figured out the details of what kind of profession this is, what skills you need to have to start, and why you should study game analytics right now.

Any game project needs analytics


Gamedev is a huge industry. Analysts at Newzoo estimated that as early as 2021, a quarter of the world's population, or more than two billion people, would be interested in games.

Already this year, the market is projected to reach $ 160 billion. When such figures appear in the business, even a small change in the in-game economy in the conventional Fortnite or GTA Online can cost the company hundreds of millions of dollars. Therefore, gaming companies use a special decision-making methodology.

Data Driven is an approach where decisions in a project are made based on data, not intuition. See, here is a conditional example of the difference between regular statistics and DD gatherers:

  • Traditional statistics - we collect reports in which we say that last week in the game sales of certain items fell. We show in dynamics how the level of sales for the whole year has changed.
  • Data Driven - we collect data that shows why players stopped buying items. We found out that after the last update, the balance in several quests changed slightly, players literally run through all the tasks without difficulty and the need for items has disappeared. In the report, we offer ways to fix it - for example, changing the characteristics of mobs in the right locations.

Game analytics uses the Data Driven approach.

What data is collected and studied by the game analyst


Game analytics is a new profession. There is no well-established classification of specialties in the industry, so the responsibilities of the analyst will differ depending on the size and type of company.

There are many analysts in the conditional Epic Games, and for several years you can only deal with the balance of the economy and its impact on monetization in a separate level of a particular game.

  • In local studios that develop 2-3 high-profile projects, you already have more opportunities - you look at all metrics and their development right down to money, suggest changes in balance or mechanics.
  • In a small studio, you will implement and study not only data from the game itself, but also from external sources. Simply put, engage in marketing analytics and create funnels for attracting and retaining users in the game.

Emotion Metrics


You track places and events where the user enjoys and, conversely, experiences annoyance. There is even a special FUUU metric in the industry . It symbolizes the person who, with a cry of FUUU, quits playing. Of course, this is a fairly biased indicator, so for objectivity it is used together with other metrics.

For example, you can track how many times players were killed in a particular location. If out of 100 people successfully passed the event on the first try, everything, then the gameplay is too easy. This can lead to boredom, so we transfer the data to the game designer and offer to correct the balance.

Based on the metrics of emotions, inconspicuous innovations are introduced into the gameplay. For example, so that players feel more excitement, before they die, their character becomes a little stronger.

The analyst‚Äôs goal is to make the gameplay addictive, to encourage players to enter the game as often as possible and stay there as long as possible.

Product Metrics


The choice of metrics also depends on the genre and type of monetization of the game. In a large MMO, revenue comes from selling add-ons and items in the store. In the idle game, you need to figure out how to leave the game addictive and interesting, but at the same time make users watch more advertising.

How to become a game analyst


Game analytics appeared not so long ago, so we could not find any university programs for it. To start in the profession, you should complete the courses or do self-training. Then look for a job at a Junior position. Or try to take an internship - this will help to make acquaintances in the industry and immerse yourself in the profession, if before that there was no experience in analytics at all.

Do I need a technical background


No, being a programmer or graduating from a technical university is optional. Game analyst is a profession at the intersection of game design and data analytics, so all the necessary knowledge can be obtained from courses from scratch.
Knowing the math to start is also optional. The work does not require a complete mechanic, and you can learn how to manage libraries to calculate models in the process. Just as well as understand the principles of probability theory. But, of course, learning with a math background will be easier and faster.

Do I need English


To start studying, English is minimally needed; you can use the Google translator to read the documentation. If you decide to develop in the profession, then it is worth raising your level. High-quality literature, YouTube channels from leading analysts, blogs and forums - most of the sources of information are foreign.

Do I need to play myself


Yes. As with game designers , gaming analytics is worth a go if you like to play. Get acquainted with game genres, understand the mechanics and types of monetization.

You do not have to fan from all the games in a row and sit out hundreds of hours for each new product. But without a common understanding of the market and trends, it will be difficult.

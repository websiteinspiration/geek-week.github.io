Connect. With success

Traditional data transmission channels will continue to perform their function for many years, however, they become affordable only in densely populated buildings. In other conditions, other solutions are needed that can provide reliable high-speed communications for a reasonable price.

In this article, you will learn how to solve communication problems where traditional channels are expensive or inaccessible. What classes of solutions exist, how they differ, and how to choose what is needed for a particular task.









There are several classes of technologies that claim to solve communication problems where traditional communication channels are absent or not economically feasible. Balancers, aggregators and adders seem to do the same thing, but fundamentally differ in the quality of solving problems. Let's get it right.



Balancers



At any one time, any one channel works. This solves the issue of reliability due to redundancy, but does not give a speed increase. At the same time, the vast majority of balancers do not check which channel is faster and switch simply to the one that works. 80% of the solutions on the market that use several SIM cards are just such Balancers - when the connection disappears through one channel, it automatically switches the connection to another.







There is a separate transition class between the balancer and the aggregator. The speed in several streams, for example from several users at the same time, will be higher than on any one. This approach, if implemented correctly, does not even require a traffic termination infrastructure and is widely used in the class of low-cost routers. The solution is able to give cumulatively high speed, but each individual user will receive the speed available only on one channel. You can download torrents on such a device very comfortably.



Problem to be solved



Improving reliability. Reservation of data transmission channels.



Key feature



  1. Switching speed from a non-working channel to a working one. The faster the device realizes that one channel is not working and you need to switch to another, the better
  2. Priority work on the fastest channel


pros



  1. The price of the device. The cheapest solution on the market
  2. No intermediate traffic termination infrastructure required
  3. Do not require qualified personnel for use and maintenance


Minuses



  1. No channel quality check. The device can switch to a channel with mediocre communication, while the adjacent channel is much faster.


Intended use



Services that do not require high data transfer rates and are ready for short downtime



Aggregators



This term came from the English aggregate. In the context of data transmission systems, it has been used for a very long time and is used in solutions for combining physical wired and optical data transmission channels.



These are more advanced systems in comparison with balancers - they use several data transmission channels simultaneously. Through each channel, a connection is created with an intermediate server on which traffic is combined and transmitted further to the target service. Therefore, if even several channels disappear, the data transfer is not interrupted. That is, there is no concept of switching from one channel to another. It should also be noted that on wireless data channels, contrary to popular belief, most of these solutions do not increase speed or increase it slightly. For example, 4 channels of 10 Mbit / s in total should give 40 Mbit, but the aggregators in the L3 tunnel will give about 12-18. These are extreme indicators of speed increase under ideal conditions. This is due to the large uneven entropy in the channels.A non-trivial task is to combine channels with different bandwidth, and most importantly, different delays.



This is certainly better than ten, but much less than the expected forty. Unscrupulous manufacturers are trying to hide this flaw using a bunch of proxy server + source address replacement. In this case, the speed rises significantly, but this only works if the connection is initiated from the side of the device. If you initiate a connection from the outside world, then this technique will not work. If you want to combine two networks, for example, a point of sale with a head office or a train with a central network, the aggregator will not cope with the task, because the speed to the device will be 10 times less than from the device. In addition, in the case of the use of a telecom operator in networks, such manipulations are guaranteed to create questions from the regulatory authorities regarding the system of operational-search measures (SORM).



Solutions for the aggregation of wireless data channels are relatively simple, do not require investments in high-tech research. Almost all of them contain only ready-made, widely described Open Source solutions. Manufacturers prefer to make a simple WEB-interface and pass it off as an innovative development. This approach is very common in Russia.

Adders



In the context of data transmission channels, this term appeared in Russia only a few years ago. These solutions are very similar to aggregators, but radically differ in that, while retaining all their advantages, they are devoid of all their shortcomings.







A more detailed scheme and principle of operation



If encryption is required from the device itself to the termination server, this option, like on-the-fly compression, is present in mature technologies on the market.



For L3 tunnels, the utilization of the data transmission channels of the adders is about 90%. For example, where the aggregator gives 40 Mbit / s, the adder confidently gives 70 Mbit / s. Therefore, it is called the adder. This is a very difficult task and it requires serious high-tech research.

A successful increase in speed in the L3 tunnel gives an even network topology without "features".



Unlike aggregators, adders do not have restrictions on the scope. They can be used on any type of data transmission channels and on any network topology. The network created by the adder is completely standard and, unlike aggregators, in operation will not cause questions for regulators and pitfalls in operation.

Complete solution



Technologies to increase reliability and throughput, in addition to their main function, should be easily manageable and scalable, be comprehensive solutions to end-user problems and not solve the problem fragmentarily shifting most of the infrastructure and operational tasks to the competence of the customer.



What is needed to make the solution comprehensive?



1. Unified network management system

It makes it possible to manage all network devices - centrally update the firmware and configuration, display warnings and crashes, balance the load on the network. It is transparent to manage all the functions of each device individually and, in some cases, see the location of the device and its key characteristics on an interactive map.

A high-quality network management system saves on engineering staff, reduces problem solving time and does everything that the ‚Äúbrain‚Äù usually does.

If the solution does not automatically know how to redistribute traffic from devices to termination servers and provide automatic failover, it is not recommended for commercial use.

What to choose



1. If you are satisfied with the work of any one channel and just want to play it safe - choose a balancer. Simple, cheap, effective. It will be a plus if the manufacturer has laid the following modes there: - The

concept of the main and backup channel. When the backup channel is turned on only when the primary is unavailable. The main channel turns on as soon as it becomes available.

-Mechanism of channel quality control without generating overhead traffic.

- It will be a big plus to increase the aggregate speed with the session-wise sharing of traffic between available channels. This will give a significant cumulative increase in speed, but will not give an increase in one session.

Effectively, these mechanisms work only together.



2. If you lack the speed of any one channel or need maximum speed - select the adders. Aggregators cost as well, but do less.

Theory


First, we need a small theoretical minimum on this topic. Let's start by understanding what tension lines are and how to count them. In fact, these lines are the merger of many tension vectors, which can be calculated as follows:E= frack‚àó|q|r2.



E calculation method


I calculated the tension vector through the similarity of triangles, thereby obtaining projections on the x and y axes dx and dy, respectively.



From the similarity it follows that the radius of the vector from the charge to the point in space r and the length of the intensity vector E is equal to the ratio of the projections of these vectors (x1 and dx, respectively) fracRE= fracxdx= fracydy. Formula of the resulting vector vecE= left( beginarrayc sumi=0N fracxi‚àó| vecEi|Ri sumi=0N fracyi‚àó| vecEi|Ri endarray right) qquad

def E(q_prop, xs, ys, nq): #q_prop=[[xq1, yq1, q1, mq1, vxq1, vyq1], [xq2, yq2, q2, mq2, vxq2, vyq2] ... ] 
    l=1
    k=9*10**9
    Ex=0
    Ey=0
    c=0
    for c in range(len(q_prop)):#                
        q=q_prop[c]
        r=((xs-q[0])**2+(ys-q[1])**2)**0.5
        dEv=(k*q[2])/r**2
        dEx=(xs-q[0])*(dEv/r)*l
        dEy=(ys-q[1])*(dEv/r)*l

        Ex+=dEx
        Ey+=dEy
    return Ex, Ey

def Update_all(q_prop):
  vx=0
  vy=0
  x=0
  y=0
  q_prop_1=np.copy(q_prop)
  for c in range(len(q_prop)):#         
    xs=q_prop[c][0]
    ys=q_prop[c][1]
    q =q_prop[c][2]
    m =q_prop[c][3]
    vx=q_prop[c][4]
    vy=q_prop[c][5]
    Ex, Ey= E(q_prop, xs, ys, c)

    x=(((Ex*q)/m)*dt**2)/2+vx*dt+xs
    y=(((Ey*q)/m)*dt**2)/2+vy*dt+ys
    vx+=((Ex*q)/m)*dt
    vy+=((Ey*q)/m)*dt
    #print(q_prop[c]-[x,y,q,m,vx,vy])
    q_prop_1[c]=[x,y,q,m,vx,vy]
  
  return q_prop_1#

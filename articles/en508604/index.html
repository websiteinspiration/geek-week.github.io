Simulators based on hardware and software platform in technical education





In the modern understanding, the learning process is considered as a process of interaction between the teacher and students in order to familiarize students with certain knowledge, skills, abilities and values. The structural components of the learning process are the means and methods of training. Teaching methods include the purpose of learning, the way of assimilation and the nature of the interaction between the subjects of instruction, and the means of instruction are material objects and objects used in the educational process as carriers of educational information and an instrument for the activity of the teacher and students to achieve their educational goals (i.e., the implementation of methods ) In this sense, learning tools and methods are interdependent, which makes it possible to initially classify the concept of software imitators as specific learning tools used in certain teaching methods:



  1. obtaining theoretical knowledge on the basis of conducting a physical training experiment (the process of obtaining and processing experimental data) (Laboratory work).
  2. training in the application of acquired knowledge in solving complex problems related to the field of activity of future specialists (Course design)
  3. assessment of skills and professional skills of specialists for the purpose of their subsequent certification or certification (Certification or certification)
  4. practical study of the device, the principle of operation, commissioning, adjustment of equipment, characteristic of the mastered profession (Workshop)
  5. the formation and improvement of trainees' professional skills and abilities necessary for them to manage a material object (Training, advanced training)






Conducting training at real facilities and equipment, in turn, is often fraught with significant technical difficulties and significant material costs:



  1. the high cost of training equipment and its operation;
  2. obsolete equipment, a small range of equipment in comparison with the conditions of production;
  3. the great distance of the student from the location of the educational equipment;
  4. high risk of work performed;
  5. high complexity of changing the configuration of equipment and environmental parameters;
  6. long duration of work;
  7. the impossibility of visual observation of the internal structure of the equipment under study, micro and macro objects and processes, fast or slow technological and natural processes or phenomena;

The search and implementation of new teaching methods and tools has led to the fact that, starting in 1980, there appeared other, much different from computer simulators, new teaching tools that also use the computing power of computers, i.e., computer training tools that solved a significant part of the stated problems of ‚Äúphysical education‚Äù and, in addition, provided a number of additional opportunities.

Currently, software simulators are increasingly used in educational institutions, since the use of simulators, based on the experience of their use, significantly increases efficiency in five key areas at once:



  • significant improvement in the quality of training;
  • significant reduction in training costs;
  • reduced training time;
  • the ability to prepare more students;
  • reducing the danger in training, increasing the efficiency of labor protection, industrial and environmental safety.

Conducting more work. The ability of simulators to ‚Äúaccelerate time‚Äù allows you to complete training faster, i.e. the use of simulators allows the implementation of more laboratory, practical work, training, etc. As a result, it is possible to use time more efficiently while achieving a higher quality of learning.



Increase in the share of individual work of students. Individual work is largely distinguished by the perception and storage of information. According to Haskett consulting inc. (HCI): "People remember 20% of what they see, 40% of what they see and hear, and 70% of what they see, hear, and do." When using real equipment for training, it is quite difficult to ensure individual work with the equipment of each student. The reasons for this situation are understandable - the impossibility of providing equipment to each student, lack of time for each student to perform work individually. An example of a real stand



The device and operation of a simulated stand. The stand is mounted on a flat metal base and consists of an electric motor unit with a tachometer, a gearbox under test - it is conditionally shown in a section, of a load device - a tape brake. The motor assembly is mounted on a cast bracket mounted on the base of the stand. The stator of the electric motor can rotate freely in ball bearings mounted in a cast bracket, relative to the axis common to the motor armature. The motor armature shaft at one end is connected through a coupling to the input shaft of the gearbox. A measuring device consisting of a lever, a rod and a sensor with a spring is mounted on the front side of the cast bracket. When the electric motor is turned on, the stator reactive moment (equal in absolute value to the moment on the armature shaft) rotates the stator. Lever arm,fixed on the stator, abuts against the spring-loaded rod of the sensor, which keeps the stator from turning. The signal from this sensor for measuring the torque transmitted from the engine to the gearbox, after processing, is displayed on the left dial device (‚ÄúEngine Torque‚Äù) and a numerical indicator Tdv. So at the stand, the torque transmitted from the engine to the gearbox is measured.



The gearbox under test consists of two cylindrical gears. Its body is conventionally made of a translucent material and is shown in section.



The load device is a tape brake, and serves to create a working load on the gearbox - the braking torque on its output shaft. The value of the braking torque is set by the toggle-load regulator. At the same time, the spring stretches stronger or weaker, which is also visible on the monitor.



A panel with controls is installed on the left side of the stand: Stand power switch; Toggle switch-regulator of engine speed; Toggle switch-regulator load (brake torque).



Measuring instruments: dial gauges (‚ÄúEngine Torque‚Äù, ‚ÄúEngine Power‚Äù, and ‚ÄúBrake Speed‚Äù); digital indication devices (‚Äúndv - engine speed‚Äù, ‚ÄúTdv - engine torque‚Äù, ‚ÄúPdv - engine power‚Äù, ‚Äúntorm - brake speed‚Äù, ‚ÄúTtm - brake torque‚Äù)

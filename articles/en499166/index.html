, Open Source




FOSS technologies are now widely used by industry leaders such as Oracle and Microsoft, who have been implementing it for many years, because such technologies make organizations more efficient and provide an environment with wide possibilities for customization, adaptation and scaling. With large players such as Amazon and IBM, who are focused on using open source as a tool for creating powerful cloud solutions, it may seem that technology is limited to a major league, but small and medium-sized businesses are also gradually getting involved in this process, TechRepublic writes. . Many people realize that open source allows them to compete at the level of larger technology companies and provides the flexibility, interoperability, and cost savings that these solutions provide.But there are also difficulties that are not faced by such big players: the need to search for competent personnel, choosing the right projects to use, operational problems and lack of support.



Open Source 50% 2019. 2020?




According to a WhiteSource team research report, the main source of growth in the number of discovered vulnerabilities in open source products is the increase in the use of such products per se, writes DevOps. There are now more open source projects, code, and community members than ever before. All these kind people are working hard not only to write more code, often with the support of major technology giants, but also to look for vulnerabilities in the code that could put users of their components at risk. The combination of more written code and the number of eyes analyzing the code for these inevitable human errors ultimately leads to the discovery of more vulnerabilities.An increase in the number of vulnerabilities in open source components will have a significant impact on software development. In recent years, we have seen that open source components play an increasingly important role in how developers create their applications. Most estimates suggest that open source components make up 60 to 80% of the code base in most modern applications. Zoom? Jitsi Open Source




From meetings and parties to dates, we all now live in video conferencing applications, writes Wired. But after a series of privacy and security breaches at Zoom, which became almost synonymous with video conferencing during the Covid-19 pandemic, many organizations and individuals are wondering which service is the most secure for our conversations. Emil Ivov says no one needs to be trusted. Yves is the creator of Jitsi's open source software for text and video chat and the head of video collaboration at 8x8, which acquired Jitsi in 2018. The firm sells services based on Jitsi code, but still pays developers to support the open source version. Jitsi Meet is a video conferencing app with convenient features,such as the ability to password protect your appointments or exclude people from the conference. But what sets it apart from most well-known video conferencing services is that it is free and can work completely on your own equipment.





Python 2




Is Python 2 dead? Not really, but from this event he is making a confident step towards his place of honor in the museum of the history of computer technology. On April 20, the last final release of Python 2.7.18 was introduced, which marked the complete cessation of support for the Python 2 branch, writes OpenNET. This event completes an era, as the StackOverflow blog puts it . If you still haven't upgraded to version 3, it's time. However, the 2nd version will continue to live on through the efforts of individual companies, for example, Red Hat will continue to maintain packages with Python 2.7 throughout the life cycle of RHEL 6 and 7 distributions, and for RHEL 8 it will generate package updates in the Application Stream until June 2024 . If this is not your option - you are welcome to see the official transition guide. But it can take a long time, for example Dropbox migrated for 3 years.

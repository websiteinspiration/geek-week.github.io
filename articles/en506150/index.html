Hit the SETI: voluntary computing for skeptics, optimists, and sophisticated crunchers


Nature has become so purified that beavers have returned to the Moika embankment in St. Petersburg, and public interest in projects of voluntary distributed computing has been returned. In this text, however, you will not see descriptions of the well-known volunteer computing initiatives.

Selectel technical writer Ulyana Malysheva talked with the leaders of several distributed computing projects, crankers (as volunteers are called) and even the administrator of the Russian site BOINC. What attracts and what repels in such volunteering? Why did the “golden age” of voluntary computing in Russia end in 2010? What projects can you not know about, even if long ago in the "topic"? The answers are under the cut.

In 2020, coronavirus caused a new wave of interest in voluntary distributed computing. The Folding @ home project, which is currently engaged in the research of SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19), outperformed all supercomputers in the top 500 in the world in terms of total performance .

As of May, he “collected” capacities by 2.3 exaflops - both volunteers and companies invested in the project. VMware not only allocated computing resources to the project, but also allowed it to connect to Folding @ Home through the vSphere platform. And Selectel donated the power of servers with graphics accelerators NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1080.

By the way, the world's first exaflops computer was supposed to appear in 2021. Intel promisedbuild a machine with a productivity of a quintillion operations per second. As a result, it is more powerful than a distributed “computer” assembled by Folding @ home literally on flops, it seems that it will not become any more.

Reasons for success


Folding @ home can be called a phenomenon among voluntary computing projects. Many of them are content with only a few teraflops of “attention”. There are several reasons for this success.

A clear and relevant problem. Coronavirus touched everyone. Often the projects of the same BOINC platform are criticized for the obscurity of the research goal and the result that is difficult to live.

Dissemination of information in the media. Among the voluntary computing projects there are stars - SETI @ home, Rosetta @ home, Einstein @ home. However, up to 80-85% of other initiatives do not go beyond the forums and their pages on the BOINC website.

Reporting the results.Folding @ home is constantly in touch with volunteers, scientists are ready to talk about the work done. The lack of news about the project is one of the reasons for the volunteers to cool down to voluntary computing.

Cross-platform. The Folding @ home client can be downloaded for Windows, macOS, and a number of Linux distributions.

Folding @ home cannot cope with two more common reasons for skepticism about voluntary computing. However, they are leveled by the social weight of the project.

There is no guarantee of the result (or it will be after a long time). Indeed, many voluntary computing projects have existed for more than ten years. But over the years there have been very few real achievements that people understand.

The answer to skeptics


According to the data relevant on June 9, 2020, the number of active participants of the BOIN platform is 105,790 people, 727,784 computers are connected to the work. In this day alone, more than 70 new volunteers joined the community.

Participation in voluntary computing projects is not always about rationality. After interviewing the crunchers, we found out what usually motivates them to join the initiatives, despite some of their obvious disadvantages.

A sense of contribution to the development of science


Participation in projects gives a sense of involvement in solving the complex problem of humanity - whether it is a mathematical problem or a challenge with a large social exhaust.

Challenges and entire championships are held on the BOINC platform. Every year it hosts a personal “Formula 1” for crunchers - Formula BOIN, where teams compete in the number of capacities invested in projects.

The championship consists of a marathon and sprints. The marathon takes into account points earned for all types of team participation in BOINC projects. Sprints mean the concentration of teams on one project, which is reported 24 hours before the start of the sprint. Thus, the platform draws the attention of the community to all initiatives, and not only to those in which it is profitable to participate.


There are several teams from Russia at BOINC. One of the largest is the Russia Team. Now it consists of 2,388 participants, of which 164 are active. A group of Russian crunchers takes 32nd place in the rating, taking into account more than 100,000 teams from around the world.

Alexander Andreev, administrator of the Russian site BOINC

There are initiatives under the “roof” of which several studies are being implemented at once. Connecting to such projects allows you to invest power in several scientific fields at once or to easily "transfer" them from one research to another.

World community grid


A site aggregator with a nice interface immediately offers to get acquainted with relevant projects. Now there are seven, for every taste. By joining the WCD, you can help research on microbiome, childhood cancer, AIDS, tuberculosis, and even forecasting rainfall in Africa. The COVID-19 study was not without its research.

Science united


This site makes the life of a cruncher, who does not want to waste time choosing projects, easier. Through it, it is possible to donate computer resources to various BOINC projects. You do not need to establish clients for each individual project - just register at Science United and choose the scientific field that you sympathize with most. This can be earth science, physics, biology and medicine, mathematics and others.

Some more projects worth paying attention to


nanoHUB @ home


This project gathers power for the nanoHub community, which includes scientists and students from around the world. Scientific field - nanotechnology. The community has a website where materials and tools for research and training in the field of nanotechnology are in the public domain. Volunteer capacities go mainly to the construction of various simulations.

GPUGRID


From the name it can be understood that within the framework of this initiative, only the power of GPUs is quoted. They are going to create high-quality biomolecular simulations. Spanish scientists from the University of Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona benefit from donations from volunteers. From the pros: the community has a live Twitter , which publishes all the important news and project updates.

Dreamlab


An option for those who do not want to join BOINC or use their computer. Mobile operator Vodafone, together with Imperial College London, have created the DreamLab mobile application that will allow you to join the fight against coronavirus. The peculiarity of the project is that it uses mobile processors to create a virtual “supercomputer”. You can simply run the application on your smartphone while it is charging, and you are asleep.

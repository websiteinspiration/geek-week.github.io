By the Day of Russian Science, the best achievements of domestic scientists of 2019 are named

Mail.ru Group and NUST “MISiS” compiled a rating of the most significant discoveries made by Russian scientists in 2019. On the Day of Russian Science, Search Mail.ru, Hi-Tech Mail.ru and NUST “MISiS” found out which achievements of Russian science aroused the greatest interest among Internet users.

According to a study by Mail.ru Group and NUST MISiS, 86% of Internet users are interested in science news. Almost 80% say they follow the achievements of Russian scientists. Nevertheless, only 29% know about the existence of such a holiday as the Day of Russian Science.

Search Mail.ru analyzed the number of requests for the achievements of Russian scientists over the past year. The top 5 in terms of the number of search queries included:

1) the launch and start of work of Spectra-RG, an observatory for building a map of the Universe;

2) the creation of the first prototype of a quantum computer ;

4) the beginning of the production of artificial meat ;

5) the discovery of the remains of the main ally of Napoleon, General Guden, near Smolensk .

Hi-tech Mail.ru conducted a survey among users and found out which of these events the Russians consider the most important and useful for humanity.

The key achievement of Russian science in 2019, users called the creation of a prototype of the first quantum computer. 45% of respondents voted for him . In second place is the launch of the Spectrum RG observatory (29%) . The top three finalists also included the production of artificial meat - 14% of the study participants voted for it . Only 9% of respondents recognized the discovery of an interstellar comet as significant and only 3% voted for a unique historical find - the discovery in Smolensk under the dance floor of the remains of General Guden.

The attention of users to work on a quantum computer caused an ambiguous reaction in the scientific community:

“It is difficult to say why people who are not related to science are showing such great interest in a quantum computer. Moreover, it will most likely never replace the devices we are used to, as it is designed to solve special problems that modern supercomputers cannot do: calculations for the synthesis of new materials and compounds, quantum chemistry and information security, optimization of the schedule and operation of complex systems such as rail and air transport, energy networks, transportation of raw materials through pipelines, the financial sector , ”commented Alexey Ustinov, head of the Superconducting Laboratory e metamaterials »NITU MISiS .


Alexey Ustinov

Another representative of the scientific community, the chairman of the Russian Science Foundation’s expert council, Alexander Klimenko, on the contrary, considers the interest in quantum technologies in the modern world quite logical: “Creating a computer and communication lines based on quantum effects can be compared in scale to the transition from analog to digital technology. Even the amateur imagines that this gave a person, and the expectations of a new revolution foster interest in her. In addition, recently there have been regularly published news that a company from the "big five" IT giants has created or plans to create the "most powerful quantum computer", which, of course, attracts the attention of the public . "


Alexander Klimenko

Also, according to Klimenko, the increased interest of users in discoveries in the space sphere is quite justified: “Such attention to space topics can be explained by the exceptional popularity of the space theme since the launch of the first Earth satellite and Yuri Gagarin’s flight, the influence of various modern TV shows and films with“ near-cosmic "plot -" Time of the First, "" Star Wars ", as well as a huge interest in the possible existence of life on other planets."

The scientific community also believes that in addition to these topics, climate issues and technology research could also have a significant impact on society in 2019:“Among the topics that should be on the list, global climate change and the associated increase in natural disasters, the creation of new weapons, artificial intelligence and its relationship with humans,” Klimenko notes.

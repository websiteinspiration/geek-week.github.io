5 most popular questions when implementing a service mesh in a corporation using Istio as an example

Not only in the world, but also in the Russian market, you can notice a wave of interest in the technology of service networks, or service mesh. Below under the cut, we will understand what tasks this technology solves, how it developed, and also what issues corporations face when adapting Istio in their IT infrastructure.







My acquaintance with the service mesh was largely accidental, when in 2017 I came across an announcement about the collaboration of Google and IBM to create a new open source project. Not to say that partnerships of the largest technology companies are rare, but it was this case that aroused my great interest. Firstly, there are still not so many examples of interaction between Google and IBM: perhaps the most famous facts will be the work around the open hardware architecture of OpenPOWER , as well as the availability of IBM services in Google Cloud. Secondly, despite the existence of examples where both Google and IBM are developing the same open source project (Kubernetes or TensorFlow), there are very few cases where both of these companies became the founders of the project, and they are quite unique. And thirdly, the presence of Lyft among the founders of the IBM and Google project- a competitor to Uber as a taxi call service, aroused questions and genuine interest. That announcement in 2017 talked about the release of Istio technology, designed to control the interaction of various services. At first, it is not clear what the problem is and why to create such a project, since quite a lot of approaches were known for managing interaction between services. To understand the issues, it is worth taking a wider view of the situation and looking at changes in the IT architecture at that point in time.



What is service mesh



The reasons for the emergence and so rapidly growing popularity of the service mesh are inextricably linked to changes in the IT architecture. It's hard to imagine the appearance of a service mesh without a microservice architecture - let's see why. One can argue for a long time what microservice architecture is, since there is no single and well-established definition (and in general this topic deserves a separate article), but for simplicity we dwell on the following key characteristics: these are small autonomous parts of business logic implemented as services, each of which It has its own build and support cycle. In this case, it is logical to think about how these small services will interact with each other and how to control this process. This is where the service mesh appears. In real life, this technology can be compared with a road navigator.Various situations can happen on the roads: accidents, traffic jams, failure of traffic lights, and the system allows you to get from point ‚ÄúA‚Äù to point ‚ÄúB‚Äù in the most optimal way, knowing about all the events that happened.



The key tasks that the service mesh takes on are:



  • traffic management
  • load balancing
  • security in the interaction of services
  • metrics collection and monitoring


Another analogue of the real-life service mesh can be a mail delivery service: it decides what to do with the parcel if you are not at home or you have moved, monitors the safety of transportation, ensures the privacy of the transmission of the parcel, and monitors statuses.



Service mesh evolution



Currently, the service mesh has become a separate layer and in many ways the standard of the de facto modern IT architecture at the infrastructure level. Is it possible to build modern solutions without a service mesh and implement this functionality somehow differently? Of course, yes, the only question is convenience, flexibility, implementation time and further support of the solution. Of course, the tasks associated with traffic management or balancing, you can solve yourself at the code level for each of the services. But since microservice architecture implies the existence of tens, hundreds or even thousands of different services, it is not at all convenient to implement the same functionality when developing each of them. Why develop the same functionality dozens or hundreds of times if you can take it out separately?



The first implementations of the service mesh were made at the application level. The most famous example is the Netflix OSS framework (or library set), available for use since 2012. It includes components that implement the functionality inherent in the service mesh: using Eureka , service discovery is performed, with Hystrix , a series of circuit breaker patterns are detected, Zuul solves dynamic routing problems.



The following factors had a strong influence on the need to change this approach:



  • support of several programming languages ‚Äã‚Äãand lack of dependence on them
  • ease of separation of application and infrastructure tasks for DevOps: the ability to make changes to the application code without affecting the infrastructure
  • container technology development


Whatever one may say, Netflix OSS was much more integrated into the application logic of the service, required the use of the Netflix technology stack, had libraries dependent on the development language, and was not sharpened for use in containers.



Istio, as an example of the next wave of the evolution of technology, uses a sidecar (Envoy from Lyft), that is, a proxy that is located next to each service and takes on the functions of managing security policies, traffic, telemetry collection, etc. This allows you to be neutral in choosing a programming language , have good integration with Kubernetes as the de facto standard for orchestrating container environments, and have no dependencies on application service logic. In fact, it was at this step in the evolution of service mesh technology that it was finally separated into a separate infrastructure layer as an independent level of modern IT architecture.



Technology Adaptation in the Corporate World



The growing popularity of microservice architecture and container technologies and the advantages that they provide did not leave aside large Russian corporations that began to think about using these approaches at home. Along with this, a number of questions arise regarding the use of the service mesh and, in particular, Istio. Many doubt that the technology is mature enough for use in the corporate world, which has sufficient functionality and is able to withstand heavy loads. Below you will find answers to the five most popular questions that corporate IT employees asked us about using Istio to help make sure that it is ready for implementation.







Question 1: Has Istio technology matured enough for corporate adoption?



Since we are talking about large organizations, for them the question of the maturity of a particular technology is one of the very first. What is meant by maturity? Maturity parameters can be functionality, stability, number of industrial implementations, maximum load, as well as compliance with a number of non-functional requirements, for example, fault tolerance or scaling. It is important to understand that for each company, the quantitative values ‚Äã‚Äãof each of the parameters will be different, depending on its scale and use cases.



What can I say about Istio? From the point of view of stability in the open source world, the release of version 1.0, which was introduced by the Istio community of contributors in the summer of 2018 , is also important. Examples of the use of technology are already dozens of companies, the most famous of which are Ebay , Yahoo and ING Bank . An interesting example is the history of the use of Istio in the operation of the F16 fighter . Separately, it is worth highlighting the fact that the leading cloud providers IBM and Google provide services based on Istio for their customers . Moreover, Istio is even used as part of the new open source frameworks. Example: Kabanero , aimed at faster development of applications on top of the container infrastructure.



In terms of non-functional requirements, corporations pay great attention to fault tolerance requirements, and here Istio provides a large number of deployment options, both within one or several Kubernetes clusters. Below in the article we will consider the issue of deployment options in more detail. I think it is unnecessary to say that all these arguments unambiguously make it possible to judge that Istio as a technology has reached a sufficient level of maturity for implementation in the corporate segment to solve business-critical tasks.



Question 2: Service mesh - for new environments with containers, and what to do with the traditional landscape on virtual machines?



The service mesh belongs to the type of cloud native technologies that were created in the era of container development. But, recalling the reality of the IT landscape of corporations, it can be noted that there are not so many industrial systems working on top of containers, and corporations are only at the beginning of the path to their mass use. Many business-critical tasks still work on virtual machines, and the question arises here: is it really impossible to use Istio for them?



The short answer is, of course, possible. Istio allows you to create different configurations using both the container infrastructure and virtual machines or even bare metal. To connect virtual machines and the service mesh, you need to perform a number of actions, for example, ensure the visibility of the cluster IP addresses with Istio, etc. A more detailed description and examples can be found.here .



Question 3: Which deployment model to choose?



This issue, by the way, is very complicated and affects many areas, including both technical things (non-functional requirements for fault tolerance, security and performance) and organizational ones (team structure). What deployment options are possible?



  • one or more clusters (multicluster)
  • one or more network segments
  • one or more control points
  • one or more grids (mesh)


At the current time, we can say that the option of multi-cluster deployment is the most popular among corporations among others - it is not by chance that this aspect pays much of the community‚Äôs work around the Istio project. What are the reasons for choosing this option? This is the presence of a large number of development teams for individual products and systems, the need to deploy in several data centers, this division into zones of development, testing and industrial operation. Because of all these requirements, the option of deploying a single cluster, even with multiple service networks inside it, does not provide the necessary level of resource isolation.



Of course, inside a multi-cluster deployment there may also be options for placing control panels: it is not necessary to place one control panel on one cluster; several clusters can use one control panel.



In practice, it takes more than one week of design to determine the deployment option, as architects need to compromise between a good level of isolation of resources and the overhead of interaction between clusters.



Question 4: And what load can it withstand?



Since we are talking about corporations, as a rule, they serve a large number of customers and, accordingly, have large loads on their systems. Therefore, the question is absolutely not idle and in practice there are not rare cases when one or another new technology did not give the desired performance and could not provide the provision of company services at the request of customers.



Of course, the most competent answer to this question: depends on many factors. How the system is deployed, what requirements for fault tolerance or isolation, which version of the technology is used - all these factors have a direct impact on performance. But this answer is unlikely to be able to give at least some estimates, so let's look at the existing experience of other companies. Unfortunately, in open sources it is difficult to find detailed information from companies on their experience using Istio, but at various meetings, meetings or conferences in early 2019, one of the companies in the field of information security presented numbers of tens of thousands of transactions per second (TPS) system using Istio. Many corporations conducted pilot projects and obtained results in several hundred transactions per second. Is it a lot or a little? For example,only a few large banks in Russia have loads of several thousand TPS on their IT systems, so an assessment of even several hundred TPS will more than cover the vast majority of the tasks of any corporation.



Question 5: When will the features we need?



During the implementation of Istio, technical specialists of organizations often turn to IBM as the founding company of the project with questions about the timing of the implementation of specific new features of the technology.



On this question, you can clearly show the difference between proprietary software from any vendor and the open source world. Many corporations, from the point of view of their expectations, relate equally to those and other technologies, although at some points the differences are quite large and it is strange to expect the same responsibility from participants in an open source project as from a manufacturer of commercial software. Of course, behind most successful open source projects is one or more companies, which, as a rule, is the founder. But unlike commercial software, the parent company cannot just take and add a feature to the project without feedback from other contributors. There are pros and cons. There were times when one of the contributors undertook the implementation of a specific feature,in which many project participants were interested, but he missed the deadlines due to employment and illness, postponing the release for several months in advance. Therefore, in the case of an open source project, it is much harder to guarantee the release of a specific feature within a specific time period than in the case of commercial software. On the other hand, there is no such direct dependence on the decision of one company, since another participant in the project can take on the implementation.



Therefore, a clear answer to this question does not exist, but there are a number of recommendations that will keep your finger on the pulse of what is happening in an open source project. Using Istio as an example, the list of project working groups is known, their meetings and discussion protocols are open, the opportunity to ask questions and describe problems is available - representatives of corporations can participate in all these and many other things. At the current time, corporations are extremely cautious in participating in the life of the community, and I really want to hope that this trend will change in the near future.



Instead of a conclusion



What can be said in the end? Despite the fact that Istio is a fairly young technology, its current level of maturity and existing functionality fully meet the requirements of corporations. It can be seen that among large Russian companies, interest in Istio has grown significantly over the past year and more and more IT teams are starting their first projects, and a number of them are already using technology in an industrial environment.

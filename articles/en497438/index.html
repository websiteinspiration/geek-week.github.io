How the registrar checks the availability of a domain name on the example of REG.RU


This article is neither advertising nor anti-advertising of the REG.RU service , as well as of other companies mentioned below. I am not a REG.RU employee or an interested party; the essence of the writing is exclusively entertaining and educational.

So let's go!

Background


To begin with, I did not and do not do cybersquatting (and even if I did, it’s obviously not through the web interface), however, one of us doesn’t have a crazy idea when buying domains to check beautiful names for accessibility (and especially on such long self-isolated evenings).

The same thing happened to me, adding to the basket what I came for, I decided to see a couple of beautiful words and brands. What was my surprise when the first two verified domains were first declared free:



And after that they were automatically added to the basket:



At the same time, the WHOIS gave the expected result for both domains :

state: REGISTERED, DELEGATED, VERIFIED

Having reached the payment page, I wrote off what I saw to a one-time share (with whom it doesn’t happen) and interrupted the registration process. The attempt to repeat was successful, but I did not dare to pay and look at the result.

The next item was checking this "feature" through a direct URL, of the form:

https://b2b.reg.ru/buy/domains?dname=<domain>

Where in the place of “domain” should be the desired domain name, in my case it was Yandex :

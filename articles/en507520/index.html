The first attempt to assemble a laser projector from hard drives



Hello!



Today I am publishing a short article about how I assembled a laser projector from old hard drives. Which turned out to be not very interesting, but the sound visualizer, surprisingly came out amazing.



If you are interested in the details, then watch the video or read the article under the cut.



In my trash for a long time lying and taking up space, two faulty hard drives from the famous brand WD. And now it‚Äôs time to bring your old idea to life, try to assemble a laser projector from them. Since the read head of the HDD is nothing more than a galvanometer and it is just perfect for moving the mirror to a given position on one axis.



Armed with a tool, I proceed to fulfill the intended goal.







To do this, I first disassemble the hard drives.







Next, I remove the head movement mechanism from it and remove the parking, magnetic pin from it.







Using a tester or visually, we find the contacts to which the conductors from the electromagnetic coil come and solder the wires to them.







To reduce the size of the device, I saw the case of the hard disk into two parts.







In order to fix the mirrors, I assemble the holders for them from two brass posts and an aluminum petal.







I saw out a mirror from an aluminum disk shot from the same HDD. Its size is 35 x 10 mm. I attach these mirrors with cyanoacrylate glue to the galvanometer. When gluing, note that the glue sets immediately. It is better to first install the mirror in the groove, and then drop a drop of glue into the junction.







I fix a Chinese laser on a copper wire, it is very weak and can hardly be seen in daylight. But for a dark room is enough.







After 2 galvanometers are assembled, I connect them to the corners from the window frames, at an angle of 90 ¬∞. To do this, I had to drill 2 holes in an aluminum base and fix these corners to them



The mechanical part of the projector is assembled, now we connect its electromagnets to the PAM8610 two-channel power amplifier. I quickly wanted to check my device in practice and I decided to send music to the input of the amplifiers. And lo and behold! I saw a beautiful sight, as if in an instant I found myself in a nightclub or at a concert of my favorite rock band. The result was a cool laser color music. She draws incredible pictures to the beat with music, which you can watch without stopping. What is most surprising is that the resulting images are so diverse and do not repeat from track to track. The biggest difference can be seen in different musical genres. After watching the video, you can clearly see this.



Now I‚Äôm trying to turn it on with free software in projector mode and here I was disappointed. Without a constant component from the output of the sound card, it is impossible to display good images or animation. Since all angles are rounded through separation capacitors, it is not possible to keep the galvanometer in one position and it immediately tends to return to its middle position. Also, galvanometers require feedback to maintain a zero point. And still it is necessary to ensure the extinction of the beam, that is, turn off the laser at the time of return to the initial position. For alteration you will need a USB sound card with at least 4 channels. I do not have an external sound card yet and I decided to continue my experiments after it arrives at me.

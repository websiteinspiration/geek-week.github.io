Do not underestimate this stage - after all, the main thing is determined during the planning: type of work, model of the intruder, significant features of the project and the general scenario of Red Teaming.

Simulation or imitation



The offensive and defensive security community is arguing about what Red Teaming is - imitation or simulation.



Imitation is an assimilation, imitation of someone or something. When simulating the actions of an intruder, the Red Team team plays, repeats the attacks of an intruder, for example, an APT group, and does this without improvisation .



If the organization wants to simulate the attacks of any APT group, it reports this at the first meeting. Further, the Red Team team studies the TTP (tactics, techniques and procedures) of this group (unless, of course, there is such analytics) and step-by-step ‚Äúperforms‚Äù all the actions. The advantage of such work is the absence of the need for long preparation and planning: the Red Team team will not change anything in the script and uses the same tools and utilities as the APT group. And the big minus is that the IoCs (indicators of compromise) of this group are already known, which means that the work scenario needs to be adjusted (for example, attackers can use the same utilities, but change tactics, go in other ways). If in such a situation the Red Team goes unnoticed, the question arises,why the security team looked at known IoCs and didn‚Äôt configure the security systems to respond to such events.



A simulation is a reproduction of an attack so that everything looks real, but again without real damage to the organization. During the simulation, the attacking team acts at its discretion, based on their own experience with Red Teaming or on the performance indicators of the most popular techniques in the world. Own developments or TTP can be used to achieve goals, regardless of which APT group they use.



Thus, Red Teaming can be carried out both in the form of a simulation (and check the readiness of the defense team to detect new attacks and new TTP), and in the form of a simulation (here it checks the readiness to repel attacks of known APT groups). The choice depends on the goals and wishes of the customer. The progress of the work performed depends on the same.



Intruder Models



You might think that the intruder model is a term from the field of so-called paper security. However, this is really important information for the Red Team, as it affects script creation. There are three intruder models in Red Teaming:



  • Remote - this can be any person or group of people. The main feature is that the intruder does not have legal physical access and access to the organization‚Äôs internal systems and networks, but can act according to any scenario: penetration through the Internet, the use of social engineering methods, attempts to obtain physical access or gain access via wireless networks.
  • Approximate - this may be an employee of auxiliary services (cleaning, security, maintenance staff of the office building). The main feature is that the intruder has legal physical access, but does not have legal access to internal systems and networks.
  • Internal - an employee of the organization who has undergone social engineering or an insider who has decided to steal the information of the organization. As an internal violator, not only employees of the organization, but also contractors with certain powers can be considered. The main feature is that the intruder has legal physical access and legal technical access to internal systems and networks.


The selected intruder model will determine the starting point of the Red Teaming project and the entire further scenario.



Scenarios



Scenarios rule the world with Red Teaming. In a sense, the scenario is determined by the model of the intruder and the goal, which gives an initial impetus to the start of the project.

The following types of scenarios can be distinguished, which differ in starting points and vectors of ongoing attacks:



  • Scenarios using wireless technologies , in which the model of a remote or close intruder is taken as a starting point, and the wireless network is used as an attack vector.

In addition, the implementation of the already approved scenario can be adjusted during the project depending on the situation and the receipt of new data. The ability of the attacking team to be flexible is critical to Red Teaming.


In some cases, the starting points of the scripts may be implemented as an alleged violation . This means that the organization assumes that the attacker has already successfully completed some steps and is able to gain access to any segment of the internal network, DMZ, or segment of the wireless network.



For example, an attacking team may be provided with:



  • access to a remote machine (supposedly obtained by launching a "malicious" document containing a payload to connect to the C2 command center);
  • password for connecting to a wireless network;
  • workplace to simulate insider actions.


Alleged violation saves time and money. But with this approach, there is a certain mistrust in the attacking team, because the organization had provided initial access to the internal infrastructure and there is no evidence that in real conditions the Red Team could really get it on their own.



Therefore, it is especially important to discuss and agree on all these points in the initial meetings related to planning Red Teaming.



Sample scenarios



Scenario for a software company

The scenario assumes that the Red Team is a group of hackers from state X. The group is tasked with gaining access and making changes to the source code of the software developed by organization Z in order to gain unauthorized access to the computers of users of this software and to monitor it. The main developer of the company Z is Ivanov Ivan. During Red Teaming, there is a search for potential attack vectors on Ivan Ivanov‚Äôs computer, all information about him, data about the services that he uses are collected. Next, the "attackers" try to get into his computer in order to later compromise the source code of the software.



Script for the bank

The scenario is based on an alleged violation using social engineering methods. The Red Team mimics the actions of an unknown group of cybercriminals who send phishing emails to companies in the financial sector. The purpose of the attackers is to gain access to the financial information of the organization and the ability to transfer funds to other accounts, as well as access to information on customers' credit cards. During an alleged violation, an employee of the organization opens an attachment with an executable load that provides attackers with access to the internal network.



Instead of a conclusion



In general, Red Teaming is a very ‚Äúcreative‚Äù service in terms of not only scenarios, but also Blue Team training formats and assessments of the overall level of security. However, I repeat, all this requires some preparatory work on the part of the customer - because in order to draw up the correct attack scenario, Red Team must understand what risks the organization is considering and check how the defenders will respond to their implementation. For example, a threat from contractors may be specified in the risk model, and then it will be necessary not only to use this vector during Red Teaming, but also to coordinate such work in advance with the contractor. And such "routine details" that need to be thought out and taken into account "on the shore" are in virtually every scenario. Therefore, quality Red Teaming is always a joint merit of the customer and the performer.

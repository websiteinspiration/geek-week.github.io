Listen at the weekend: a Russian-language podcast about home cinema halls, the “audiolist wishlist” and the sound shop

We present to your attention only selected issues of one of the few programs on audio technology in Russian. We are discussing how to equip the cinema, what it can be like, what gifts the “audioman” will like, and what you need to know about the work of the sound engineer. Dolby Atmos and his friends [ Habr ] [ Web player ] [ Apple Podcasts ]. Together with an expert in home cinema, they discussed how things are going in the industry - why they called Dolby Atmos “a breath of fresh air” and how the principle of creating sound immersion now looks. We also talked about how the audience’s requests changed, gave a couple of tips regarding the arrangement of a home theater: who is suitable for the “out of the box” option, and who better to attend to a more expensive solution. Plus - talked about where you can evaluate the sound quality before buying.





How to make a sound for a movie [ Habr ] [ Web player ] [ Apple Podcasts ]. We interviewed Yevgeny Pereyaslavtsev, who studied film production in Los Angeles and worked on various projects as a sound engineer. To begin with, we found out who works in the "sound shop" and how to get into it.

More about home theaters [ Habr ] [ Web player ] [ Apple Podcasts ]. We decided to discuss what changes have occurred in the field of cinema halls. We touched on Dolby Atmos and Auro-3D, figured out which technologies went to the masses, and which ones did not achieve popularity. We also tried to suggest how sound immersion will develop and touched on 3D themes for the home.



They also talked about how to upgrade equipment to meet new technologies, which of the audio equipment should be updated, and what can be left for a long period. A large block was dedicated to the choice of a projector and its role in the home theater ecosystem.

Old new vinyl [ Habr ] [ Web player ] [ Apple Podcasts ]. We are talking about how plate production technologies have changed compared to the middle of the last century, whether vinyl has an “expiration date” and what affects the quality of media storage. We explain how to focus on quality if you decide to collect records, and what affects their collection value. We also did not forget to talk about players: how to set them up so as not to ruin anything, what accessories are there, what elements of equipment you need to monitor, and how to store all this correctly.

8 features of Google Translate that you are most likely not using

Google Translate is the largest system for working with languages. According to the status as of June 2020, it has fully realized 108 languages ‚Äã‚Äãof the world.

Every day, Google Translate translates over 100 billion words. But many people use only its main function and do not even know that in addition to the standard translation, it has a number of quite convenient tools.

In this article we will talk about them. And try to find out if Google Translate helps to learn English at all.




Translation of site pages


The function is well known and is used often. The point is to translate the page of the site, fully preserving the interface.

The Google Translate extension is already embedded in the Google Chrome browser. To translate a page, you just need to right-click and click ‚ÄúTranslate‚Äù.



If you are using a different browser, you will need to install the extension. Here are the versions for Firefox and Opera .

By the way, the extensions have some more interesting features. For example, the translation of words in a pop-up window. Just click - and immediately see the translation. You do not even need to switch to another tab or climb into the mobile dictionary.

There is another way to translate a page on a site. To do this, insert the site URL into the translation window, select the desired language and click on the result in another window. The page will open in the specified language.



Translation of documents


A handy feature if you have a bunch of text files that need to be translated. To do this, you do not need to manually open each of them and copy the text. Just click on the ‚ÄúDocuments‚Äù tab and upload the whole file to Google Translate.



The service understands almost all text file types and even presentations in Power Point. Here is the complete list: DOC, DOCX, ODF, PDF, PPT, PPTX, PS, RTF, TXT, XLS, XLSX.

The system can read and translate PDF files if the text is recognized in them. Even partially preserve the original layout (only without images). But the files of e-books MOBI, FB2, EPUB cannot be read.

Important! Legal documents and contracts cannot be translated in this way. The Google translation does not provide the necessary accuracy, which is why signing such documents can even be dangerous from a legal point of view.


Google search translation


You can even translate words and phrases using the regular Google search bar. If the search engine algorithm understands that you want to translate a word or phrase into another language, it will first display the Google Translate window with the translation.



Moreover, this works not only on the Google start page, but also in the address bar of the browser, if Google is set as the default search engine.

Own dictionary of words and phrases


If you often use Google Translate to translate individual words, phrases or phrases, you can create your own dictionary.

If you want to save a phrase or word, click the star in the upper right corner. And to display the entire dictionary, the "Saved" icon at the bottom.



The function is quite useful if, for example, you need to make a small pocket phrasebook when traveling to another country. The Google Translate application also works without the Internet, so if you need to speak with a native speaker or ask for directions. This will help even in areas where there is no Wi-Fi access.

Dialogue translation


Along with synchronous, the translation algorithm during the dialogue was updated. The function has become even more convenient.

To start the translation, you need to click the "Conversation" button in the application. A double window will open. When you press the microphone button in one of them, the program will perceive this language and translate into the second.

To start translating from a second language, you just need to press the microphone button there.



Now the function is available in 32 languages. There is also Russian, so the application will help you communicate with a foreigner, even if you know the language very poorly or do not know at all.

Translation using a camera


An interesting tool that will help you translate signs and signs on signs, shop windows or street advertising. You can read the inscriptions on the map or the menu in the restaurant - in general, just a godsend for the traveler.

To enable Google Word Lens, simply turn on the Google Translate application, click on the camera button and point it at the text. The screen will immediately translate the inscription.



The text recognition engine is not yet perfect. The developers say that the system may not understand too stylized fonts or design styles of letters.

Computer Science Club Lecture Video

Computer Science Club is an open lecture on computer science at the St. Petersburg branch of the Mathematical Institute of the Russian Academy of Sciences. CS Club branches operate in Novosibirsk and Kazan .

The main goal of the club is to talk about the current state of affairs and introduce open tasks in various areas of computer science. For example, here are spring semester courses in St. Petersburg in one picture.



All courses are open to visitors, admission is free, registration is not needed.

During the existence of the club, more than a hundred different courses were read. Thanks to lektorium.tv, most of the courses are recorded on video and available to everyone.

So, if you have not yet figured out what to do on the weekend, then look at the list of club courses - for sure, some of them will seem interesting to you. For example, you may be interested in a lecture on an encryption device in the Signal messenger , a mini-course on type inference from Hindley-Milner to GHC 8.8 , a course on computer graphics, or something else.

Well, if you live in St. Petersburg, come tomorrow to a course on coding theory .

PS If you want to follow the news of the CS club - subscribe to us on social networks. Links can be found on the club's website .

PPS If you are interested in other training formats in St. Petersburg, then pay attention to the Computer Science Center and the JetBrains corporate master's program at ITMO .

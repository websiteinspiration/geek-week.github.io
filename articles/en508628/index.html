Propellerolet: tiltrotor for monitoring extended objects





Once at the institute, the head of the department told us about our future profession:

“Our profession is very important and responsible. Now you are working on the appearance of the future flagship of Russian aviation. You pour ideas, trying to predict the tasks that he will solve, take responsibility and make decisions. And after 15 years, when the aircraft is finally built, honors will be distributed to everyone, but no one will remember about you. ”

I didn’t like this campaign, I’m not ready to wait 15 years for the result.



Aviation is a highly developed industry, but high reliability requirements and a planned future do not allow the introduction of new technologies quickly. But drones, on the contrary, have no special requirements yet, so they grow like mushrooms after rain, filling in niches for air monitoring, advertising, agricultural assistance, video recordings and the like.

So I decided, let them build their fighters there somehow without me, and while I am young and hot, I will go into drones. Fortunately, there is a model airplane past.



What are the ways to develop aviation? In my opinion, aviation can develop in two ways:



  • The use of innovative technologies for components (new efficient power plants with low weight, avionics, etc.).
  • Changing the aerodynamic design.


I am more impressed by the change in the aerodynamic design of the aircraft. In this way such aerodynamic schemes as “duck” and “flying wing” appeared. But this was not enough for the engineers, they wanted the aerodynamics to change right in flight. So there were aircraft with variable wing geometry (Su-24, Tu-22M, Tu-160, F-111 and the like), aircraft with a controlled thrust vector (Mig-29OVT, Mig-35, Su-30, 37, 57, F-22 and others) and, of course, convertiplanes (for example, the well-known Bell V-22 Osprey).



This is all, of course, good, but not quite to my liking. The idea with variable wing geometry is interesting, but it is necessary that if the wings are folded, then so that at all, and not a miserable few degrees. But the idea with rotary power plants impresses me. So the idea was born to make your own convertiplane. Schematically, our idea can be described as follows:





I assembled a team and soon we decided. The idea of ​​our tiltrotor was to combine a flying wing airplane scheme and a twin-screw longitudinal helicopter scheme. We decided that the wings would fold down, not half the wing, like ship planes, but as a whole, so as not to create additional resistance when climbing in helicopter mode.



The hovercraft will take off with its wings folded vertically down, then the motors will turn and the wings will unfold smoothly. The flight itself will take place in the flying wing mode, and if necessary it will always be possible to hover in place. When landing, it can sit vertically, on the belly or, in the case of an emergency landing, on a parachute.



What are the advantages of this aerodynamic design?



  • Due to the huge wing, we get high aerodynamic quality in airplane mode, which significantly increases the flight range compared to other convertiplanes.
  • Vertical take-off and landing (does not require a runway).
  • The ability to hang in the air.

After all participants in this project, studies began at different institutes, and we abandoned this project. Gradually, the team parted somewhere. When the studies at the institute came to an end, and the graduation project was approaching, we all came up with the idea of ​​returning to this aircraft. No one will doubt that such a unit is multifaceted, and everyone will always find work there. Therefore, everyone took a piece that came as close as possible to his specialty ...

Somehow met a beauty, a plumber, a tourist, an IT specialist and a schoolboy. And the plumber says, “The diploma is close, you have to build a plane.”



We got together again and started making a second prototype. Now we had at our disposal: a garage equipped with the latest technology, a lathe, CNC, a 3D printer, as well as experience and knowledge gained at the institute. Immediately determined the purpose of the drone, it will monitor the pipelines. For this task, the optimal LTH was selected (this is a separate story). We calculated and drew all units and components. We calculated the rescue system and stitched a parachute. Developed a system for opening and folding wings. With the prototype built, we took part in MAKS-2019, participated in the competition from “Russian Helicopters” and continue to work on its revisions in our free time.



conclusions



Going your own way is always incredibly difficult. We are constantly told that this, of course, is great, but it won’t work and will never fly, or we don’t have enough desire to bring it to a functional state. However, we are pleased to hear constructive criticism.



We are still “burning” with the desire to see our plans in the air, and we are making every possible effort to do so.



PS



In this project, when setting goals and objectives, I am guided by the requirements and conditions for UAVs in monitoring tasks, the application of all ideas of approaches and prototypes created in other industries can be considered only after reaching the goals that are set now. I consider this approach rational in the circumstances with limited material, labor and time resources. I used these opinions quite often in the process of working on this project, because I look forward to seeing what I am doing in action.

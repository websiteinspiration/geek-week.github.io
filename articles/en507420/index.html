Comparison of the brain with the neural network





One can come across many criticisms that the biological brain or biological neural networks work completely different from the current popular computer neural networks. Various experts resort to such remarks, both from biologists, neurophysiologists, and from specialists in computer science and machine learning, but there are very few concrete comments and suggestions. In this article we will try to analyze this problem and identify particular differences between the operation of a biological and computer neural network, and propose ways to improve computer neural networks that will bring their work closer to their biological counterpart.





Frontier of knowledge



First, I want to explain why, in my opinion, the issue of creating strong artificial intelligence is still so sad, despite the tremendous successes in computer science and knowledge of the biological brain. First of all, this is connected with the great ideological gap between the two pillars of science. Computer science requires a certain schematic simplicity, rigor and conciseness in the description of systems, a kind of systematic approach, in discarding unnecessary and clear structuring sufficient for registration in program code. In biology, however, the detail of the description of the observed systems dominates; nothing can be discarded or ignored from the observations. All observable facts should be included in the described systems. Therefore, it is difficult for biologists to apply a systematic approach to their extensive knowledge to create brain algorithms.Indeed, in order to create the constructive structure of the aircraft, a lot of things had to be reviewed and discarded from the image of the bird.



On the other hand, it is easy to understand scientists and engineers who, when immersed in the study of computer neural networks from a description of the principles of the brain, are content with a short paragraph of text about a neuron that ‚Äúlistens‚Äù to other neurons with the help of synapses on dendrites and transmits the result of layer summation calculations using a single axon further, without applying any critical assessment to this knowledge. Even neurobiologists use the McCullock-Pitts formal neuron to describe the principles of the biological neuron, but they do it for another reason, because there are no worthy alternatives, there is no clear description in the biology of what the neuron does, what logic it performs, despite extensive knowledge about him.



If someone tries to reengineer the work of the brain, then he will meet a whole layer of accumulated conflicting knowledge, which even a biologist will not have enough life to understand, not to mention a system engineer who is accustomed to a more different form of knowledge. It is possible to work with such a volume of information only through the prism of a certain general theory of brain function, which is not yet available.



Humanity has the technology of colossal computing power and a huge amount of knowledge about the brain, but can not get a synthesis of these things. Let‚Äôs try to solve this problem and erase this line of knowledge.



Brain it should just be



The first very important principle that should be followed is the idea that the brain should work according to some very simple rules, i.e. all cognitive processes, no matter how complex they may seem, are based on simple basic principles. This is different from what we are used to hearing about the brain. For a long time, the lack of a general theory of the work of the brain has generated a lot of speculation about the fact that the brain is some incomprehensibly complex object, or the nature of its work goes far beyond the scope of scientific methods of study that apply to it. For example, they compare the brain with a quantum computer, or undeservedly attribute to individual neurons the properties of complex calculators, which, together with their number in the nervous system, make the requirements for computing power for modeling the brain unattainable.



In my opinion, scientists who express the idea that Humanity should never comprehend the complexity of the human brain need to be deprived of scientific degrees, such statements can only undermine the fighting spirit of people who want to devote themselves to solving this problem.



So what is the evidence for the simplicity of the brain? Here I give a completely paradoxical example. If we take a grape snail and bring the electrodes to one neuron of its large ganglion, according to all the requirements that apply to such experiments, then we can get a graph of the activity of an individual neuron, and try to analyze it, we will get a very complex character of its activity. Even if we take into account the nature of the invasiveness of our experiment, the fact that our electrodes cause serious damage to the cochlea and the limitation of its vital activity, the nature of the activity of the neuron still looks very complicated. We will see both spontaneous activity and a change in the number and frequency of spikes over time. Many scientists have been struggling to explain this complex neuron behavior for a long time,looking for any pattern in this.



These facts make the neuron a kind of sophisticated computer that works according to a complex algorithm. Considering that there are about 20 thousand such neurons in the cochlear nervous system, it can be said that the computational power of the nervous system of the common cochlea is comparable to the mainframe. I think this should inspire you with awe of these animals. But let's see how complicated the behavior of snails is. A snail is a kind of biological automaton, and it does have a certain degree of variation in behavior, but it is very small. This is a set of unconditioned reflexes, often very simple, that can be explained by already existing knowledge about neurons, synapses and reflex acts and there will be no place for complicated calculations.



In confirmation of the above, I want to make a reference to my last article, which describes a model of a frog‚Äôs tadpole, in which, thanks to the nervous system, from several tens of neurons you can get quite complex behavior of a waterfowl. Moreover, from very simple neurons, the model of which is based on facts known in science.

So where does this complex neuron behavior come from, and why are there so many of them? Here, in fact, one follows from the other. In nature, there is a paradoxical phenomenon that can be called a paradox of neuron efficiency. It lies in the fact that with an increase and complication of the nervous system, the effectiveness or role of an individual neuron in this system decreases.







If we analyze the nervous system of the ringworm c.elegans, an animal whose connection of 301 neurons is completely composed, we will see that not only individual neurons are important in the proper functioning of its nervous system, but even individual synapses also matter. That is, we can assign 100% efficiency to a single ringworm neuron. If we look at the human nervous system from this point of view, it‚Äôs difficult to assign neurons a significant value of effectiveness that can be taken out with a crowbar from the skull, while preserving the life of a person and even his social integration, well, almost preserving. *

You can regularly see articles that describe cases where people who live full lives and are socially adaptive suddenly find that their brain is devoid of any areas or lobes. It is not surprising that such facts give rise to the idea that it is not neurons at all, and indeed not the brain.



If we observe the activity of a healthy brain, then we will not see any extra neurons, each neuron will be involved, to a different extent, of course, each will have its own role. How the brain does this, what the neuron algorithm must be for this to happen, with the low efficiency of the neuron, I will explain below.



The paradox of neuron efficiency can be explained by the fact that with an increase in the number of neurons in the nervous system, the ‚Äúattention‚Äù of evolutionary processes to individual neurons decreases. Therefore, ringworm neurons, we can say, work like clockwork, very accurately, the neurons of the grape snail and the Human cannot boast such accuracy, they can see both spontaneous activity and the lack of an answer where it should be, as well as its instability .



So, two theories can be presented on the complex activity of a neuron: a neuron is a complex calculator, the operation algorithm of which is difficult to understand and justify, or the neuron just works very unstable, which is compensated by its excessive amount, which is the simplest solution from the point of evolution. Apply the Occam Razor rule to these theories, according to which you need to leave ideas that have the simplest explanation and most likely these ideas will be true.



On the one hand, the paradox of neuron efficiency gives us positive hope that the required computing power for modeling the brain will be much less than with a direct estimate of the number of neurons and synapses in the human brain. On the other hand, this greatly complicates the study of the biological brain. We can create a sufficiently detailed model of a small fragment of the cerebral cortex, having spent large computational power and in this model we can‚Äôt see any significant processes that would indicate how cognitive mechanisms flow in the nervous system. Such attempts have already been made.



At first glance, the simplest and most straightforward approach to creating a general theory of brain function is to create a detailed model of the brain, in accordance with many scientific facts known about the neuron and synapses. Modeling is the most practical scientific tool in the study of any complex systems. The model literally reveals the essence of the object being studied, allows you to immerse yourself and influence the internal processes in the simulated system, making it possible to better understand them.



The neuron does not have any exceptional organelles that would calculate, but its membrane has a number of features, and allow the neuron to perform certain work. This work can be determined using a system of equations called the Hodgkin-Huxley model, which was developed in 1952, for which its authors received the Nobel Prize.







These formulas contain several coefficients that determine some parameters of the neuron membrane, such as the reaction rate of ion channels, their conductivity, etc. This magic model describes several phenomena at once, in addition to changes in the charge on the surface of the membrane of a neuron. Firstly, it describes the activation function of a neuron, or the mechanism of summation, it is quite simple. If the initial charge is insufficient, then the model remains in equilibrium. If the charge goes over a certain threshold, then the model responds with one spike. If the charge significantly exceeds this threshold, then the model responds with a series of spikes. Computer neural networks use a wide variety of options for the activation function, the closest to biology can be the Heaviside function (single step) and a linear rectifier (Rectifier). But you need to understandwhat we are describing is a fairly simple aspect of the neuron's work - summation. In my work on the tadpole mentioned above, I applied a very simple version of the summation model, which can be figuratively represented as a vessel accumulating a stimulus factor, if this factor exceeded a certain threshold, then the neuron activated. In order for this adder to work in real time, the influence factor slowly expired from the figurative vessel.from the figurative vessel, the exposure factor slowly expired.from the figurative vessel, the exposure factor slowly expired.







This model of summation made it possible to sum the signals that arrived at the neuron asynchronously, and it works quite realistically. In my opinion, the easier it is to describe this process, the better, and this is an unprincipled difference between biological and computer networks.

Secondly, the Hodgkin-Huxley model describes a change in charge at one point in the membrane, but if, for example, we create a topologically accurate 3D model of a neuron and divide this model into a uniform grid, we can apply the Hodgkin-Huxley model at each vertex (node) of this grid, with the condition that the charge affects the value at neighboring vertices along the grid. Thus, we get a model of the propagation of excitation along a neuron close to how this happens in a living neuron.



The main conclusions that can be drawn from this model are that the excitation arising on any part of the membrane extends to the entire membrane, including propagation along the long axon to the most distant synapses. The Hodgkin-Huxley model is very resource-intensive, therefore, for the purpose of modeling, less expensive models with very similar schedules are used, several models have been invented.



Within the framework of the Human Brain Project (HBP), a model of a small fragment of the mouse cortex was created; its creators took into account a lot. 3D models of neurons were recreated using real neurons, one of the versions of the Hodgkin-Huxley model was used, various types of neurons and neurotransmitters were taken into account, and there is no doubt that the model really corresponds to the biological counterpart. A lot of resources and time were spent on this, but it did not give significant results due to the fact that in such a small size, due to the paradox of neuron efficiency, it was impossible to see significant processes. Therefore, the path of a detailed repetition of biology is very, very time-consuming. The key to success is the ability to understand how the nervous tissue and neurons work on a wider scale.



Let's look at how the brain processes information using a particular example, processing visual information. We will draw up a diagram of a neural network performing this task.







Information from the retina of the eye through the optic nerve is transmitted to the thalamus, where information is practically not subjected to significant transformations. Then it is transmitted to the primary visual zone of the cerebral cortex (V1). Six layers are distinguished in the cerebral cortex, but these layers are histologically or morphologically. Probably here we are dealing with two layers, as some structures are repeated twice. But even so, we are not dealing with two separate independent layers, layers of nerve cells working in tandem.



We characterize the zone of the visual cortex V1 as the first layer in which information is processed. Zone V1 also has feedbacks with the thalamus. Similar feedbacks exist between all subsequent layers. These bonds form cyclic excitation transfers between layers called reverbs.



After zone V1, information is transmitted to the next zone V2, all subsequent zones will have smaller areas. Depending on what the brain is observing, it was an object, a symbol, a person‚Äôs face, place or something else information from V2 can be transmitted to various areas of V3, V4, V5. That is, already in this visual area V2 there is a serious categorization of visual images. And approximately already on the third or fourth layer it will be possible to isolate neurons-detectors of certain images. For example, we can select a neuron detector of the letter ‚ÄúA‚Äù, the numbers 3, or the faces of Jennifer Aniston. By activating these neuron detectors, we can judge what the brain is currently observing. A fairly simple architecture of a neural network, if we compare it with the architecture of computer neural networks specialized in the recognition of visual images,convolutional neural networks.

Formal neuron



Ever since school years, I was worried about the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãcreating artificial intelligence, I was interested in studying the literature on neurophysiology, and I did not know anything about artificial neural networks. I met neural networks later, as a student. Acquaintance with the formal McCulloch-Pits neuron, which is the basis for all modern neural networks, puzzled and disappointed me, due to the great emphasis on dendritic synapses.







The McCulloch-Pits formal neuron can be represented as a certain function with many arguments and one answer. The input arguments are multiplied with the corresponding coefficients called weights (W1, W2, ... Wn), then these values ‚Äã‚Äãare added up and the resulting amount passes through the activation function, the result of which is the result of neuron calculations. The main thing is to choose the right weight, that is, to train the neural network. This neuron model may seem simple and obvious, but it has a strong emphasis on dendritic synapses.



In the chemical synapse, two important parts can be distinguished: these are presynaps and postsynaps. Presynapses are located at the ends of a long single process of the axon, which can branch out many times. Presynapse is presented in the form of a small seal at the tips, it refers to a neuron that transmits excitation. Postsynapses are located on short branching processes of dendrites, they belong to the neuron to which excitation is transmitted.







The presynapse contains vesicles, vesicles with portions of the neurotransmitter substance. It was in presynapses that synapse ambiguity was previously revealed, presynapses differ in the number of portions of the neurotransmitter stored in it, as well as in the number of secreted neurotransmitter when it is activated. The weight or strength of the presynapse is denoted by the letter S.



On the surface of the postsynapse membrane are receptors that respond to a neurotransmitter. The number of these receptors determines how sensitive the synapse will be. That is, postsynaps can also be characterized by some characteristic, weight. We denote this weight by the letter A. Of course, you can represent these two parameters as one W, which determines the strength of the entire synapse, but these parameters must be configured differently during training and they relate to different neurons.







Such a representation of a neuron is more realistic, but at the same time it is very complicated, since now it remains to be understood how to configure all these parameters during training.



I want to introduce my version of the algorithm by which changes occur in postsynapses, i.e. dendritic synapses. It is based on the fact that a biological neuron needs to maintain a certain level of activity. The fact is that a neuron as a cell is very resource-intensive for the body, it cannot feed on its own, for it satellite cells, glia do it. Therefore, if a neuron for some reason does not perform its functions, then the best option is to get rid of it in order to ensure the effectiveness of the whole organism. With a prolonged absence of activations in the neuron, the apoptosis process can start, satellite cells actively support this process, literally breaking and pulling the neuron apart. Therefore, in order to survive a neuron in conditions of an insufficient source of activation, it is necessary to develop branches of dendrites,increase the sensitivity of synapses on dendrites and sometimes even migrate to other areas (this happens extremely rarely and under certain conditions), well, or produce spontaneous activity. This is evidenced, for example, by visual or auditory hallucinations in people whose organs of vision or hearing are subjected to deprivation, or degradation due to aging. This is described in more detail by Oliver Sachs in his book ‚ÄúThe Man Who Took His Wife for a Hat‚Äù.who mistook his wife for a hat. "who mistook his wife for a hat. "











On the other hand, excessive activity of a neuron can also lead to its death. Neuron activity is a very complex process that requires the precise execution of many mechanisms, and any failure of their implementation will lead to fatal consequences for the entire cell. If the sources of activity are redundant, then neurons begin the process of degradation of some branches of dendrites and decrease the sensitivity of their postsynapses. Thus, the neuron is trying to find some balance in the level of its activity by regulating dendritic synapses. A neuron acting as an independent agent acting in its own interests provides amazing adaptability and plasticity of the whole brain. Despite the paradox of neuron efficiency, a healthy brain works very harmoniously, and each neuron plays a role.Due to this mechanism, neurons of the visual zones of the cerebral cortex of blind people will be involved in other nervous processes that are not associated with the processing of visual images. And the redundancy in the number of nerve cells makes the nervous system very reliable, and if some parts of the nervous tissue are damaged, neurons can take on the functions and roles of lost cells.



Based on this version, dendritic synapses play a role that affects the adaptive qualities of the entire nervous system, and not some logical functions that determine cognitive processes.



An algorithm, the so-called Hebb rule, already exists for changes in the presynaps of the axon synapses.

If the axon of cell A is close enough to excite cell B, and repeatedly or constantly takes part in its excitation, then there is a certain process of growth or metabolic changes in one or both cells, leading to an increase in the efficiency of A, as one of the cells of excitatory B.

Presynaps and postsynaps certainly affect each other. For example, with a deficit of activation, a neuron will first increase the sensitivity of the postsynapse that is used more often. And if it is necessary to reduce the level of activation, those postsynapses that were used less often will first degrade. This is due to the importance of preserving learning logic in adaptive processes.



If we want to create an artificial neural network, then we can neglect adaptive mechanisms, after all, biological systems are more demanding to save resources by each element than artificial models.



It turns out that the basis of computer neural networks is a neuron model, in which the emphasis is on the contrary, than that of a biological neuron. Analysis and Synthesis



Neurophysiology is a young, not yet mature science, it does not yet have strict fundamental laws like the laws in physics, although it contains a large number of theories and facts. It seems to me that such laws can be the postulates and principles of the reflex theory of Ivan Petrovich Pavlov. They can be compared with Newton's laws in physics. When creating new theories in neurophysiology, we must ask questions: how reflexes occur and form in the framework of our theory, and how the processes of synthesis and analysis manifest themselves.



Analysis and synthesis require special attention. These concepts seem very abstract, but these are specific processes that occur in the nervous system. I.P. Pavlov believed that analysis and synthesis continuously flow in the cerebral cortex. These processes are the basis for cognitive activity. I will try to convey clearly what these processes are, it is very important in order to recreate cognitive processes in neural networks.



Synthesis is a mechanism for combining, generalizing various signs into one image or action.







An example from the experiments of I.P. Pavlova:

A specially trained model animal - a dog isolated from other external stimuli and immobilized (restrained) when feeding includes the sound of a metronome, which was previously indifferent to it, indifferent. After several such combinations, the dog will develop a conditioned reflex, that is, gastric juice can be produced on the sound of only a metronome in a model animal, as when feeding.



Analysis is a mechanism for highlighting, ranking (ranking the ranks, significance) of each attribute from a limited set of attributes.







An example from the works of I.P. Pavlova:

The previously trained model animal, which has a conditioned reflex to the sound of the metronome producing gastric juice, changes the experimental conditions, now the animals receive food with the sound of the metronome 120 beats per minute, and with the sound of 160 beats per minute they will not be supported by anything. At first, the learned food conditioned reflex worked on both sounds of the metronome, but by the coincidence of many repetitions, moreover, a much larger number of times than during the experiment with synthesis. The dog begins to distinguish between these two very similar stimuli and ceases to respond to the sound of the metronome with a frequency that is not reinforced.



Let's qualitatively compare these two cognitive processes.



Synthesis is a relatively fast mechanism because it requires a small number of examples; in turn, the analysis requires significantly larger repetitions. Synthesis can occur in some passive form, that is, the main thing here is the simultaneous combination of stimuli or signs so that they can be combined. An Analysis always requires emotional reinforcement or some kind of feedback, which will determine what attributes increase or decrease the importance, rank. The synthesis always precedes the Analysis, that is, the attributes must first be combined into a group within which ranking can already be performed (analysis process).



Analysis always leads to a reduction in the number of errors, as it gives the data additional informational content: ranks or significance of individual attributes. Pure Synthesis creates a lot of errors, as it leads to a decrease in the information content of the source data, combining it, summarizing them into single groups.



Now armed with an understanding of these processes, we analyze computer neural networks for their presence.



The back propagation of error is a pure Analysis, it is the process of ranking the inputs of neurons according to the results of the entire neural network. There is no synthesis as a mechanism in neural networks. Each neuron has already formed a group of inputs, this group does not change in the process of learning according to the Synthesis principle. A false representation of the presence of Synthesis in neural networks may arise due to their ability to classify data, but this is the result of the work of the Data Analysis mechanism. Synthesis is the ability to generalize, merge data, rather than grouping by common features.



As a result, the high generalization ability that is inherent in human intelligence is so much lacking in computer neural networks, and this is offset by the need to use a large number of examples in training.



It must be understood that for algorithms in which there is an emphasis on Analysis, there will still be an advantage in certain tasks. For example, in the task of searching for patterns in a large amount of data, or recognizing faces from a millionth database, no algorithm can be compared with modern neural networks. But in a task where it is required to apply the experience gained on a small number of examples in various and varied situations, for example, the problem of autopilot, other new algorithms based on Synthesis and Analysis are required here, similar to how this happens in the brain.

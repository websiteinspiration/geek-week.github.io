Deadly sins of site security: what we learned from the vulnerability scanner statistics for the year

About a year ago, we at DataLine launched a service for searching and analyzing vulnerabilities in IT applications. The service is based on Qualys cloud solution, which we already talked about . For the year of working with the solution, we conducted 291 scans for different sites and accumulated statistics on common vulnerabilities in web applications. 

In the article below I will show which specific security holes are hidden behind different levels of criticality. Let's see what vulnerabilities the scanner found most often, why they can occur and how to protect themselves. 



Qualys divides all vulnerabilities of web applications into three levels of severity: low, medium and high. If you look at the distribution of "severity", it seems that everything is not so bad. Vulnerabilities with a high level of criticality are few, mostly all non-critical:

Do not reinvent the wheel - use proven frameworks . Popular frameworks are generally more secure. For .NET, these are ASP.NET MVC and ASP.NET Core, for Python - Django or Flask, for Ruby - Ruby on Rails, for PHP - Symfony, Laravel, Yii, for JavaScript - Node.JS- Express.js, for Java - Spring MVC.

Stay tuned for vendor updates and update regularly . They will find the vulnerability, then write an exploit, put it in public access, and everything will be repeated in a new way. Sign up for updates to stable versions from the software vendor.

Check permissions. On the server side, always treat your code as if it were all, from the first to the last letter, written by your most hated enemy, who wants to break your site and violate the integrity of your data. Moreover, sometimes this is really so.

Use clones, test sites, and then pour on the prod . This will help, firstly, to avoid mistakes and mistakes in a productive environment: a productive environment brings money, a simple productive environment is critical. When adding, fixing or closing a problem, it is worthwhile to work in a test environment, then check the functionality and vulnerabilities found, and then plan to work with a productive environment. 

Protect your web application with Web Application Firewalland integrate reports from the vulnerability scanner with it . For example, in DataLine, Qualys and FortiWeb are used as a bundle of services.

Developer Framing



In sociology there is the concept of ‚Äú frame ‚Äù - this is a cognitive structure (frame), the social context that defines our behavior. Once in a place or situation, we find ourselves in a frame - a system of permitted and inappropriate actions, certain settings. For example, when we come to the office, we understand where we are. We know what can be done and what cannot be done here. What actions are welcomed or expected, and which are not allowed. That is, an ‚Äúoffice‚Äù is a frame (frame, frame), consisting of a set of social attitudes, rules and expectations.

There are also frames in software development. And the main one is backlog. This frame implies that the more backlog items we implement, the more success we will achieve. However, due to the fact that developers are forced to create features as quickly as possible, they begin to "cut corners", to hack, and this depresses them. 

You can fix this if you go from backlog to the frame of the impact of your product on the market and customers . The company will become more successful and the developers happier.

How did the backlog frame appear


The Internet has greatly changed the scope of software development. Clear business! Due to the heyday of Internet companies, we have to constantly monitor the market and listen to customers. It is no longer possible to develop software for a long time and apart. The market is changing, competitors appear not once every ten years, but every month. Moreover, many of them are quite large and with good funding. In general, everyone wants to focus on the developers themselves and quick changes, with frequent interim adjustments instead of long planned development cycles.

In the late 1990s, a methodology called Extreme Programming (XP)) She introduced many developers to Agile principles and iterative development. This methodology had the concept of an ‚Äúon-site customer‚Äù: developers should always have access to the customer, ideally so that he was nearby and could communicate face-to-face with him. In this case, the developers had to find out from the customer what and how to develop. That is, the developers were at the helm, and customers could be sure that everything was done as it should. However, for various reasons, the methodology did not take root. Mainly because of the Internet, companies have millions of customers, and extreme programming was created in the context of internal software development. It has become too difficult to adhere to the concept of "the customer is always there." Perhaps another reason wasthat developers couldn‚Äôt ‚Äújust develop‚Äù the code, they were blamed for the success of the product. It was expected that they will manage the development and communicate with customers.

Then came Scrum. It was very different from extreme programming. Scrum did not dictate to developers what to do. He did not require pair programming or unit testing. In extreme programming, developers were responsible for what they were creating, and in Scrum, they were responsible for the development intensity and deadlines. Scrum helped to choose what to create and build interaction. The methodology introduced strict rules for holding meetings. However, Scrum did not touch on the technical side of the issue.

The most important change due to Scrum was the role of the product owner. The team no longer owned the product. Now the owner decided what the developers should work on. They no longer stood at the helm. They did not ask the customer what the product should be, but the owner of the product dictated to the developers what features the customer needed. Most likely, many were happy with this change, including because salaries began to grow by leaps and bounds.

Also, new protagonists appeared on the scene - product experts (product managers, product manager). Successful companies began to use them, which the others followed. The emergence of Agile and product experts took place simultaneously. Companies began to move from hierarchical management to self-organizing teams. It was necessary to interact with them and transfer tasks to them. Previously, products were discussed in meetings with CTOs. Then the results of the discussion were released to lower managers, turned into projects and passed on to developers. However, with the advent of Agile and self-organizing teams, this scheme stopped working.

In addition, when analysts began to participate in product development, it was necessary to optimize KPI. But the developers did not want to do this, they were not interested in the business sphere. With whom could business representatives discuss the development of features? It was difficult to communicate with developers directly, and the role of intermediaries was taken by product experts. Fortunately, thanks to Scrum, we also got an organizational structure into which product experts easily integrated. More precisely, product owners! Since then, the role of product owner in Scrum has almost always been described as the work of a product scientist. Today, both roles are often confused and used as synonyms. Many are no longer aware that the owner of the product and the product specialist are different roles.

The developers were glad that now someone else is responsible for communicating with customers and choosing features for implementation. From now on, the owner of the product was engaged in such unpleasant things as managing product requirements and communicating with marketers and sellers. However, the developers no longer controlled the situation. A sense of control is one of the main factors of happiness. Yes, everyone loves developers, they are like hot cakes, salaries are constantly growing. But developers are less and less happy to create something incomprehensible, meaningless or beyond their control.

Backlog frame


Backlog is one of the dominant frames in software development. The product technologist (product owner) and employees of different departments contribute to the backlog the functionality that needs to be implemented. The Cambridge Dictionary tells us that backlog is ‚Äúa large number of things that you had to do before and which you need to do now .‚Äù That is, the term itself makes you think that you have not done something all the time. In this context, backlog completion is tantamount to success. If you create software, trying to quickly implement all the features from the list, then we will succeed. If we do everything that I, as the owner of the product, consider it necessary, then we will succeed. 

This frame has always existed implicitly in development. And when product owners and a backlog came along with Scrum, the context associated with it became apparent and strengthened. Product engineers have filled the void between developers and business, between technology and CEOs. A backlog helped manage these relationships. A product technologist uses this frame in conversations with developers, business representatives, company founders, and directors.

If we proceed from the backlog, then to achieve success you need to implement all the items on the list. And if we did not succeed, then we realized too little. ‚Äú Therefore, we need to work as quickly and efficiently as possible, because our success depends on it". CEOs often wonder why their developers do not work as long as marketers do. Executives believe startup success depends on whether developers work at night. After all, ‚Äú the more features we implement from backlog, the more successful we will be .‚Äù This coincides with the interests of the technical director: the more developers we have, the more features we will implement and the more success we will have. With this perception of the world, developers turn into a resource. The more of them, the better. The more efficiently the resource is used, the better.

This frame reduces the task of techies to the execution and delivery of features. CTOs often take pride in how delivery is organized. And when problems arise, CEOs think that ‚Äú techies haven‚Äôt delivered the right features.‚Äù". Allegedly, the cause of the problems is that " IT is slowly implementing backlog ." At the same time, developers do not feel responsible for success and believe that product experts should tell them what to work on. And if the product failed, then the product specialist ordered me to work not on what I needed. It's funny that only developers think so, and everyone else believes that " techies did not deliver features ."



Thinking in the context of backlog.

If the product is successful, then this is due to the vision of the product specialist. And if the product failed, then this is because we have not fully realized the vision of the product specialist. ‚Äú We would succeed if the developers worked faster or more. Or if there were more developers". In this system, success and failure are clearly separated. Not surprisingly, the backlog frame is so popular.

Due to the fact that developers are forced to work as quickly as possible, they begin to ‚Äúcut corners‚Äù. That is, features are simplified or not fully implemented. Save on information protection mechanisms. Conduct less tests, or do not conduct them at all (" only for this release "). All in order to have time to write more features and increase the likelihood of success. ‚Äú Do you want a startup to survive?‚Äù Or do you want to succeed? ¬ªDevelopment is reduced to the delivery of functionality, and the best practices of development are neglected. 

The developers have adapted to this order of things. We coined the term ‚Äútechnical debt‚Äù because it sounds better than ‚Äúcutting corners‚Äù and because we hope to close this debt. To deliver more features, we forgot about best practices. It‚Äôs funny that for developers, ‚Äútechnical duty‚Äù is something that a product technologist must pay over time. And business representatives think this way: the developers ‚Äúcut corners‚Äù and must then compensate for this. It is strange why this is not called ‚Äúfood debt‚Äù. Indeed, when a product specialist gets his feature faster, he thus takes a loan from developers. And in order to return it, the product specialist must spend time cleaning up his product. 

Influence frame


What is the disadvantage of a backlog frame? 

If the iPhone entered the market two months later, nothing would have changed. An iPhone would have turned the world upside down anyway. Apple would still be one of the most important companies. But if she had not released an iPhone, then the world would have been different. Steve Jobs would not affect the market the way it happened in reality. The key to Apple's success was not the effectiveness of the developers or delivery of all the features. After all, the first iPhone did not have many features that today we consider commonplace, for example, the App Store. The company was so successful because it focused not on efficiency, but on making an impact. Steve Jobs wanted to change the phone market. iPhone was created based on this task, and not in accordance with a detailed list of features from different departments of Apple. 

Today you need to determine your success by whether your product has influenced : significantly changed the market or customer behavior. For example, Pipedrive offers SaaS-CRM, which helps other companies sell their products and products. Thanks to Pipedrive, sellers began to act in accordance with the concept of a sales funnel. That is, Pipedrive affects their daily work.

Based on the influence, product experts no longer need to discuss the effectiveness and number of developers, deadlines and backlogs with anyone. We need to create something that will influence, and do not waste energy on the fact that it will not have an effect. But instead, many companies embody what product experts like, or what‚Äôs interesting to specific departments. When you ask CEOs how this feature affects their company or customers, they respond that they don‚Äôt know, but "you need to do this." Probably to satisfy someone in the company. And someone generally develops products not for the market, but for investors. Ask them what will happen if they don‚Äôt create it. You will not be able to answer. 

When our task is to influence the market, we focus on the important. And if you think that the more you do, the more successful you become, then many of the created features will not be needed by customers. Success depending on backlog execution is development focused on input data. And the desire to influence the market is a development focused on the result. 

Failure is when your product hasn‚Äôt affected anything. Do not waste time and energy on such development. When evaluating an idea, try to understand whether it will change your customers or the market? By this criterion, filter out most ideas. To do this, you need to decide: 

  • How to filter out ideas at the stage of their generation? 
  • Who is doing this? 
  • Should 90% of ideas be screened out or 90% implemented? 

Also discard bad ideas that are already in development. Have you spent time, energy and money on something, and realized that this will not affect the market or customers? Stop! Throw it out. Apple developed a lot of things and did not bring it to the market. It‚Äôs normal to drop something that has no effect. Although I have not met companies that stopped in the middle of product development or features - ‚Äú oh, we spent resources! ". To do this, the efficiency frame interferes.

You can say that we are already filtering out ideas with OKR (goals and key results), custom scripts and KPI:

  • OKR should help us focus on key results;
  • User scenarios should focus on the customer‚Äôs point of view;
  • KPIs should reflect our influence. 

But in reality, we still focus on backlog. We learned how to combine this frame with OKR, user scripts and KPI.

Remember the unknown developer who prayed for help? Working in the context of influence, developers feel happier. They create something that changes the market or customers, and gain more control by helping to screen out ideas. This frame is not sharpened by speed. There is no cutting corners or abandoning good development practices. There is no division of responsibility for success and failure. This frame helps companies be more successful. It is time for a change .

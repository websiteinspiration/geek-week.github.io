The importance of research at the start of a project

Hello! My name is Stasya, I am the UX-lead of the Center for Development of Financial Technologies at the Russian Agricultural Bank.

Our team is developing an ecosystem for micro, small and medium-sized farms, the purpose of which is to combine in one place all the services and goods that farmers need.



In the spring of 2019, we began to study the concept of the system. We had a small team of researchers and a lot of enthusiasm, but what was missing:



  • Understanding who our target audience is and how it lives
  • What can we offer our future customers to solve problems


To find answers to all the questions, we literally went deep into field research - we went to the fields, to farmers to observe, listen, study.



We decided to share some results that turned out to be interesting, unexpected and clearly demonstrating how important it is to reach our users as early as possible.



Case 1. Package offers



One of our first tasks was the development of a top-level navigation system.

Because the main goal of the system is to combine all the goods and services needed by farmers - we had to understand in what form we can offer them so that the farmers do not have their eyes wide.



One of the first versions of the concept was to offer farmers ready-made packages of services and services. We divided the farmers into several categories and compiled service packages for each group. Conventionally, we divided the farmers into livestock breeders, plant breeders, beginners and experienced. After arranging the services, we got 6 package offers and we went to offer them to farmers.



The first option of package offers:





Result



The farmers did not manage to pick up a package that completely suits them, because they could not unambiguously classify themselves in any group. It turned out that most farmers are doing a little bit of everything. For example, farmers containing cattle (cattle) often grow vegetables, fruits or greens in addition to dairy or meat products. It turns out a virtually waste-free production cycle when animal manure can be used as organic fertilizer for planting - this allows you to save on fertilizers and significantly expand the range of products.



As a result, we refused package offers and came to the concept of an ecosystem where the farmer is not obliged to become attached to any particular role, but immediately has access to absolutely all services and can choose the services that are needed more precisely.



Case 2. Formation of a pool of services for MVP1



We needed to understand what services will be included in our first product - to find a middle ground between ‚Äúwe can quickly develop and implement it‚Äù and ‚Äúwe can help farmers to start solving urgent problems effectively‚Äù.



After studying open sources on the Internet and several series of interviews, we got an impressive list of problems and needs of farmers. We divided the list into several semantic groups and assigned a priority service for each group.



Based on the list we compiled the first map of services and again went to the fields.



Now we built interviews around our map: we gave it to farmers and asked them to mark the most interesting directions and cross out the most uninteresting ones; then asked to explain their choice.





Result The



direction of ‚ÄúAttracting investments‚Äù turned out to be the most unclaimed, as in Russia this area is still underdeveloped and there are no corresponding laws that would allow productive work in this direction. Leasing and insurance services were also not the most interesting, because perceived as a waste of money, which is not permissible for the farmer. These directions went to the end of the backlog.



After several iterations, we prioritized the list of services and were able to see the big picture: in the top were goods for agricultural producers, staff recruitment and information services. So there was an understanding of the first MVP, which includes now:



  • Marketplace with goods for agricultural producers
  • Staff recruitment
  • Agricultural News
  • Crop picker


Case 3. Testing a prototype service for staff recruitment



We appreciated the importance of quickly receiving feedback from users after the very first testing of a prototype service for finding employees in the agricultural sector.



The very first version of the resume page looked like this:





During testing, we found out:



  • In the field of agriculture, there are no clear job titles; farmers often look for just a ‚Äúperson‚Äù with a certain set of skills from different areas. For example, you often need a person who can graze goats, and fertilize, and repair equipment - "do everything." Farmers are literally numb before the search line, as They didn‚Äôt know how to call such a post.
  • One of the most important parameters when looking for an employee: willingness to live with the employer. This is due to the irregular work schedule on the farm: depending on the season, work can begin at 5 or 7 in the morning and go on breaks until the evening. In addition, farms are often far from settlements and it is problematic to get to work without their own cars. Therefore, many farmers provide housing directly on the farm.
  • Farmers are willing to hire couples or formed teams. This is convenient for both employers and job seekers: it is more convenient to solve the problem of housing for the duration of the work. In addition, working with loved ones creates a favorable atmosphere in the team and increases efficiency.


Result



Based on the results of the study, we made the following changes:



  • We processed the resume card and moved away from the traditional concept when there is a resume title (it is also the name of the desired position) and, secondarily, there is a list of candidate skills (as, for example, implemented on hh). We placed the skills in a prominent place in the search results and on the resume page, and also added the function of searching for skills.
  • Added an opportunity for the employer - indicate the availability of housing and living conditions; for the applicant - indicate the need for housing at the time of work.
  • When creating a resume, they added the ability to indicate the number of people who are looking for work. And also added a filter for the employer - now the farmer can immediately indicate how many staff he needs.

Case 4. Testing the prototype of the service News



Unexpected insights were found even when testing a seemingly fairly simple news service.



The first version of the service looked like this:





We found out the following:



  • The main page of our news portal did not ‚Äúcatch on‚Äù - it looked like a regular news site, where agricultural news is not usually published.
  • Farmers are interested in news (they even have to keep abreast of news so as not to miss new laws and changes), but they rarely have time to read them. They are constantly on the run and often they don‚Äôt even have a couple of minutes to leaf through the tape in the social. networks or a news site, unlike city dwellers, who can carve out for this time, for example, while going down in the elevator or standing in line.
  • Due to the ‚Äúon the run‚Äù lifestyle, the whole life of the farmer is concentrated in the smartphone. Accordingly, the picture "the farmer reads the news from the computer" is clearly unrealistic.
  • Again, due to lack of time, there is a need to find out news on the necessary topics as quickly as possible. Endless news feeds did not impress our practical and rational users at all :)


Result



What changed after processing the main page of the news portal:



  • We introduced a flexible tag system that performs several functions at once: 1. Show that the portal covers news topics relevant to farmers; 2. They make it possible to quickly create the necessary selection of news on a topic of interest.
  • They ‚Äúcleaned‚Äù the page, made it more clear and concise, removed large pictures.
  • So that farmers could not waste time reading the entire news feed, they added a quick filter by popularity - ‚ÄúThey often read‚Äù, which contains all the main agronomy news for the last month.
